# photo-packs — market plan (researched, cited)

> **Money protocol:** every channel below needs an OWNER account and OWNER-supplied
> full-res files uploaded OFF this public repo. No agent spends money, opens
> accounts, publishes, or uploads. Each channel ends in a six-field OWNER-ACTION.
> Numbers are cited inline; anything not cited is marked **(not verified)**.

> **Conservative expectation across ALL channels: $0–30 in the first 90 days
> without an existing audience.** Stock licensing in particular pays pennies-to-cents
> per download and needs volume the owner does not have. Nothing below is overstated.

---

## (a) Gumroad / Ko-fi wallpaper packs ($3–10; 4K / phone / desktop / ultrawide bundles)

**Platform economics (cited):**
- **Gumroad:** flat **10% platform fee + $0.50 per transaction** on direct sales;
  payment processing (~2.9% + $0.30) brings the practical total to roughly
  **~12.9% + $0.80 per sale**. Products surfaced via Gumroad's **Discover**
  marketplace are charged **30%** (processing included). Since Jan 1 2025 Gumroad
  acts as merchant of record and handles sales tax within the 10%.
  Sources: <https://gumroad.com/pricing>, <https://gumroad.com/help/article/66-gumroads-fees>
- **Ko-fi:** **0% on tips; 5% on Shop / Memberships / Commissions** on the free plan
  (plus normal ~3% + $0.30 processor fee). **Ko-fi Gold ($12/mo)** drops the 5% to
  **0%** and only pays for itself above ~$240/mo of shop revenue.
  Sources: <https://ko-fi.com/pricing>, <https://help.ko-fi.com/hc/en-us/articles/360002506494-Does-Ko-fi-take-a-fee>

**What a sale nets (worked example, cited fees applied):** a $5 pack sold direct on
Gumroad nets roughly $5 − ($0.50 + 10%) − (~2.9% + $0.30) ≈ **$3.56**; the same $5
pack sold via Gumroad Discover nets **$3.50** (flat 30%). On Ko-fi free, a $5 pack
nets roughly $5 − 5% − (~3% + $0.30) ≈ **$4.30**. (Arithmetic from the cited fee
schedules; per-sale net is exact only for a given processor rate.)

**Demand reality (cited):** creators report a wide spread. Some reached "$1000+"
selling wallpaper packs — but explicitly note it took a **10k+ audience and a
specific niche**. Another creator selling wallpapers on Gumroad made **$10 total**.
Sources: <https://mtgproductions.medium.com/i-made-nearly-2000-on-gumroad-selling-wallpaper-packs-13d1b4b77ff6>,
<https://shakiroslann.medium.com/how-i-made-10-selling-wallpapers-on-gumroad-f56af543a53e>
The honest read: without an audience, the **$10** outcome is the realistic anchor,
not the "$1000."

> **OWNER-ACTION — Gumroad/Ko-fi pack listing**
> - **what:** open a free storefront and list one curated $3–5 wallpaper pack (phone
>   + desktop 4K + ultrawide tiers), uploading owner's full-res files to the platform.
> - **where:** gumroad.com (or ko-fi.com/shop) — owner account.
> - **how:** create account → new product → upload zip of tiers built to PACK-SPEC
>   dims → set $3–5 → tag "4k wallpaper / macro / wallpaper" for Discover browse.
> - **why:** cheapest same-day path to a first paid download; Discover gives
>   audience-free category search traffic.
> - **unblocks:** validation signal (a) — first ≥3 paid pack sales.
> - **verified-when:** the product page is live at a public URL and Gumroad/Ko-fi
>   dashboard shows it purchasable (test with a $0 or lowest-tier view, no self-buy).

## (b) Stock licensing (Adobe Stock / Shutterstock / Alamy)

**Royalty rates (cited):**
- **Adobe Stock:** **33% of the net price** per licensed photo. Worked example from
  Adobe's own docs: a $29.99/mo, 10-license plan yields **~$0.99 per licensed photo**
  ($29.99 / 10 × 33%). Minimum-royalty floors of **$0.33 (new) to $0.38 (10k+ sales)**
  per subscription license. Source: <https://helpx.adobe.com/stock/contributor/help/royalty-details.html>
- **Shutterstock:** **6 tiers, 15% → 40%**; all contributors **reset to Level 1 (15%)
  every Jan 1**. Entry-level standard subscription downloads pay roughly **$0.10–$0.38
  per download**. Sources: <https://submit.shutterstock.com/help/en/articles/10594604-how-much-will-i-be-paid-as-a-contributor-to-shutterstock>,
  <https://submit.shutterstock.com/help/en/articles/10594581-what-happens-when-i-reach-a-new-earnings-level>
- **Alamy:** everyone starts **Gold = 40%** on direct sales; drops to **Silver = 20%**
  after a year if you sell < $250 gross; **Platinum = 50%** only above $25,000 gross/yr.
  Distributor-network sales pay 20–40% of the (already reduced) net.
  Sources: <https://www.alamy.com/terms/contributor-commission-table/>, <https://www.alamy.com/blog/new-contributor-commission-structure>

**Volume reality (the blunt part):** stock is a **pennies-per-download, volume**
business. Adobe's own example tops out near **$1** per subscription license and
Shutterstock entry-level is **~$0.10**. An estimate for an *average* Shutterstock
contributor was **~$1.18 per download in 2023** (photos + video averaged — treat as
**(not verified)** for photo-only). Source: <https://xpiksapp.com/blog/how-much-shutterstock-pays-contributors/>
To clear even $30 at $0.10–$1.00 per download you need **tens to hundreds of
downloads** — implausible cold, in 90 days, without a large keyworded portfolio.

**Tailwind worth noting (cited):** Getty Images and Shutterstock have **banned
AI-generated submissions**, and multiple sources report **rising demand for
authentic, human-made photography** as AI stock floods the market — a real edge for
"shot on a real camera by a human." Sources: <https://www.digitalcameraworld.com/news/getty-and-shutterstock-are-the-latest-platforms-to-ban-ai-generated-images>,
<https://www.gwsmedia.com/articles/ai-image-generation-vs-stock-photography-what-does-future-hold>

> **OWNER-ACTION — Adobe Stock (best royalty of the three) contributor upload**
> - **what:** upload a curated, keyworded set of the macro-bug + nature photos.
> - **where:** contributor.stock.adobe.com — owner account (free to join).
> - **how:** create contributor account → pass the ID/quality review → upload with
>   accurate titles + keywords → set releases where people/property appear.
> - **why:** 33% royalty (highest reliable of the three) and keyword-search buyers
>   who are NOT the owner's audience; the human-authenticity ban on AI helps.
> - **unblocks:** validation signal (b) — first paid stock license.
> - **verified-when:** assets show "Approved/For sale" in the contributor dashboard
>   and appear in an Adobe Stock public search for their keywords.

## (c) Print-on-demand posters / postcards

**Margin reality (cited):** POD non-apparel (posters, postcards) can be set to
20–90% margin, but real per-order profit is thin. Printful's own worked example: an
8×10 framed poster costs ~$23, sells at ~$29 market average → **$5 profit, ~20.7%
margin**. On **Redbubble** the platform takes a **20% cut of your artist margin** and
typical earnings land at **$2–$6 per order**. Sources: <https://www.printful.com/blog/what-is-a-good-profit-margin-for-print-on-demand>,
<https://www.printful.com/printful-vs-redbubble>
Postcards are cheaper to make but proportionally lower absolute margin **(not
verified for exact postcard costs)**.

**Honest read:** POD adds a physical-fulfillment channel with **$2–6/order** margins
and the same cold-start traffic problem — it does not fix distribution, it just adds
a format. Lower priority than (a) for a first paid signal.

> **OWNER-ACTION — POD poster/postcard listing (optional, lower priority)**
> - **what:** list 2–3 best macro-bug / sunset images as posters + postcards.
> - **where:** Printful+own-store, or Redbubble marketplace — owner account.
> - **how:** upload print-res file → pick poster/postcard products → set retail so
>   margin ≈ $5+ → publish listing.
> - **why:** a physical format some buyers prefer; Redbubble adds marketplace browse.
> - **unblocks:** validation signal (c) — first POD poster/postcard sale.
> - **verified-when:** the listing is live and orderable and a proof/preview renders
>   correctly at print resolution (no self-purchase needed to verify listing).

## (d) Niche angle: macro-bug packs for entomology educators / nature blogs / science communicators

**Why this is the strongest wedge (reasoning, not a market-size claim):** generic
sunset/landscape wallpapers are the most-saturated corner of a huge FREE supply
(Unsplash, Pexels, OS art). **Macro insect/bug photography is far more
differentiated** — fewer good free sources, and a concrete audience that
*specifically wants* it: entomology educators, nature/science bloggers, museum &
science communicators, iNaturalist-adjacent hobbyists. Combined with the cited
**authentic-human-photography** tailwind (section b), "real macro bug shots, shot on
a camera, licensed for teaching/blogging" is a claim a $5 sunset pack cannot make.
Exact audience size and willingness-to-pay here are **(not verified)** — this is a
qualitative differentiation argument, and the kill-rule still holds ($0–30/90d cold).

> **OWNER-ACTION — direct macro-bug outreach to niche buyers**
> - **what:** offer a curated macro-bug pack (with a licensing note for
>   educational/blog reuse) directly to 3–5 nature/science-education sites.
> - **where:** email/DM to owners of entomology-education blogs, nature-blog editors,
>   science-communicator newsletters — owner outreach, owner-hosted download link.
> - **how:** short pitch + 2–3 watermarked preview images (built per PACK-SPEC) +
>   a paid or attribution-license offer; link the Gumroad/Ko-fi pack for purchase.
> - **why:** the one channel that reaches buyers who value the differentiated subject
>   over free generic wallpaper; no marketplace fee if sold via own storefront link.
> - **unblocks:** validation signal (a)/(b) via a niche audience that actually wants
>   the subject — the highest-quality path to a first paid stranger sale.
> - **verified-when:** at least one recipient replies with intent to license/buy, or a
>   sale arrives attributable to an outreach link (UTM/coupon code, owner-tracked).

## Channel priority (honest)
1. **(d) macro-bug niche outreach + (a) Gumroad pack** — the differentiated wedge and
   the cheapest same-day paid path. Do these first.
2. **(b) Adobe Stock** — worth uploading once (33% royalty, AI-ban tailwind) but
   pennies-per-download; a background bet, not a 90-day earner.
3. **(c) POD posters/postcards** — optional format add; $2–6/order, same traffic
   problem. Last.

If no paid signal (any of a/b/c) arrives within 90 days OR agent effort exceeds the
