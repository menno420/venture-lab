# YA Novels — Intake (honest scope)

**What this is:** an original young-adult novel category for the venture-lab creative portfolio. First title: *The Undertow* — a complete ~27,781-word dystopian sci-fi novella.

**What it cost (this title, honest):** ~0.45M tokens metered end-to-end — recon, parallel drafting of 15 chapters across five writers, a full-manuscript continuity/polish pass, and packaging. This is well above the ~150k advisory cap; a complete ~28k-word novella with a thorough continuity pass is inherently large (comparable to the three-language Star Pirates build). Reported honestly, not padded.

**Market scope (plain):** YA is a large, durable trade-fiction market, but it is crowded and hit-driven; a single novella is a portfolio sample, not a revenue guarantee. Realistic paths: (a) develop to a full novel and query traditional YA, or (b) self-publish a polished, cover-designed edition (e.g. KDP). Both require owner spend and decisions (editing, cover, listing).

**What's owner-gated:** cover art, professional line-edit, ISBN, pricing, and every publish/listing path. This seat produced the manuscript only — no accounts touched, no money moved, no images generated, no earnings claimed.
