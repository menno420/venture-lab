# Hollowtide

> **Status:** `draft`

A complete standalone dark-fantasy thriller novella for young adults (ages 14–17). ~28,595 words, 16 chapters. Book wave 2 — original premise, no derivative material; deliberately distinct from the "Lull" dream-series (no dreams, no memory-erasure, no faction war, no portal-world; third-person past tense rather than Lull's first-person).

## Pitch

In the moorland mining town of Nethercote, every child is born carrying a small living warmth beneath the sternum — a *wick* — and the town's joined light is the only thing keeping the old, hungry dark in the flooded mine below asleep. When a wick is snuffed you go *hollow*: cold, quiet, and drawn down into the Deepings within three nights. Everyone says the dark simply *chooses*.

It doesn't. Sixteen-year-old Wren Cawley learns the town's lamplighters have secretly fed their hollowed children to the thing below for generations to buy the rest a little more light — and that her nine-year-old brother Fen was chosen before his wick ever went out. With three nights and no help, Wren goes down into the dark alone to bring him back, and discovers the one thing the dark has never been able to snuff: a light that refuses to stand alone.

## Content note

Dark is the point; gratuitous is not. On the page: child endangerment and peril; the eerie "hollowing" (people going cold and empty); grief and bereavement; the restrained, non-graphic deaths of family and allies, including one sacrificial death. Not on the page: sexual content, graphic gore, torture. Appropriate for the 14–17 band — closer to a folk-horror-tinged fantasy than to splatter.
