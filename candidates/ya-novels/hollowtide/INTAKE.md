# INTAKE (lite) — Hollowtide

> **Status:** `draft`

Owner-directed creative candidate (book wave 2). Lite intake — this is portfolio fiction, not a productized kit; the honest commercial view is below, no overstatement.

- **What it is:** a complete ~28,595-word standalone YA dark-fantasy thriller novella (16 chapters), original premise, English.
- **Revenue today:** **$0.** No accounts, no listing, no external publish. Every publish path is owner-gated (see money protocol). Nothing here has earned or can earn until the owner chooses to publish.
- **First-ten-customers path (if pursued, owner-gated):** the realistic routes are (a) self-publish the novella as a low-priced ebook / serialize on a reading platform to test pull, (b) use it as a proof-of-voice sample toward a traditional YA query/agent path, or (c) bundle it into a larger fiction pack. None of these is executed here — they are options for an owner LAUNCH decision, with conservative earnings and payback to be costed at that time.
- **Kill rule:** if, on an owner read, the premise/voice doesn't clear the bar for a publish attempt, this parks as a portfolio sample (writing-quality proof) rather than a product — it scores down, it doesn't linger as a false "revenue" item.
- **Distribution-first honesty:** fiction is a hard, slow, crowded market; treat any future revenue projection as speculative and conservative. This slice's value is a finished, publishable-quality manuscript on the shelf — optionality, not booked income.
- **Token cost (this slice):** heavy — a complete 16-chapter novella is inherently token-expensive. Metered spend ran well over the ~150k soft cap the coordinator set (recon + story bible + 6 parallel chapter-writers + assembly ≈ ~330k subagent tokens). Flagged honestly rather than hidden; the deliverable is a full novella, not an outline.
