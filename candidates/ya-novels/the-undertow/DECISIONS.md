# The Undertow — Creative Decisions (decide-and-flag)

Owner delegation (2026-07-12, book wave 2): every creative call is delegated to the seat, decide-and-flag; the owner can veto any of them. This log records what was chosen and why.

## Premise & genre
Original dystopian sci-fi: air/breath as rationed currency in a sealed, drowned dome-city, enforced by a throat implant (the "reed"), with the underclass's metered collective breathing partly powering the scrubbers that ration them (the "Choir"). **Why:** a fresh, visceral, single-mechanic dystopia — breath = money = class = voice — that sidesteps the well-worn Hunger-Games/Divergent template while keeping a strong YA coming-of-age engine (Calibration at sixteen). All characters, world, and terms are original; no derivative or protected material.

## Format & length
One complete, self-contained novella, ~27,781 words, English only, 15 chapters + a short epigraph and epilogue. **Why:** the brief asked for a full story with a satisfying ending (~20–30k) the owner can read and judge — not a fragment or outline. NL/DE localization deferred as an owner-approval follow-up (out of scope this session).

## Voice & POV
First person, present tense, narrator Nari. **Why:** immediacy suits YA dystopia and lets the prose rhythm mirror breath — fragmenting when she is short of air, opening out when she breathes free — a device tied to the book's core image.

## Age band
13–17 (upper YA). **Why:** real political menace, grief, and peril, handled without graphic violence, sexual content, or profanity. See the content note in [en/README.md](en/README.md).

## Ending (deliberately not a clean victory)
The climax exposes the truth city-wide and gives the Undertow one free breath, but the Concord is cracked, not toppled; the sister is recovered but slowly; a movement is seeded. **Why:** an honest, earned, bittersweet-hopeful close reads truer than a tidy revolution and leaves natural room for the full-novel/series expansion sketched in [en/EXPANSION.md](en/EXPANSION.md).

## Names & canon
Fixed spellings for the city (Aequor), the systems (the reed, air-scrip, Calibration, R-Class, the Choir), and the cast (Nari/Narine Solenne, Tess, Juno, Orrin Vale, Calibrator Vane, Papa/Elias Solenne, Salt). **Why:** consistency across a multi-pass drafting-and-continuity process.

## Production status (owner-gated)
Manuscript only. Cover art, interior design/typesetting, ISBN, and every publish/print/listing path (KDP or otherwise) remain OWNER-ACTIONs — none performed here, no images generated, no revenue claimed.
