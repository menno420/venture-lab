## D-001 · Premise fidelity to the vetted packet (2026-07-13)

## D-002 · POV / tense / register / YA contract (2026-07-13)
- **Decision:** third-person limited, past tense, close on Iris Vane (15–16, bookbinder's daughter — her trade makes the bibliographic device native to the POV instead of info-dumped). Dark-academia atmosphere carried by texture (pewter light, pressmarks, pencil grades) with propulsive chapter end-hooks throughout.
- **Age-appropriateness:** no graphic content, no on-page violence beyond one snatched packet, no romance content, no self-harm; the menace is institutional (framing, coercion, class contempt) and the confrontations are verbal. The 1994 victim is alive, restored, and given the last private word — no dead-girl trope.
- **Emotional throughline:** found family / belonging — four members who "were not permitted to join anything else" (scholarship girl, day student, legacy boy hiding from his own name, the librarian who stayed), plus the mother/shop thread (ch. 1, 10, 14) grounding what the scholarship costs and what the trade is worth.
- **Owner veto:** open.

## D-003 · Bibliographic realism without info-dumps (2026-07-13)
- **Decision:** the marginalia device is carried by working detail in scene: annotation hands censused like shop stock (ch. 2); pencil grades and sharpening (knife-faceted wood-case line vs. 0.5 mm propelling, ch. 4); pencil-vs-ink as the Society's own law ("Ink is for owners," constitution Article Two); ownership marks as plot (Verrall armorial plates 1912 vs. the school's post-1971 printed plates — the clue that breaks the 1994 theft report); editions (1862 Macmillan *Goblin Market* first edition); pressmarks as addressing (V.7.16), accession register, conservation column, raking-light impression reading, endpaper structure, frame-as-binding. Every item is used by the plot at least once; none is delivered as a lecture.
- **Owner veto:** open.
