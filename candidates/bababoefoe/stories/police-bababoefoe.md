# The Police Bababoefoe

*Origin story · slug: `police-bababoefoe`*

The blue Bababoefoe found a little cap with a shiny badge, and the moment it put the
cap on, its heart went *thump* with a brand-new feeling: it wanted to **help**.

Not to catch anyone. Not to chase anyone. A Police Bababoefoe's whole job is much
gentler than that — it looks after the small and the lost, and it makes sure
everyone gets safely home. That is the only law in the Boefoe Burrow, and it is a
good one.

In its **hidden pocket**, the Police Bababoefoe kept a **tiny silver whistle**, no
bigger than a seed. It never used the whistle to frighten anyone. It used it the way
you'd use a lighthouse: one small, clear note that meant *this way — you're not
lost anymore.*

One misty morning, the whistle was needed.

A line of ducklings had wandered off the path — four of them, then five, then a
sixth peeping somewhere in the reeds — and their mother was turning in worried
circles. The mist was thick. The puddles all looked the same. It was exactly the
kind of morning a small thing gets lost in.

The Police Bababoefoe did not panic and did not scold. It climbed the tallest
pebble it could find, took the silver whistle from its pocket, and blew one soft,
round note into the fog. *Peep.* And it waited. *Peep.* And it waited. One by one,
the ducklings followed the sound out of the reeds, blinking, until all six were
counted and none were missing.

But the path home was long, and the ducklings were tired, and the mist had not
lifted. So the Police Bababoefoe did what Bababoefoes do best. It reached out its
paw — magnetic, like every Bababoefoe paw — and took the hand of a friend, who took
another, until a **boefoe-chain** stretched along the whole muddy path like a warm
blue railing. The ducklings walked safely down the middle of the chain, held on
both sides, all the way to their mother.

"There," said the Police Bababoefoe, tucking the whistle back into its pocket.
"Everybody's home. That's the whole job."

It did not want a thank-you, though it got six wet duckling kisses anyway, which is
better than a thank-you.

If you ever meet a Police Bababoefoe, look for the whistle in its pocket. And if you
are ever a little lost yourself, offer it your hand — its paw will hold on, and it
knows the way home.
