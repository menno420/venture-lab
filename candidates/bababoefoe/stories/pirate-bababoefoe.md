# The Pirate Bababoefoe

*Origin story · slug: `pirate-bababoefoe`*

On the day the teal little Bababoefoe left the Boefoe Burrow, it found a hat.

Not a big hat. A small, three-cornered pirate hat, exactly Bababoefoe-sized, blown
in on the wind and caught in the grass by the door. When it tried the hat on, the
hat fit as if it had always been waiting. And when a hat fits like that, well —
there is nothing to do but become a pirate.

"But pirates need a ship," said the Bababoefoe to nobody, because it was the first
one awake.

So it made a ship out of a leaf, an acorn cap for a bucket, and a blade of grass
for a mast. It was a very small ship. It was, in fact, the smallest pirate ship in
the history of the sea, which that morning was a puddle after the rain.

The Pirate Bababoefoe did not sail the puddle to find gold. Gold is heavy and cold
and no good for hugging. It sailed to find the **best buried snack in all the
world** — and it had a map, drawn in crumbs, folded up small and tucked into its
**hidden pocket** where no seagull could steal it.

The map led across the puddle, around a pebble the size of a mountain, and up to
the foot of the old oak. There, marked with a tiny X of twigs, the Pirate
Bababoefoe dug with its stubby paws until it found the treasure: one perfect,
sun-warm blackberry, saved from last summer by a forgetful squirrel.

It was the biggest blackberry a small pirate had ever seen. Far too big to eat
alone.

So the Pirate Bababoefoe did the most pirate thing of all. It reached out one paw —
and its paw was magnetic, the way all Bababoefoe paws are — and it took the hand of
the next little critter waking at the burrow door. And that one took another hand,
and that one another, until there was a whole **boefoe-chain** stretching from the
oak all the way home, passing the blackberry paw to paw to paw.

"A treasure's no treasure," said the Pirate Bababoefoe, licking one purple whisker,
"unless the whole crew tastes it."

And it folded the crumb-map back into its hidden pocket, in case another adventure
was ever needed. There usually was.

To this day, if you find a Pirate Bababoefoe, check its pocket. There is always a
map inside. And its paw, if you offer yours, will hold on tight — because a good
pirate never sails alone.
