# The Santa Bababoefoe

*Origin story · slug: `santa-bababoefoe` · seasonal*

On the coldest, quietest night of the year, one small Bababoefoe could not sleep.

It was a brown one, round as a chestnut, and on the shelf above its little bed sat a
soft red **Santa hat** with a white bobble. It had been saving that hat all year for
exactly this night. Now it put the hat on, and — the way it always happens — a warm
and secret feeling filled it right up: tonight, it would give.

Not big gifts. A Santa Bababoefoe is far too small for chimneys and sleighs. But it
knew a secret that all the big Santas know too: the best presents are tiny, and the
best part is that nobody sees you leave them.

In its **hidden pocket** the Santa Bababoefoe carried **one small golden bell**,
just a whisper of a jingle, so quiet you had to be very still to hear it. The bell
was how it found the sleepers who needed something most — it would hold the bell up
to the cold air, and wherever the note sounded loneliest, that was where to go.

That night the bell rang softly toward the far, dark end of the burrow, where a
little critter had fallen asleep without a blanket, shivering in the frost.

The Santa Bababoefoe had no blanket to give. It had only itself, and its warm fluff,
and its paws. But a Bababoefoe is never really alone, and neither is anyone it
watches over. It reached out its paw — magnetic, like every Bababoefoe paw — and
gently woke the nearest friend, who took another paw, and another, until a whole
**boefoe-chain** of round, warm little bodies curled around the cold sleeper like
the coziest blanket ever knitted. Fluff to fluff, paw in paw, the shivering stopped.

Then the Santa Bababoefoe did the last and most secret thing. It slipped the golden
bell from its pocket and tucked it, very quietly, under the sleeper's paw — so that
in the morning they would wake to a small gift and never quite know who left it.

"That's the trick of it," the Santa Bababoefoe whispered to the frost. "You give the
warm thing *and* the little thing, and then you tiptoe away."

And it did tiptoe away, back to its own bed, hat still on, and finally — finally —
it slept.

If you ever meet a Santa Bababoefoe, do not look too hard for the bell in its
pocket. It may already be gone. That means it found someone who needed it more.
