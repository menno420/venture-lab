# Included — file manifest

Every file in the Kill-Rule Intake Kit and what it does. Template files use
`[[fill: ...]]` slots where your candidate's specifics belong.

## Pack contents

| file | what it does |
| --- | --- |
| `pack/INTAKE-TEMPLATE.md` | The **fillable intake**: one-line pitch, what-it-is, the weighted scoring table, the three binding kill-rule fields (validation signal, first-ten-customers path, max effort budget), a conservative revenue line, a "Why this might fail" section you must fill honestly, and a gated launch-actions stub. Copy per candidate. |
| `pack/SCORING-RUBRIC.md` | The **distribution-first weighted rubric** behind the table: the five axes (distribution 35%, buildability 20%, launch-effort 15%, speed-to-first-dollar 15%, WTP/moat 15%), what 0–5 means on each, the anti-gaming rules, and the worked arithmetic. |
| `pack/KILL-RULES.md` | The **one-page binding discipline**: why intakes precede builds, the three fields as a unit, over-budget-without-signal = ledgered negative, and the honesty bar for "Why this might fail". |
| `pack/NEGATIVE-LEDGER-TEMPLATE.md` | The **negative-result ledger** entry template: what died, which kill condition fired, actual spend vs budget, and the lesson — so a killed idea stays killed. |
| `pack/examples/EXAMPLE-1-borderline-build.md` | Worked intake for a niche developer cookbook that scored **3.55/5** and (barely) earned its build — shows honest low scores on distribution and shelf life surviving next to a "build" verdict. |
| `pack/examples/EXAMPLE-2-honest-longshot.md` | Worked intake for a free-tool-plus-paid-companion funnel experiment that scored **3.10/5** and names itself the low-confidence option — shows the discipline of writing "realistically ~$0" about your own idea. |

## Provenance

The templates and rules are the working documents of a real
autonomous-agent revenue lane, generalized for any builder. The two
examples are adapted (redacted and genericized) from that lane's actual
candidate intakes — including their unflattering scores and their honest
"why this might fail" sections. Nothing is invented for marketing effect.
