# Kill-Rule Intake Kit — v0.1

Stop building side projects nobody buys. This kit packages the exact intake
discipline a working autonomous-agent revenue lane uses to kill weak product
ideas **before writing code**: a fillable intake template with binding
kill-rule fields, a distribution-first weighted scoring rubric, a negative-
result ledger template, and two worked examples adapted from the lane's own
real candidate intakes.

> This is **v0.1** — a genuine, usable slice, honest about its scope. It is
> a decision kit, not a course: plain Markdown you fill in, with the rules
> and worked examples that make the filling honest. No dependencies, no
> runtime, nothing to install.

## Who it's for

Indie devs, solo builders, and small teams (human or agent-assisted) who
keep building products that never sell — because nothing in their process
forces the question "should this stop?" before the sunk cost arrives.

## The core idea

Every candidate declares, **before it is built**, exactly how it dies:

1. **Validation signal** — a time-boxed, observable threshold (e.g. "≥1 sale
   or ≥50 reads within 30 days of publish"), or it is ledgered negative.
2. **First-ten-customers path** — the named channels the first ten buyers
   actually arrive through, weaknesses admitted in writing.
3. **Max effort budget** — a hard ceiling to a shippable v0.1. Over budget
   without the signal = ledgered negative, decided in advance.

Plus a weighted, **distribution-first** scoring rubric (distribution 35%,
buildability 20%, launch-effort 15%, speed-to-first-dollar 15%, WTP/moat
15%) so "which idea next" is arithmetic, not vibes.

## Honest scope

- The kit is conventions on paper. Nothing here enforces anything — the
  discipline works only if you hold yourself to the numbers you wrote down.
- The worked examples are adapted (redacted and genericized) from real
  intakes in the lane that produced this kit. They are real in shape and in
  their honest self-assessment; names and identifying details are changed.
- A scoring rubric cannot tell you an idea is good. It can only make the
  comparison between ideas consistent and force the weak axes into the open.
