# Quickstart — Kill-Rule Intake Kit

Run one candidate through the full intake in about 20 minutes. Plain
Markdown, no dependencies, no runtime.

## The fill order (five steps)

1. **Read the rules once.** `pack/KILL-RULES.md` is one page. The whole kit
   is that page; everything else is scaffolding to make you actually do it.

2. **Copy the template.** Copy `pack/INTAKE-TEMPLATE.md` to
   `candidates/<your-idea>/INTAKE.md` (or wherever your project notes live).
   One intake file per candidate — never a shared list.

3. **Score before you argue.** Open `pack/SCORING-RUBRIC.md` side-by-side
   and fill the scoring table FIRST, weights as given (distribution is 35%
   on purpose — it is where side projects die). Show the arithmetic in the
   total row. If you catch yourself nudging a score up to save the idea,
   that is the rubric working.

4. **Write the kill-rule fields.** All of them, before any building:
   validation signal (deadline + trigger + concrete thresholds),
   first-ten-customers path (named channels, weaknesses admitted), max
   effort budget (a number, not "we'll see"). An intake that cannot state
   its own kill conditions has not earned the build.

5. **Compare against the examples.** Read both files in `pack/examples/`.
   If your intake sounds more confident than the borderline-build example
   while scoring lower, rewrite the "Why this might fail" section until it
   would survive a hostile reader.

## When a candidate dies

Copy `pack/NEGATIVE-LEDGER-TEMPLATE.md` into your ledger and fill it the
same day. A kill that isn't ledgered gets rebuilt six months later by
someone (possibly you) who forgot why it died.

## When a candidate lives

Build to the budget, watch the deadline, and judge it by the intake's own
numbers — not by how the launch felt.
