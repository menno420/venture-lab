# Scoring rubric — distribution-first, weighted 0–5

Five axes, fixed weights, one weighted total. The weights encode the
lesson most side projects die of: **distribution beats product**. Score
each axis 0–5 using the anchors below, multiply by the weight, show the
arithmetic in the total row. Never change the weights per-candidate.

| Axis | Weight | The question |
|---|---|---|
| Distribution | **35%** | Is there a named, reachable surface where buyers with this pain already look — and can YOU reach it? |
| Buildability | **20%** | Can you ship a real v0.1 with the skills/tools you have, without new accounts, hires, or unknowns? |
| Launch-effort cost | **15%** | How many manual, gated, or high-friction steps stand between "built" and "purchasable"? |
| Speed to first dollar | **15%** | How soon after starting could the FIRST dollar arrive (not scale — the first dollar)? |
| WTP / moat | **15%** | Will this audience actually PAY, and is there anything a free substitute can't copy in an afternoon? |

## Anchors (what 0–5 means)

**Distribution (35%)**
- 5 — an owned or proven channel with demonstrated pull (an audience, a
  ranking page, a marketplace category you already convert in).
- 3 — a real, named channel exists but is crowded, low-intent, or gated by
  someone else's approval (communities with self-promo rules, directories).
- 1 — "I'll post it and people will find it."
- 0 — no named channel at all.

**Buildability (20%)**
- 5 — fully buildable from what you already have; no new accounts or paid
  services needed for v0.1.
- 3 — buildable with one or two learnable unknowns or minor dependencies.
- 1 — depends on a skill, dataset, or access you do not have today.

**Launch-effort cost (15%)**
- 5 — one publish click on an existing storefront.
- 3 — several manual steps (listings, submissions, payout setup).
- 1 — approvals, reviews, compliance, or partner sign-off before day one.

**Speed to first dollar (15%)**
- 5 — sellable the week it's built.
- 3 — sellable after a build measured in weeks, or dependent on an
  upstream artifact shipping first.
- 1 — revenue only after traction on a free layer (funnel-first plays).

**WTP / moat (15%)**
- 5 — buyers already pay for worse versions of this; switching or DIY is
  genuinely painful.
- 3 — narrow but real pain; few alternatives for those who have it.
- 1 — advice, templates, or calculators with abundant free substitutes.

## Anti-gaming rules

1. **Score distribution first**, before you're attached to the total.
2. **Rationale is mandatory** per axis — a score without a sentence is a
   feeling, not a score.
3. **The two lowest scores are the honest ones.** Copy them into "Why this
   might fail" in plain words.
4. **Show the arithmetic** in the total row so a reviewer can recompute it.
5. **Never tune weights to save a candidate** (Kill Rule 4).

## Worked arithmetic (from EXAMPLE-1)

Scores: distribution 3, buildability 5, launch-effort 4, speed 3, WTP 3.

```
0.35·3 + 0.20·5 + 0.15·4 + 0.15·3 + 0.15·3
= 1.05  + 1.00  + 0.60   + 0.45   + 0.45   = 3.55
```

## Reading the total (comparative, not absolute)

There is no magic pass mark. The total ranks candidates against each
other; the kill-rule fields (deadline, path, budget) decide when a built
one dies. As a rough band from the lane this kit comes from: below ~3.0
meant "do not build without a named exception in writing"; 3.0–3.5 meant
"borderline — only with a tight budget"; above 3.5 meant "best available
candidate", which is still not a promise anyone will buy it.
