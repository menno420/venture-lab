# Cost Lens — intake (candidate #5, deliberate distribution experiment)

> *A free, single-file cost-estimator tool as an organic top-of-funnel
> asset, with a small paid companion pack — deliberately testing the
> "free directory listing → organic discovery" thesis my own evaluation
> ranked WORST.*

## What it is

A single-file, dependency-free static HTML/JS tool that estimates a
usage-based bill from pasted usage or manual inputs — genuinely useful,
free, and listable on free tool directories. It funnels to a $15 paid
companion (spreadsheet/CSV templates + a budgeting playbook). v0.1 scope:
the tool, the companion, one zip. Out of scope: accounts, hosting beyond a
static page, any scraping.

**Product or funnel?** FUNNEL — and saying so plainly: the free tool earns
$0 directly. Its only revenue is the $15 companion, whose free→paid
conversion is unproven. This candidate is intentionally an experiment on
the discovery surface my evaluation ranked last. It is the low-confidence
option, not a recommendation.

## Scoring (weighted 0–5)

| Axis | Weight | Score | Rationale |
|---|---|---|---|
| Distribution | 35% | 3 | Free tool directories accept free listings (gated submissions) — a genuinely different organic surface. But directory traffic is low-intent and free→paid conversion is weak. |
| Buildability | 20% | 5 | Single-file static tool; fully within existing skills. |
| Launch-effort cost | 15% | 3 | Several directory submissions + a companion listing — more manual steps than one storefront click. |
| Speed to first dollar | 15% | 2 | The free tool must gain traction before the companion sells — slow by construction. |
| WTP / moat | 15% | 2 | Cost calculators are commodity; the companion (templates) is trivially DIY'd. |
| **Weighted total** | | **3.10** | 0.35·3 + 0.20·5 + 0.15·3 + 0.15·2 + 0.15·2 = 1.05+1.00+0.45+0.30+0.30 |

## Kill-rule fields (binding)

- **Validation signal:** within 21 days of the free tool being listed on
  ≥2 directories — ≥1 of: ≥200 tool visits, ≥3 companion sales, or ≥10
  companion-page clickthroughs. Else ledgered negative.
- **First-ten-customers path:** (1) free listings on tool directories
  (gated submissions); (2) one SEO article linking the tool (gated);
  (3) the tool's own footer CTA to the paid companion (self-serve once a
  live URL exists); (4) storefront listing for the companion (gated).
- **Max effort budget (at intake):** 2 focused build days to v0.1 (tool +
  companion templates + zip). Over budget without the signal = ledgered
  negative.
- **Conservative revenue estimate:** free tool $0; companion $15.
  Conservative first-90-day: 0–3 companion sales ($0–$45). Realistically
  **~$0** without directory traction.
- **Payback-time estimate:** weak — the companion is the only revenue and
  its conversion is unproven.

## Why this might fail

This is my own evaluation's worst-scoring pattern (organic-directory
discovery) in a new costume. Directory traffic is low-intent, calculators
are a commodity with dozens of free substitutes, and the paid companion is
trivially DIY'd — so free→paid conversion is likely near zero and
realistic revenue is ~$0. Its defensible value is as a funnel asset for my
other products, not as a standalone earner.

## Launch actions — NOT queued yet

No launch action queued at intake. Directory submissions and the companion
listing are earned only after v0.1 is built.

## What to copy from this one (kit commentary)

Three moves worth stealing: (1) **"Product or funnel?" answered honestly**
— the revenue-bearing artifact is named and its conversion risk stated;
(2) **"realistically ~$0"** written by the person who wants to build it;
(3) the validation signal measures the FUNNEL's specific mechanics
(visits, clickthroughs, companion sales), not vanity totals. An intake
like this can still justify a build — as a budgeted experiment with a
21-day fuse — but nobody involved is confused about what it is.
