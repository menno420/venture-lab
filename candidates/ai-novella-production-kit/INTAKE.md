## What it is

The generalized distillation of THIS repo's live fiction-production
lane: the one-session ~15k-word manuscript method that produced 16
complete manuscripts (10 adult titles / 11 EN manuscripts, 5 YA, 1 MG
— all committed, word counts honest `wc -w` measurements cited to
merged PRs), the series-bible `CANON.md` convention that keeps
multi-book continuity an O(1) lookup, the length-band + edition-variant
practice (novella cuts, serial editions, translations, large-print
EDITION-SPECs derived from one base manuscript), the dead-session
recovery path proven by a real production death (PR #157 died mid-turn
with zero manuscript words pushed; resumed as PR #159 and merged at a
complete, measured 15,995 words), and the fiction vetting-packet
grammar that gates "written" from "publishable." Ships as: README +
QUICKSTART + INCLUDED + 7 guide chapters (each ending in a
PROVENANCE-FOOTER citing committed `file@sha` sources) + `templates/`
starter files (CANON template, chapter-plan, vetting-packet skeleton,
recovery checklist, edition-variant matrix) + allow-list `package.sh` →
`dist/ai-novella-production-kit-vX.Y.zip`. Deliberately NOT the books:
no manuscript text ships; fleet-specific order text, session ids, and
trigger ids are stripped; the templates are blank-slate starters.

## Why this might fail

The batch's own words, plus what the build cannot fix: **WTP 2.5 is
the weak axis and it is structural.** The AI-writing space is thick
with cheap prompt packs and courses — buyers demonstrably pay for
worse versions of this, but free substitutes are endless, most advice
on "finishing your AI novel" costs nothing, and the method itself is
copyable in an afternoon by anyone who reads the listing carefully.
The moat is the receipts — 16 finished manuscripts with PR-cited word
counts — and receipts are only verifiable by people who read public
repos, a skill the target buyer (a novelist, not a developer) may not
have and may not value. The channel is UNPROVEN in the strongest
sense: the lane has never posted, listed, or sold anything to writers;
distribution 3 was scored on the surface existing, not on us reaching
it, and the proven sibling channel (agent-ops) has produced 0 organic
sales across an eleven-product click-queued catalog. There is also a
method-without-the-agent gap: the workflow was executed by coordinated
coding-agent sessions with a CI gate and a repo discipline behind
them; a buyer without a Claude-Code-style fleet — most of the target
audience — gets the templates and the discipline but not the executor,
so the kit must honestly sell "structure that survives your tool"
rather than "push-button novel," and some buyers will want the button.
Finally the buyer may want finished fiction, not a method for
finishing fiction — the adjacent purchase (a $3.99 novella) is cheaper
than the $29 method. Score 3.525 tops a batch whose better draws are
already spent; band-edge builds fail more often than they pay.
