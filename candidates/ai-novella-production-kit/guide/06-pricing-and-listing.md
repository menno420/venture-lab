# 6. Pricing and listing the finished book

## Price from the band, not from hope

The production catalog prices every title from a **verified category
band** — comparable titles actually observed at those prices, cited
with links and access dates in the plan document — then picks a point
in the band with the royalty arithmetic written out. The worked
pattern for adult/literary novellas: band $3.99–$5.99 inside the
platform's 70%-royalty range ($2.99–$9.99); a seasonal gift-adjacent
title priced at the floor ($3.99 ≈ $2.79/sale at 70%); and the
explicit rejection of $0.99 impulse pricing because dropping to the
35% tier means 70%-of-$3.99 beats 35%-of-$0.99 at any plausible
volume ratio. Whatever your genre's numbers are, the *procedure*
transfers: verify the band, cite it, do the tier arithmetic, write
the reasoning down where the next book can reuse it.

## The subtitle is a tool, not decoration

The collision scan (chapter 4) often resolves through the subtitle. A
title whose exact phrase is crowded by non-fiction (a historical
common noun, a place name) can keep its resonant title if a
fiction-signalling subtitle is made MANDATORY and every keyword
carries a fiction marker — the production catalog shipped exactly
this mitigation, recorded as a locked decision, not a suggestion.

## Keyword tiling across a catalog

One book picks keywords; a catalog *allocates* them. The production
convention is a catalog-level keyword map — one row per phrase,
phrase → owning title — consulted at listing time so that titles tile
the search space instead of cannibalizing each other. The moves that
recur: era/theme *sub-nodes* under a parent category another title
owns (Victorian under Historical Fiction); register fences doubling
as keyword fences (the cozy-mystery stems belong to the cozy mystery,
the folk-dark stems to the dark seasonal title); conflicts recorded
and decided, not discovered live in a storefront form.

## Seasonality is a launch parameter

If the book has a sales window, the packet states it plainly ("≈ late
November – early January; near-dormant otherwise") and the launch
plan works backward from it (publish by mid-November or park until
next year). A seasonal title mis-launched in February pays a full
year of carrying cost for the impatience.

## Listing copy from the finished text

Blurbs are drafted at concept stage but REWRITTEN from the finished
manuscript before listing — keeping every promise the concept blurb
made (the promise manifest cuts both ways: the blurb's phrases must
exist in the book, and the book's register must not contradict the
blurb). Categories: two, chosen against the keyword map. Keywords:
seven, every one carrying the register the collision scan demanded.

## Honesty block

The catalog behind these numbers has **zero verified book sales** —
its pricing discipline is verified-by-citation (real comps, real tier
arithmetic, real collision scans), not verified-by-revenue. That is
exactly the honest state of most careful self-publishing plans before
launch, and the kill rules (chapter 7) are what keep that state from
quietly becoming permanent.
