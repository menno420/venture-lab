# AI Novella Production Kit

Get from "the model writes promising openings" to "a finished,
publishable ~15,000-word manuscript exists" — repeatably.

This kit is the production method distilled from a repository whose
fiction lane shipped **16 complete manuscripts** (10 adult titles, 5
YA, 1 middle-grade — every one committed, every word count an honest
`wc -w` measurement cited to a merged pull request): the one-session
drafting method and its written-down decisions, the series-bible
`CANON.md` convention that keeps multi-book continuity honest, the
length-band and edition-variant strategy that turns one manuscript
into a product line, the recovery protocol proven by a real mid-run
death (a session that died with zero words pushed was resumed to a
merged 15,995-word manuscript), the vetting-packet grammar that gates
"written" from "publishable," and the kill rules that stop you writing
book five for an audience that never bought book one.

**What this kit is NOT: it is not the books.** No manuscript text
ships in this kit — the manuscripts are separate products. You are
buying the method, the templates, and the worked-example *structure*,
with every claim cited to the committed production files it was
distilled from.

## What's inside

- `QUICKSTART.md` — set up your first production run in ~20 minutes.
- `INCLUDED.md` — full file inventory.
- `guide/` — seven chapters, each ending in a Sources footer citing
  the committed production files it was distilled from:
  1. **The one-session method** — chapter files, honest word counts,
     the promise manifest, register fences, and the DECISIONS record.
  2. **The series bible (CANON.md)** — laws byte-exact, hooks with
     coordinates, canon recovery as an O(1) lookup.
  3. **Length bands and editions** — pick a band and honor it; derive
     novella cuts, serials, translations, and large print from ONE
     base manuscript.
  4. **The publish gate** — the vetting-packet grammar that separates
     "a draft exists" from "this can be listed" (a planned standalone
     kit, folded in here as a chapter instead — the honest verdict was
     that it's a subset of this method, not its own product).
  5. **Dead-session recovery** — forward-only continuity; what to do
     when a drafting run dies mid-flight, from a real worked case.
  6. **Pricing and listing the finished book** — price bands, the
     subtitle-as-collision-mitigation move, keyword tiling across a
     catalog, seasonal windows.
  7. **Kill rules for fiction** — validation signals, kill clocks,
     and the negative ledger, applied to books.
- `templates/` — five blank-slate starters you copy per book or
  series: a CANON series bible, a chapter plan, a vetting-packet
  skeleton, a recovery checklist, and an edition-variant matrix.

## Requirements

- Any AI writing assistant that can hold a chapter brief — the method
  is tool-agnostic — plus a plain-text editor. Word counts assume
  `wc -w` or any equivalent counter. Version control (git) is
  strongly recommended and assumed by the recovery chapter, but
  chapters 1–4 and 6–7 work without it.

## What this is NOT

- **Not finished fiction, and not a fiction generator.** No
  manuscript text ships here, and nothing in this kit executes. The
  method was run in production by coordinated coding-agent sessions;
  if your setup is one person and one chat window, you get the
  discipline and the templates, not the automation — chapter 1 says
  exactly which parts transfer.
- Not a prompt pack. There are no "magic prompts" — the kit's claim
  is that finishing is a *structure* problem (bands, briefs, gates,
  recovery), not a prompting problem.
- Not a publishing service and not marketing advice beyond the gate:
  the pricing chapter shows how the catalog priced and tiled, with
  the sources cited; your storefront clicks are your own.

## Provenance

Distilled from committed, production-run files in the seller's own
repository; every chapter's Sources footer cites them at `file@sha`,
and the manuscript word counts are cited to the merged pull requests
that landed them. Claims are verified by citation — audit the footers
yourself. Where a quote from a series bible appears, it is a sentence
or two at most, cited, and there to show the *shape* of the artifact
— never to resell the content.
