## 2. Recent quarters — revenue, margins, foundry

**Q1 FY2026 (reported 2026-04-23):**

- **Revenue: $13.6B, +7% YoY**, beating the ~$12.4B Wall Street consensus.
- Segment mix: **Client Computing (CCG) $7.7B (+1% YoY)**, **Data Center & AI (DCAI) $5.1B (+22% YoY)**,
  **Intel Foundry $5.4B (+16% YoY)**; external foundry revenue was **$174M**.
- **Intel Foundry operating loss: $2.4B**, improved ~$72M QoQ as better Intel 4/3/18A yields lifted
  gross margin.
- **GAAP gross margin 39.4%** (up from 36.9%); **non-GAAP operating margin 12.3%** (up from 5.4% YoY).
- **GAAP net loss $4.28B (−$0.73/sh)**, widened YoY, driven primarily by restructuring charges.
  ([cnbc.com, 2026-04-23](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/04/23/intel-intc-q1-2026-earnings-report.html) · [futurumgroup.com Q1 FY2026](https://futurumgroup.com/insights/intel-q1-fy-2026-earnings-point-to-agentic-cpu-demand-and-foundry-upside/) · [fool.com Q1 2026 transcript](https://www.fool.com/earnings/call-transcripts/2026/04/23/intel-intc-q1-2026-earnings-transcript/), accessed 2026-07-12)

**Read (descriptive):** top line and margins are improving and DCAI/foundry revenue are growing,
but **the foundry is still a multi-billion-dollar quarterly loss** and the company remains
GAAP-loss-making on restructuring. The bull case is a foundry/18A ramp; the risk is execution and
market share.

## 5. Volatility profile

- **ATR:** **ATR(14) ≈ 5.52** (dated 2026-05-05); **ATR(20)% ≈ 5.17%**. Against a ~$110 price, that is
  an **ATR% of roughly ~5%** — i.e. Intel's *typical* recent daily true range is on the order of ~5%
  of price. This is a **high-volatility** profile for a mega-cap.
  ([barchart.com/stocks/quotes/INTC/technical-analysis](https://www.barchart.com/stocks/quotes/INTC/technical-analysis) · [macroaxis.com INTC ATR](https://www.macroaxis.com/stocks/Volatility-Indicators/Average-True-Range/INTC), accessed 2026-07-12)
- **Recent single-session data point:** on 2026-07-10 INTC closed **−2.40%** with an **intraday range
  of ~3.16%** ($107.45–$110.85).
  ([historicaloptiondata.com INTC 2026-07-10](https://historicaloptiondata.com/intc-trading-analysis-07-10-2026-1019-am/), accessed 2026-07-12)
- **Frequency of ±1–2% days (the owner's target move size):** with ATR% ≈ 5%, a **±1–2% day is
  *smaller* than Intel's typical daily range**, so days that move at least ±1–2% are the norm rather
  than the exception — INTC routinely exceeds ±2% (the 2026-07-10 −2.40% close is one such day). The
  **exact frequency/percentage of sessions landing in the ±1–2% band** could not be sourced from a
  clean, citable histogram: `[unverified — could not source an exact ±1–2%-day frequency count as of
  2026-07-12]`. The defensible, ATR-grounded statement is: *±1–2% intraday moves are well within
  Intel's normal daily range and occur very frequently.*

These map directly to the mechanical anchor-rotation trigger specced in `INTAKE.md` §G (they are
**descriptions**, not predictions):
