## Kept vs dropped (vs the picture book)

**Kept:** Juno and Wick/Pit/Docht; the tiny yellow door at the lighthouse foot; the teacup-sized town; the dark Great Lantern; the three-things quest (spark = glow-beetle, thread = spider silver, wind = shell) with its built-in counting refrain (one, two, three); the tower climb; the relighting; the berry-sized keepsake lantern; the ONE core emotional beat — small Juno makes the cold little town warm and bright again. The counting structure and "up, up, up" climb give toddlers the chant-along hooks.

**Dropped:** the knocking-is-polite aside, toast-and-candlewax smells, and star keyhole detail; the townsfolk exposition ("folks lose their way and their toes go cold"); the crumbling-step peril and the "brave is just scared, still walking" line (the explicit bravery thesis is a 4–8 payoff; peril softened out for 0–3); "It had all been real" wonder-was-it-real ending (replaced by a direct goodnight close, making it usable as a bedtime book); Wick's dialogue beyond the setup; the tiny band and celebration detail.

This is a standalone read-aloud — find a door, count to three, climb up, make light, goodnight. A toddler needs no knowledge of the picture book.
