# The Lantern Door — Board-Book Edition

- **Title:** The Lantern Door (board-book cut)
- **Language:** English (master — voice source of truth)
- **Age band:** board book, ages 0–3
- **Format:** 12 spreads, standalone toddler read-aloud — NOT a summary of the picture book
- **Word count:** honest `wc -w` per language in [`NOTES.md`](NOTES.md)
- **Pitch:** Juno finds a tiny door, a tiny town, and one dark lantern — and with one, two, three little things, she lights it up again.

> Version of the ages 4–8 picture book (`../../the-lantern-door.en.md`). Same characters (Juno, Wick), one core beat kept: little Juno relights the Great Lantern and makes the tiny town warm again. Every spread carries an illustration note in italics; the seat generates no art.

---

[Spread 1]
*Illustration: The mossy foot of a tall lighthouse at dusk; a tiny butter-yellow door; Juno crouching, knuckles raised.*
At the bottom of the big lighthouse, Juno finds a tiny yellow door. Knock, knock, knock!

[Spread 2]
*Illustration: The little door wide open, warm gold light spilling out; a teacup-sized town glowing inside.*
The little door swings open. Warm golden light! Hello, tiny town.

[Spread 3]
*Illustration: Juno now tiny on a cobbled lane, greeted by Wick with his soot-smudged nose and long lamp-pole.*
Juno steps through — and now Juno is tiny too! "Hello! I'm Wick."

[Spread 4]
*Illustration: The dark tower looming over shivering townsfolk in scarves; a thimble-sized cat at Juno's ankle.*
"The Great Lantern went out," says Wick. "Brrr. The whole town is dark and cold."

[Spread 5]
*Illustration: Wick counting on three fingers before a chalk sketch: a spark, a thread, a curl of wind.*
"We need three little things: a spark, a thread, a puff of wind. Can you find them, Juno?"

[Spread 6]
*Illustration: A moonlit moss meadow; Juno cupping her hands around a sleepy glow-beetle.*
In the moss meadow, a sleepy glow-beetle blinks. A spark! That's one.

[Spread 7]
*Illustration: Under a little stone bridge, a friendly spider spinning silver into Juno's fingers.*
Under the bridge, a friendly spider spins. A silver thread! That's two.

[Spread 8]
*Illustration: A windy hilltop; Juno catching a gust inside a curled seashell, hair streaming.*
On the windy hill — whoosh! — Juno catches wind in a shell. That's three!

[Spread 9]
*Illustration: The spiral stair winding up into the dark; Juno climbing knee-high steps, Wick right behind.*
Now up the tower. Up, up, up! Big steps. Brave Juno. Up, up, up!

[Spread 10]
*Illustration: At the top, the Great Lantern blazing to life — gold light everywhere.*
Thread... spark... whoooosh! The Great Lantern wakes up. Gold! Warm! Bright!

[Spread 11]
*Illustration: Light pouring over every rooftop; tiny townsfolk cheering with pink cheeks; Wick spinning happily.*
Light in every window. Light in every lane. The little town is warm again. Hooray for Juno!

[Spread 12]
*Illustration: Juno big again outside in the dusk, a berry-sized lantern glowing in her pocket; the tiny door clicking shut.*
Back through the tiny door goes Juno. In her pocket: one tiny lantern, still warm. Goodnight, little town. Goodnight, Juno.

*The end.*
