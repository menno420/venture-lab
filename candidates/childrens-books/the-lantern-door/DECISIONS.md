# the-lantern-door — manuscript decisions

Concept source: fresh original, invented for this seat (book wave 2, owner delegation 2026-07-12, decide-and-flag — owner vetoes later). Not derived from any existing book or trademark.

> Owner-gated: everything below is a working decision made under delegated authority. The owner may veto or redirect any of it. Where a real-world commitment is implied (art, print, ISBN, pricing, distribution), nothing has been actioned — see the final section.

## Age band

Read-aloud picture book, ages 4–8. Sentences kept short and rhythmic for a lap-reader; one clear emotional beat per spread. A single, gentle wobble (the crumbling step, Spread 11) is the only tension — resolved within the same spread so it stays adventurous, never frightening. Home-safe-by-bedtime frame reinforces the cozy contract.

## Length & structure

14 spreads. Clean quest architecture: discovery (1–4) → problem (5) → the rule of three (6) → three gather-quests, one per spread (7 spark, 8 thread, 9 wind) → the climb with its one wobble (10–11) → payoff/relight (12) → celebration (13) → keepsake + safe return by bedtime (14). Each spread opens with one italic illustration note, then prose. Body word counts: EN 621, NL 666, DE 654 — all inside the 600–700 target.

## Which story

Portal/quest fantasy from the premise seed. Chosen anchor: a palm-sized butter-yellow door at the base of an old **lighthouse** (a lighthouse is itself a keeper of light, which rhymes thematically with a town whose Great Lantern has gone dark). Inside: a teacup-sized town kept warm by one Great Lantern. The child shrinks, gathers three tiny things (spark / thread / breath of wind — chosen over "climb only" so the middle has three distinct, concrete, kid-graspable mini-quests), then climbs the tower to relight it. Keepsake: a berry-sized lantern that stays warm in her pocket — the "proof it was real" beat. Companion: a small lamplighter.

## Character-name decisions

- **Child: Juno** — kept identical across all three languages. Short, warm, pronounceable and natural in EN/NL/DE; no localization gain from changing it.
- **Companion (the little lamplighter): a translated pun on "wick".** EN **Wick** / NL **Pit** (Dutch for a candle/lamp wick) / DE **Docht** (German for wick). The companion's name literally *is* the thing that holds the flame — a quiet echo of the quest. Bonus in German: at Spread 12 the silver thread is laid "an den Docht", so the character name and the object rhyme in one line, which reads as charming rather than confusing in context.
- **The lantern: "the Great Lantern"** — EN the Great Lantern / NL de Grote Lantaarn / DE die Große Laterne. Deliberately translatable rather than a coined proper noun.

## Language approach

EN is the master and voice source of truth. NL and DE are native-quality re-tellings, re-authored for rhythm and idiom — not glossed word-for-word.

- **NL (owner is Dutch — must be the strongest):** leans on Dutch's natural diminutives (deurtje, bruggetjes, lantaarntje, klokje, orkestje, armbandje) for built-in warmth and smallness; idiomatic turns like "want kloppen hoort zo", "hapte naar adem", "raken de weg kwijt", "Rustig maar". Spread 11's moral is rendered "Dapper … is gewoon bang zijn en tóch doorlopen" with the accent on *tóch* to carry the read-aloud stress. Spread 14 ends "Het was echt geweest. Alles." — the trailing "Alles." mirrors the EN two-beat close and lands better in Dutch than a single long sentence.
- **DE:** native picture-book cadence with proper compounds (Kopfsteinpflastergassen, Laternenstange, Sternenhimmel) and idiom ("weil sich Klopfen gehört", "ihr stockte der Atem", "Ganz ruhig", "machte einen Satz"). Subjunctive "hätte brennen sollen / gepasst hätte" gives the wistful storybook register. Moral rendered "Mutig … heißt einfach Angst haben und trotzdem weitergehen."

## Concept gaps filled

- Portal location fixed as an old lighthouse (seed offered several); door made butter-yellow with a peppercorn knob and star keyhole for a concrete, drawable image.
- The three gather-quests given specific, kid-scaled sources: a sleepy glow-beetle (spark), a silver-spinning spider under a bridge (thread), a gust caught in a curled seashell (wind).
- Shrinking mechanism kept magical and unexplained (stepping through the door), to avoid rules-lawyering in a cozy book.
- Keepsake specified as a berry-sized lantern that stays warm — concrete and re-checkable in her pocket at the end.
- Bedtime deadline delivered via a far-off bell so the "home safe by bedtime" contract is felt, not stated.
