# Concept — Crew of the Comet Biscuit

- **Track:** Modern
- **Logline:** Four wildly mismatched animals fly a wobbly, cookie-tin-shaped spaceship across a friendly cartoon galaxy, taking any odd job they can crumb their way into — and somehow saving the day by teamwork, snacks, and tape.
- **Age band:** picture book (3–6) — with strong read-aloud early-reader crossover
- **Tone + narrator voice:** Fast, funny, and warm — a narrator who's basically the fifth crew member, half breathless sports announcer, half best friend leaning in to tell you a secret. Big sound words, punchy short lines, gentle peril that always melts into a hug. Every gag lands, every crew member is loved even when they're being ridiculous.
- **Story shape:** episodic missions — each book (or spread-set) is one delivery gone sideways, which keeps the format snappy, endlessly re-runnable, and perfect for a series; the crew and ship are the constant, the galaxy is the joke machine.

## Sample opening

The Comet Biscuit went *ker-CHUNK.*

Then it went *ker-CHUNK* again, just to show it meant it.

"CREW!" shouted Captain Nutmeg, standing on three cushions and a lunchbox so she could see over the console. "This is your captain speaking! We have taken on a VERY important mission! A mission of COURAGE! A mission of GLORY! A mission of—"

"Jam," said Sprocket, without looking up from the engine. The gecko had one foot stuck to the ceiling and a cheese cracker in his mouth. "We're delivering jam."

"—a mission of jam!" said Captain Nutmeg, not missing a beat. "Starberry jam! Forty jars! To the Moon of Muffins! And NOTHING can stop us!"

*Ker-CHUNK.*

Everyone looked at the ceiling.

"Course plotted," said Barnaby the sloth. Very calmly. Very slowly. He had begun plotting it last Tuesday. His paw drifted toward a button roughly the size and speed of a cloud. "Turn... left... in... three..."

"BARNABY WE ALREADY TURNED LEFT—"

"...perfect," said Barnaby. And it was. It was always, somehow, perfect.

The Comet Biscuit rattled, wobbled, popped a bolt, caught the bolt, gave it back, and zoomed off into the sparkly dark with all forty jars of jam sloshing behind them.

"See?" beamed Captain Nutmeg. "NOTHING can stop us."

From the cargo hold, a small, glowing, extremely homesick jellyfish went *bloop.*

## Recurring cast

- **Captain Nutmeg** — a teeny hamster with GIANT confidence; can barely see over the console, gives rousing speeches nobody is listening to, has a plan for everything (the plans are bad, but the heart is huge). *Role: Captain.*
- **Barnaby** — an unbelievably calm, unbelievably slow sloth who plots every course a beat too late and yet is *never once wrong.* Speaks in long, dreamy pauses that turn out to be exact. *Role: Navigator.*
- **Sprocket** — a chaos-gremlin gecko who fixes the entire ship with tape, snacks, and vibes; sticks to any surface, upside-down is his favorite way to work, mildly responsible for most of the problems. *Role: Engineer.*
- **Bloop** — a small, glowing space-jellyfish who stowed away by accident and is very, very homesick; drifts around going *bloop*, turns out to be the secret weapon (glows in the dark, senses trouble, hugs everyone). *Role: Stowaway → honorary crew.*

## Example episodes / scenes

1. **The Great Jam Wobble** — The crew must deliver forty jars of starberry jam to the Moon of Muffins, but the cargo lid pops and jam starts floating everywhere in zero gravity. The whole ship becomes a sticky, drifting jam-storm — and the only way to catch it all is Sprocket's tape, Barnaby's perfectly-timed turn, and Bloop, who is now delicious and glowing.
2. **Tow the Lonely Moon** — A little moon has drifted off course and is too shy to ask for help, just sniffling in the dark. The Biscuit tries to tow it home, but the tow-rope is a very long licorice whip and the moon keeps hiding behind bigger planets out of embarrassment. Captain Nutmeg gives it a pep talk; Bloop just floats over and glows next to it until it feels brave.
3. **The Nebula Bake-Off** — The crew enters the galaxy's biggest baking contest to win a new engine part, but their only ingredients are engine grease, three crackers, and one nervous jellyfish. Sprocket "improves" the recipe, the oven goes *whooomp,* and they accidentally bake a cake so magnificent (and so slightly on fire) that the judges weep. They lose on presentation, win Most Delicious Chaos.

## Visuals

- **Illustration style direction:** bold flat-color modern cartoon, thick confident outlines, big expressive googly eyes, squash-and-stretch energy, lots of motion lines and comic sound-words baked right into the art (*KER-CHUNK*, *BLOOP*, *WHOOOSH*). Uncluttered backgrounds so the crew's faces pop.
- **Palette:** deep grape-jelly purple and midnight blue for space; hot bubblegum pink, tangerine orange, buttery custard yellow, mint-cream green; starberry red for the jam; and Bloop's signature glow-in-the-dark cyan-lime.
- **Character design notes (in words):**
  - **Captain Nutmeg** — round golden-brown hamster, cheeks always a little full, oversized crash helmet that slides over her eyes, tiny cape, standing on a stack of cushions + a lunchbox. Pure swagger in a small package.
  - **Barnaby** — big, soft, moss-green sloth with half-closed dreamy eyes and a permanent gentle smile; moves in slow-motion, one arm perpetually mid-reach toward a button. Wears a crooked navigator's headset.
  - **Sprocket** — skinny bright-teal gecko, curly tail, suction-cup toes, tool-belt full of tape and snack crumbs, usually drawn upside-down or sideways stuck to something, one googly eye bigger than the other.
  - **Bloop** — palm-sized translucent glowing jellyfish, soft cyan-lime, big worried sparkly eyes, little trailing tentacles that curl into hearts when happy; leaves a faint glow-trail wherever it drifts.
  - **The Comet Biscuit** — a round, dented, lovable spaceship shaped like an old cookie tin, complete with a slightly-peeling label; mismatched patches and tape holding it together, three wobbly rocket-legs, a big round porthole window where you can always see the crew squished together, and a little chimney puffing star-shaped smoke.
- **Key illustration moments:**
  1. The zero-gravity jam-storm: forty jars' worth of glittering red starberry jam floating in giant slow blobs through the whole ship, the crew mid-scramble, Sprocket taped to the ceiling catching blobs, Bloop entirely encased in a floating jam-bubble looking delighted.
  2. Towing the shy little moon home on a stretchy licorice-whip rope, the tiny moon peeking bashfully from behind a giant planet while the whole Biscuit strains and wobbles and Nutmeg leans out the porthole mid-pep-talk.
  3. The Nebula Bake-Off reveal: the crew's towering, gloriously lopsided, faintly-on-fire cake under a spotlight, judges in the foreground gasping, Sprocket proudly holding a roll of tape, Bloop peeking out of the frosting.

## Language note

English primary. Dutch-first version: no — the humor here runs on English sound-words, rhythm, and read-aloud punch (*KER-CHUNK*, "a mission of JAM"), which are the whole engine of the modern hook and land best in English first. A Dutch edition is easy to follow (the gags are visual and situational, so they translate cleanly — "een missie van JAM!" works fine) — owner decides, but English leads.
