# Concept — Fennec and the Neon Desert

- **Track:** Modern
- **Logline:** When the sun sinks and the dunes bloom with glowing flowers, a tiny big-eared fennec fox follows the smallest sounds of wonder into a floating night market of light, tea, and starlight.
- **Age band:** picture book (3–6) / bedtime
- **Tone + narrator voice:** Lyrical and hushed, like a lullaby that keeps its eyes open one page longer. Warm, sensory, unhurried — every spread is meant to make a small listener whisper *ohhh*. A pinch of soft humor tucked into the awe, so the wonder never gets too grand for a giggle.
- **Story shape:** Single lyrical arc — one magical night, dusk to almost-dawn — because a bedtime book wants a single downward curve of light toward sleep. (Episodic "corners of the market" can become companion books; the first should be one unbroken, dreaming breath.)

## Sample opening

All day the desert is the color of a sleeping lion.

Fennec waits inside the cool blue shadow of her burrow, her enormous ears folded down like two soft tents, and she listens to the heat go quiet. She is very small. Her ears are not.

Then the sun slides off the edge of the world — *plip* — the way a coin slides off a table.

And the desert wakes up.

First one dune, then another, then all of them at once begin to glow. Tiny flowers no bigger than buttons unfurl in the sand and blush *neon-magenta*, *electric-teal*, *lantern-gold*, until every hill is stitched with light like a blanket full of stars that fell asleep in the ground. The night air smells like cold honey.

Fennec's ears lift.

*Twitch.*

Somewhere far out in the glowing dark, something goes *ting.* Then *tinktinkting* — small and silvery and curious, the sound of a bell no bigger than a raindrop.

Fennec has never heard that sound before. And Fennec has heard *everything.*

Her paws are already in the sand, warm and grainy and cooling fast. Her ears swivel — this way, that way — pointing her toward the tiny music the way a flower points at the moon.

"Just to the top of the dune," she tells herself. "Just to look."

But the top of the dune is where the whole night is waiting.

## Recurring cast

- **Fennec** — the tiniest fox in the desert, all ears and eyes and questions; shy at the edges, brave in the middle, led everywhere by the small sounds only she can hear.
- **Qamar the starlight-camel** — a slow, kindly camel who carries a swaying rack of glass bottles, each one holding a scoop of captured starlight; she trades a bottle for a good story or a very good question.
- **The lantern-beetles** — a drifting, blinking crowd of round beetles who *are* the market's streetlamps; they hum when they're happy and glow brighter when someone is kind.
- **Old Toko, the tortoise tea-house keeper** — a mountainous, mossy tortoise whose shell *is* the tea-house; steam curls from a little chimney between his shoulder blades, and he pours moon-mint tea for anyone who needs to slow down.
- **Zip the jerboa** — a spring-legged DJ with headphones made of two curled seedpods, spinning desert-night beats on a turntable of polished stone; Fennec's first and fastest friend.
- **The Moth-Lamplighters** — pale winged sisters who ferry loose sparks from bloom to bloom, keeping the desert lit.
- **A bottled-starlight lizard, a fig-selling gecko, a sand-glass owl** — the glowing ensemble that fills the floating stalls.

## Example episodes / scenes

1. **The First Ting** — Fennec follows the silvery bell-sound to its source and discovers it is Zip the jerboa, tuning his stone turntable with a tiny hammer; he invites her, wide-eyed, into the market she never knew glowed just beyond her burrow.
2. **A Bottle of Starlight** — At Qamar's swaying stall, Fennec has nothing to trade — no coins, no figs — until the camel leans down and asks, "What did the quiet sound like today?" Fennec's honest, funny answer is worth exactly one bottle of light.
3. **Tea at the Slowest Table** — When the market's dazzle grows too big and Fennec's small heart races, Old Toko pours her a cup of moon-mint tea inside his warm shell tea-house, and teaches her that wonder tastes better when you sip it slowly. The night begins, gently, to fold toward sleep.

## Visuals

- **Illustration style direction:** Luminous painterly digital with the soft grain of glowing gouache — deep, velvety night skies against bioluminescent neon flora. Light should feel *emitted*, not painted on: blooms, bottles, and beetles cast real colored glow onto the sand and onto Fennec's fur. This is the visual showcase concept; every spread should look like it could hum.
- **Palette:** Indigo night and deep-violet dune-shadow as the base; neon-magenta and electric-teal blooms; lantern-amber and honey-gold for warm market light; a single cool silver for starlight and Fennec's whisker-tips. Restraint by day (dusty lion-gold), full spectrum by night.
- **Character design notes (in words):** Fennec is tiny and rounded — a soft apricot-and-cream body dwarfed by two colossal, translucent, blush-pink-lined ears that catch and glow with whatever light is nearest. Huge dark eyes with a pin of reflected neon in each. Qamar is tall, dune-shaped, draped in a rack of glowing bottles that sway like wind chimes. Lantern-beetles are round and jelly-soft with warm bulbs for bellies. Old Toko is a mossy hill of a tortoise with a lit paper-lantern tea-house grown into his shell. Zip is all hind-legs and seedpod headphones, mid-bounce.
- **Key illustration moments:**
  1. **The desert lighting up** — a wide dusk spread: the sun a thin dissolving coin at the horizon while, foreground to distance, the dunes ignite bloom by bloom; tiny Fennec silhouetted on a ridge, ears rising, the very first neon flower opening at her feet.
  2. **The floating night market** — the show-stopper: lantern-beetle streetlamps drifting between stalls that hover a hand's-width above the sand, Qamar's bottles glowing like a chandelier, Zip mid-spin, moth-sisters ferrying sparks, all reflected in a shallow mirror of night-cooled sand.
  3. **Tea inside Toko's shell** — an intimate, amber-warm interior: steam and firefly-light, Fennec cupping a glowing cup with both paws, her enormous ears finally, softly, at rest — the whole neon night visible small and dreamy through the tea-house window.

## Language note

English primary. Dutch-first version: yes — the writer works in the Netherlands, and this book's appeal is almost entirely visual and sonic (small sounds, cozy ritual, glowing awe), so the lyrical bedtime voice translates gracefully to Dutch (*Fennec en de Neonwoestijn*) without leaning on English wordplay; a Dutch-first edition suits the local market and the gentle read-aloud cadence.
