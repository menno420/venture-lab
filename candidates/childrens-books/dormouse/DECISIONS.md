# Dormouse — manuscript decisions

Decision log for the full *Dormouse* manuscripts. Concept source:
`../adaptations/B1-dormouse-who-dreams-awake.md`.
Own IP — a picture-book spin-off of the lane's DREAMLINE series (canon at
`../../dream-series/`, **read-only, not edited here**). Owner-gated: no images
generated here; illustration + any publish path remain the owner's call.

## Age band
- **Chosen: read-aloud picture book, ages 3–6.** Matches Tummel and the concept's
  own call ("picture book (3–6), bedtime"). The whole point is a hushed, glowy,
  tuck-in read-aloud — bedtime-first, not reading-alone-first.

## Length & structure
- **One self-contained story, laid out in 12 spreads** (`[Spread N]` / `[Plaat N]`
  / `[Bild N]` markers + one italic illustration note each) — the same shape as
  Tummel, for a matching portfolio.
- **Body length EN 634 / NL 619 / DE 607 words** (actual counts; method below).
- **Decide-and-flag — Dormouse runs ~90–110 words longer than Tummel (~520–545).**
  Reason: this is an ORIGIN. It must both introduce a second lead (Heron) and
  *teach* the core mending action (the stitch), which needs more dialogue than
  Tummel's solo-repair arc. Still squarely picture-book (EN 634 sits inside the
  ~600–800-word read-aloud band), still 12 spreads, still ages 3–6 — "consistent-ish"
  as briefed, with the small stretch flagged here rather than buried.
- Structure: tuck-in ritual (curl the tail, *one, two, curl*) → she wakes *inside*
  the dream → wonder on the moonlight bridge → she finds the tear → she can't mend
  it yet → Heron arrives ("Gently now. Small and gentle.") → he explains the
  soft places + the sleepy-fog → she's afraid, he folds a wing around her → they
  draw the first moonlight stitch together → the tear closes, the canal goes silver
  → home, back to bed, tail curled, lands on sleep. A deliberate bedtime landing.

## Which story
- Built the **ORIGIN — "The Night Pippa Woke Up Inside a Dream"** (the concept's
  recommended option) into the complete manuscript, titled **"Pippa and the Tear
  in the Night."** It is the strongest stand-alone because it introduces Heron and
  the core mending action in one arc; the concept's sample opening was folded into
  Spreads 1–6. (The alt episode "The Lullaby That Got Loose" is left as a future
  book — it presumes Heron and the stitch already exist.)

## Per-language titles
| Lang | Series title | Book title | Note |
|------|--------------|------------|------|
| EN | The Dormouse Who Dreams Awake | Pippa and the Tear in the Night | master / voice source of truth |
| NL | Het Slaapmuisje dat Wakker Droomt | Pippa en het Scheurtje in de Nacht | *scheurtje* = the little tear; diminutive keeps it tender |
| DE | Das Siebenschläferchen, das Wach Träumt | Pippa und der Riss in der Nacht | see Siebenschläfer note below |

- **DE — *Siebenschläfer* wordplay kept.** A *Siebenschläfer* is literally a dormouse
  **and** a "seven-sleeper" — a gift for a book about a champion sleeper who wakes
  inside sleep. Used the diminutive *Siebenschläferchen* for the tot register; a one-
  line note in the DE series blurb points out the double meaning for grown-up readers.
  (Alt *Haselmäuschen* was available but throws away the sleeper pun — declined.)
- **Book titles localized, not transliterated:** the tear image drives each title
  (*tear / scheurtje / Riss*) so the cover word matches the story's heart.

## Character-name decisions
- **Pippa — KEPT in all three languages.** Short, soft, universally readable; no reason
  to localize a proper name that already sings.
- **Heron — LOCALIZED to the common bird-name as a proper name:** EN "Heron" /
  NL "Reiger" / DE "Reiher". A real canal night-bird; keeping it as the character's
  name (capitalized) roots the setting and reads warm in each language.
- **Mama Dormouse** — common noun, localized: NL "Mama Slaapmuis" / DE "Mama
  Siebenschläfer".
- **The Lamplighter Beetle** — descriptive, localized: NL "Lantaarnkevertje" /
  DE "Laternenkäferchen"; it marks time (dims the last lamp at the start, "brings up
  the morning" at the end — the go-in / come-home clock from the concept cast).
- **Otto the Otter** — cast in the concept as an optional cameo; **not used** in the
  origin (it needs only Pippa + Heron to stay clean and bedtime-tight). Held for a
  later episode. Name would stay "Otto" in all three if used.

## Canon guardrail (DREAMLINE)
- **Tot-register only; no canon terms invented or contradicted.** DREAMLINE's keeper
  faction, elite-dreamer titles, forgetting-fog and thin-place terms are still open
  owner picks in `../../dream-series/bible/names.md` — so this book deliberately uses
  **plain tot-words** that can never clash with whatever the owner finalizes:
  - "waking up inside your dream" (not any canon ability-name)
  - "night-watchman" / "darner" (not the keeper-faction's canon name)
  - "the sleepy-fog grown-ups get" (not the forgetting-fog's canon name)
  - "the soft place where dream and night touch" (not the canon thin-place term)
  - "sewing the snag shut with moonlight thread" (the concept's cozy mending image)
- If the owner later fixes canon names, none of this text needs a rewrite — it lives
  one register below canon on purpose.

## Language approach
- **Not literal translations.** Each language re-authored for natural rhythm and idiom;
  **NL prioritized** as the owner's native language (naturalness of the *voorleesstem*
  over word-for-word fidelity), DE second, EN the voice source of truth.
- **Signature sound-soft words localized but kept sonic:**
  *plip* → NL/DE *plip* (kept — it already works in all three).
  *shimmer* → NL *glinster* / DE *Schimmer*. *shiver* → NL *ril* / DE *Schauder*.
  The bounce of the bridge: EN *bounce* / NL *wip* / DE *wipp*.
- **The falling-asleep ritual kept as a 3-beat across all three:**
  EN "one, two, curl" / NL "een, twee, krul" / DE "eins, zwei, Ringel" — repeated at
  the open and the close to bracket the book in the same soothing beat.
- **Heron's catchphrase kept steady:** EN "Gently now. Small and gentle." /
  NL "Rustig maar. Klein en zachtjes." / DE "Ganz ruhig. Klein und sanft." — recurs
  three times as the read-aloud anchor.

## Concept gaps filled
1. **No full manuscript existed** — only a sample opening + a cast + three episode
   seeds. Wrote the complete 12-spread origin arc.
2. **Gave Heron an arrival and a mending lesson.** The concept describes Heron and the
   stitch but never stages Pippa's *first* one — supplied the meeting, the wing-around
   reassurance, and the in/out stitch beats consistent with his "Gently now" voice.
3. **Turned the fear beat into the heart of the story** — "I'm very small" answered by
   "Small and gentle is exactly what mends best" — the gentle-braver single arc the
   concept names, made concrete in one scene.
4. **Chose sleep as the closing image** (Pippa home, tail curled, Mama in the sleepy-
   fog) to make the origin a true bedtime book and to close the same beat it opened on.

## Not done here (owner-gated / out of scope)
- Illustration (this seat generates no art; each spread carries a paste-ready note, and
  the adaptation file holds a full illustration prompt sheet).
- Print layout, ISBN, listing, and the English-first vs Dutch-first market lead — all
  owner decisions. (DREAMLINE is Netherlands-first, so a Dutch-first edition is a
  natural fit — flagged, not decided.)
- Any DREAMLINE canon name choices — the owner's open picks in the series bible.

## Word-count method
Body prose only, counting tokens that contain a letter/number (standalone em-dashes
and pure punctuation not counted); front matter, the series note, `[Spread]` markers,
and italic illustration notes excluded. The same method reproduces Worker 1's stated
Tummel counts exactly (EN 536 / NL 545 / DE 522), so the two books' figures are
directly comparable.
