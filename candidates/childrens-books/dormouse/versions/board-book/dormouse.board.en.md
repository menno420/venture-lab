# Pippa and the Tear in the Night — Board-Book Edition

- **Title:** Pippa and the Tear in the Night (board-book cut)
- **Language:** English (master — voice source of truth)
- **Age band:** board book, ages 0–3
- **Format:** 12 spreads, standalone toddler read-aloud — NOT a summary of the picture book
- **Word count:** honest `wc -w` per language in [`NOTES.md`](NOTES.md)
- **Pitch:** The smallest dormouse wakes up inside her own dream, finds a little tear in the night — and, with kind old Heron guiding her paws, sews it shut with moonlight.

> Version of the ages 3–6 picture book (`../../dormouse.en.md`). Same characters (Pippa, Mama Dormouse, Heron, the Lamplighter Beetle), one core beat kept: small and gentle is exactly what mends best. Every spread carries an illustration note in italics; the seat generates no art.

---

[Spread 1]
*Illustration: the sleeping canal-city at dusk — humpbacked bridges, tilted gabled houses; a round glowing beetle dimming the last lamp; Mama tucking tiny Pippa in under her leaf.*
Night-night, canal-city. The Beetle dims the last little lamp. "Sleep sweet, small one," whispers Mama.

[Spread 2]
*Illustration: extreme close-up — tiny Pippa in her leaf-bed, huge dark eyes, curling her long tail up over her nose.*
Pippa curls her tail over her nose — one, two, curl. Down she goes, into the warm dark of sleep.

[Spread 3]
*Illustration: Pippa standing wonderstruck on a glowing bridge of moonlight over a canal full of reflected stars.*
But oh! Tonight Pippa wakes up INSIDE her dream! A bridge of moonlight. A canal of stars.

[Spread 4]
*Illustration: Pippa wiggling her toes; the moonlight-bridge bouncing soft as a pillow; little dream-ripples going plip.*
Bounce, bounce goes the bridge. Plip, plip goes the dream-water. Pippa looks and looks.

[Spread 5]
*Illustration: at the bridge's edge, a small luminous snag in the air, like a loose loop pulled from a dark woolly blanket; a thin cold thread slipping through.*
Oh no. A little tear, right where the dream ends. Shimmer... shiver... Cold night is slipping through.

[Spread 6]
*Illustration: Pippa leaning close to the glowing snag, small and brave, one paw reaching but not quite touching.*
"You should not be open," says Pippa kindly. But she does not know how to close it. Not yet.

[Spread 7]
*Illustration: a tall gentle old heron stepping from the dark, dusk-blue and silver-grey, a glinting needle of moonlight behind his ear, stooping to Pippa's height.*
Here comes Heron, the night-watchman, tall and slow and kind. "Gently now," he says. "Small and gentle."

[Spread 8]
*Illustration: Pippa's knees knocking, her tail trembling; Heron's great soft wing curved warm around her.*
"I'm very small," says Pippa. Her knees go knock, knock. Heron folds a warm wing around her.

[Spread 9]
*Illustration: Heron kneeling low, threading the needle of moonlight; Pippa taking the shining thread in her two small paws.*
"Small and gentle is just what mends best," says Heron. "I will guide your paws."

[Spread 10]
*Illustration: the small dormouse and the tall heron together, drawing one thread of glowing moonlight through the shimmering snag.*
In goes the stitch. Out goes the stitch. Plip! In, and out. One moonlight stitch at a time. "Gently now. Small and gentle."

[Spread 11]
*Illustration: the tear closing with warm light; the whole canal turning soft silver, the cold thread gone.*
The tear closes up. The shiver goes still. All along the canal, the water turns soft and silver. All mended. All safe.

[Spread 12]
*Illustration: Pippa back in her leaf-bed, tail curled one-two-curl, Mama near; one moonlit window; the whole city in deep restful dark.*
"Well stitched, small one," says Heron. "Home you go." And Pippa curls up in her own little bed — one, two, curl. Sleep sweet, Pippa.

*The end.*
