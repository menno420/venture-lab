# The Puddle Museum

- **Title:** The Puddle Museum
- **Language:** English (master — voice source of truth)
- **Age band:** read-aloud picture book, ages 4–8
- **Word count:** 584 (manuscript body, excluding spread/illustration notes)
- **Pitch:** After the rain, Juno opens her museum — admission: one leaf. Every puddle on the lane is an exhibit, from the Sky Mirror to the puddle shaped exactly like a whale. But the sun is coming out, and a museum that evaporates teaches its curator (and one skeptical big brother) what collections are really for.

> Series: soft series shape *Juno's Museums* — stand-alone. Every spread carries an illustration note in italics; the seat generates no art. Art brief is reflection-led: exhibit spreads pair the real world above the waterline with the museum-world below it.

---

[Spread 1]
*Illustration: Rain streaming down a window; Juno's face close to the glass, eyes tracking one drop; behind her reflection in the pane, the gray lane already gathering puddles.*
It rained all morning on Juno's lane. Fat rain, thin rain, sideways rain. Juno sat with her nose against the window and watched every single drop land. Other people saw bad weather. Juno saw new exhibits arriving.

[Spread 2]
*Illustration: The garden gate with a hand-lettered sign: THE PUDDLE MUSEUM — OPEN TODAY ONLY — ADMISSION: ONE LEAF. Juno in boots, arms crossed, official; Theo reading the sign with one eyebrow up.*
The moment the rain stopped, Juno pulled on her boots and hung a sign on the garden gate: THE PUDDLE MUSEUM — OPEN TODAY ONLY — ADMISSION: ONE LEAF. Her big brother Theo came out and read it twice. "A museum of puddles?" he said. "Puddles are just wet holes." "Rules are rules," said Juno, and held out her hand. Theo sighed and paid one leaf.

[Spread 3]
*Illustration: A wide, still puddle filling the middle of the lane, roped off with skipping ropes; the whole sky and its clouds mirrored perfectly in the water; Theo leaning over, his face among the clouds.*
"Mind the ropes," said Juno. (They were skipping ropes.) "We begin with our most famous exhibit: the Sky Mirror." The Sky Mirror lay wide and still in the middle of the lane, holding the whole sky inside it — clouds and all. "Huh," said Theo, leaning over two sailing clouds. "It's deeper than it looks."

[Spread 4]
*Illustration: Under the big oak, a puddle holding the entire tree upside down — trunk plunging, branches reaching deep, every leaf mirrored; a bird on a real branch above, its twin below.*
"Next: the Upside-Down Tree." Under the big oak, a puddle held the whole tree, standing on its head, every branch pointing down and every leaf still on. "Don't the birds fall out?" asked Theo. "Only upward," said Juno.

[Spread 5]
*Illustration: Wet pavement with three stranded worms; Juno and Theo carrying one each on flat leaves like little stretchers toward a brown flowerbed; a small hand-lettered card: WORM RESCUE WING.*
"This is the Worm Rescue Wing." Three worms lay stranded on the wet pavement. "Visitors may help," said Juno, and they carried the worms, one by one, to the soft brown flowerbed. "That's not art," said Theo. "It's better," said Juno. "It's a rescue."

[Spread 6]
*Illustration: Two smaller exhibits — a puddle mid-splash with boot spray frozen in the air (card: SPLASH GALLERY — JUMPING ALLOWED), and a small puddle wearing a swirled rainbow film, the two children puffing their cheeks at it.*
There was the Splash Gallery, the only room where jumping was allowed — SPLOOSH — and the Puddle of Many Colors, wearing a thin rainbow skin that swirled when you breathed on it.

[Spread 7]
*Illustration: By the drainpipe, a puddle shaped exactly like a whale — nose, tail, and a tuft of grass for a spout; inside the water, clouds drift through the whale's body; Theo kneeling, genuinely amazed.*
"And now," said Juno, "the greatest treasure in the whole collection." By the drainpipe lay a puddle shaped exactly like a whale — nose, tail, and a little spout of grass on top. "Whoa," said Theo, quite forgetting to be bored. "The Whale," whispered Juno. "Please do not touch."

[Spread 8]
*Illustration: The sun breaking through, gold flooding the lane; every puddle suddenly blazing with light; Juno shading her eyes, her reflection going pale at the edges.*
Then the sun came out. It came out warm and gold, and it lay down on the whole lane at once. "Uh-oh," said Juno. Museums and sunshine are not always friends.

[Spread 9]
*Illustration: The Sky Mirror shrunk to a plate-sized disc of sky; the Upside-Down Tree missing its outer branches; the Whale's tail fading into damp pavement; Theo pointing, concerned now.*
The Sky Mirror shrank to the size of a dinner plate. The Upside-Down Tree faded from its edges in. And at the Whale, the tail was already gone. "Your museum," said Theo slowly, "is disappearing."

[Spread 10]
*Illustration: Juno at the Whale's edge with a bucket half-raised, frozen mid-decision; in the shrinking water, her own reflection looks back at her; Theo watching.*
Juno ran for her bucket. She could scoop the Whale up — she could keep it! But right at the edge, she stopped. A whale in a bucket isn't a whale anymore. It's just water. You can hang a painting on a wall. You can't hang a puddle.

[Spread 11]
*Illustration: The two children kneeling side by side as the Whale shrinks — nose, spout, tummy — the last of it a bright whale-shaped gleam; then only a dark whale-shaped patch on the ground.*
So the curator and her brother knelt beside the Whale and watched it go. Nose, spout, tummy — smaller and smaller, shinier and shinier, until only a whale-shaped shadow was left on the ground. "It was a really good whale," said Theo. "Best in any museum," said Juno.

[Spread 12]
*Illustration: A last coin-sized puddle holding one tiny piece of blue sky, winking out; Juno turning her sign around: THE PUDDLE MUSEUM — CLOSED. THANK YOU FOR LOOKING; Theo's hands in his pockets, no longer skeptical.*
"What kind of museum disappears by lunchtime?" asked Theo, as the last exhibit — a puddle no bigger than a coin, holding one small piece of sky — winked out. Juno turned her sign around: THE PUDDLE MUSEUM — CLOSED. THANK YOU FOR LOOKING. "The best kind," she said. "You don't get to keep the exhibits. You get to keep the looking."

[Spread 13]
*Illustration: Evening — new gray clouds sailing in over the rooftops; two pairs of boots ready by the door; on the doormat, one perfect leaf; Juno and Theo at the window, both noses to the glass.*
That evening, new clouds sailed in over the rooftops, gray and full and promising. Two pairs of boots stood ready by the door. And on the doormat lay one leaf, put there by somebody tall who pretended it was nothing. "For next time," said Theo. "Front of the line."

*The end.*
