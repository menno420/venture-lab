# Bram the Yak Cannot Whisper

- **Title:** Bram the Yak Cannot Whisper
- **Language:** English (master — voice source of truth)
- **Age band:** read-aloud picture book, ages 4–8
- **Word count:** 646 (manuscript body, excluding spread/illustration notes)
- **Pitch:** Bram is the gentlest yak in the valley, with the softest heart and the LOUDEST voice — and today he is going to try, very hard, to be quiet.

> Series: *The Very Loud Valley* — read-aloud comedy, stand-alone. Every spread carries an illustration note in italics; the seat generates no art.

---

[Spread 1]

*Illustration: a huge shaggy yak beaming on a green hill, tiny birds knocked sideways off a fence behind him.*

Bram was a big, gentle yak with a big, gentle heart.

He loved warm hay, small friends, and quiet mornings.

There was only one tiny problem.

Bram could not, would not, absolutely COULD NOT whisper.

[Spread 2]

*Illustration: Bram cupping a hoof to his mouth; a nearby scarecrow's hat has just launched into the sky.*

Every time Bram tried to be quiet, it went like this.

Bram took a tiny breath...

and let out a NOT-tiny sound:

**BRRROOOOONK!**

Three hats flew off. A window went *rattle-rattle-CLINK.*

"Sorry," said Bram. It came out as **BRRROOONK,** too.

[Spread 3]

*Illustration: a grand library, books tumbling off shelves like dominoes, an owl librarian clutching her glasses.*

On Monday, Bram tiptoed into the library.

"I will be SO quiet," he whispered to himself.

Bram took a tiny breath...

and let out a NOT-tiny sound:

**BRRROOOOONK!**

Books went *flap-flap-FLUMP.* The owl fell off her stool.

"OUT," she hooted. "You loud, lovely lump."

[Spread 4]

*Illustration: a tiny goat baby mid-air above its cradle, blanket flying, a very tired mother goat gasping.*

On Tuesday, Bram visited baby Nana, who was fast asleep.

"I will whisper like a snowflake," he whispered.

Bram took a tiny breath...

and let out a NOT-tiny sound:

**BRRROOOOONK!**

Baby Nana shot up like toast. *"WAAAH!"*

"Sorry," honked Bram, and that made her cry MORE.

[Spread 5]

*Illustration: a game of hide-and-seek in the woods; every animal peeking out from a bush, a rock, a hollow log, all pointing at Bram.*

On Wednesday, they played hide-and-seek.

Bram squeezed behind the thinnest tree and held very still.

Bram took a tiny breath...

and let out a NOT-tiny sound:

**BRRROOOOONK!**

"FOUND you," everyone groaned. "We ALWAYS find you, Bram."

[Spread 6]

*Illustration: a fancy candlelit dinner, soup splashing out of every bowl, a snooty swan wearing soup on his head.*

On Thursday came Mayor Swan's Very Fancy Dinner.

"Not one peep," Bram promised, holding his soup spoon.

Bram took a tiny breath...

and let out a NOT-tiny sound:

**BRRROOOOONK!**

Soup went *sloosh.* The candles went *poof.* The swan went purple.

"Perhaps," he sniffed, "you should sit OUTSIDE."

[Spread 7]

*Illustration: Bram alone on a hill at dusk, chin on the grass, small and sad under a big sky.*

So Bram sat outside.

All alone.

"Nobody wants a yak who is too, too loud," he sighed.

And even his sigh went **brrooonk,** just a little.

[Spread 8]

*Illustration: thick grey fog rolling down the valley, swallowing the trees, tiny worried faces gathering.*

That night, a great grey fog rolled in.

Thick as porridge. You could not see your own nose.

And somewhere out in the fog, a small voice cried:

"Help! I'm lost! It's me, little Pip!"

[Spread 9]

*Illustration: animals stumbling in the fog, bumping heads, whisper-shouting into cotton-wool nothing.*

Everyone searched. Everyone called.

But their little voices went *pfft,* soft and swallowed.

"Pip can't hear us," they wept. "The fog is too thick.

If only someone could be heard from FAR, FAR away..."

[Spread 10]

*Illustration: Bram's eyes going wide with a giant idea, ears perking up, chest puffing like a balloon.*

Bram stood up.

Bram lifted his shaggy head.

Bram thought: *This is a job for a NOT-tiny sound.*

Bram took a HUGE breath...

[Spread 11]

*Illustration: Bram mid-honk, the fog blasting apart in rings, the whole valley lighting up with his voice.*

and let out the BIGGEST sound of his whole life:

**BRRROOOOOOOOOOONK!**

It rolled over the hills. It parted the fog. It rattled the moon.

And a small, happy voice called back:

"THAT'S BRAM! I'll follow BRAM!"

[Spread 12]

*Illustration: little Pip the lamb galloping out of the fog straight into Bram's warm shaggy hug, everyone cheering.*

Pip followed the honk all the way home.

Everyone cheered. The owl. The swan. Even baby Nana clapped.

"Bram," said Pip, "you are the loudest, BEST yak in the world."

And Bram? Bram was too happy to whisper.

[Spread 13]

*Illustration: Bram standing proudly on the hilltop as the town's official foghorn, a little medal round his neck, hats flying off in the background — joyfully this time.*

Now, whenever the fog rolls in, or someone is lost,

or dinner is ready and nobody will come,

the whole valley calls for one big, gentle yak.

Bram takes a tiny breath...

and lets out a NOT-tiny sound:

**BRRROOOOONK!**

*The end.*
