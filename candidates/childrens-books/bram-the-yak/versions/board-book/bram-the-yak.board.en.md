# Bram the Yak Cannot Whisper — Board-Book Edition

- **Title:** Bram the Yak Cannot Whisper (board-book cut)
- **Language:** English (master — voice source of truth)
- **Age band:** board book, ages 0–3
- **Format:** 12 spreads, standalone toddler read-aloud — NOT a summary of the picture book
- **Word count:** honest `wc -w` per language in [`NOTES.md`](NOTES.md)
- **Pitch:** Bram the yak cannot whisper — BRONK! — but one foggy night, loud is exactly right.

> Version of the ages 4–8 picture book (`../../bram-the-yak.en.md`). Same characters (Bram, little Pip), one core beat kept: the voice that was too loud turns out to be just right. Every spread carries an illustration note in italics; the seat generates no art.

---

[Spread 1]
*Illustration: A huge shaggy yak beaming on a green hill, tiny birds blown sideways off a fence.*
Bram is a big, gentle yak. Big head. Big heart. BIG voice.

[Spread 2]
*Illustration: Bram cupping a hoof to his mouth; a scarecrow's hat launching into the sky.*
Bram tries to whisper. He takes a tiny breath... BRONK! Oh, Bram!

[Spread 3]
*Illustration: The library; books tumbling off shelves; the owl librarian clutching her glasses.*
Shh! The library. A tiny breath... BRONK! Books go flump. Oh, Bram!

[Spread 4]
*Illustration: A baby goat shooting up out of her cradle, blanket flying.*
Shh! The baby is sleeping. A tiny breath... BRONK! "Waah!" Oh, Bram!

[Spread 5]
*Illustration: Hide-and-seek in the woods; every animal pointing at Bram behind the thinnest tree.*
Shh! Hide-and-seek. A tiny breath... BRONK! "Found you, Bram!"

[Spread 6]
*Illustration: Bram alone on a hill at dusk, chin on the grass, small and sad under a big sky.*
Poor Bram sits all alone. Even his sigh goes bronk. A little, sad bronk.

[Spread 7]
*Illustration: Thick grey fog rolling down the valley, swallowing the trees; worried faces gathering.*
Then — fog! Thick, grey fog. Where is little Pip? "Help! I'm lost!"

[Spread 8]
*Illustration: Animals calling into the cotton-wool fog, hooves cupped round mouths, voices going nowhere.*
Everyone calls. But their voices are too small. Pfft. Pip can't hear them.

[Spread 9]
*Illustration: All the animals turning to look at Bram; Bram's eyes going wide with a giant idea.*
Who can be loud? Who can be VERY loud? BRAM can!

[Spread 10]
*Illustration: Bram on the hilltop, chest puffing up like a balloon, ears back.*
Bram stands tall. Bram takes a BIG, BIG breath...

[Spread 11]
*Illustration: Bram mid-honk, the fog blasting apart in rings; far off, a little lamb turning toward the sound.*
BRRROOONK! The biggest bronk in the world! The fog rolls away. "That's Bram! I'll follow Bram!"

[Spread 12]
*Illustration: Pip the lamb galloping into Bram's warm shaggy hug; everyone cheering, hats flying — joyfully this time.*
Pip is home! Everyone cheers. "Bram, you are the loudest, BEST yak of all!" And Bram is too happy to whisper. BRONK!

*The end.*
