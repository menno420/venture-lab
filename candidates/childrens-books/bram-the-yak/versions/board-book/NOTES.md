## Kept vs dropped (vs the picture book)

**Kept:** Bram and little Pip; the refrain compressed to toddler length ("A tiny breath... BRONK!"); three of the four failure episodes (library, sleeping baby, hide-and-seek); the sad-alone beat with the sighing bronk (kept — it IS the emotional pivot); the fog, the lost lamb, the one giant rescue BRONK; the ONE core emotional beat — the too-loud voice is exactly what saves the day, and Bram is loved as he is.

**Dropped:** the Mayor Swan fancy-dinner episode (fourth failure, most socially complex — being sent outside by an adult authority is a 4–8 nuance); the weekday structure (Monday/Tuesday/... means nothing at 0–3); all named side characters except Pip (owl, swan, baby Nana/Naatje/Nele appear only in art); the foghorn-job epilogue with the medal (the final spread lands on the hug and one last happy BRONK instead); most narration between refrains.

This is a standalone read-aloud — refrain, refrain, refrain, sad beat, rescue, hug. A toddler needs no knowledge of the picture book, and the BRONK invites the child to shout the honk on every page.
