## Kept vs dropped (vs the picture book)

**Kept:** Wren/Fenna/Frieda and Milo/Bram/Jonas; the harbor town (Saltwick/Havenlo/Kleinhafen); the three gift stones (boat, cat, star); the boathouse reveal; the ONE core emotional beat — the stones are a shy kid's hello and the mystery ends in a friend; the pay-it-forward closing image (a new stone for somebody new).

**Dropped:** the notebook and the detective refrain ("Notebook out. Follow the clue, find the answer" — abstract for 0–3, replaced by two chantable toddler refrains: "Who painted the stone?" and "Follow, follow!"); the ribbon and footprint clues (trail cut to two: paint smudge, chalk arrows); the fresh-paint deduction; the reciprocal red-raincoat stone (the emotional "he noticed her too" solve is a 4–8 payoff); all interior monologue; "THE CASE OF..." case-heading device.

This is a standalone read-aloud — a toddler who never sees the picture book gets a complete find-the-friend story; a family that owns both gets the same characters at two ages.
