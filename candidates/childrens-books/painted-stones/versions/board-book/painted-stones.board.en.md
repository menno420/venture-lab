# The Painted Stones — Board-Book Edition

- **Title:** The Painted Stones (board-book cut)
- **Language:** English (master — voice source of truth)
- **Age band:** board book, ages 0–3
- **Format:** 12 spreads, standalone toddler read-aloud — NOT a summary of the picture book
- **Word count:** honest `wc -w` per language in [`NOTES.md`](NOTES.md)
- **Pitch:** Somebody is leaving tiny painted stones for Wren — and somebody is hoping to be her friend.

> Version of the ages 4–8 picture book (`../../painted-stones.en.md`). Same characters (Wren, Milo, Saltwick), one core beat kept: the stones are a shy hello, and the mystery ends in a friend. Every spread carries an illustration note in italics; the seat generates no art.

[Spread 1]
*Illustration: Wren in her red raincoat stomping happily along the harbor path at dawn; boats and gulls.*
Tap, tap, tap go Wren's boots. Good morning, boats! Good morning, gulls! Good morning, Saltwick!

[Spread 2]
*Illustration: A smooth grey stone on the cobbles, a tiny blue boat painted on it; Wren crouching, delighted.*
Look, Wren, look! A little stone. A tiny blue boat on it. Who painted the stone?

[Spread 3]
*Illustration: A second stone in the same spot, a little orange cat on it; Wren's eyes wide.*
Look, Wren, look! Another stone. A little orange cat on it. Who painted the stone?

[Spread 4]
*Illustration: A third stone with a gold star; Wren holding it up to the light.*
Look, Wren, look! One more stone. A shiny gold star on it. Who painted the stone?

[Spread 5]
*Illustration: A bright blue paint smudge on the weathered harbor fence; Wren pointing, marching.*
A smudge of blue paint on the fence. A clue! Follow it, Wren. Follow, follow!

[Spread 6]
*Illustration: Little chalk arrows on the cobblestones curving along the quay; Wren tiptoeing.*
Little chalk arrows on the path. More clues! Follow them, Wren. Follow, follow!

[Spread 7]
*Illustration: The old blue boathouse, door open a crack, warm light inside; Wren pausing, finger to lips.*
The old blue boathouse. The door is open... just a crack. Shh.

[Spread 8]
*Illustration: Through the gap — jars of brushes, pots of paint, stones drying in a sunbeam, and a shy boy peeking out.*
Peek! Jars of brushes. Pots of paint. Stones drying in the sun. And — a boy!

[Spread 9]
*Illustration: The boy holding up three painted stones, cheeks pink.*
"Hello. I'm Milo. I painted the stones. One, two, three. All for you."

[Spread 10]
*Illustration: Milo looking up hopefully; Wren kneeling in the doorway light.*
"I was too shy to say hello. Will you be my friend?"

[Spread 11]
*Illustration: Wren beaming, holding the stones close.*
Wren smiles a big, big smile. "Yes! Mystery solved. Friend found!"

[Spread 12]
*Illustration: The two friends painting one stone together; final panel — a fresh stone waiting on the dawn path.*
Now two friends paint stones together. Splish, blue! Splash, red! And tomorrow — a new little stone, for somebody new.

*The end.*
