# Comet Biscuit manuscripts — decision log

All open creative calls were delegated (owner note, 2026-07-12: "finished books to read

## Scope & structure
- **Age band: 3–6** (picture book, read-aloud early-reader crossover). Taken straight from the
  concept's locked signal; the short sentences, big sound-words and gentle peril all fit 3–6.
- **Three books, not one.** The concept named three ready episodes — *The Great Jam Wobble*,
  *Tow the Lonely Moon*, *The Nebula Bake-Off* — so the deliverable is the opening trio of a
  series rather than a single title. Gives the owner a real series to evaluate.
- **Length/shape: 15 spreads (~500–630 words) per book.** Standard 32-page picture-book length;
  every spread is a page-turn beat. Book 1 opens on the concept's canon sample opening, expanded
  into a full arc.
- **Bloop throughline.** Each mission is resolved by Bloop — the smallest, shyest crew member —
  which turns the series' running heart ("the little one saves the day by being kind/glowing")
  into structure. Concept gap filled; reversible if the owner prefers stand-alone plots.

## Language & localization
- **English is the master.** Dutch and German are native re-tellings (idiom and rhythm first),
  not literal translations — the humour runs on sound-words and read-aloud punch, which are
  re-flowed to land in each language.
- **Names — localize only where the joke needs it:**

  | Element | EN | NL | DE | Why |
  |---|---|---|---|---|
  | Series | Crew of the Comet Biscuit | De Bemanning van de Komeetkoek | Die Crew vom Kometenkeks | literal "comet biscuit/cake" — keeps the cookie-tin gag |
  | Ship | the Comet Biscuit | de Komeetkoek | der Kometenkeks | as above |
  | Captain | Captain Nutmeg | Kapitein Nootmuskaat | Kapitän Muskat | "nutmeg" is a spice word whose charm needs to be understood |
  | Navigator | Barnaby | Barnaby | Barnaby | reads as a name, not a word — kept everywhere |
  | Engineer | Sprocket | Sprocket | Sprocket | reads as a name — kept everywhere |
  | Jellyfish | Bloop | Bloep | Blubb | the name IS the sound; matched to each language's onomatopoeia |
  | Jam | starberry jam | sterbessenjam | Sternbeermarmelade | invented fruit, translated literally |
  | Destination | Moon of Muffins | de Muffinmaan | der Muffinmond | literal |
  | Tow-rope | licorice whip | dropveter | Lakritzschnur | NL "dropveter" is a real Dutch sweet — funnier than a plain rope |
  | Bake-off | Nebula Bake-Off | Nevel-Bakwedstrijd | Nebel-Backduell | literal |

- **Sound-words, localized for read-aloud:** KER-CHUNK → KE-TSJONK (NL) / KA-TSCHUNK (DE);
  WHOOOSH → WHOESJ / WUSCH; POP → PLOP / PLOPP; bloop → bloep / blubb; glug → glok / gluck.
- **Barnaby's catchphrase** "…perfect." stays "…perfect." (NL) / "…perfekt." (DE).
  **Nutmeg's** "NOTHING can stop us" → "NIETS kan ons tegenhouden!" / "NICHTS kann uns aufhalten!".
