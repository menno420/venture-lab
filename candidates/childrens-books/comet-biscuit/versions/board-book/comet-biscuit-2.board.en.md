# Tow the Lonely Moon — Board-Book Edition

- **Title:** Tow the Lonely Moon (board-book cut)
- **Series:** Crew of the Comet Biscuit · Book 2
- **Language:** English (master — voice source of truth)
- **Age band:** board book, ages 0–3
- **Format:** 12 spreads, standalone toddler read-aloud — NOT a summary of the picture book
- **Word count:** honest `wc -w` per language in [`NOTES.md`](NOTES.md)
- **Pitch:** A tiny lost moon is too shy to come out — until little glowing Bloop sits down beside it in the dark.

> Version of the ages 3–6 picture book (`../../en/book-2-tow-the-lonely-moon.md`). Same crew (Captain Nutmeg, Sprocket, Barnaby, Bloop), one core beat kept: the littlest crew member helps not by pulling or talking, but just by sitting close and glowing. Every spread carries an illustration note in italics; the seat generates no art.

---

[Spread 1]
*Illustration: the dented cookie-tin spaceship puttering through deep-blue nighttime space; the crew leaning toward a tiny sound.*
The Comet Biscuit flies through the nighttime stars. Ker-CHUNK! Ker-CHUNK! Shh... what's that? Sniff. Sniff.

[Spread 2]
*Illustration: a teeny round moon all alone in the dark, two big shiny eyes, one enormous wobbly lip.*
A little moon! All alone. All lost. "I want to go home," says the little moon. Sniff.

[Spread 3]
*Illustration: Sprocket the gecko grinning, holding up an endlessly long red licorice whip; Nutmeg striking a heroic pose.*
"We will take you home!" says Captain Nutmeg. Sprocket finds a rope — a long, long licorice rope!

[Spread 4]
*Illustration: the red licorice looped gently round the little moon; the ship straining forward, licorice stretching.*
Round the little moon goes the licorice. Pull, pull... sproing! Off we go!

[Spread 5]
*Illustration: a huge striped planet looming ahead; the little moon ducking behind it, only its eyes peeking out.*
But look — a great BIG planet! The little moon feels shy. The little moon hides.

[Spread 6]
*Illustration: the licorice snapping the ship backwards; the crew tumbling head over heels in a tangle.*
Twang! The licorice snaps back. Everyone wobbles. Where did the little moon go?

[Spread 7]
*Illustration: Nutmeg speechifying on the porthole, Sprocket pulling an upside-down silly face, Barnaby lifting a slow-motion thumb; the moon still hidden.*
Nutmeg makes a speech. Sprocket makes a silly face. Barnaby lifts one slow, slow thumb. But the little moon still hides. Sniff.

[Spread 8]
*Illustration: the small cyan-glowing jellyfish drifting quietly out of the porthole, all the way across the dark, settling beside the moon.*
Here comes Bloop. Bloop! Bloop floats all the way over — and sits down right beside the moon. And glows.

[Spread 9]
*Illustration: two soft glows side by side in the big dark — the moon peeking out, warming, coming out from behind the planet.*
Soft and warm and steady. The little moon peeks out. Two little lights in the big dark. "Okay," says the moon. "I'm ready."

[Spread 10]
*Illustration: the sloth's paw settling serenely onto the big red button; ship, moon and jellyfish sailing together across the sky on the licorice line.*
"Tow away!" calls Captain Nutmeg. Barnaby presses the big red button. "...perfect." WHOOOSH! Pull, pull... sproing!

[Spread 11]
*Illustration: the little moon nestling into its own orbit between two beaming, relieved stars.*
Home! Right between two friendly stars. "There you are!" they cry. "We missed you so!"

[Spread 12]
*Illustration: the moon glowing bright and waving; Bloop's tentacles curled into two tiny hearts; the ship puttering off into the dark.*
The little moon glows and glows — not shy at all. Bloop! And the Comet Biscuit wobbles home. Ker-CHUNK. Ker-CHUNK. Goodnight, little moon.

*The end.*
