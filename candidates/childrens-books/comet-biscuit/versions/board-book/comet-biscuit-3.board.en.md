# The Nebula Bake-Off — Board-Book Edition

- **Title:** The Nebula Bake-Off (board-book cut)
- **Series:** Crew of the Comet Biscuit · Book 3
- **Language:** English (master — voice source of truth)
- **Age band:** board book, ages 0–3
- **Format:** 12 spreads, standalone toddler read-aloud — NOT a summary of the picture book
- **Word count:** honest `wc -w` per language in [`NOTES.md`](NOTES.md)
- **Pitch:** The ship needs a new part, the cupboard is nearly empty — so the crew stirs in a whole lot of love and bakes the biggest, wonkiest cake in space.

> Version of the ages 3–6 picture book (`../../en/book-3-the-nebula-bake-off.md`). Same crew (Captain Nutmeg, Sprocket, Barnaby, Bloop), one core beat kept: the wonky, home-made, made-with-love thing turns out to be the most wonderful thing of all — with Bloop glowing on top. Every spread carries an illustration note in italics; the seat generates no art.

---

[Spread 1]
*Illustration: a rocket-leg tumbling off the dented cookie-tin spaceship; Sprocket the gecko catching it, baffled.*
Ker-CHUNK! Oh no! A rocket-leg falls right off the Comet Biscuit. The ship needs a new part.

[Spread 2]
*Illustration: the tiny hamster captain brandishing a wooden spoon like a scepter, eyes sparkling.*
"Then we will bake a cake!" says Captain Nutmeg. "The best cake in space wins a shiny new part!"

[Spread 3]
*Illustration: the nearly-bare cupboard: one grease tin, three bendy crackers, and a jittery little glowing jellyfish between them.*
What's in the cupboard? One tin of grease. Three bendy crackers. And one nervous little Bloop. Bloop!

[Spread 4]
*Illustration: the sloth spelling out the recipe line by line; Nutmeg tipping a heart-shaped cloud into the bowl.*
Barnaby reads the recipe. Very. Slowly. "...one... cup... of... love." "We have LOADS of that!" says Nutmeg. In it goes!

[Spread 5]
*Illustration: everyone stirring the big bowl; Sprocket winking and mashing a big button behind his back.*
Stir, stir, stir! A squirt of grease. A crumble of cracker. Then Sprocket pushes a button for luck. Uh-oh.

[Spread 6]
*Illustration: the oven blazing like a small friendly sun, star-smoke everywhere; Bloop plunging headfirst into the batter.*
WHOOOMP! The oven glows like a little sun. Smoke everywhere! And Bloop dives into the batter to hide. Bloop... glug.

[Spread 7]
*Illustration: a towering, wobbling cake rising up through the smoke, cracker layers and shiny swirls, past the ceiling.*
But look! The cake is rising. Up, up, UP! Taller than Sprocket. Taller than the WHOLE Comet Biscuit!

[Spread 8]
*Illustration: Bloop popping out of the cake-summit, beaming, glowing softly through the frosting.*
And right on top, glowing through the frosting... Bloop! The biggest, wonkiest, most wonderful cake ever.

[Spread 9]
*Illustration: three stony-faced judges at a long table; the glowing, leaning cake-tower wobbling in; three forks lifting one bite.*
The judges of the bake-off never, ever smile. In wobbles the cake. They take one little bite...

[Spread 10]
*Illustration: the stern judges bursting into happy tears, forks in hand, handing over a bow-wrapped engine part.*
...and cry big happy tears! "The most DELICIOUS cake ever!" And the prize? One shiny new part!

[Spread 11]
*Illustration: Sprocket bolting the shiny new part onto the ship; the whole crew listening, ears up.*
Sprocket bolts it on. Ker-CHUNK... purrrr. Oh! The Comet Biscuit hums, soft as a sleepy cat.

[Spread 12]
*Illustration: the cozy ship puttering home through the sparkly dark; Bloop licking frosting off its tentacles; everyone yawning.*
Home flies the crew, full of cake. Bloop licks frosting off every tentacle. Bloop. Purrrr, says the ship. Goodnight, little ship. Goodnight, stars.

*The end.*
