# The Great Jam Wobble — Board-Book Edition

- **Title:** The Great Jam Wobble (board-book cut)
- **Series:** Crew of the Comet Biscuit · Book 1
- **Language:** English (master — voice source of truth)
- **Age band:** board book, ages 0–3
- **Format:** 12 spreads, standalone toddler read-aloud — NOT a summary of the picture book
- **Word count:** honest `wc -w` per language in [`NOTES.md`](NOTES.md)
- **Pitch:** The jam floats loose in the wobbly cookie-tin spaceship — and little glowing Bloop is the light that brings it all back home.

> Version of the ages 3–6 picture book (`../../en/book-1-the-great-jam-wobble.md`). Same crew (Captain Nutmeg, Sprocket, Barnaby, Bloop), one core beat kept: the littlest, shyest crew member saves the day just by glowing. Every spread carries an illustration note in italics; the seat generates no art.

---

[Spread 1]
*Illustration: the dented cookie-tin spaceship puttering through grape-jelly-purple space, star-shaped smoke puffing from its little chimney.*
The Comet Biscuit flies through the stars. Ker-CHUNK! Ker-CHUNK! Off we go!

[Spread 2]
*Illustration: the tiny hamster captain on her stack of three cushions and a lunchbox, striking a heroic pose over the console.*
"Crew!" calls Captain Nutmeg. "Jam! Forty jars of jam! To the Moon of Muffins!"

[Spread 3]
*Illustration: down in the cargo hold, a small cyan-glowing jellyfish waves shyly from between the crates.*
And down in the hold, somebody small is glowing. Somebody small goes bloop. Hello, Bloop!

[Spread 4]
*Illustration: zero gravity — Nutmeg's helmet drifts over her eyes, Sprocket's crackers float out of his mouth, everyone giggling mid-air.*
Up in the stars, everything floats. The helmet floats. The crackers float. Whee!

[Spread 5]
*Illustration: the crate lid lifting off; big wobbling red jam-blobs rising up through the ship.*
And the jam box goes... POP! Out comes the jam! Wobble, wobble, glug, glug, GLUG!

[Spread 6]
*Illustration: the gecko stuck to the ceiling, frantically taping floating red blobs to walls, floor, and his own tail.*
Jam on the walls! Jam on the floor! Sprocket tapes and tapes — one, two, TEN! Too much jam!

[Spread 7]
*Illustration: Nutmeg tumbling upside down mid-air, a blob of jam on her nose, one paw raised heroically.*
"I have a plan!" says Captain Nutmeg. Upside down. Jam on her nose. Hmm.

[Spread 8]
*Illustration: Bloop drifting gently up into the very biggest jam-blob and settling inside it, beaming.*
Here comes Bloop. Bloop! Right into the biggest blob of jam!

[Spread 9]
*Illustration: Bloop glowing cyan through the starberry red like a lantern; Nutmeg's eyes going wide with the idea.*
Bloop glows and glows, like a little lamp. "That's it!" says Nutmeg. "BLOOP is the plan! Follow the light!"

[Spread 10]
*Illustration: the sloth's paw settling serenely onto the big red button; the ship starting a huge loop.*
Barnaby presses the big red button. "...perfect." WHOOOSH! Loop-the-loop!

[Spread 11]
*Illustration: every red blob spiraling after Bloop's glow, back into the crate; Sprocket slamming and taping the lid.*
All the jam chases Bloop's little light — wobble, wobble, glug, glug, GLUG — back into the box! Lid on. Tape, tape!

[Spread 12]
*Illustration: the Moon of Muffins, warm and golden; one big sticky group hug with Bloop still glowing in the middle; the ship puttering home.*
The Moon of Muffins! Forty jars of jam — all there. One big sticky hug for Bloop. Bloop! And the Comet Biscuit wobbles home. Ker-CHUNK. Ker-CHUNK. Goodnight, stars.

*The end.*
