## Book 1 — The Great Jam Wobble: kept vs dropped

Toddler arc: intro → floats → jam out → jam back → hug (spreads 2–4's mission-briefing comedy and the spread-14 delivery beat compressed).

**Kept:** the whole crew by name and shtick — Nutmeg on her cushion-stack, Sprocket taping, Barnaby's slow "...perfect." button-press; the Ker-CHUNK sound bracket opening AND closing the book; the zero-g float beat (helmet, crackers); the POP crate lid; the jam escape and return; the ONE core emotional beat — the littlest, shyest crew member saves the day just by glowing (Bloop drifts into the biggest blob, becomes the light the jam follows home); the sticky group hug with Bloop still in the jam. A toddler refrain engine built from the picture book's own sounds: "Wobble, wobble, glug, glug, GLUG!" chanted on the jam-out spread and again (reversed to homecoming) on the jam-back spread, plus Bloop's name-as-sound as a call-and-response through the book.

**Dropped:** all interior narration and joke-scaffolding (the ship going ker-CHUNK "just to show it meant it"; the MISSION-of-COURAGE/GLORY/JAM briefing routine; "The plan was bad. The heart was huge."; "a little sticky, a little glowing, entirely on time"); Barnaby's last-Tuesday running gag (his button-press stays, the time-joke goes — it's a 4–6 joke); the plan-logic spread's explanatory chain (compressed to "Follow the light!"); Bloop's homesickness framing (0–3 gets a shy hello, not a mood); the "NOTHING can stop us" catchphrase-and-payoff structure; spread 14's Tuesday-mornings smell line; the "just a little bit delicious" aside. A new goodnight landing ("Goodnight, stars" / "Welterusten, sterren" / "Gute Nacht, Sterne") replaces the series-hook ending so the cut lands on sleep like a board book should.

## Book 2 — Tow the Lonely Moon: kept vs dropped

Toddler arc: sniff in the dark → lost little moon → licorice tow → moon hides shy → Bloop sits beside it and glows → moon comes out → tow home → goodnight (the picture book's spreads 7–8 pep-talk sequence compressed to one spread, spreads 13–15's triple ending folded into two).

**Kept:** the sniff-in-the-night opening (the book's best listening beat — a toddler can sniff along); the licorice-whip tow rope with its stretch-and-snap sounds (pull-pull-sproing as the refrain, Twang!/TOING!/Ratsch-Boing! for the snap, per edition); the crew's three failed comfort attempts in miniature (speech / silly face / slow thumb — Barnaby's slowness kept as pure physical comedy); the ONE core emotional beat — Bloop helps not by pulling or talking but by sitting close and glowing, and the moon finds its own glow next to Bloop's ("two little lights in the big dark"); the homecoming between the two worried friendly stars; Barnaby's "...perfect." button-press; the Ker-CHUNK bracket. Goodnight landing: "Goodnight, little moon" — the source book's own subject IS the sleeper being tucked in, so the landing tucks the moon in rather than importing book 1's "Goodnight, stars."

**Dropped:** the "did the ship just sniff?" dialogue-comedy opening (kept as pure sound); the moon's shame framing ("I didn't want them to see me being lost" — 0–3 gets shy-and-hiding, not embarrassment); Nutmeg's lost-in-the-kitchen captain monologue; the Bloop-translation parenthetical ("which meant: neither are you, now"); the "Captains don't cry. Captains LEAK." exchange (a 4–6 joke, and a second emotional register the toddler cut doesn't need); the moon's "bring the glowy one!" series-hook call-back and the tentacle-hearts closing gag (the hearts survive in the final illustration note only).

## Book 3 — The Nebula Bake-Off: kept vs dropped

Toddler arc: leg falls off → bake a cake to win the part → almost-empty cupboard → stir in love → oven WHOOMP → cake rises with Bloop inside → glowing on top → judges cry happy tears → prize part → the ship purrs → goodnight (the contest's rules-subplot — no first prize, the invented CHAOS medal — compressed to a single prize beat).

**Kept:** the falling rocket-leg opener with the Ker-CHUNK bracket; the almost-bare cupboard inventory as a counting-and-naming spread (one tin, three crackers, one Bloop); Barnaby reading the recipe Very. Slowly. and the cup-of-love beat (the book's heart, and a perfect read-aloud slow-down); Sprocket's button-push-for-luck as the toddler-legible cause of the WHOOOMP; Bloop hiding in the batter and popping out glowing through the frosting on top (the series' standing core beat — the littlest one becomes the light — here in its book-3 form); the cake rising Up, up, UP! as the physical refrain; the never-smiling judges crying happy tears; the new part and the ship's first-ever smooth purr. Goodnight landing: the purr itself — "soft as a sleepy cat... Goodnight, little ship. Goodnight, stars."

**Dropped:** the stars-as-money economy ("those cost stars, we don't have stars" — needs explaining); the "It's not a fire, it's ATMOSPHERE" routine and the fire entirely (a lit cake is a 4–6 joke and a 0–3 hazard-model problem); the rules/presentation disqualification and the invented MOST DELICIOUS CHAOS medal (two-step irony, compressed to "the most DELICIOUS cake ever!" + prize); "CHAOS! That's US!"; the perfect/disaster/"Same thing!" exchange; the next-mission-that-cannot-go-wrong series-hook ending, replaced by the purring-ship goodnight landing.
