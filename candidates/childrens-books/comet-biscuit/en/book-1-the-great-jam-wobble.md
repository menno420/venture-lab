# The Great Jam Wobble
### Crew of the Comet Biscuit · Book 1

**Series:** Crew of the Comet Biscuit
**Book:** 1
**Age band:** 3–6 (picture book; read-aloud early-reader crossover)
**Word count:** ~520
**Format:** 32-page picture book — 15 spreads
**Pitch:** Forty jars of starberry jam float loose in zero gravity — and only one small, glowing, homesick jellyfish can round them all back up.

---

**SPREAD 1**
The Comet Biscuit went *ker-CHUNK.*
Then it went *ker-CHUNK* again, just to show it meant it.
[Illustration: the dented cookie-tin spaceship wobbling through grape-jelly-purple space, star-shaped smoke puffing from its little chimney.]

**SPREAD 2**
"CREW!" shouted Captain Nutmeg, standing on three cushions and a lunchbox so she could see over the console. "This is your captain speaking! We have a VERY important mission! A mission of COURAGE! A mission of GLORY! A mission of—"
"Jam," said Sprocket, without looking up from the engine. The gecko had one foot stuck to the ceiling and a cheese cracker in his mouth. "We're delivering jam."
[Illustration: the tiny hamster captain strikes a heroic pose on her cushion-stack; the gecko fixes the engine upside down.]

**SPREAD 3**
"—a mission of JAM!" said Captain Nutmeg, not missing a beat. "Starberry jam! Forty jars! To the Moon of Muffins! And NOTHING can stop us!"
*Ker-CHUNK.*
Everyone looked at the ceiling.
[Illustration: Nutmeg throws a triumphant paw in the air; every crew member glances up at the rattling ceiling.]

**SPREAD 4**
"Course plotted," said Barnaby the sloth. Very calmly. Very slowly. He had begun plotting it last Tuesday. His paw drifted toward a button the size and speed of a cloud. "Turn... left... in... three..."
"BARNABY, WE ALREADY TURNED LEFT—"
"...perfect," said Barnaby. And it was. It was always, somehow, perfect.
[Illustration: the dreamy sloth points serenely one way while the ship already sails exactly there.]

**SPREAD 5**
The Comet Biscuit rattled, wobbled, popped a bolt, caught the bolt, gave it back, and zoomed off into the sparkly dark.
"See?" beamed Captain Nutmeg. "NOTHING can stop us."
From the cargo hold, a small, glowing, extremely homesick jellyfish went *bloop.*
[Illustration: the ship streaks through the stars; down in a porthole, tiny Bloop glows shyly.]

**SPREAD 6**
Up in the stars, the ship went light as a feather. Everything floated. Nutmeg's helmet floated over her eyes. Sprocket's crackers floated out of his mouth.
And in the hold, the lid of the jam crate went...
*POP.*
[Illustration: zero gravity! Helmet, crackers and crew drift in the air; the crate lid lifts off.]

**SPREAD 7**
Out came the jam.
Not a drip. Not a dribble.
Forty jars of starberry jam, floating up in great big wobbling red blobs — glug, glug, GLUG — all through the ship.
"Um," said Sprocket. "Captain? The jam is escaping."
[Illustration: enormous wobbling red jam-blobs drift through the cabin; the crew stares.]

**SPREAD 8**
"NOBODY PANIC!" said Captain Nutmeg, panicking beautifully, upside down, a blob of jam on her nose. "This is FINE! This is a captain thing! I have a PLAN!"
The plan was bad. The heart was huge.
[Illustration: Nutmeg tumbles upside down mid-air, jam on her nose, one paw raised heroically.]

**SPREAD 9**
Sprocket did what Sprocket does best. He stuck to the ceiling, whipped out his tape, and started catching blobs — one, two, five, ten — taping jam to the walls, the floor, his own tail.
It was not working. There was SO much jam.
[Illustration: the gecko clings to the ceiling, frantically taping floating red blobs everywhere.]

**SPREAD 10**
And then, drifting up through the middle of it all, went a small glowing jellyfish.
*Bloop.*
Straight into the biggest blob of jam.
And stopped. Completely surrounded. Glowing cyan through the starberry red.
Bloop looked out at everyone... and smiled the happiest smile in the whole galaxy.
[Illustration: Bloop sits beaming inside a giant red jam-bubble, cyan glow shining through.]

**SPREAD 11**
"...That's it," whispered Nutmeg. "That's the plan! BLOOP is the plan!"
Because a glowing jellyfish in a jam-blob is a light. And a light is something you can follow. And something you can follow is something you can CATCH.
[Illustration: Nutmeg's eyes light up with an idea; the whole crew turns toward the glowing Bloop.]

**SPREAD 12**
"Barnaby!" called the Captain. "We need a turn! A big one! Right... NOW!"
Barnaby smiled his slow smile. He had begun reaching for the button last Tuesday.
His paw touched down.
"...perfect."
[Illustration: Barnaby's paw settles serenely onto the big red button.]

**SPREAD 13**
The Comet Biscuit swung — WHOOOSH — one great big loop-the-loop.
Every blob of jam slid together, round and round, chasing Bloop's little glow, straight back into the crate — glug, glug, GLUG — forty jars, every drop.
Sprocket slammed the lid. Taped it. Taped it again.
[Illustration: the ship loops wildly; all the red blobs spiral after Bloop back into the crate.]

**SPREAD 14**
The Moon of Muffins smelled of warm butter and Tuesday mornings.
The crew delivered forty jars of starberry jam — a little sticky, a little glowing, entirely on time.
"NOTHING," said Captain Nutmeg proudly, jam in her ears, "can stop this crew."
And nothing ever did.
[Illustration: the fragrant Moon of Muffins; the sticky, proud crew hands over the jam jars.]

**SPREAD 15**
Well. Almost nothing.
"...Bloop's still in the jam," said Barnaby.
*Bloop,* agreed Bloop, glowing, delighted, and just a little bit delicious.
So the whole crew squished into one big sticky hug — and the Comet Biscuit wobbled home, puffing little star-shaped clouds into the sparkly dark.

THE END
[Illustration: one big sticky group hug; the ship tuckers home puffing star-shaped clouds.]
