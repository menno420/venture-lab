# The Nebula Bake-Off
### Crew of the Comet Biscuit · Book 3

**Series:** Crew of the Comet Biscuit
**Book:** 3
**Age band:** 3–6 (picture book; read-aloud early-reader crossover)
**Word count:** ~575
**Format:** 32-page picture book — 15 spreads
**Pitch:** With a spoon, a dollop of engine grease and a whole lot of love, the crew bakes the wonkiest, glowiest, most delicious cake in the galaxy.

---

**SPREAD 1**
The Comet Biscuit went *ker-CHUNK* — and this time, a whole rocket-leg fell off.
"We need a new engine part," said Sprocket, holding the leg. "A GOOD one. And those cost stars. We don't have stars."
"No," said Captain Nutmeg, eyes gleaming. "But we have a SPOON."
[Illustration: a rocket-leg tumbles off the ship; the gecko holds it, baffled, as Nutmeg's eyes sparkle.]

**SPREAD 2**
"The Nebula Bake-Off!" she announced. "The biggest baking contest in the galaxy! First prize: one shiny new engine part! All we have to do... is bake the greatest cake in space!"
"With what?" asked Sprocket.
Everyone looked in the cupboard.
[Illustration: Nutmeg brandishes a wooden spoon like a scepter; the crew peers into a bare cupboard.]

**SPREAD 3**
The cupboard had:
one tin of engine grease,
three crackers (two a bit soft),
and one nervous jellyfish going *bloop.*
"...Perfect," said Captain Nutmeg.
"That is NOT perfect," said Sprocket. "That is a disaster."
"Same thing!" said the Captain. "Ovens ON!"
[Illustration: the bare cupboard: a grease tin, three bendy crackers, and a jittery little Bloop.]

**SPREAD 4**
Barnaby read the recipe. Very. Slowly.
"...it says... add one cup... of... love..."
"WE HAVE LOADS OF THAT!" cried Nutmeg, and dumped the whole crew's worth in.
[Illustration: Barnaby spells out the recipe; Nutmeg tips a heart-shaped cloud into the bowl.]

**SPREAD 5**
Sprocket "improved" things. Sprocket always improves things.
A squirt of grease (for shine). A crumble of cracker (for crunch). A little extra button-push (for luck).
"Sprocket," said Barnaby, slowly, "...that button... was the... rockets—"
[Illustration: the gecko winks and mashes a big button; Barnaby raises a slow-motion warning paw.]

**SPREAD 6**
*WHOOOMP.*
The oven lit up like a small friendly sun. The whole kitchen wobbled. Star-smoke everywhere. Nutmeg's helmet spun clean around.
And Bloop — poor nervous Bloop — dove into the cake batter to hide.
[Illustration: the oven blazes like a little sun, smoke everywhere, Nutmeg's helmet spinning, Bloop plunging into the batter.]

**SPREAD 7**
Out of the smoke, the crew stared.
Because the cake was...
RISING.
Up and up and up — cracker layers and grease-shine swirls, taller than Sprocket, taller than the Captain's cushions, taller than the WHOLE Comet Biscuit.
[Illustration: a towering, wobbling cake rises up through the smoke to the ceiling.]

**SPREAD 8**
And right at the very top, glowing softly through the frosting, one small delighted jellyfish popped out.
*Bloop.*
The most beautiful, most enormous, most gloriously lopsided cake in the entire galaxy.
Also, very slightly, on fire.
[Illustration: Bloop pops up beaming from the lopsided cake-summit; a tiny flame flickers merrily on one side.]

**SPREAD 9**
"...Should we put the fire out?" asked Sprocket.
"It's not a fire," said Captain Nutmeg grandly. "It's ATMOSPHERE. Wheel it in!"
[Illustration: Nutmeg proudly presents the flaming cake-tower; Sprocket looks doubtful, holding a bucket.]

**SPREAD 10**
The judges of the Nebula Bake-Off had tasted every cake in the cosmos. They were very serious. They had never, not once, smiled.
Then the Comet Biscuit's cake wobbled in — glowing, smoking, leaning like a happy tower, one jellyfish waving from the top.
[Illustration: three stony-faced judges at a table as the glowing, leaning cake-tower wobbles in.]

**SPREAD 11**
The judges took one bite.
Grease-shine. Cracker-crunch. A whole crew's worth of love. And the tiniest, warmest glow.
And the very serious judges of the Nebula Bake-Off... burst into tears.
"It's HIDEOUS," they wept. "It's LEANING. It's ON FIRE. It's the most DELICIOUS thing we've ever eaten."
[Illustration: the stern judges sob with joy, forks in hand.]

**SPREAD 12**
"On presentation," sniffed the head judge, dabbing her eyes, "...we cannot give you first prize. The cake is on fire. There are RULES."
The crew's faces fell.
[Illustration: the head judge holds up a rule-book apologetically; the crew looks crestfallen.]

**SPREAD 13**
"HOWEVER." The judge held up a brand-new, extra-shiny, never-before-awarded medal.
"For the first time in Bake-Off history... the prize for MOST DELICIOUS CHAOS!"
And she handed them a shiny new engine part, wrapped in a bow.
[Illustration: the judge beams, presenting a medal and a bow-wrapped engine part.]

**SPREAD 14**
"CHAOS!" beamed Captain Nutmeg, holding it high. "That's US! That's our BEST thing!"
Sprocket bolted the new part on. The Comet Biscuit went *ker-CHUNK*... and then, for once, a lovely smooth *purrrr.*
[Illustration: Nutmeg hoists the medal; Sprocket happily bolts the shiny part onto the ship.]

**SPREAD 15**
So the crew flew home, full of cake, a medal on the wall, a jellyfish licking frosting off its own tentacles.
"Next mission?" asked Sprocket.
"Something calm," said Captain Nutmeg. "Something simple. Something that CANNOT possibly go wrong."
*Ker-CHUNK,* said the Comet Biscuit.
*Bloop,* said Bloop.
And off they wobbled, into the sparkly dark.

THE END
[Illustration: the cozy ship tuckers home, medal on the wall, Bloop licking frosting off its tentacles.]
