# Tow the Lonely Moon
### Crew of the Comet Biscuit · Book 2

**Series:** Crew of the Comet Biscuit
**Book:** 2
**Age band:** 3–6 (picture book; read-aloud early-reader crossover)
**Word count:** ~575
**Format:** 32-page picture book — 15 spreads
**Pitch:** A tiny, too-shy little moon has drifted off course — and a small glowing jellyfish shows it that being seen isn't so scary after all.

---

**SPREAD 1**
One quiet night in the sparkly dark, the Comet Biscuit heard a very small sound.
*sniff.*
"Did the ship just sniff?" asked Captain Nutmeg.
"That wasn't the ship," said Barnaby, slowly. "That was... a moon."
[Illustration: the ship drifts quietly through the night; the crew leans in, listening.]

**SPREAD 2**
And there it was. A teeny little moon, all alone, drifting the wrong way, with two big shiny eyes and one enormous wobbly lip.
*sniff.* "...I got lost," it whispered. "And I'm too shy to ask anyone for help."
"Well!" said Nutmeg. "You didn't ask! WE noticed! That's completely different!"
[Illustration: a tiny round moon with huge shiny eyes and a wobbling lip floats alone in space.]

**SPREAD 3**
"Crew," announced the Captain, "we are towing this moon HOME. Sprocket — we need a rope!"
Sprocket rummaged in the hold. Tape? No. Wire? No.
"...Will licorice do?" He held up the longest, stretchiest, reddest licorice whip in the galaxy.
"PERFECT," said the whole crew.
[Illustration: the gecko grins, holding up an endlessly long red licorice whip.]

**SPREAD 4**
They looped the licorice gently round the little moon.
"Hold on tight!" said Nutmeg. "Next stop: HOME! Barnaby, which way?"
"...that way," said Barnaby, pointing at nothing in particular, exactly correctly.
[Illustration: the red licorice loops softly round the little moon; Barnaby points serenely at empty space.]

**SPREAD 5**
The Comet Biscuit pulled. The licorice stretched — sproing — long and thin and thinner...
And then the little moon saw a great BIG planet up ahead.
And got shy.
And hid.
[Illustration: the licorice stretches taut; the little moon ducks nervously behind a huge planet.]

**SPREAD 6**
*Twang!*
The licorice snapped the ship backwards. The moon peeked out from behind the giant planet, cheeks glowing.
"...Sorry," it whispered. "Everyone's so big. And I'm so small. I didn't want them to see me being lost."
[Illustration: the ship snaps backward; the moon peeks out with glowing cheeks.]

**SPREAD 7**
"Ohh," said Captain Nutmeg, softly, climbing right up onto the porthole. "Little moon. Listen.
EVERYBODY gets lost! I got lost this MORNING! In the KITCHEN! I'm the CAPTAIN!"
The little moon almost smiled. Almost. Then a bigger planet drifted by — and it hid again.
[Illustration: tiny Nutmeg stands heroically on the porthole, giving the moon a pep talk.]

**SPREAD 8**
The crew tried everything.
Nutmeg made speeches. (Loud ones.)
Sprocket made silly faces. (Upside down.)
Barnaby made... a very slow encouraging thumbs-up that arrived some time next week.
The little moon just sniffled behind the planets.
[Illustration: Nutmeg speechifies, Sprocket pulls a face upside down, Barnaby lifts a slow-motion thumb.]

**SPREAD 9**
And then, without a word, a small glowing jellyfish drifted out of the porthole.
*Bloop.*
It floated all the way over to the little moon. It didn't make a speech. It didn't pull. It just... settled beside it. And glowed. Soft and warm and steady.
[Illustration: Bloop drifts quietly across space and nestles, glowing, beside the shy moon.]

**SPREAD 10**
"...You're not hiding," whispered the moon.
*Bloop,* said Bloop. (Which meant: neither are you, now. We're right here.)
And the little moon looked at the small brave glowing thing beside it — smaller even than itself — and something inside it went warm.
[Illustration: moon and jellyfish side by side, both glowing softly in the dark.]

**SPREAD 11**
The moon took a big breath.
And came out from behind the planet.
All the way out. Glowing its own soft glow, right next to Bloop's.
"...okay," it said. "Take me home. I'm ready. I don't mind being seen."
[Illustration: the little moon floats bravely into the open, glowing beside Bloop.]

**SPREAD 12**
"CREW!" roared Captain Nutmeg. "TOW AWAY!"
The Comet Biscuit heaved. The licorice stretched. Barnaby's paw came down — "...perfect" — and WHOOOSH, moon and ship and jellyfish sailed together across the whole sky, past every big planet, brave as anything.
[Illustration: ship, moon and jellyfish sweep together across the starry sky on the licorice line.]

**SPREAD 13**
They tucked the little moon back into its own little orbit, exactly where it belonged, between two friendly stars who'd been worried sick.
"THERE it is!" they cried. "We missed you!"
The little moon glowed brighter than it ever had.
[Illustration: the moon nestles happily between two relieved, friendly stars.]

**SPREAD 14**
"See?" said Captain Nutmeg, sniffling just a bit herself. "Nothing to it."
"...you're crying," said Barnaby.
"CAPTAINS don't cry. Captains LEAK. It's different."
[Illustration: Nutmeg secretly wipes away a tear; Barnaby watches kindly.]

**SPREAD 15**
And as the Comet Biscuit wobbled home, the little moon called after them, not shy at all now:
"THANK YOU! COME BACK SOON! ...and bring the glowy one!"
*Bloop,* said Bloop, tentacles curling into two tiny hearts.

THE END
[Illustration: the moon waves brightly after the ship; Bloop curls its tentacles into two little hearts.]
