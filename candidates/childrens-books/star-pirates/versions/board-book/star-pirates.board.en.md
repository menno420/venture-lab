# Goodnight, Bright Revenge — Star Pirates Board Book

- **Title:** Goodnight, Bright Revenge
- **Series:** Star Pirates (from Book 1: The Wrong Way Down)
- **Language:** English (master — voice source of truth)
- **Age band:** board book, ages 0–3
- **Format:** 12 spreads, standalone toddler read-aloud bedtime story — NOT a cut and NOT a summary of the chapter book
- **Word count:** honest `wc -w` per language in [`NOTES.md`](NOTES.md)
- **Pitch:** The sun goes down over the pirate sea, and everyone on the glowing ship says goodnight in their own way — and Squish translates the whole night into kindness.

> Standalone distillation from the 7–9 chapter book (`../../en/`, chapters 01–10) — the source is ~11.4k words of plot, so this board book borrows only its characters and gentlest motifs (the ship that glows, Squish's kind mistranslations, gentle giant Gorm, gruff Captain Ironjaw) and tells a new goodnight story with them; see `NOTES.md` for the honest format note. Every spread carries an illustration note in italics; the seat generates no art.

---

[Spread 1]
*Illustration: the black galleon Bright Revenge rocking gently at anchor as the sun melts gold into the sea.*
The sun sinks into the sea. The pirate ship Bright Revenge rocks slow... and slow... and slow. Time for bed, little pirates.

[Spread 2]
*Illustration: little Pip high in the crow's nest, pointing up as the first stars come out.*
High in the crow's nest, Pip calls: "Nighttime, ho! One star. Two stars. Stars, ho!"

[Spread 3]
*Illustration: enormous Gorm sitting on the forecastle, hands folded, one giant happy tear falling toward the water.*
Gorm watches the sunset. Gorm is very, very big. The sunset is very, very beautiful. One happy tear. Plink!

[Spread 4]
*Illustration: seagulls wheeling over the mast; the little one-eyed translator blob beaming from the rail.*
Mew! Mew! cry the seagulls. "They say: goodnight, sleep tight," says Squish. Squish makes everything sound kind.

[Spread 5]
*Illustration: the silver robot folding all four arms neatly, one after another, eyes dimming to a soft glow.*
Bolt folds his arms. One, two, three, four. "Goodnight," says Bolt. For Bolt, that is a whole song.

[Spread 6]
*Illustration: the little alien captain in his neat jacket, reading solemnly from his laminated card by lantern-light.*
Captain Fizzwick reads his little card: "Rule one hundred: wish everyone a good night." "Goodnight, everyone!"

[Spread 7]
*Illustration: dark waves lapping softly against the hull, silver-tipped by the first stars.*
Shhh, shhh, go the waves. "They say: hush now, little pirates," says Squish.

[Spread 8]
*Illustration: the big pirate captain stomping along a row of hammocks, straightening a blanket with one gruff finger.*
Captain Ironjaw stomps from hammock to hammock. "Captains don't tuck anyone in," she growls. "Captains CHECK." She checks everyone twice.

[Spread 9]
*Illustration: the ship's odd little wonders lighting up one by one — a smokeless lantern, a rope tying itself, a patch of softly humming deck.*
And all over the ship, the little glowing things wake up. The lantern glows. The rope glows. The deck hums its tiny hum.

[Spread 10]
*Illustration: the whole ship aglow from within, a warm lamp riding the dark sea under the stars.*
Glow, little ship, glow. Soft and warm and steady, like a lamp for the whole dark sea.

[Spread 11]
*Illustration: the wind curling through the rigging; Squish half-asleep on Fizzwick's shoulder, whispering.*
The wind sings in the ropes. "It says: sleep... sleep... sleep," whispers Squish. And this time, Squish is exactly right.

[Spread 12]
*Illustration: hammocks full of sleeping pirates and aliens; the glowing ship small and warm on the wide quiet sea.*
Goodnight, Gorm. Goodnight, Bolt. Goodnight, Squish. Goodnight, Captains. The Bright Revenge glows on the quiet water. Shhh. Goodnight, sea.

*The end.*
