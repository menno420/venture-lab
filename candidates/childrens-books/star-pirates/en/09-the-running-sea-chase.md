# Chapter 9 — The Running Sea-Chase

They caught the black fleet at mid-morning, in open water, under a wide grey sky.

There were seven of them — seven dark ships spread across the sea in a great menacing crescent, with Kroll's own vessel, the *Iron Gull*, biggest and blackest of all, riding in the centre. And there, tiny against her huge black hull, chained to the mainmast where everyone could see, were Gorm and, beside him in his net, Squish.

"There they are," breathed Fizzwick, up in the bow. "Oh — there they are."

"Then let's go and be rude," said Ironjaw, and she spun the wheel.

The *Bright Revenge* came in fast — skimming, light, dancing over the swell while the heavy black ships wallowed and turned. Kroll's fleet opened fire. Cannons boomed all along the crescent, and the sea around the *Bright Revenge* went up in white fountains, and a cannonball came screaming straight for the bow —

— and struck the figurehead.

Now, the figurehead of the *Bright Revenge* had, until last night, been a plain carved mermaid. But Bolt and Tessa had worked on her in the dark, and now she was something else — and the instant the cannonball reached her, a great shimmering bubble bloomed out from her carved hands, a soap-coloured dome of light that wrapped the whole front of the ship, and the cannonball hit it and went *boing* — bounced clean off, sailed high into the air, and dropped harmlessly into the sea with a small embarrassed *plop*.

The pirates stared.

"Did it just —" began Sal.

"*Boing,*" confirmed Pumble, reaching for his ledger. "I'm writing 'boing.'"

Another volley came. *Boing. Boing. Boing-boing-boing.* Cannonballs bounced off the shimmering bubble like peas off a drum and pattered into the waves all around, and not one of them so much as scratched the paint. On the black ships, Kroll's gunners reloaded and fired and reloaded and fired and grew, visibly, from the deck of the *Bright Revenge*, more and more upset.

"Return fire!" Ironjaw shouted. "Bolt — your turn!"

Bolt did not fire cannonballs. Bolt fired *nets*.

He had built a cannon that, instead of shot, launched a small tight bundle — and when the bundle sailed over an enemy deck it *bloomed*, all at once, into an enormous net that came drifting down soft as a jellyfish and settled over the whole crew, tangling arms and legs and sails and rudders, tying up an entire ship's-worth of pirates without hurting a single one of them. *Fwump.* The first black ship's crew went down under a net like puppies in a laundry basket, all shouting, none harmed. *Fwump.* The second ship's sails were webbed to its own masts. *Fwump.* The third ship's captain was gently, completely, and very indignantly wrapped to his own wheel.

"This is *marvellous*," said Fizzwick, watching. "Nobody's hurt at all! It's the *politest* battle I've ever seen!"

"It is the only battle you have ever seen," said Bolt, loading another net.

Ship by ship, the black fleet tangled and drifted and fell out of the fight — until only the *Iron Gull* was left, huge and furious, with Kroll himself at the rail, his coat's buttons blazing, roaring at his crew to *catch that ship, catch it, catch it NOW*.

Ironjaw brought the *Bright Revenge* skimming in alongside.

"Gorm!" Fizzwick shouted across the gap. "GORM! We're here! We came!"

And Gorm, chained to the black mast, lifted his great head — and saw his crew — and something happened in the enormous gentle heart of him. Because there is one thing stronger than a mountain, and that is a mountain that has finally had *enough*. Gorm did not roar. He simply stood up, and set his shoulders, and pulled — and six loops of heavy iron chain, forged to hold anything, stretched, and groaned, and burst apart like wet string.

Kroll's crew rushed him. And this was the moment for Squish.

Squish, still in his net at Gorm's feet, took a deep breath, blinked his one enormous eye at the charging pirates, and translated — loudly, confidently, and to absolutely nobody — an order that had never been given, in the ringing voice of ultimate authority:

"*LORD KROLL SAYS: STOP AT ONCE! IT IS ALL A TEST! THE MAN WHO TOUCHES THE BIG ONE IS FIRED, AND ALSO THERE IS FREE JAM ON THE OTHER SHIP!*"

The black crew skidded to a halt in total confusion.

"He said *what*?" said one.

"A test?"

"Free jam?"

"Did the boss say jam? I heard jam."

"He did NOT say jam!" screamed the real Kroll, going purple. "IGNORE THE BLOB! GRAB THEM! GRAB —"

But it was too late, because his own crew was now arguing with itself about whether they were fired, and whether there was jam, and Gorm had already scooped up Squish's net in one huge hand and stepped clean over the rail onto the *Bright Revenge* as easily as stepping over a puddle, and the two stolen friends were *home*.

"GO!" bellowed Ironjaw, and the wheel spun, and the ship leapt away.

Kroll went mad. He shoved his own confused men aside, seized the wheel of the *Iron Gull* himself, and drove his great black ship straight after the *Bright Revenge* — too fast, too hard, too angry to think — as she skimmed round a jut of black rock at the mouth of a narrow channel.

The *Bright Revenge*, with her humming keel, turned like a swallow.

The *Iron Gull* did not.

Kroll saw the rocks too late. He hauled at the wheel, and his heavy black ship slewed, and missed the rocks — but not the enormous, drifting, soap-coloured *bubble-shield* that Bolt had let go loose across the channel behind them. The *Iron Gull* hit the bubble at full speed, and the bubble did what bubbles do. It stretched. It held. And then it flung the whole ship gently backward, like a giant catapult made of jelly, and Lord Barnabus Kroll was launched clean off his own deck, up and over the rail, coat and buttons and all, in a long and beautiful arc —

— and landed in the sea with an enormous, undignified *SPLOOSH*.

For a moment there was nothing but ripples.

Then Kroll came up, spluttering, hatless, his hundred buttons dragging him down, his fine coat ballooned around him like a soggy black jellyfish, shrieking threats that came out as bubbles. His own tangled crew had to fish him out with a net — the very kind of net he'd used on Squish — and haul him aboard dripping and coughing and utterly, completely, world-endingly humiliated, while the whole sea, and the whole crew of the *Bright Revenge*, roared with laughter.

Not a drop of blood. Not a single soul harmed. Just one soaked and spluttering pirate lord, his fleet in nets, his black ships drifting in confusion, and a legend — the day Lord Kroll got bounced into the sea by a bubble — that would follow him into every port for the rest of his miserable life.

On the deck of the *Bright Revenge*, Gorm gathered all three of the others up in his enormous arms at once, and Squish popped free of his net and oozed onto Fizzwick's shoulder where he belonged, and Ironjaw looked back at the scattered, dripping, defeated fleet, and let her theatrical eyebrow rise in slow and total triumph.

"Home," she said. "Let's take our crew home."

The *Bright Revenge* turned her nose toward Gallows Cove and flew.

They were safe. They were together. They had won.

But waiting on the beach at the end of it all — quiet and patient under a tarp, wingless in the salvage — was one small silver ship, and a question none of them had answered yet.
