# Star Pirates — manuscript DECISIONS log

Owner delegated (2026-07-12) that every open creative decision be made and logged, veto-able later. Here is every call and why.

## 1. Age band — 7–9 chapter book
The concept lists primary "middle grade (~8–12)" and explicitly allows different *builds* of the same premise. Chose the **low end (7–9 chapter book)**: accessible vocabulary, ~1,000–1,300-word chapters, heavy dialogue, chapter-ending hooks. Why: it yields a *complete, satisfying, self-contained Book 1* in all three languages within budget, and reads aloud for the youngest end while still gripping a nine-year-old. **A full 8–12 middle-grade expansion (longer chapters, richer subplots) is a clean owner-gated follow-up**, not attempted here.

## 2. Length & structure — 10 chapters, contained Book-1 arc
Ten chapters realising the concept's own contained arc: crash → bluff aboard → earn the crew → face the rival lord → choose to stay. The concept supplied three set-pieces (harbour-race upgrade, rival pirate lord, repair-vs-stay dilemma); chapters 2, 3, 6 and 8 were authored to bridge them into a full plot (bluff aboard, life-aboard comedy, the family-forming heart chapter, and Kroll's night raid). Word counts: EN 11,401 · NL 12,080 · DE 11,806.

## 3. Titles
- English: **Star Pirates — Book 1: The Wrong Way Down** (brand kept English).
- Dutch: **Sterrenpiraten** — chose the linking-*n* form (*Sterren-*), more idiomatic than the concept's tentative "Sterrepiraten".
- German: **Sternenpiraten** (natural *Sternen-* compound).

## 4. Character names — stable across languages
Personal names are kept identical in all three editions for series/brand recognition: Fizzwick, Bolt, Squish, Gorm, Mr. Pumble, Old Tessa, Lord Barnabus Kroll, Sal, Pip. Only *meaningful* epithets, places and ship names were localized:

## 5. Localization, not translation
The Dutch and German editions are native-quality localizations: idioms, sound-words, pirate flavour and — critically — Squish's running mistranslation gag were re-crafted so they are genuinely funny in each language, not calqued. Dutch quality was the top priority (owner is a native Dutch speaker).

## 6. New original detail invented to complete the book
To finish a full manuscript from a concept, net-new original elements were added, all consistent with the concept and its series engine: the cook Barnacle Bess, a superstitious lookout, Kroll's ship the *Iron Gull*, the pirate oath "the Deep Ones", and the Book-2 tease toward the Frozen Straits. Original IP only; no protected characters.

## 7. Placement & convention
New candidate subfolder `candidates/childrens-books/star-pirates/{en,nl,de}/` with numbered chapter files + a per-language `README.md` front matter, mirroring the repo's established chapters-subfolder pattern (see `agent-fleet-field-manual/chapters/`, `bababoefoe/stories/`). This is the first manuscript directory for the candidate.

Still a creative manuscript, not a shippable product. Illustration and every publish/print/listing path (KDP, print-on-demand, illustrator hire, app) remain **owner-gated OWNER-ACTIONs** requiring spend and accounts — none performed here. No images generated. No revenue claimed.
