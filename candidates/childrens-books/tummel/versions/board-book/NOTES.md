## Kept vs dropped (vs the picture book)

**Kept:** Tummel small-as-a-thumb, the walnut-shell bed, dewdrop lantern, thimble oil-can; the mice's "too high" courage-check; Oehoe's mentor line ("Listen with your whole self" — compressed); the find (one dry joint), the single drop of oil, the ONE core emotional beat — somebody very small hears a very small cry and helps; the deliberate bedtime landing (the mill sighs back to sleep, goodnight bracket). A new toddler refrain built from the picture book's own comfort line: "I'm coming" grows into the chantable "I'm coming, little squeak!" (x3, on the climb spreads); the Tick/Creak/Tock sound-set brackets open and close.

**Dropped:** all interior narration and simile texture (the old-dog-by-the-fire settling, the flour-sack coat that smells of warm bread, the church-door and road-wide comparisons); Tummel's characterization aside ("who worried about most things, and mended them anyway" — 4–8 register); the mill "talking in its sleep" sound-taxonomy spread (beam groan vs rain patter); the wind-on-cheeks vertigo beat (don't-look-down is enough for 0–3); Grietje and the miller (never on-page in the cut); the "they both did" closing sentence-turn (replaced by a direct goodnight call-and-response toddlers can echo).

This is a standalone read-aloud — a toddler who never sees the picture book gets a complete hear-the-cry-and-help story that lands on sleep; a family that owns both gets the same mill and the same tiny helper at two ages.
