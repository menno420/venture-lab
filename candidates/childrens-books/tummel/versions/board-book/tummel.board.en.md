# Tummel and the Squeak in the Dark — Board-Book Edition

- **Title:** Tummel and the Squeak in the Dark (board-book cut)
- **Language:** English (master — voice source of truth)
- **Age band:** board book, ages 0–3
- **Format:** 12 spreads, standalone toddler read-aloud — NOT a summary of the picture book
- **Word count:** honest `wc -w` per language in [`NOTES.md`](NOTES.md)
- **Pitch:** A little squeak cries in the dark windmill — and Tummel, small as a thumb, climbs up with one drop of oil to help.

> Version of the ages 3–6 picture book (`../../tummel.en.md`). Same characters (Tummel, the loft mice, Old Oehoe), one core beat kept: somebody very small hears a very small cry, and helps. Every spread carries an illustration note in italics; the seat generates no art.

---

[Spread 1]
*Illustration: the great windmill at dusk by the canal, one window glowing gold; everything soft and sleepy.*
Hush now. The wind lies down. The water lies down. The old windmill goes to sleep. Tick. Creak. Tock.

[Spread 2]
*Illustration: extreme scale — tiny Tummel sitting up in his walnut-shell bed, vast gentle gears looming in the dark above.*
But somebody wakes up! Tummel, small as a thumb, in his walnut-shell bed.

[Spread 3]
*Illustration: Tummel with one hand cupped to his ear; a thin jagged little sound-line coming down from high in the rafters.*
Listen! Eee-eee! A squeak! A little unhappy squeak, high up in the dark.

[Spread 4]
*Illustration: Tummel lifting his glowing dewdrop lantern, thimble-sized oil-can on his belt.*
Tummel lights his lantern, small as a dewdrop. He takes his oil-can, small as a thimble. "I'm coming, little squeak!"

[Spread 5]
*Illustration: Tummel climbing over huge sleeping grain sacks, each one a hill.*
Up the big sacks — up, up! "I'm coming, little squeak!"

[Spread 6]
*Illustration: Tummel balancing along a rope as wide as a road, then climbing the enormous moonlit cog-wheel, one tooth like a stair.*
Along the rope — careful, careful. Up the great wheel, tooth by tooth. "I'm coming, little squeak!"

[Spread 7]
*Illustration: two round-eyed loft mice peeking from behind a beam; Tummel climbing on, knees wobbling but chin up.*
"Too high!" squeak the mice. "Come down!" "I can't," says Tummel. "Someone up there needs me."

[Spread 8]
*Illustration: a huge soft dust-grey owl on a rafter, half-lidded eyes; Tummel tiny beneath.*
High in the rafters sits Old Oehoe the owl. "Listen, small one," says Oehoe. "Listen with your whole self."

[Spread 9]
*Illustration: Tummel with eyes closed, listening; his lantern-light falling on one small dry joint where two old beams meet.*
Tummel closes his eyes. He listens... Eee-eee! There! One little dry spot, where two old beams rub.

[Spread 10]
*Illustration: one gold drop of oil hanging from the tiny spout, about to fall into the dry joint.*
"There you are," whispers Tummel. "You only want a little help." He tips his tiny can. One drop of oil, gold as honey... drip!

[Spread 11]
*Illustration: the joint soft and glad; the whole mill exhaling, settling deeper into restful dark.*
The squeak goes quiet. Ahhh. The mill sighs a big, slow sigh and settles back to sleep.

[Spread 12]
*Illustration: Tummel tucked in his walnut-shell bed, lantern out, one moonlit window; the mill deep asleep.*
Tummel climbs down to his walnut-shell bed. "Sleep now, mill," he whispers. Tick. Creak. Tock. Goodnight, Tummel. Goodnight, mill.

*The end.*
