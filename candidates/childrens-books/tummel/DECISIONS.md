# Tummel — manuscript decisions

Decision log for the full *Tummel* manuscripts. Concept source: `../concepts/01-tummel-windmill.md`.
Owner-gated: no images generated here; illustration + any publish path remain the owner's call.

## Age band
- **Chosen: read-aloud picture book, ages 3–6.**
- The concept offered "read-aloud (3–6) / early reader (6–8)." Picked 3–6 read-aloud for the flagship
  because the intended voice is an explicit bedtime, tuck-in narrator — bedtime-first beats reading-alone-first.
  An early-reader (6–8) cut can be derived later by lengthening sentences and thinning the illustration load.

## Length & structure
- **One self-contained story, laid out in 12 spreads** (`[Spread N]` markers + one italic illustration note each).
- **Body length ~520–545 words** per language (EN 536, NL 545, DE 522) — squarely in picture-book range.
- Structure: unrest (a wrong squeak) → call to act → the climb (rising scale + a repeated "I'm coming" refrain)
  → the mentor nudge (Oehoe: "listen with your whole self") → find the one dry joint → one drop of oil →
  the mill sighs and sleeps. Resolves at rest, ending on sleep — a deliberate bedtime landing.

## Which story
- Built **Episode 1 "The Squeak in the Dark"** (named in the concept) into the complete manuscript: it is the
  cleanest embodiment of the concept theme ("listening, and small kindnesses being the ones that matter"),
  needs the fewest characters, and lands on sleep. The concept's own sample opening was folded into Spreads 1–5.

## Per-language titles
| Lang | Title | Note |
|------|-------|------|
| EN | Tummel and the Squeak in the Dark | master / voice source of truth |
| NL | Tummel en het Piepje in het Donker | *piepje* = the little squeak; diminutive keeps it tender |
| DE | Tummel und das Piepsen im Dunkeln | *Piepsen* reads naturally; diminutive would over-cute the title |

## Character-name decisions
- **Tummel — KEPT in all three languages.** It already reads Dutch/German-flavored (a *molenmannetje*)
  and evokes German *tummeln* (to bustle about) — a happy accidental fit. No localization needed.
- **Grietje — KEPT in all three.** The miller's daughter roots the Dutch canal-and-tulips setting; a warm,
  recognizable little-girl name in DE too (cousin of Gretel/Gretchen). (Cameo-only in this episode.)
- **The miller** — common noun, localized: EN "the miller" / NL "de molenaar" / DE "der Müller".
- **The two loft mice — LOCALIZED** (their names are descriptive, so a literal keep would read oddly):
  EN Snuffle & Fluff / NL Snuffel & Pluis / DE Schnuffel & Fussel.
- **The owl — LOCALIZED to each language's word for the eagle-owl**, which is onomatopoeic and charming:
  NL "Oehoe" / DE "Uhu" (German for eagle-owl — a perfect native fit) / EN kept "Oehoe" as a proper name
  for its storybook flavor.

## Language approach
- **Not literal translations.** Each language re-authored for natural rhythm and idiom; NL prioritized as the
  owner's native language (naturalness of the voorleesstem over word-for-word fidelity). Sound-words localized:
  EN *Tick. Creak. Tock.* / NL *Tik. Kraak. Tok.* / DE *Tick. Knarr. Tock.* The squeak *eeee* → NL/DE *iiie*.
  The comfort refrain kept across all three ("I'm coming" / "Ik kom" / "Ich komme").

## Concept gaps filled
1. **No full manuscript existed** — only an opening + three episode seeds. Wrote the complete 12-spread arc.
2. **Added Oehoe's mentor line** ("listen with your whole self") as the turning beat; the concept names Oehoe
   as "a nudge of courage" but gives no words — supplied a line consistent with the riddling-owl description.
3. **Added the mice as a brief courage-check cameo** (Fluff says "too high"), using the concept's greedy/timid
   pairing, to externalize Tummel's fear without a new character.
4. **Chose sleep as the closing image** (not in the seed) to make the flagship a true bedtime book.
