# Adaptation — The Dormouse Who Dreams Awake

- **Track:** Adaptation — Source B (our own IP)
- **Source work:** DREAMLINE series — the lane's own IP (canon: candidates/dream-series/, read-only; not edited here). A younger picture-book spin-off in the same world.
- **Logline:** Pippa is a tiny dormouse who learns to wake up *inside* her own dreams, where a kind old heron night-watchman teaches her to gently mend the little tears where dreaming and the real night touch.
- **Age band:** picture book (3–6) / bedtime — **picture book (3–6), bedtime**
- **Tone + narrator voice:** short, fun, gentle, bedtime-magical — a hushed cozy read-aloud, soft and glowy, safe as a tucked-in blanket
- **Story shape:** episodic little-mendings — each night Pippa "goes in" to a dream and darns one small tear (a lost lullaby, a runaway moon, a puddle that won't wake up), with a gentle single arc of her growing braver and surer as a little darner.

## Sample opening

Pippa was the smallest dormouse in the whole canal-city, and the smallest dormice have the biggest sleeps.

When the lamps went soft and the water went still, Pippa curled her tail over her nose — one, two, curl — and down she went into the warm dark of sleep.

But tonight something was different.

Tonight, in the middle of her dream, Pippa *woke up.*

Not in her bed. Not under her leaf. She woke up *inside the dream itself* — standing on a bridge made of moonlight, over a canal made of stars.

"Oh," whispered Pippa. "Oh my whiskers."

She could feel the cool dream-air. She could hear the little dream-water going *plip.* And when she wiggled her toes, the moonlight-bridge went *bounce, bounce* under her paws, soft as a pillow.

Pippa had never been *awake* in a dream before. She looked and looked.

And that was when she saw it.

Right at the edge of the bridge, where the dream ended and the real night began, there was a tiny... *tear.* A little loose place, like a snag in a woolly blanket. Through it, Pippa could see her own dark bedroom, and her own sleeping tail, still curled — one, two, curl.

The tear went *shimmer.* It went *shiver.* A cold thread of night was slipping through.

"You're not meant to be open," Pippa told it, very kindly.

She did not know how to close it. Not yet.

But she knew, the way small creatures know things, that somebody ought to.

## Recurring cast

- **Pippa** — the smallest dormouse in the canal-city; big sleeps, bigger heart, curls her tail *one, two, curl* to fall asleep. The rare little one who wakes up *inside* her dreams and remembers every bit.
- **Heron** — a tall, slow, kindly old night-watchman bird who has darned the little tears for a hundred quiet years; carries a needle of moonlight and speaks in soft, sure words: *"Gently now. Small and gentle."*
- **The Lamplighter Beetle** — a round glowing beetle who lights the canal lamps at dusk and dims them at dawn; marks when it is time for Pippa to go in, and time to come home.
- **Otto the Otter** — a splashy, sleepy canal otter who is forever half-dreaming; his dropped dreams (a mislaid moon, a runaway lullaby) are often what Pippa must gently gather up.
- **The Museum Cats** — two velvet-pawed night cats who guard the sleeping museum, where paintings dream too; they nod Pippa through the quiet halls.
- **Mama Dormouse** — warm and near, tucks Pippa in and never quite sees the magic (the sleepy-fog), but always says the dream sounded lovely.

## Example scenes

1. **The Lullaby That Got Loose** — A baby duckling's lullaby has slipped out through a tear and is floating down the canal, all its soft notes coming undone. Pippa cups the little tune in her paws like a firefly, hums it whole again, and lets Heron stitch the snag shut so the song can never wander off before bedtime again.
2. **The Moon That Rolled Out of a Dream** — Otto dreamed too hard and his dream-moon rolled clean through the thin place and got stuck, cold and dim, in a chimney-pot. Pippa climbs up, rocks it loose, and rolls the glowing moon *gently, gently* back through the soft place where dream and night touch — and the whole city goes silver again.
3. **The Puddle That Wouldn't Wake Up** — A canal puddle is holding a reflection of yesterday and won't let it go, so a little frog can't find *today.* Pippa kneels, whispers "time to wake up now," and with one moonlight stitch closes the snag — the old picture ripples away, and the puddle blinks open bright and new.

## Visuals

- **Illustration style direction:** dreamy glowing night watercolor — a soft nocturne, deep velvety canal-dark washed with warm lamplight and cool dream-shimmer; gentle grainy paper texture, rounded cozy shapes, everything lit as if from within, like a nightlight in book form.
- **Palette:** canal-night blue and deep indigo, dream-glow silver-lilac, lantern-amber and honey-gold, soft moon-white, and a whisper of misty grey-green for the sleepy-fog.
- **Character design notes (in words):** *Pippa* — a very small round dormouse, warm caramel fur, huge dark shiny eyes, big soft ears, a long curly tail she tucks over her nose; wears nothing but a tiny bit of moonlight when she's "awake" inside a dream. *Heron* — tall, slender, dignified, dusk-blue and silver-grey feathers, a long gentle beak, a stitched patchwork satchel and a glinting needle of moonlight; posture calm and kindly, always slightly stooped down to Pippa's height. *The thin-place look* — a soft luminous snag in the air, like a loose loop of glowing thread pulled from a dark woolly blanket, shimmering at the edges where dream-glow leaks into canal-night blue.
- **Key illustration moments:**
  1. Pippa waking up inside her dream on a bridge of moonlight over a canal of stars, tiny and wide-eyed, one paw raised, the little shimmering tear glowing at the bridge's edge.
  2. Heron kneeling low beside Pippa, guiding her small paws as together they draw a thread of moonlight through a glowing snag, the tear closing with a soft warm light.
  3. The whole sleeping canal-city from above — humpbacked bridges, tilted gabled houses, lamp-glow doubled in the still black water, a rolled-back dream-moon settling silver over the rooftops.
