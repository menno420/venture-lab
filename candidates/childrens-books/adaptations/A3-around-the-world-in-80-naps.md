# Adaptation — Around the World in 80 Naps

- **Track:** Adaptation — Source A (public domain)
- **Source work:** Around the World in Eighty Days, Jules Verne (1873) — **Rights status:** public domain (author d. 1905; original novel & pre-1929 translations out of copyright). Fresh original retelling in our own words.
- **Logline:** The slowest animal in the world bets he can circle the whole planet before his eightieth nap — and drags one very fast, very tiny hummingbird along for the ride.
- **Age band:** picture book (3–6) / early reader — **label: picture book (3–6)**
- **Tone + narrator voice:** short, fun, childish, breezy and comic — a giggly narrator who is always slightly out of breath trying to keep up.
- **Story shape:** episodic legs of the journey — one silly country (and one glorious nap) per spread, all racing the ticking nap-count home.

## Sample opening

Sir Poke was a tortoise.

He did everything slowly. He buttered his toast slowly. He blinked slowly. He said "good morning" so slowly that it was usually afternoon.

At the Very Proper Club for Very Proper Animals, Sir Poke stood up. This took eleven minutes.

"I," he announced, "shall travel around the entire world. Before my eightieth nap."

The whole club laughed. A walrus laughed so hard his monocle fell in his soup.

"YOU?" honked a goose. "You take a nap between every step!"

"Precisely," said Sir Poke, calm as a pond. "Eighty naps. Not one more."

WHIZZ. WHIZZ-WHIZZ-WHIZZZ.

That was Zip. Zip was a hummingbird, and Zip was Sir Poke's valet, and Zip could not sit still, not even a tiny bit, not even for one second, no thank you, absolutely not.

"AROUND THE WORLD?!" Zip zoomed round Sir Poke's head. "OCEANS? MOUNTAINS? SNACKS FROM FARAWAY PLACES? I packed! I already packed! I packed twice! Can we go now can we go NOW—"

"After my first nap," said Sir Poke.

And he pulled his head into his shell.

"Oh no," whispered Zip, hovering. "Nap number one. Only seventy-nine naps left to see the ENTIRE PLANET."

Tick. Tock. Snore.

The race was on.

## Animal-cast mapping

| Original character | Becomes | Why it's charming |
|---|---|---|
| Phileas Fogg | Sir Poke, a tortoise | slowest animal, fastest quest — the whole joke in one shell |
| Passepartout | Zip, a hummingbird valet | a blur of energy strapped to the world's calmest boss; opposites who adore each other |
| Detective Fix | Detective Fidget, a suspicious meerkat | certain the bet is a trick, pops up on every leg convinced Sir Poke "stole the whole trip" |
| Aouda (rescued traveler) | Willow, a stranded flamingo | rescued from a sinking pier, joins the crew, wonderfully unbothered and elegant |
| The stubborn ship captain | Captain Barnacle, a walrus with a leaky boat | grumbles, then gets swept up in the adventure; bakes surprisingly good sea-biscuits |

## Recurring cast

- **Sir Poke** — a tortoise; unhurried, unflappable, deeply polite; naps exactly on schedule.
- **Zip** — a hummingbird valet; talks fast, flies faster, loves every single new snack.
- **Detective Fidget** — a meerkat who is Sure Something Fishy Is Going On (nothing is).
- **Willow** — a flamingo rescued mid-journey; graceful, funny, brilliant at spotting shortcuts.
- **Captain Barnacle** — a walrus sea-dog with a rickety boat and a big soft heart.
- **The Nap Clock** — a cheerful cuckoo clock that pops out to announce each nap ("NAP! NAP! NAP!").

## Example scenes

1. **The Balloon Over the Alps** — The crew floats up in a stripy hot-air balloon, and Sir Poke calmly announces it is Naptime. He snoozes; Zip must steer around a very tall, very surprised mountain goat while whisper-yelling "WAKE UP, WAKE UP, gently, WAKE UP."
2. **Willow and the Sinking Pier** — A pink flamingo is stuck on a pier that is slowly going glug-glug-glug into the sea. Zip zooms, Barnacle rows, and Sir Poke — very slowly, very grandly — offers Willow a ride, right as the pier vanishes with a *bloop*.
3. **The Great Train Chase** — Detective Fidget finally corners them on a train, waving handcuffs the size of hula-hoops. But the tracks run out! Everyone piles onto Zip, who tows the whole snoring, honking, flapping crew across the gap — nap number seventy-nine ticking down.

## Visuals

- **Illustration style direction:** bright, comic, globe-trotting storybook — rounded shapes, bouncy motion lines, a cheerful cartoon-map feel, like a picture-book atlas that came to life. Big warm faces, gentle exaggeration, plenty of "whoosh."
- **Palette:** sunny tangerine, sky teal, tortoise-shell moss green, flamingo pink, balloon-stripe cream, and a deep cozy navy for nap-time spreads.
- **Character design notes (in words):** **Sir Poke** — a domed tortoise with a tidy little bow tie and half-moon spectacles, always mid-slow-smile. **Zip** — a teardrop-sized hummingbird, iridescent teal-green, wings drawn as a soft blur, tiny luggage strapped to his back. **Willow** — a tall elegant flamingo with a jaunty travel scarf. **Captain Barnacle** — a round walrus in a striped sweater and a captain's hat two sizes too small. **The vehicles** — a candy-striped hot-air balloon, a lopsided little boat named *The Grumble*, and a friendly red steam train with a face on the front.
- **Key illustration moments:**
  1. Sir Poke standing tall (for a tortoise) at the club, announcing the bet, while Zip is a full teal streak looping around his head and a walrus's monocle plops into soup.
  2. The stripy balloon drifting over snowy peaks at golden hour — Sir Poke fast asleep in the basket, Zip frantically steering past a startled mountain goat.
  3. The whole crew airborne, towed by one determined tiny hummingbird across a broken train bridge, the Nap Clock cuckoo mid-"NAP!"
- **Character design notes continued:** keep every animal soft and huggable; motion is the star — Sir Poke = stillness, everyone else = whoosh.
