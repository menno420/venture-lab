# Adaptation — Mika's Long Way Home

- **Track:** Adaptation — Source A (public domain)
- **Source work:** Homer's Odyssey (ancient) — **Rights status:** public domain (ancient epic, out of copyright). Fresh original retelling in our own words; NOT based on any specific in-copyright modern translation.
- **Logline:** A tiny field-mouse named Mika is blown far, far from her cozy burrow and must find her clever, brave way back home across the enormous world.
- **Age band:** picture book (3–6) / early reader
- **Tone + narrator voice:** short, fun, childish, gently brave (no real peril; cleverness wins)
- **Story shape:** episodic journey — Mika crosses one wide new place at a time, meeting one friendly-or-tricky animal each stop, always pointed toward home.

## Sample opening

One windy morning, Mika the field-mouse was picking the fattest, roundest seed of her whole life.

Then — WHOOSH!

The wind came big and bouncy and grabbed her right up. Mika held on to her seed. She held on to her whiskers. She held on to a floaty dandelion puff, up and up and OVER, past the tall grass, past the tall fence, past the tall, tall trees.

Bump. Plop. Down.

Mika sat up. She looked around. And around. And ALL the way around.

She did not know this place.

The grass was the wrong grass. The hills were the wrong hills. Even the smell was wrong — no warm-burrow smell, no seed-shelf smell, no mama-mouse smell.

Mika's tummy went wobbly. Her nose went sniffly. A tiny tear got ready in her eye.

But Mika was a field-mouse. And field-mice are small — but field-mice are brave.

She wiped her nose. She stood up tall (which was not very tall). She looked at the faraway line where the sky sat down on the ground.

"Home is that way," said Mika. "Or maybe THAT way. Or the OTHER that way." She thought hard. "I will try all the ways until one of them is home."

She tucked her fat round seed under her arm, like a little brown pillow.

And off went Mika — one small mouse, one big world, one long, long way home.

## Animal-cast mapping

| Original element | Becomes | Why it's charming |
|---|---|---|
| Odysseus | Mika, a field-mouse | The smallest, softest hero winning by being clever and kind makes the epic cozy and kid-sized. |
| The Cyclops | Barnaby, a one-eyed barn-cat | A big sleepy cat with one squinty eye is spooky-fun, not scary; Mika tip-toes and out-thinks him instead of fighting. |
| The Sirens | the sweet-singing swallows | Beautiful "stay-here, stay-here" songs are a gentle lesson about not letting nice sounds pull you off your path. |
| Circe (enchantress) | Winnowa, a kind old hedgehog with a cozy hut | She almost keeps Mika forever with warm soup and snuggly beds — the sweet temptation to stop trying. |
| The whirlpool / sea | the Spinny Stream and the Bubble-Twirl | A swirly little brook makes a giant-feeling danger that clever timing (not muscle) gets a mouse safely past. |
| The crew / companions | Pip the beetle and Fenn the sparrow | Tiny travel friends give Mika someone to help and be helped by, so bravery is shared, not lonely. |
| Penelope & home (Ithaca) | Mama-mouse and the seed-shelf burrow | The warm burrow waiting at the end gives every page its gentle pull and its happy, cuddly finish. |

## Recurring cast

- **Mika** — a small field-mouse with a fat round seed she carries the whole way; clever, wobbly-brave, kind.
- **Pip** — a shiny green beetle who rolls along beside her and knows shortcuts (and dead-ends).
- **Fenn** — a young sparrow who can fly a little and points out what's over the next hill.
- **Barnaby** — a big, slow, one-eyed barn-cat; more sleepy than mean, but you must NOT wake him.
- **Winnowa** — a cozy old hedgehog whose warm hut is lovely, and a teeny bit too easy to stay in.
- **The swallows** — swoopy singers whose "stay, stay" song sounds like a lullaby.
- **Mama-mouse** — waiting at home on the seed-shelf, keeping the burrow warm.

## Example scenes

1. **The One-Eyed Sleeper** — Mika must cross the dark barn where Barnaby the cat snores like thunder. She tip-toes across the sleeping-cat's warm tail bridge, holding her breath and her seed, and slips out the mouse-hole before the big squinty eye can blink open.
2. **The Stay-Here Song** — The sweet-singing swallows swoop low, singing "staaay, staaay, the sky is niiice." Mika's paws start to slow. So she pops two little seed-crumbs in her ears, hums her own home-song louder, and keeps walking until the pretty singing fades behind her.
3. **The Spinny Stream** — A swirly brook twirls round and round, too fast to swim. Mika watches, counts the bubbles — one, two, THREE — and hops onto a leaf-boat at the exact quiet spinning-moment, twirling safely across while Pip cheers.

## Visuals

- **Illustration style direction:** warm, adventurous storybook — soft painterly watercolor with gentle ink outlines, big skies and tiny heroes, everything drawn low and close so the reader feels mouse-sized.
- **Palette:** wheat gold, meadow green, dandelion cream, dusk lavender, cozy-burrow brown, and one warm sunset coral for hopeful moments.
- **Character design notes (in words):** Mika is a round little brown-gold mouse with big dark eyes, pink ears, a stitched-look scarf, and her fat seed always tucked under one arm. Barnaby is an enormous soft-grey barn-cat, one eye squinty-closed, one drowsy amber eye, drawn sleepy not scary. Pip is a glossy pea-green beetle with a cheerful face; Fenn a fluffy speckled sparrow with an eager tilt to his head; Winnowa a plump silver hedgehog in a knitted shawl.
- **Key illustration moments:**
  1. Tiny Mika mid-air on a dandelion puff, whiskers streaming, the whole wide patchwork world spread far below.
  2. Mika tip-toeing across the vast sleeping barn-cat's tail, moonlight through the barn cracks, one squinty eye huge in the dark.
  3. Mika home at last on the warm seed-shelf, hugging mama-mouse, her fat round seed finally set down.
