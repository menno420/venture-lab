# Adaptation — Kit in Wonderland

- **Track:** Adaptation — Source A (public domain)
- **Source work:** Alice's Adventures in Wonderland, Lewis Carroll (1865) — **Rights status:** public domain (author d. 1898; original text out of copyright worldwide). Fresh original retelling, not a copy of any in-copyright edition or illustrations.
- **Logline:** When a curious kitten named Kit chases a nervous, waistcoat-wearing hare down a hole, she tumbles into a topsy-turvy world where she grows too big, shrinks too small, and sips tea with the silliest creatures of all.
- **Age band:** picture book (3–6) / early reader — **picture book (3–6)**
- **Tone + narrator voice:** short, fun, childish, bouncy read-aloud rhythm
- **Story shape:** single arc (one tumble through Wonderland) — Kit falls in, gets muddled by growing and shrinking, meets a grin, a tea party, and a bossy Queen, then wakes up warm and safe at home.

## Sample opening

Kit was a little kitten, and little kittens are curious.

*Too* curious, said her mama. But her mama was napping.

So when a hare in a *waistcoat* went hopping past — tick-tock, tick-tock, checking a big round watch — Kit's whiskers went *twang!*

"A hare in a coat?" said Kit. "With a WATCH?"

"Late, late, LATE!" cried the hare. "Oh my paws, oh my ears, I'm ever so late!"

And he popped down a hole in the roots of the old oak tree.

Now, a sensible kitten would have stayed for her nap.

But Kit was not a sensible kitten. Kit was a curious one.

So down she went after him — *whoosh!*

Only the hole was not a little hole. The hole went down, and down, and DOWN.

Down past a shelf of jam (no spoon).

Down past a wobbly clock going *tick... tock... tick...*

Down past a painting of a fish in a hat, who tipped it and said, "Afternoon!"

"Afternoooon," said Kit, floating slow as a feather, "this is the LONGEST fall I ever fell."

And still she fell. Twirl, twirl, twirl.

Until —

*plop!*

Kit landed soft in a heap of leaves, at the bottom of everywhere, in a hall full of doors.

Little doors. Big doors. A teeny door no bigger than a mouse.

And the hare? Gone. Just a puff of dust and a faraway *"LAAATE!"*

"Well," said Kit, dusting her paws. "Now what?"

## Animal-cast mapping

| Original character | Becomes | Why it's charming |
|---|---|---|
| Alice | Kit, a curious kitten | A pounce-first, think-later kitten is exactly the kind of small, brave, wobbly hero little listeners already feel like. |
| White Rabbit | Waistcoat Hare | A long-eared worrier in a too-tight coat who is always LATE — his tick-tock panic is funny and easy for tots to mimic. |
| Cheshire Cat | Grin, a stripey tree-cat | A cat who fades away leaving only a floating smile is pure giggly magic, and a cat guiding a kitten is a cozy fit. |
| Mad Hatter | Badger the Hat-Maker | A round, rumpled badger buried in mismatched hats hosts the muddled tea party with warm nonsense. |
| Queen of Hearts | Queen Flamingo | A tall, pink, VERY bossy bird who shouts "OFF WITH THEIR HATS!" — loud but never scary, because it's only hats. |
| March Hare | Twitch the Jumpy Rabbit | Badger's tea-party pal who cannot sit still and sloshes tea everywhere — bouncy comic relief. |
| Dormouse | Snug the Sleepy Mouse | Naps in the teapot and mumbles dreamy nonsense; the softest, sweetest creature at the table. |
| Caterpillar | Puff the Fuzzy Caterpillar | A slow, sleepy caterpillar on a toadstool who asks big questions between yawns and blows dandelion clouds. |

## Recurring cast

- **Kit** — a curious grey kitten with a crooked whisker; asks "now what?" and always follows her nose.
- **Waistcoat Hare** — a fretful brown hare in a snug orange coat, forever late, forever checking his watch.
- **Grin** — a stripey purple tree-cat who appears smile-first and vanishes tail-last, and knows every path.
- **Badger the Hat-Maker** — a jolly, jumbled badger in a tower of hats who serves tea and tall tales.
- **Twitch** — a jittery white rabbit who cannot sit still and never finishes a sentence.
- **Snug** — a tiny dormouse who sleeps in the teapot and talks in dreams.
- **Puff** — a fuzzy blue caterpillar on a spotty toadstool, wise and slow and yawny.
- **Queen Flamingo** — a towering pink bird in a crooked crown who bosses everyone and loses at her own games.

## Example scenes

1. **The Too-Small Door** — Behind a curtain Kit finds a teeny door and a bottle labeled *SIP ME*. One sip and — *shrink!* — she's mouse-sized... but now the key's up high on the table. One nibble of a cake marked *NIBBLE ME* and — *sproing!* — she's tall as a tree with her head in the rafters. Growing and shrinking, up and down, until she gets it JUST right.
2. **The Grin in the Tree** — Lost among the toadstools, Kit meets a smile with no cat, then a cat with the smile. Grin curls his tail into arrows pointing every-which-way and purrs, "This way's mad, that way's madder — pick your favorite kind of mad!" Then he fades, smile last, twinkling like a moon.
3. **The Muddled Tea Party** — At a long crooked table, Badger and Twitch shout "No room! No room!" (there's LOADS of room), pour tea into cups with no bottoms, and sing the birthday song to a mouse asleep in the pot. Kit can't stop giggling — until a flamingo's shadow falls across the cakes.

## Visuals

- **Illustration style direction:** bright, whimsical hand-painted storybook — soft watercolor washes with springy ink outlines, cozy and dreamy, a touch of golden-afternoon light, everything just slightly wonky and wonderful.
- **Palette:** honey gold, mossy green, dandelion yellow, plummy purple, teacup blue, and flamingo pink — warm and bouncy, never harsh.
- **Character design notes (in words):** *Kit* — small round grey kitten, big curious green eyes, one crooked whisker, soft paws too big for her. *Waistcoat Hare* — lanky brown hare in a snug buttoned orange waistcoat, floppy watch far too big for his paw, ears in a permanent worried droop. *Grin* — a plump purple-and-lilac stripey cat, impossibly wide crescent smile, body that dissolves into dots and stars. *Badger* — round rumpled badger under a leaning tower of hats. *Queen Flamingo* — tall willowy pink bird, tiny tilted crown, one wing always pointing and bossing.
- **Key illustration moments:**
  1. Kit floating down the twirly hole past shelves of jam and clocks, ears up, eyes huge, leaves spinning around her.
  2. Kit stretched tall as a tree, head bumping the rafters, tiny key on the table far, far below her enormous paw.
  3. The whole tea party from above — crooked table, spilled tea, mouse in the pot, Badger mid-cheer, the flamingo shadow creeping over the cakes.
