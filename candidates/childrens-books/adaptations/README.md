# Children's Book Adaptation Track — v0.1

Retellings of known stories with animal casts, in a short, fun, childish voice. Companion to the original concepts in `../concepts/`.

## ⚠️ Copyright guardrail (read first — binding for this track)

Adaptations may ONLY draw from:
- **Source A — PUBLIC DOMAIN:** works whose text is out of copyright (folklore & fairy tales, Homer, Verne, Carroll, Grimm/Andersen, etc.). **Verify status per work** and note it in the concept file. Write a FRESH original retelling in our own words — do not copy any specific in-copyright modern translation, edition, or illustration set.
- **Source B — OUR OWN IP:** spin-offs of the lane's own books (e.g. the DREAMLINE dream-series; canon lives in `../../dream-series/`, read-only — never edited from here).

**NOT ALLOWED:** retelling any contemporary/copyrighted book. A "[popular modern series]-with-animals" retelling is a derivative work and is off-limits. Contemporary books may only ever be a loose *inspiration* for wholly ORIGINAL stories — never an adaptation source. This rule is binding for every future file in this track.

This is a creative portfolio; every publish path (print, listing, hiring an illustrator) is owner-gated. This seat cannot generate or publish images — each concept file ends with a ready-to-paste illustration prompt sheet instead.

Loglines:
- **A1 — Kit in Wonderland:** When a curious kitten named Kit chases a nervous, waistcoat-wearing hare down a hole, she tumbles into a topsy-turvy world where she grows too big, shrinks too small, and sips tea with the silliest creatures of all.
- **A2 — Mika's Long Way Home:** A tiny field-mouse named Mika is blown far, far from her cozy burrow and must find her clever, brave way back home across the enormous world.
- **A3 — Around the World in 80 Naps:** The slowest animal in the world bets he can circle the whole planet before his eightieth nap — and drags one very fast, very tiny hummingbird along for the ride.
- **B1 — The Dormouse Who Dreams Awake:** Pippa is a tiny dormouse who learns to wake up *inside* her own dreams, where a kind old heron night-watchman teaches her to gently mend the little tears where dreaming and the real night touch.

## Ranking + fit with the portfolio

1. **B1 — The Dormouse Who Dreams Awake (DREAMLINE, our own IP)** — the strongest complement to the portfolio. Being own IP, it carries zero public-domain ambiguity, ties straight into the dream-series audience and the lane's Netherlands-first Dutch heritage (Amsterdam canals, night city), and adds real franchise synergy as a younger picture-book on-ramp into DREAMLINE. **Pairs best with the dream-series line** in `../../dream-series/`.
2. **A1 — Kit in Wonderland** — the most recognizable evergreen hook on the board; Alice is instantly legible to buyers and the bouncy shrink-and-grow, tea-party set-pieces are proven read-aloud gold. **Pairs with the picture-book line** (the modern Comet Biscuit / Pip register — bright, funny, series-able).
3. **A3 — Around the World in 80 Naps** — a strong comic-and-visual hook (slowest animal, fastest quest; a nap-counting refrain that reads aloud beautifully) with a natural atlas/globe format that scales across books.
4. **A2 — Mika's Long Way Home** — a lovely, gentle small-hero epic, but the least immediately commercial of the four: the Odyssey is a less child-recognizable source and the quiet episodic journey leans more on craft than on an instant hook.

Register spread: B1 slots under the DREAMLINE/dream-series line; A1 and A3 sit with the modern picture-book originals (Comet Biscuit / Pip); A2 is the quietest, closest in spirit to the old-fashioned cozy-wonder originals.
