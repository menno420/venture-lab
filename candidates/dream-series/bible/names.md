# DREAMLINE — Series Bible: Names (owner picks)

> **Status:** `draft`
>
> Four naming decisions. Three are the owner's intentional blanks in the source
> fragment (faction / elite-dreamer title / big-manipulation ability); the fourth
> is the perception filter the owner added 2026-07-11 (the "Mist"-analogue —
> explicitly NOT that name). Each lists 4–6 options with etymology and rationale;
> the recommended pick is marked ⭐. The keeper-faction (not blanked by the owner)
> is author-named **the Vigil**; alternatives are offered at the end. The owner
> picks the final set — the bible and Book-1 drafts currently use the recommended
> set so the story reads coherently. Swapping any name is a find-replace.

## Blank 1 — the power-faction (rewriting history/future for total power)
Source: *"de mensen die de geschiedenis en de toekomst willen veranderen noemen zichzelf _______"*

1. ⭐ **The Palimpsest** — *Greek* palímpsēstos, "scraped again": a manuscript scraped clean and written over, where the old text bleeds back through. Names both their method (revision) and their fatal signature (the **bleed** — every edit leaves the old text showing, world.md §2/§4). Literary, sinister, memorable; works as "a Palimpsest agent," "the Palimpsest." The name doubles as a plot mechanic.
2. **The Somnarchy** — *Latin* somnus "sleep" + *-archy* "rule": rule through sleep. Names their goal (total power) directly; members "Somnarchs." Grand and villainous; less tied to the bleed.
3. **The Revision** (or **the Revisionists**) — plain-language menace; they literally call their edits "revisions." Most accessible for younger readers; least exotic.
4. **The Errata** — from "errata," the printed slip of corrections. Those who "correct" history to taste. Cold and clever; a touch obscure.
5. **The Overhand** — "a hand that writes over yours," and "the upper hand." Blunt, kid-legible, muscular; less elegant.
6. **The Meridian** — the line the sun crosses at its height. A hidden elite standing "above" the timeline. Elegant, but names status, not method.

**Recommended: The Palimpsest** — the only option whose name is also a working plot device (the bleed is both their weakness and the reader's catchable glitch).

## Blank 2 — the elite-dreamer title (rare; manifests in a crowded square, moves large objects)
Source: *"maar er zijn bepaalde mensen, die noemen ze ______"*

1. ⭐ **Vivid** — *Latin* vividus, "full of life." A dreamer vivid enough to stay real in a bright, crowded, waking place. One clean syllable of wonder; Percy-Jackson-legible ("She's a Vivid"); contrasts nicely with the drab tiers below it (Sleeper, Waker).
2. **Lucent** — *Latin* lucere, "to shine": "the Lucents." Pretty and luminous; sits close to "lucid," which risks blurring with lucid-dreaming.
3. **Daymark** — a real navigational landmark visible by day. A dreamer who holds even in daylight; nautical, fits the canals/harbour; slightly less punchy as a person-word.
4. **The Manifest** — plain-language: they can manifest. Clear and confident; a touch flat.
5. **Keystone** — the wedge stone that holds an arch together. The dreamer who holds a working together; cool but abstract as a title.
6. **Lumen** — *Latin*, "light"; also the unit of luminous flux. Modern, short, evocative; reads a little technical.

**Recommended: Vivid** — most accessible and wondrous, and it sets up a clean ladder: Sleeper → Waker → Vivid.

## Blank 3 — the big-manipulation ability (holding large objects across the line)
Source: *"ze kunnen bijvoorbeeld ook grote objecten maipuleren, dat noemen ze _____"*

1. ⭐ **Anchoring** — to pull a large object (or oneself) across the line and *pin* it so solidly it stays after you let go. Verb "to Anchor"; a strong Vivid has an "Anchor-hold." Concrete, physical, harbour-flavoured (fits Amsterdam / the pont / Venice); the reader instantly grasps mass + permanence. Doesn't collide with the tier name (Vivid), so "she can Anchor" reads cleanly.
2. **Haling** — archaic "to hale," to haul or drag. Dragging heavy mass across the seam; muscular and old-word-flavoured, but obscure for middle-grade.
3. **Massing** — moving mass; "to mass an object over." Clear and physical; a bit clinical.
4. **Ballasting** — harbour word: loading heavy weight to hold a ship steady. Strongly on-theme for a Dutch shipping city; clunky as an action verb.
5. **Rendering** — to make an image solid/real; "to render it across." Modern and clever; maybe too digital-sounding for the tone.
6. **Grounding** — to bring down to earth and fix in place. Simple and legible; the word is overloaded with other meanings.

**Recommended: Anchoring** — physical, permanent, and setting-appropriate, and it pairs with the story's water/mirror imagery.

## Blank 4 — the perception filter (owner addition, 2026-07-11; the "Mist"-analogue — NOT "the Mist")
The device by which regular people "see what they want to believe" and rationalize the impossible. Per owner canon (2026-07-11) it **is** an expression of the dream premise: ordinary minds file impossible events as "I must have dreamed that / misremembered," so the same mechanism that hides the magic also powers the dream-vs-awake ambiguity (world.md §5).

1. ⭐ **The Lull** — *to lull:* to soothe toward sleep; the root of "lullaby." The world lulls a witness back to a story they can rest on — the waking cousin of dream-forgetting. Unifies concealment and dream-ambiguity in one word: what quiets a dream on waking is what quiets a bystander's memory of the impossible. "The Lull took the square." "Hold against the Lull."
2. **The Drowse** — drowsiness; witnesses drowse over the impossible and wake with a tidy story. On-theme, softer than the Lull.
3. **The Reverie** — a waking daydream; witnesses slip into reverie and rationalize. Elegant and dream-adjacent; slightly literary for the youngest readers.
4. **The Glaze** — eyes and minds "glaze over"; also a fired ceramic surface sealing what's beneath. Vivid and tactile; reads a touch cold.
5. **The Hush** — a hush both quiets and covers; "the Hush took the platform." Simple and ominous; less tied to sleep specifically.
6. **The Overcast** — cast over the mind; weather-flavoured (fits Dutch grey skies), and hints at something written-over. A little long as a term.

Explicitly avoided: "the Mist," "the Fog," "the Veil" — too close to existing franchises / too generic.
**Recommended: The Lull** — the only option that ties concealment directly to sleep and dream-forgetting, which is exactly the owner's unified-device canon.

## Not a blank — the keeper-faction (author-named; owner may rename)
Used in the bible/drafts as ⭐ **The Vigil** — *Latin* vigilia, "the watch." Those who keep watch over the true record and mend what the Palimpsest overwrites. Alternatives if the owner prefers: **the Keepers** (plain); **the Darners** (from their craft, "darning" a bleed); **the Wardens**; **the Undreamt**; **the True**. (Avoid "the Ledger" — that word already labels the attention-cost rule, world.md §3.)

## Recommended set (one line)
Faction **The Palimpsest** · Elite title **Vivid** · Ability **Anchoring** · Filter **the Lull** · (keeper-faction **the Vigil**). The owner picks; swapping any name is a find-replace in the drafts.
