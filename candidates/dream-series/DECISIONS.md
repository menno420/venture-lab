## D-001 · Series name — **Lull** (2026-07-12)

**Decision:** The series is named **Lull**. Book 1 keeps its title *The Fourth
Hour* (published form: *Lull · Book One: The Fourth Hour*).

**Delegated by:** the owner (the four naming picks and the series title were left
to the coordinator, `INTAKE.md`; the name was delegated to this seat with the
coordinator default **"Lull"**).

**Shortlist considered (from the record):** Palimpsest · Vivid · Anchoring ·
**Lull** · Vigil. All five are in-world terms from the bible (`bible/names.md`):
the revisionist faction (Palimpsest), the elite-dreamer tier (Vivid), the
big-mass ability (Anchoring), the perception filter (the Lull), and the keeper
faction (the Vigil).

**Rationale (one line):** *Lull* is the story's engine — the perception filter
that unifies concealment and the real-vs-fake ambiguity — so it names the exact
feeling the whole series lives on; it is short, dream-evocative (the root of
*lullaby*), and reads cleanly in EN/DE/NL.

**Why it beats the field, on the content of ch1–3 specifically:**
- The Lull is the one in-world term the drafted chapters *dramatize twice and
  name on the page*: against Sam in Ch. 2 ("That calm — remember the calm. That
  was the Lull, and I didn't have a name for it yet"), and from Sam's POV
  watching the whole ferry un-see a man in Ch. 3. The title points straight at
  the book's central experience.
- `world.md §0` states the series "lives on one feeling: the line between real
  and fake is thin" — and `§5` makes the Lull *the device that produces that
  feeling.* Naming the series after the ambiguity engine is naming its theme.
- The rejected candidates are weaker as a title: **Palimpsest** is strong and
  on-theme (overwriting history) but long, harder to say, less clean across
  languages, and names the antagonists; **Vigil** (the protagonists, "keep the
  record true") is resonant and a close second, but it names a side, not the
  reader's core experience; **Vivid** and **Anchoring** name a tier and a power,
  narrower than the whole.

**Naming convention (codename vs. published title):**
- **Lull** is the *published series title*, applied to reader-facing chapter
  front-matter (`book1/ch*.md`).
- **DREAMLINE** is retained as the *project/working codename* in planning
  material (the bible, `series-arc.md`, `INTAKE.md`, `outline.md`, cross-repo
  session cards and retros that already reference it). This avoids a sprawling,
  breakage-prone find-replace of the codename across files outside this slice,
  while making the story itself carry the chosen title. The directory slug
  (`candidates/dream-series/`) is a generic path, not the title, and is left as
  is so nothing that points at it breaks.

**Owner veto:** if the owner prefers another shortlist name (or wants to keep
DREAMLINE as the published title), swapping is a front-matter find-replace across
`book1/ch*.md` plus a one-line edit here — cheap and reversible.
