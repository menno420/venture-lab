## Where Book 2 leaves us
Sam has drafted-on-the-page done the middle-book work exactly:
- Trained with the Vigil in Venice and learned **Anchoring**.
- Uncovered the Vigil's buried forgery (the 1630-plague rewrite that saved
  Venice and drowns it still) — "the crack the Palimpsest exploits."
- Survived a **betrayal** (Serafina's merciful sum) and a heist-through-history
  into 1630.
- **Made a bigger change than he should — and it stuck** — forging a night to
  save Fenna, confirming he is arc-level. Cost: his **mother's face** to the
  backwash.
- Been reclassified by both factions from *recruit* to *possession*: they now
  **fight over him** and will only circle until the one day worth a war.
- **Identified the nightmare's place: Pompeii** — the hardest-frozen moment in
  history, the Palimpsest's holy site, where his father Joris went under and
  still is, on the date the nightmare carries.
- Learned the Lantern Man is a diver/rescuer wading toward him — **Joris, or
  Sam's own older self** (held ambiguous; Book 2's final image plants both).

## Book 3 — The Fourth Hour Comes (per `../series-arc.md`)
- **Setting:** Pompeii, at the frozen moment of the eruption — the ash, the
  rising bay, the fourth hour — on the date the nightmare finally names.
- **Complete adventure + series climax:** both factions converge in the water;
  the Vigil to stop Sam rewriting the hardest moment there is (they have the
  Salute to prove the cost), the Palimpsest to *make* him crack it open and
  write the whole world over inside ten thousand anchors, Lena undecided.
- **The choice:** let a true and terrible thing stay true, or rewrite it to
  save his father — knowing that to rewrite it makes him exactly the thing he
  fought. This is the confession frame's payoff ("the thing that made everyone
  angry").
- **Resolutions to land:** the **Lantern Man's identity** (Joris / future-Sam);
  the **girl who says *not yet*** (who she is, why she has held the door for
  forty years across a father and a son, her tie to time/Pompeii); whether Sam
  wins back his lost parent-memories or refuses to, keeping the record true at
  the ultimate personal cost; **who the narrator is confessing to, and why it
  had to stay secret** (frame close).
