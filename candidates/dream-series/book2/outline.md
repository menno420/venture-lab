## Frame / where Book 1 left us
Sam held the Night Watch shut, chose the Vigil, and paid a backwash that took
his clearest memory of his father. The Lantern crossed fully into waking. Book
2 opens just past Book 1's cliff: Sam has gone back down into the dream and
**read the date** — the nightmare is a *future*, a real day still to come, and
his father is in it. Both factions now know what he is.

## Part One — The Loud Bell
- **Ch. 1 · The Date** *(drafted)* — Frame cold-open; the narrator finally gives the reader the *fact* of the date (not the date itself — held, per the frame's unreliability). The dream now bleeds into daylight; Amsterdam has become the nightmare (the classroom flood). Wessel: the city is too thin for a snag this loud — the Vigil must move him to **Venice**, where his father once trained. Cliff: Anna, at the dark kitchen table with a shoebox, says "You've read the date. Your father read it too."
- **Ch. 2 · Everyone Knew But Me** *(drafted)* — Anna's reveal: she married a darner and has always known; she gives Sam Joris's Venice keepsakes (the shackle-key, the old photo of Joris with **Serafina**) and her blessing — and the load-bearing line, "come home knowing my face." **Human-thread payoff:** Fenna has built up immunity to being lulled (a worn groove); she refuses it, forces the whole truth, and insists on coming. Cliff: they leave for Venice.
- **Ch. 3 · The Mirror City** *(drafted)* — Arrival over the lagoon; Venice established as the city built on the line (the one true tell here: **reflections run a half-second late** — a *true* mirror is the trap). Meet **Nico**, young Venetian Waker/guide. Cliff: **Serafina** opens the door, sees Joris's face on Sam, and breaks — "He sent me his child to finish it."

## Part Two — The Craft and the Crack
- **Ch. 4 · Anchoring** *(drafted)* — Serafina's *specchieria* (mirror workshop) as a Venetian still-work — the seam managed in covered mirrors, not stitched shut. She teaches **Anchoring** (pull-and-*pin* large mass). Sam over-anchors (arc-level). Cliff: the great forbidden mirror uncovers itself in the night and shows Sam a dead city under a bruised sky — the *inside* of his nightmare; Serafina slams it shut.
- **Ch. 5 · The Warm Voice Returns** *(drafted)* — Sam privately clocks that the mirror-place is *not* Venice and starts secretly hunting it (Fenna warns him, on the record). **Lena returns as half-ally:** she warns Sam the Vigil is lying — they once forged a moment "for the greater good," and it's drowning Venice. Cliff: a smooth (true) reflection — was the whole scene a dream?
- **Ch. 6 · The White Church** *(drafted)* — **The darkening, whole:** Serafina tells the 1630-plague forgery — the Venetian Vigil rewrote the worst night to save the city, built the Salute in thanks, and has darned the endless bleed (the rising water) for four hundred years. There is no clean edit. Cliff: the **Palimpsest attacks the Salute** — not to drown Venice, but to *un-forge* it and prove the Vigil is exactly what they are; only an arc-level door can hold the moment shut. They need Sam.
- **Ch. 7 · Into the Worst Night** *(drafted)* — **The heist-through-history:** Sam opens the door into 1630; Fenna (the record-keeper) and Nico come. Wading the plague-night; the dead see Fenna. They reach the point where the forgery is being made — and the First Draft is there. Cliff: "We didn't come for the church. We came for *you* — ask her why the door was left open behind you."

## Part Three — The Sum
- **Ch. 8 · What a Venetian Will Spend** *(drafted)* — **The betrayal:** Serafina admits she promised the Palimpsest the door (Sam) to spare her city — the same merciful sum, from the Vigil side. She then *breaks* the deal and turns on them. The First Draft lets the whole moment **collapse**; the plague-flood rises. Cliff: Fenna is torn under; Serafina screams "Leave her — do the sum!"; the girl says "Not yet."
- **Ch. 9 · The Bigger Thing** *(drafted)* — **Series turn:** Sam defies both the girl's "not yet" and the sum, and — to save Fenna — makes a bigger change than he should: he *forges* a night and makes it **stick** (confirms arc-level). The backwash takes **his mother's face** (escalation of Book 1's father's-face cost — no clean edit even to save the one you love). Cliff: every mirror uncovers, showing the dead city; both factions say "There you are."
- **Ch. 10 · Nobody Recruits a God** *(drafted)* — **Both factions stop recruiting, start fighting *over* him.** Sam is now too dangerous to hold — a loaded gun on a table; both sides will only *circle and guard* him until the one day worth a war. Lena: "I'll see you at the water — and I don't know which side I'll be on." Cliff: Serafina, broken and honest, "Let me tell you about **Pompeii**."
- **Ch. 11 · The Diver** *(drafted)* — **The place named.** Pompeii = the hardest-frozen moment in history (the Palimpsest's holy site); the nightmare layers ash + the rising bay; the Lantern Man is a rescuer wading toward Sam. Serafina's last truth: **Joris** went into Pompeii to change it, reached too deep, and *that* is what "going under" was — he's still down there, on the date. The diver in the brass helmet = a dreamer diving all the way down. Cliff: she gives Sam the forbidden mirror (his father's) — "look when you're ready."
- **Ch. 12 · The Fourth Hour Coming** *(drafted, frame-close)* — Home to Amsterdam; Sam doesn't know Anna's face until she speaks. The war's new shape (Pompeii + the date = the battlefield; both sides circling). Frame ties back to Book 1 ("the thing that made everyone angry"). **Book-3 hook:** on the date, at Pompeii, Sam will dive for his father and have to become the thing he fought. Final beat: the face behind the fogged helmet is his father's — *or his own, older.* Closes on the series refrain: "*Not yet. But soon.*"

## Threads carried to Book 3 (see `part3-direction.md`)
- The **date + Pompeii** = the battlefield; the Lantern Man's identity (Joris / future-Sam) — settle in Book 3 (series-arc §Book 3).
- **Sam has now forged a moment and made it stick** — he is arc-level, publicly; and he has done the Palimpsest's own act *for love*. The slide is real.
- **His mother's face lost** (backwash) — both parents now only photographs; the boy who keeps the record can't keep his own. Win it back, or refuse to, at the ultimate cost (Book 3).
- **The girl who says *not yet*** — Serafina's clue: Joris heard her too, across forty years, and thought *not yet* meant "not yet — but when it's time, you must." Her identity and her tie to Pompeii/time is the running mystery.
- **The Vigil's buried forgery is now out** — the crack the Palimpsest exploited; "keep the record true" survives only as *the drowning IS the rule*.
- **Lena** — genuinely undecided which side she'll take at the water. The one wild card.
