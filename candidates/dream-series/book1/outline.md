# DREAMLINE: The Fourth Hour — Book 1 Outline

> **Status:** `draft`
>
> Chapter-by-chapter. Opens from the owner's fragment: confession frame →
> sixth-birthday recurring nightmare → discovery → recruitment/conflict. Short,
> cliff-ended chapters (Percy-Jackson pacing). Chapters 1–3 are drafted in this
> folder; 4–14 are beats. Recommended name set throughout. Recurring prop: the
> **Lantern** (prop-echo, world.md §6.5). Catchable **bleeds** planted from
> Ch. 2 (world.md §6.2). The **Lull** is demonstrated twice in the drafted
> chapters — against the narrator (Ch. 2) and from the narrator watching
> bystanders (Ch. 3).

## Part One — The Snag
- **Ch. 1 · The Fourth Hour** *(drafted)* — Confession-frame cold open (English adaptation of the fragment: "before you read on, know this was meant to stay secret; I found out by accident and made a lot of people angry"). Drop to Sam's sixth birthday, 4 a.m.: the recurring nightmare in full — a flooded Dam square at night, the Lantern Man wading toward him, a girl's voice saying *"not yet."* Teach the dream-tells (letters swim, the clock won't hold) but let ONE tell hold, unsettling the reader. Wake. Frame-narrator: I've had it ever since. Cliff: "and six years later, it started coming true."
- **Ch. 2 · Almost Twelve** *(drafted)* — Present day; Sam days from twelve; ordinary Amsterdam (school, canal, Fenna, Anna; the ache of Joris "away"). A re-readable "normal morning" that shares props with the nightmare (§6.3). **Lull demo against the narrator:** the Lantern (a small brass ship's lantern) turns up where it can't be, and Sam rationalizes it away — "I must have dreamed it," the exact Lull cover story — which re-reads later as the first real bleed. Plant 2–3 catchable bleeds (a tram number, a misspelled sign, a recurring stranger). Cliff: the stranger is on his street again.
- **Ch. 3 · The Man on the Pont** *(drafted)* — Dusk; the IJ ferry. Something impossible happens in front of everyone, and Sam watches the other passengers Lull it away in real time (**Lull demo from the narrator's POV**). Wessel, the ferry hand, is the one person unbothered — because he made it happen. First contact with the Vigil; a first glimpse of a shadow-step. Across the water, watching, is Lena. Cliff: Wessel says, low, "You're a snag, kid. They can all hear you."

## Part Two — Two Doors
- **Ch. 4 · Keep the Record True** — Wessel takes Sam through the ordinary door off the Spui into the Begijnhof still-room; the Vigil; the rules laid out (dreams are real; the seam; the bleed; the tax). Sam's first taught pull. He learns his father didn't leave — Joris *went under*. Cliff: a Palimpsest calling-card bleed appears in the still-room, which should be impossible.
- **Ch. 5 · The Girl Who Says Not Yet** — Sam dreams on purpose for the first time; inside a shared dream he meets the girl whose voice he has heard for six years. She warns him off *both* factions and tells him the nightmare has a date.
- **Ch. 6 · A Better World** — Lena's counter-recruitment: dazzling, kind, making the Vigil look like frightened janitors. She shows Sam a dream-fair at Centraal, 4 a.m., on a platform that isn't there. The Palimpsest's pitch: history is a rough draft; we make it kinder; join us and we'll find your father. Cliff: she shows him a photo of Joris — recent, impossible.
- **Ch. 7 · The Tells** — Training with stakes: Sam learns the tells and the Lull, botches a pull (backwash — loses a real memory, played for real cost), first shadow-step in a chase. Fenna half-notices something's wrong with Sam (keeps the human thread alive).
- **Ch. 8 · The Bleed on Kalverstraat** — Sam spots a revision-in-progress in the waking city (a bleed pattern). The cold war made concrete; he must choose whose read to trust. Cliff: the revision points at the Rijksmuseum.

## Part Three — The Painting
- **Ch. 9 · Night Watch** — Sam and Wessel into the Rijksmuseum night galleries; paintings as held moments; the Vermeer-surname payoff (Sam steps into a painting more easily than anyone should). They find what the Palimpsest means to revise.
- **Ch. 10 · Into the Frame** — Set-piece inside a painting's moment; the rules stretch; Sam sees a Palimpsest Vivid at work — **Anchoring** introduced in action (a large object held across the line). Twist: Lena is here.
- **Ch. 11 · Not Yet** — The girl's warning pays off; Sam realizes the revision is bait to test *him* — both factions want to see if he's arc-level. He does the thing he shouldn't: makes a small change to the moment *stick*.
- **Ch. 12 · The Choice** — Climax: Sam foils the small revision (self-contained win) and must pick a faction under fire. He chooses the Vigil — barely — refusing Lena, who escapes. The Lantern crosses fully into waking (prop-echo completes; proof the nightmare was real).

## Part Four — The Date
- **Ch. 13 · Backwash** — Aftermath and cost (the waking tax; a memory lost; Anna frightened). The Vigil takes him in properly.
- **Ch. 14 · The Fourth Hour Comes** — Frame-close: Sam reads the date carried in the nightmare (the letters finally *hold* — the weaponized tell, §6.1) and it is a day that hasn't happened. The nightmare is a future. His father is in it. The final line ties back to the confession frame and launches Book 2 (Venice, the mirror city).

## Note on length
The above is a lean 14 short chapters. For a fuller Percy-Jackson count (~18–20), Ch. 7 (training) and Ch. 10 (the set-piece) each split cleanly in two, and a mid-book Fenna/normal-life chapter can be added between Ch. 6 and Ch. 7. Flagged for the owner; the beats above are the spine either way.
