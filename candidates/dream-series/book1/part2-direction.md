## Where ch. 12 leaves us

Sam has done the impossible on the page — held the Night Watch shut, foiled the
Palimpsest's perfect-crime revision, and *chosen the Vigil under fire.* He paid
the arc-level price: a backwash that took his clearest living memory of his
father (the sixth-birthday warm hand), leaving him only Lena's three-week-old
photograph of Joris asleep under. The Lantern has crossed fully into waking and
sits lit and warm on his sill — proof the nightmare was always real. Both
factions now know exactly what he is. Lena has withdrawn (patient, not beaten)
with a promise to return "when it costs you everything." The girl who says *not
yet* has spent herself keeping the date at bay and warned Sam the letters will
hold now. Ch. 12 closes on Sam going down into the dream one last time to read
the date — the frame narrator stops us at the water's edge.

## Part 2 (the rest of Book 1: ch. 13–14) — closing Book 1

- **Ch. 13 · Backwash.** Aftermath and human cost. Sam wakes wrecked (the waking
  tax). Anna sees the state of him and is frightened — the first crack in the
  secret he's keeping from his mother; seed her knowing more than she's let on
  (she was married to a darner). The Vigil takes Sam in properly; Truus and
  Wessel begin his real training. Re-anchor Fenna as the human thread — Sam,
  changed and hollowed, has to decide whether to keep lulling her or let her in
  (set up a Book-2 payoff either way).
- **Ch. 14 · The Fourth Hour Comes (frame-close).** Inside the dream the letters
  finally *hold* (the weaponized tell, world.md §6.1): the clock/signage resolve
  into a **date that has not yet arrived.** The nightmare is a *future,* not a
  past — and the face behind the fogged helmet, the Lantern Man wading toward
  him, is tied to his father (identity held ambiguous: Joris, or Sam's own future
  self — settle in drafting, series-arc §Book 3). Final beat ties back to the
  confession frame ("I found out by accident and made a lot of people angry") and
  launches Book 2. Cliff: the date, and who is standing in the water.

## Beyond Book 1 (series spine, from `../series-arc.md`)

- **Book 2 — The Mirror City (Venice).** Sam trains with the Vigil in a city
  built on mirrors where the seam never fully closes; hunts the *place* in his
  nightmare; learns **Anchoring** himself; Lena returns as half-ally. The
  darkening: the Vigil isn't clean — they once forged a moment "for the greater
  good," the crack the Palimpsest exploits. Sam makes a bigger change that
  sticks (confirms arc-level); both factions stop recruiting and start fighting
  *over* him. He identifies the nightmare's place: **Pompeii.**
- **Book 3 — The Fourth Hour Comes.** At the frozen moment of Pompeii, on the
  date the nightmare names, Sam chooses between letting a true and terrible thing
  happen and rewriting it — knowing that to rewrite it makes him the thing he
  fought. The Lantern Man's identity and the confession frame both resolve (who
  he's confessing to, and why it stayed secret).

## Threads planted in ch. 4–12 to pay off later

- **The lost father-memory (ch. 12)** — the backwash took the warm hand; Book 3
  is the chance to win it back (or refuse to, keeping the record true at the
  ultimate cost). Sam now navigates by a photograph, not a memory — literalizes
  the Vigil's "a recording is the closest thing to proof, and it always slips."
- **Sam lulled Fenna himself (ch. 7)** — he has the Palimpsest's own trick in his
  hands and it felt like tucking her in. His slow slide toward being able to do
  the thing he despises is a core Book-2 tension.
- **The clock that holds / the date (ch. 1, 5, 11, 12)** — the one tell someone
  spends enormous power to break *for* Sam. Wessel's unease ("a gift you didn't
  ask for is the most expensive thing there is") is a Book-2/3 mystery: who holds
  the clock steady, and why.
- **"Hello, Joris" bled into the darned still-room (ch. 4)** — something reached
  the unreachable room. Either the Palimpsest is stronger/closer than the Vigil
  admits, or the Vigil's buried bleed (series-arc, Book 2) is the leak. Payoff
  in Book 2's "the Vigil isn't clean."
- **The girl who says *not yet*** — her identity, why she's protected Sam for six
  years, and what she spent to do it, is a running series mystery (candidate tie
  to the Lantern Man / the future).

## ⚑ Follow-up owner decision (OUT of scope this slice): language / translation

The drafts (ch. 1–12) are in **English**, primary. The owner's source fragment
is **Dutch**, and the language of the series is explicitly the **owner's call**
(`INTAKE.md`). **Translation of these chapters to DE/NL is OUT of scope for this
slice and not attempted here.** It is a real fork the owner should decide before
much more prose accrues, because the choice is cheaper to make now than after
Book 1 is fully drafted:
- **(a) English-primary** (current state) — widest trade-fiction market; the
  Dutch texture (hagelslag, grachten, *gefeliciteerd*, the pont) stays as
  flavour in an English book.
- **(b) Dutch-primary** — truest to the source and setting; smaller market;
  would mean re-drafting ch. 1–12 in Dutch (a translation/transcreation pass,
  not a mechanical translation, to keep the voice).
- **(c) Dual-language** — draft/maintain both EN and NL (and possibly DE); most
  work, most reach, most owner spend.

No translation work should start until the owner picks. Flagging for the owner
queue as a creative decision (not a spend/publish click).
