## Description (KDP "Book Description" — plain text, paste as-is)

You can buy a ticket to the worst moment in the history of the world. Sam Vermeer has to dive into it.

For seven years, Sam's 4 a.m. nightmare has counted down to a single date — and now he knows it. It's his own birthday. It's tomorrow. And it leads to Pompeii: the hardest-frozen moment there is, ten thousand people holding their own end steady since before anyone wrote anything down — where his father went under, forty years ago, and never surfaced.

Both sides are already at the water. The Palimpsest means to crack the moment open and rewrite the whole world inside it. The Vigil means to shut it forever — by anchoring an arc-level dreamer into it, to hold it true with his whole self, and stay. One offer will give Sam his father back and make him a god. The other will make him a hero and a prisoner. Both are the same terrible arithmetic wearing different faces.

But there's a girl down there who has been holding a door for two thousand years, and a best friend up top with nothing but a notebook and the truth, and between them they know the one thing neither faction ever learned: that you don't keep the record true by holding it. You keep it true by telling it.

To save everyone he loves, Sam will have to find the third thing — and pay for it with the only currency big enough, which is the wonder itself.

The finale of Lull. The fourth hour comes for everyone who's still awake at ten to. Sam Vermeer has been awake his whole life.

*Book Three of the Lull trilogy — the conclusion. Follows* The Fourth Hour *(Book One) and* The Mirror City *(Book Two). Every question the series has carried is answered here. The trilogy is complete.*
