## Frame / where Book 2 left us
Sam trained in Venice, learned Anchoring, uncovered the Vigil's buried 1630
forgery, survived Serafina's betrayal, and — to save Fenna — forged a night and
made it *stick* (arc-level, publicly). The backwash took his mother's face (both
parents now only photographs). Both factions stopped recruiting him and started
fighting *over* him — a loaded gun on a table — circling until the one day worth
a war. The nightmare's place is named: **Pompeii**, where his father Joris went
under diving to un-write it. The Lantern Man is a rescuer wading toward Sam —
Joris, or Sam's own older self (held ambiguous, to resolve here). Lena is
genuinely undecided which side she'll take "at the water."

## Part One — The Circling
- **Ch. 1 · The Fourth Hour Comes** *(drafted)* — Frame cold-open; the narrator finally pays the reader **the date** (at cost, per the frame's rule) — it is Sam's own birthday, the fourth hour, the anniversary the nightmare has always carried. The year of both sides circling; Sam's memory going softer; the dream has changed — now *he's* the one in the brass suit. Cliff: the date is tomorrow; Wessel comes for him — "it's time."
- **Ch. 2 · Everyone at the Water** *(drafted)* — Assembling the players for the battlefield. Anna gives Sam Joris's darner kit and the truth she's held back: she chose to forget Joris's face on purpose, once, to keep going — the family's private grief made a rule. Fenna insists on coming (the notebooks — the true record). Cliff: they go south; the bay of Naples, the mountain, and the ruins waiting under it.
- **Ch. 3 · The Holiest Site** *(drafted)* — Pompeii by day (real, visitable ground). The two plans laid bare: the **Vigil** means to anchor the moment shut forever and wants Sam to be the anchor that stays; the **Palimpsest** means to crack it open and rewrite the world inside ten thousand anchors. Serafina and Nico rendezvous; Serafina's last truth about the girl. Cliff: night; the dive begins; **Lena** steps out of the dark — and won't say which side.

## Part Two — The Dive
- **Ch. 4 · Down** *(drafted)* — Sam puts on his father's brass suit and dives, the **Lantern going back down** with him (prop-echo completes). The descent into the frozen moment; Fenna holds the record above. The true Pompeii — the ten thousand, mid-step, mid-word. Cliff: he finds the girl.
- **Ch. 5 · The Girl Who Says Not Yet** *(drafted)* — **Her identity, resolved:** a Pompeii child, herself arc-level, who woke inside her own death-moment and chose to hold it *true from the inside* rather than let it be scraped — the first and longest anchor, two thousand years alone, whispering *not yet* to every dreamer the moment snags (father and son both). "Not yet" never meant *never* — it meant *not yet, and not by force.* She has been waiting to be **relieved**, not replaced. Cliff: she shows Sam his father.
- **Ch. 6 · The Diver** *(drafted)* — **The Lantern Man, resolved.** Joris, trapped — an *intruder drowning in* the moment, not part of it; he tried to un-write Pompeii and it took him. The fogged face behind the helmet is his father's — and Sam's own, older — because a diver going down for someone they love looks the same across forty years. It was never a monster; it was always love in the same brass suit. Cliff: both factions arrive in the water; the trap closes.
- **Ch. 7 · The Sum, One Last Time** *(drafted)* — The confrontation. Palimpsest (Lena's warm voice / the First Draft): crack it, save your father *and* remake the world, be a god. Vigil (Serafina, grief-honest): anchor it shut forever, take the girl's place, keep it true by *force* — your father stays too. The impossible either/or, the merciful sum at series scale. Cliff: Sam refuses both — "there's a third thing, and Fenna's had it the whole time."

## Part Three — The True Record
- **Ch. 8 · What Fenna Knew** *(drafted)* — **The series thesis paid off:** you don't keep the record true by force (anchor/forge) — you *witness* it and *tell it true*; a record freely told and freely remembered is un-scrapeable, because no one can un-write what people choose to keep. Fenna's notebooks are the proof; the Vigil's four centuries of forced darning were never necessary. Sam begins to *witness* Pompeii whole instead of editing it. Cliff: it's working — but the cost of holding ten thousand true at once comes up under him like the tide.
- **Ch. 9 · The Fourth Hour** *(drafted)* — **The climax.** Sam witnesses the true moment whole and refuses both edits; he pulls the two *intruders* — his father, and the girl — **out** as a rescue, not a forgery, and lets Pompeii stay true. **Lena chooses Sam** (betrays the Palimpsest, holds the seam). The Palimpsest's two-thousand-year door slams shut forever. The girl's last *not yet* becomes, at last, *now.* Cliff: the backwash comes due, and it is arc-level.
- **Ch. 10 · The Cost** *(drafted)* — **The price, and "the thing that made everyone angry."** To seal the door behind him so he can never be used and Pompeii can never be cracked again, Sam spends the one thing big enough: his own reach — he gives up dreaming itself, forever. In exchange the true record is restored: he gets his parents' faces back. Both factions are enraged — the Palimpsest robbed of its holy site, the Vigil unmade (its forgeries proven unnecessary; Serafina's peace; Venice can heal by testimony). Cliff: coming up — and does Joris come up too?

## Part Four — After
- **Ch. 11 · Coming Up** *(drafted)* — The surface. **Joris comes home** (a rescue, not a rewrite). Anna. The faces restored — Sam knows his mother and father at last. The **girl freed** (what becomes of her). **Lena's** fate as the wild card who landed human. Wessel. Serafina and the healing of Venice. Every faction thread tied. Cliff into the frame.
- **Ch. 12 · Not Yet** *(drafted, frame-close)* — **THE END.** The narrator revealed: Sam, grown and no longer a dreamer, telling the whole story — carefully, in a safe room, with the door shut — to a child who has just started waking at four: being the person no one was for him (closes hard against Book 1, Ch. 1). Why it had to stay secret (told, not made loud; a story kept soft can't be anchored and taken). The frame's unreliability explained at last (he held too much true at once; the Lull is deepest in him now). The lantern. The series refrain resolved: *Not yet* — *but now.* THE END.
