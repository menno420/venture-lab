## The one image
The end of the trilogy, and the completion of the element-arc (water → mirror → **fire**). **A boy in an old brass diving suit, small against a vast frozen wave, wading INTO a drowned-and-burning city under a bruised purple-yellow sky** — the ash falling like grey snow, a black wall of sea hanging above him two thousand years from breaking, the leaning pale streets of a dead town beyond. He carries the **Lantern** high — the one warm point of light in the whole cold catastrophe — and this time it is going *down* with him, not toward him (the prop-echo completing: in Book 1 the lantern came up out of the dream; here it goes back down). Series-consistent, the narrator's face is unseen — hidden inside the brass helmet's little fogged window, so the reader still cannot quite tell whose face is behind the glass (the whole trilogy's mystery, held to the last cover).

Deep in the composition, at the vanishing point where the wave meets the falling sky, a second small figure faces the boy across the water — a diver exactly like him, lantern dark, going under — so the cover carries the finale's answer without spelling it: two divers, father and son, the same shape reaching for each other across the worst moment there is.

## Mood & palette
- **Mood:** awe, dread, and warmth-against-the-dark — the "is this real?" feeling at series scale, tipped now toward a hard, final courage. Beautiful and terrible at once.
- **Palette:** the warmest of the three covers — ash-grey and bruise purple-yellow (the Pompeii sky), volcanic near-black, the cold black-green of the rising bay — with the lantern-gold accent doing all the emotional work, hotter and more central than on Books 1–2 (cold → doubled → warm, completed).
- **Light:** the single warm lantern source against a sourceless ashen glow — the dream-tell "light has no single source" (§6.1), paid off one last time.

## Type & series system
- Big, kid-legible title; **LULL** as the series mark (small, consistent across all three spines); *THE FOURTH HOUR COMES* as the book title, largest.
- **Completes the three-book spine system:** Book 1 water/Amsterdam-cold, Book 2 mirror/Venice-doubled, Book 3 fire/Pompeii-heat — same layout, the element and accent-temperature arc landing on warmth. Lined up on a shelf, the three spines should read as one rising temperature and one continuous horizon of water.

## Must-haves / must-nots
- **Must:** the lantern (the series prop-echo, now going *down*); the brass diving suit; the frozen wave; ash; the hidden second diver; the narrator's face unseen inside the helmet; visual rhyme with Books 1–2's composition.
- **Must-not:** no gore, no literal corpses or plaster-cast bodies (age-appropriate — peril not horror); no photo-real Pompeii/Vesuvius postcard; no character's face; the volcano is present only as the bruised sky and the falling ash and the wave — atmosphere, never a disaster-movie money-shot.

## Age & positioning line (for the cover's back-flap feel)
Middle-grade, 9–13, crossover to YA. "The finale — for readers who always knew the figure in the water was coming, and stayed up to find out who it was."
