# Decisions — The Marmalade Post

Run under the day-run BOOKS lane (2026-07-13). First complete manuscript for the concept vetted in `docs/publishing/vetting/the-marmalade-post.md` (round-2 concept-stage packet, landed via PR #143); this is the write-slice that packet parks on.

- **Premise fidelity** — Delivers the vetted packet exactly: Vera Cusworth, new sub-postmistress of Little Dunnock, receives a jar of prize-winning marmalade posted first-class to Walter Finch, dead ten years, and half the village takes a pointed interest in whether it goes back to sender. The packet's §6 blurb beats are all on the page (the counter as the village confessional, the return-to-sender pressure, the "weighed, stamped, and occasionally steamed open" register — Vera pointedly declines to steam; the law does the opening). Series tag honored on the title page: *A Little Dunnock Post Office Mystery — Book One*.
- **Genre contract** — Cozy MYSTERY, fair-play. The puzzle: who has spent ten years afraid of the post — resolved as the show treasurer, Gerald Pocock, who collected the 2016 cash box at 9:40 p.m., let a dead man and an innocent young woman carry the blame, and comes after the parcel because Walter's notebook logs the handover. Clue chain planted before use: the decade-old house name; hoarded 2015 stamps + the spare stamp under the string (old counter courtesy); the Hedges counter hitch (sender was a Saturday girl); the Truro postmark + Porthgullow prize sticker; "the count was never finished" vs. Gerald's exact £3,147 slip (ch. 5, paid off ch. 9–10); the keyed, re-locked, nothing-taken break-in aimed at the *old* pending drawer; the 2016 "no police" minutes; "never you mind the back pages." Red herrings with honest resolutions: Prue Hollis (recipe plagiarism + the torn scrapbook page, not the money) and Martin Finch (the Lyle's-tin bee-money he mistook for the stolen sum and anonymously donated to the roof fund). Bloodless throughout: the only deaths are natural and a decade old; the on-page crimes are the old theft-by-silence and one break-in with nobody hurt.
- **Scope/length** — One complete, standalone cozy mystery, English, full arc, THE END. Honest count: `wc -w` = **15,040** on `en/the-marmalade-post.md` (includes front matter and chapter headings), inside the 15,000–16,000 brief target. Note: the packet's §3 planned length class is ~45k–55k (short cozy novel); this run's coordinator brief set 15k–16k book-parity as the default, and the shorter target won — flagged honestly here and in the session card rather than padded. A future expansion pass (subplots: the footpath dispute, the show classes, Maldenbury procedural texture) is the natural route to the packet's cozy-novel band.
- **POV/tense** — Close third person on Vera, past tense, single POV. Her Returned Letter Centre craft (reading the outside of things, custody chains, "who had it last?") is the detection engine, per the packet's postmistress-sleuth premise.
- **Structure** — 12 chapters, three movements: I (ch. 1–4) the parcel arrives, the interested parties declare themselves, the 2016 history, the sender identified in kind; II (ch. 5–8) executor channel, the exact-figure slip, the break-in, the parcel opened with witnesses, three confessions; III (ch. 9–12) the letter to Cornwall, the decoy-parcel night watch, the show-day public accounting (receipt signed ten years late), and the churchyard delivery. Reveal is confession-under-evidence, cozy register: tea is poured before the official words are finished.
- **Craft texture** — Post-office detail researched-real and period-consistent: sub-postmaster licence transfer, the National Returns Centre (Belfast) vs. the old "dead letter office" name (the impostor's vocabulary slip), executor delivery of a deceased addressee's mail, registered post, surcharge allowance, pending-drawer counter practice, barcoded vs. pre-barcode commemorative stamps (2015 honeybee issue). Village-show texture: entry cards, class judging, WI assessor, cup engraving, committee minutes.
- **Series shape** — One misdelivered parcel per book, per the packet. Book-2 hook planted in the closing page without cliffhanging the main case (case fully resolved, restitution made, magistrates scheduled): a seawater-stained wooden box addressed in faded copperplate to *Miss Patience Crumb, The Quiet Woman, Little Dunnock* — a pub that burned down in 1963 and a name absent from the parish in living memory.

Nothing published, no accounts touched, no spend by this session. Keyword-map rows remain name-level reservations until graduation per the packet header.
- **Cover** — Cover-only production gate per packet §5 (flat-colour cozy code: village post office facade, parcel tied with string, marmalade-jar seal as the O; series-template layout for books 2+; 1600×2560 px min); any art spend is an owner decision.
