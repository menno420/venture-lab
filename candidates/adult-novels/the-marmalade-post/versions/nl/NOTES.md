# Nederlandse editie (NL) — NOTES

## What this is

A complete literary Dutch (Netherlands register) translation of *The
Marmalade Post*. Source text: `../../en/the-marmalade-post.md` at its
merged state on `main` @ `991aff16c566eee361c44f3c6a088d2135459405`
(verbatim `wc -w` 15,040 words including title, front matter, and chapter
headings; re-measured identical at the worktree base `aa04700`). All 12
chapters translated in full — every line finished prose, no summary
placeholders. The EN manuscript stays the source of truth for the story;
fixes propagate EN → NL, never the reverse. The fair-play clue chain is
held exactly: the decade-old house name, the hoarded 2015 honeybee stamps
plus the spare stamp under the string, the counter hitch with the standing
loop, the Truro postmark and prize sticker, the exact-figure slip
(drieduizend honderdzevenenveertig pond), the keyed re-locked break-in
aimed at the old pending drawer, the 2016 "geen politie" minutes, and
"van de achterste bladzijden blijf je af" — each planted at the same
story position as in the EN.

Run under **ORDER 011 item 2 (owner night run 2026-07-13)**, relaying
ORDER 008 item 1 ("versions are cheap once the research exists" — this
version adds a different-**language** audience). Unlike the catalog's
first three NL editions this book is *not* market-native: it is a
deliberately English village cozy, so the translation keeps the English
world intact (names, places, Royal Mail institutions) and renders only
the language — standard NL practice for the imported-cozy shelf, which
is an established Dutch category (Engelse dorpsmysteries in vertaling).

**Series safety (Book One):** the series tag is rendered *Een
postkantoormysterie uit Little Dunnock — Boek een*, which scales to any
future book without renaming; "Little Dunnock" and all recurring proper
nouns (Vera Cusworth, Dilys Pring, Frank, St Botolph's, The Quiet Woman,
Miss Patience Crumb — the Book-2 hook) are carried unchanged so later NL
volumes inherit them verbatim.

## Title decision

**Chosen: *De Marmeladepost*** — the direct rendering, which the book
itself mints on the page: the villagers coin the phrase *de marmeladepost*
at the show-day urn in Hoofdstuk elf, exactly as the EN coins "the
marmalade post," so the title stays diegetic in Dutch. Alternatives
considered:

- ***Retour afzender*** — strong Dutch postal idiom and the Ch 2 title,
  but generic on the NL shelf (multiple existing titles/films) and
  surrenders the marmalade, the cozy genre signal.
- ***Post voor Walter Finch*** — warm and clear, but reads as feelgood
  fiction rather than mystery, and names a character the series will
  leave behind after Book One.
- ***Eerste post*** — the frame phrase, but invisible in search and
  claims nothing about the book.

**Collision scan (dated):** run 2026-07-13 (UTC) via web search on
"De Marmeladepost" boek and "marmeladepost" against the Dutch book
market. No book, ebook, or series with this or a confusable title
surfaced; results were unrelated (Dutch library catalogues, philately
book lists, *Kruimeltje* editions, generic bol.com literature category
pages — [bibliotheek.nl catalogus](https://www.bibliotheek.nl/catalogus.catalogus.50.html?d=schema:genre%7CNon-Fictie&q=De+post),
[remeijer.nl filatelie boekenlijst](https://www.remeijer.nl/filatelie/boeken),
[bol.com literatuur & romans](https://www.bol.com/nl/nl/l/literatuur-romans/24410/)).
"Marmeladepost" appears to be an unclaimed coinage in NL. ⚑ Title
ratification is an owner gate (below), and availability must be
re-checked at upload per the standing packet rule.

**Gloss-reversion list** — empty by construction for this title: the EN
glosses no Dutch realia (the book is set in England), so there is nothing
to un-gloss. The transform runs the other way here, and was applied
sparingly: English realia are kept as proper nouns without explanation
where the EN gives none (Royal Mail, Women's Institute, National Returns
Centre, Lyle's, The Limekilns, The Quiet Woman), and translated where the
EN meaning does story work (PENDING → BEWAREN, the prize sticker, the
committee letter's officialese). Addresses on mail pieces stay verbatim
EN (*Mr Walter Finch, The Limekilns, Little Dunnock*; *Miss Patience
Crumb, The Quiet Woman, Little Dunnock*) — they are physical English
objects and the Book-2 hook must match across editions.

All personal names unchanged (Vera Cusworth, Ted, Dilys Pring, Frank,
Walter John Finch, Annie, Ruth Tanner/Pascoe, Gerald Pocock, Prue Hollis,
Martin Finch, Hattie Bourne, Mrs Aldous → mevrouw Aldous, the Miss
Tremletts → de juffrouwen Tremlett, Ernie, Ivy Peverill, Mr Hedges,
PC Draycott, Patience Crumb). All topography unchanged (Little Dunnock,
Church Row, Vane Lane, Speedwell Close, Maldenbury, Truro, Porthgullow,
Falmouth, Exeter, Devon, Cornwall, Belfast, St Botolph's, The Limekilns /
Orchard End, the Grange). Chapter titles translated for sense ("First
Post" → "Eerste post", "Night Watch" → "Nachtwake", "Delivered" →
"Bezorgd"). Idioms adapted, not calqued ("the way a robin says
something's moved in the hedge" → "zoals een roodborstje zegt dat er iets
bewogen heeft in de heg"; "her heart going like a spin cycle" → "mijn
hart als een centrifuge"). Forms of address: villagers → Vera "u"/
"mevrouw Cusworth" both ways with Gerald, Prue, Martin, Ruth, and the
vicar; Vera → Dilys "je" + first name, Dilys → Vera "u" (employer
register); Vera → young Draycott "je" + "agent", Draycott → Vera "u";
Ruth's letter and graveside words to Walter "jij" (intimate). Dialogue in
single quotes, scene breaks ⁂, chapter headers "Hoofdstuk een/twee/…" —
all mirroring the landed NL-edition formatting convention.

  production), same tier logic as the EN edition; the EN cover brief
  (village post office facade, string-tied parcel, marmalade-jar seal as
  the O; series template for books 2+) translates unchanged.

  (the M.C. Beaton/Richard Osman import lane), and the postal furniture
  reads warmly familiar rather than exotic. That is a qualitative
  argument only: **no NL comps pulled, no NL-storefront category/keyword
  pricing research done tonight — NL-market specifics not measured.**

  €3.49–€4.99 equivalent), not re-derived for NL.
