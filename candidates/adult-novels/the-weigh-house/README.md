# The Weigh House

*An Amsterdam crime novel — novella (~36,000 words, English).*

## Pitch
When Amsterdam police diver Sanne Kessler pulls a weighted body from a canal off the Nieuwmarkt, she recognises the killing: it is the same method that drowned her own husband two years ago, in a death the department ruled an accident. To prove it, she has to go back down into the black water that took him — on a safety line held by the one man she trusts most.

## Content note
Adult crime fiction. Contains murder, drowning, non-graphic violence, grief and the death of a spouse, criminal-finance themes, and brief strong language. No sexually explicit content; no gratuitous gore. For adult readers.

## Chapters
1. **The Recovery** — A winter dawn dive; Sanne recovers a weighted body and notices the belts are police-issue.
2. **Ruling** — A too-fast verdict of misadventure; she meets Detective Nadia Okonkwo and feels the echo of Daan's death.
3. **The Log** — The equipment log shows the belts were team stock "signed out and lost"; Bram warns her not to make it heavier.
4. **Nadia** — Off the record, the two women decide to find out who the dead man was: a compliance clerk at a notary.
5. **The Room** — Victor's careful, frightened flat; a wiped laptop, a habit of mailing things to himself, six canal-house addresses.
6. **The Houses** — Empty, freshly renovated canal houses bought and resold; the fixer Ruud Slootweg surfaces, charming and watchful.
7. **Daan's Shadow** — Sanne opens the box she has avoided for two years and finds her husband traced the same notary — and was murdered for it.
8. **The Signature** — The weighting on the body matches the weighting on Daan: the same diver's hand. The killer is one of the team.
9. **The Mail** — They recover Victor's ledger — doctored valuations and laundered sums — and no name for the man inside the dive unit.
10. **Narrowing** — The insider can only be Bram; a neighbour saw his boat the night Victor vanished. The man who comforted her killed Daan.
11. **Pressure** — Bram isolates her and reminds her who holds the line underwater; Nadia is leaned on from above.
12. **The Offer** — Money, then a threat; the junior diver Tim is taken as bait to draw Sanne into a dive she knows is a trap.
13. **Into the Water** — The trap springs, Bram tending the line topside, his confession human and terrible.
14. **The Line** — The underwater climax; Sanne survives on redundancy and craft, reaches Tim, and chooses not to kill him.
15. **Surfacing** — Bram drowns by his own belt; Sanne chooses the evidence, and the honest machinery moves on the notary and the fixer.
16. **The Weigh House** — Weeks later; a last private dive to the Amstel, and something let go.
