# Decisions — The Weigh House

Owner delegation 2026-07-12: creative decisions delegated to this session (decide-and-flag).

- **Scope** — One complete, standalone crime-thriller novella, ~36,000 words, English; quality over length. NL/DE translations deferred to a follow-on session (mirrors the childrens-books lane). Scope set by session per coordinator brief.
- **Premise origin** — Fully original (book wave 2). No derivative or licensed material.
- **Setting** — Amsterdam, winter: police dive unit (duikteam) + canal-house money-laundering. Chosen for authenticity and because the dive-recovery angle gives the protagonist a distinctive lens on the crime.
- **Protagonist** — Sanne Kessler, a police diver and widow; the investigator is the person who pulls bodies from the water, which ties the murder-method clue to her own expertise.
- **Antagonist reveal** — The killer is Bram Verhoeven, Sanne's paternal dive supervisor and her late husband's friend — the man holding her safety line. Withheld through Acts I–II, confirmed at the midpoint.
- **Ending** — Bram drowns in the climactic dive, dragged down by his own weight belt; Sanne makes the costly choice to secure the evidence rather than save him. Daan's death is reclassified as murder. Chosen over a courtroom-tidy resolution to keep the moral cost on the page.
- **Content** — Adult themes (murder, drowning, grief, criminal finance, brief strong language); nothing gratuitous or sexually explicit.
- **Production/metering** — Drafted then expanded via parallel worker sessions against a fixed story bible, then assembled and continuity-checked. Session token spend ran above the ~150k coordinator target because of the draft-then-expand pass; recorded here per the no-overstatement rule.
