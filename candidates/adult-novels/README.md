# Adult Novels

Original adult-fiction candidates (novella / novel length), one slug directory per work.
Convention mirrors `candidates/childrens-books/`:

- `<slug>/README.md` — title, pitch, content note, chapter map, expansion note.
- `<slug>/DECISIONS.md` — creative decision log (decide-and-flag).
- `<slug>/en/` — English manuscript. (NL/DE translations are follow-on scope.)

## Works
- `the-glass-rectory/` — classic-register ghost story. Honor Vane keeps house for her curate brother in a moorland rectory whose glass wing has been warm, green, and empty since the last rector walked into it.
- `the-marmalade-post/` — cozy village mystery (Little Dunnock Post Office, Book One). A new sub-postmistress receives a jar of prize marmalade addressed to a man ten years dead.
- `the-night-kiln/` — cozy fantasy novella series (Books One–Two on disk). Edda the witch fires memories into clay you can hold; the second book asks whether a fire that shut a thing can open it.
- `the-paper-orange/` — Hunger Winter novel, Amsterdam 1944. A printer's widow discovers the workshop can still print the difference between eating and not.
- `the-salvage-orchard/` — solarpunk novella. A neighborhood orchard grafted through a dead vehicle yard gets one growing season to prove an orchard is infrastructure.
- `the-seed-catalogue-courtship/` — clean & wholesome epistolary romance, a novel in letters. A closing seed firm's last daughter and the engineer restoring a walled garden, across one growing year.
- `the-slow-word/` — literary science-fiction novella (first contact / deep-time communication), told in twelve chapter files.
- `the-twelfth-cake/` — seasonal Victorian holiday novella (*A Twelfth Night Novella*). Pridd's bakery's last night, twelve snowbound strangers, and the bean in the most inconvenient slice in London.
- `the-weigh-house/` — Amsterdam crime thriller novella. A police diver pulls a weighted body from a canal and recognises the murder method that killed her own husband.
- `ultramarine/` — literary historical novella, Delft 1654. After the powder-magazine blast, a widowed colour-grinder digs her life and the world's most precious blue out of the rubble.
