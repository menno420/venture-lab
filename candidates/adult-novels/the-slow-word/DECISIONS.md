# The Slow Word — decision log

Owner delegation 2026-07-12 (venture-lab creative wave 2, Money seat): write one original adult novel-length science-fiction work; all creative decisions delegated to the session, decide-and-flag here. Fresh original premise, no derivative material.

## Scope & structure
- **Format:** standalone novella, 12 chapters, ~29,400 words (coordinator scope was ~25–40k; landed mid-range for a tight, complete arc with a real resolution).
- **Subgenre (committed):** first contact — deep-time communication. Chose ONE lane per the brief. Deliberately NOT AI-themed, to avoid any reading as fleet self-insertion.
- **Category:** opened a new `candidates/adult-novels/` line (none existed). Mirrored the `candidates/childrens-books/star-pirates/` manuscript convention: top-level README index + this DECISIONS.md + `en/` master folder with a front-matter README and `NN-slug.md` chapters.
- **Resolution:** the cosmic conversation stays open by design (it spans centuries) but the human arc fully resolves — the reply is sent, the observatory is saved for the island, the successor is chosen, and the epilogue confirms the answer began to arrive. A complete story, not a cliffhanger.

## Craft calls
- **POV/tense:** third-person limited, past tense, on Tuli; the epilogue shifts to Lani decades later to pay off the deep-time theme structurally.
- **Central idea:** phonemes ~112 years apart mean the message can only be answered by continuity, not data — so the story's engine is "what human technology carries meaning faithfully across centuries?" Answer: oral genealogy (the gafa), not institutions.
- **Cultural grounding:** the protagonist is of the island (Samoan-descended), returning to a heritage she left — a reclamation arc, deliberately not a white-savior or noble-savage frame. Samoan terms (gafa, fa‘alupega, tulāfale, va) are woven with light in-text glossing and used respectfully as the story's thematic spine. Vaituga is a fictional Pacific nation, to avoid misrepresenting a specific real polity.
- **"Adult" scope:** mature themes (mortality, colonial seizure) plus one restrained, non-graphic intimate scene — literary, not erotica. Flagged in the content note.
