# The Slow Word

**Type:** Standalone novella — adult / literary science fiction
**Subgenre:** First contact (deep-time communication)
**Word count:** ~29,400 words (total across all 12 chapter files)
**Chapters:** 12
**Readership:** Adult (mature themes)

## Pitch

Dr. Teuila "Tuli" Va‘a is the last scientist at Fenua Observatory, a radio array on a Pacific atoll her family has fished for a thousand years — and the Consortium that funds it has come to switch off the ears for good. In the final weeks before decommissioning, boxing three decades of data for deletion, she finds an impossibly slow modulation buried in a pulsar's light: a message ten thousand years long, sent by minds for whom a human life is less than one heartbeat. Decoded, it is a greeting shaped like a question — one that can only be answered by a civilization continuous enough to keep speaking for centuries. No institution, no language, no memory lasts that long. But the island's genealogy — the gafa Tuli left behind as a girl — has carried names and meaning faithfully across thirty generations without a single written word. Dying, and racing a defense contractor that wants to seize the discovery and haul it off the reef, Tuli must marry the machine to the lineage: encode humanity's reply, and the promise to keep sending it, into a chant that will outlive her, her successor, and everyone who will ever hear her name. A story about deep time, inheritance, and the only kind of meaning large enough to answer the stars — the kind that outlives the one who begins it.

## Content note

Written for an adult readership. Contains: grief and mortality, including a central character living with a terminal cardiac diagnosis; themes of colonialism and the attempted seizure of Indigenous land and heritage; one restrained, non-graphic intimate scene (Chapter 8); and occasional strong language. No sexual violence; no graphic content.
