# Decisions — The Salvage Orchard

Run under the day-run BOOKS lane (2026-07-13). First complete manuscript for the concept vetted in `docs/publishing/vetting/the-salvage-orchard.md` (round-2 concept-stage packet, landed via PR #143, collision Low); this is the write-slice that packet parks on (§3/§5: "no manuscript").

- **Premise fidelity** — Delivers the vetted packet exactly: a generation after the grid came apart and got rebuilt smaller, Ash Okafor grafts fruit trees through the rusting acres of a decommissioned vehicle yard (Public Works Fleet Yard No. 3, Millbrook Flats, city of Rende) while the neighborhood argues about what the land is FOR. The packet §6 blurb's promises are each on the page: the yard died before Ash was born and the orchard is their idea (ch. 1); graft by graft (ch. 3/5/9), meeting by interminable neighborhood meeting (ch. 2/6, minuted, palms-up consensus); trees literally through windshields (the apricot in the flatbed cab, ch. 1) and around engine blocks (pear-on-hawthorn through the stacked blocks, ch. 1); soil argued with as much as fed (ch. 4, Marisol's lead map and raised-row protocol); the city posts the strategic-reserve notice with one growing season to the review (ch. 1, October 14); orchard-as-infrastructure proven (ch. 7 storm gauges, ch. 11 designation); and the closing theme planted verbatim — a walnut, the tree you won't live to pick (ch. 12). Pitch fidelity too: apples out of axle grease, cherries through crushed glass, consensus meetings that run long, and nobody shoots anybody — the conflict is civic and resolved civically.
- **Genre contract** — Solarpunk in the repair register, hopeful without being saccharine: real losses stay lost (a fifth of the cherries to frost, the plums and the convent pear to the storm, four hundred grafts, Old Mo's ribs, the grandmother dead since January), the win is provisional and audited (dual-use pilot designation, revocable covenant, five-year reviews), and the antagonist is a reasonable assessor with a half-blind rubric, not a villain. Not a lecture: the ecological/technical texture (whip-and-tongue and chip budding, cambium alignment, scionwood storage, lead immobilization by compost/bone-meal/pH rather than dig-and-dump, oyster-mushroom hydrocarbon remediation honestly bounded — "a patient, not a field", swales and check dams on contour, phytoremediation myths explicitly debunked) is carried in scene and dialogue by characters doing work. Register-distinct from The Slow Word by construction: near-future, Earthbound, no aliens, no signals — verified zero hits for radio/signal/first-contact/telescope/deep-time stems in the final text (two incidental uses caught and rephrased in draft).
- **Scope/length** — One complete, standalone solarpunk novella, English, full arc, THE END. Honest count: `wc -w` = **15,045** on `en/the-salvage-orchard.md` (includes front matter and chapter headings), inside the 15,000–16,000 brief band. Note: the packet's §3 planned length class is ~25k–35k (novella); this run's coordinator brief set 15k–16k book-parity as the default and the shorter target won — flagged honestly here and in the session card rather than padded. A future expansion pass (the Kettle Row households as fuller characters, the ring-farm exchange, the construction-liaison year) is the natural route to the packet's planned band.
- **POV/tense** — Close third person on Ash Okafor (they/them), past tense, single POV. Ash's grafting craft is the novella's controlling metaphor, stated once by an apprentice and never by the narrator: joining two living things so both continue — knife-work, meetings, and the ending alike.
- **Structure** — 12 chapters, one growing season (March notice → October review → the following March), three movements: I (ch. 1–4) the notice, the assembly's consensus to measure everything, the scion-roll inheritance, the assessor's visit and the missing rubric column; II (ch. 5–8) the teaching Saturdays, the frost mobilization, the open-gates argument (what the land is for), the storm that proves the swales and the reversal that makes the yard the sole candidate; III (ch. 9–12) the pivot from defense to distribution (the hardpan offer + the Scion Library), the evidentiary harvest and first-pressing naming, the working-session designation (dual-use pilot, revocable, a proposed "community capacity" column), and the walnut. Emotional arc: Ash's hoarder's grief ("the tin voice") regrafted, beat by beat, into the grandmother's rule — *a graft is a gift, or it dies* — closed by giving the split scion roll to a departing apprentice and planting a two-hundred-year tree.
- **Craft texture** — Researched-real and honestly bounded: fruit-tree grafting practice (willow practice wood, tongue-cut geometry, wax/parafilm, labeling discipline, top-working, budwood seasons), brownfield orcharding (fill soils, capped asphalt, raised windrow rows, cored planting holes, tree fruit's low lead translocation vs. the leafy-green/root-crop risk, testing protocol), mycoremediation limits, storm-water arithmetic (swales, check dams, capped storm drain, gauge boards), rebuilt-small energy texture (rooftop panels, neighborhood battery sheds, streetcar, forecast collective), and consensus-process texture (facilitation stack, minuted fears, stand-asides, blocks, the meeting that runs long as a *feature*).
