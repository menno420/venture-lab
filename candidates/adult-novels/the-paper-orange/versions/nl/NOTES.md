# Nederlandse editie (NL) — NOTES

## What this is

A complete literary Dutch (Netherlands register) translation of *The Paper
Orange*. Source text: `../../en/the-paper-orange.md` at its merged state on
`main` (landed via PR #122; verbatim `wc -w` 15,811 words including title,
front matter, and chapter headings). All 12 chapters translated in full —
every line finished prose, no summary placeholders. The EN manuscript stays
the source of truth for the story; fixes propagate EN → NL, never the
reverse. Historical facts are held exactly to the EN text and the
`../../DECISIONS.md` verified-vs-invented ledger — nothing was added to or
drifted from that ledger in translation (all named characters remain
fictional; the invented network name "de vrienden van Sint Nicolaas" is
translated as invented; coupon appearance details remain deliberately
non-facsimile).

Run under ORDER 008 (owner night run 2026-07-13, BOOKS clause: versions
across "different angles, audiences, lengths" — this version adds a
different-**language** audience). For this book the Dutch edition is
arguably the native register: the novel is set entirely in Amsterdam and
the Hongerwinter is living family memory in the Dutch market; the EN
DECISIONS.md itself flagged the NL edition as the most natural translation
target in the catalog and pre-named the title.

## Title decision

**Chosen: *De papieren sinaasappel*** (subtitle *Een roman over de
Hongerwinter*) — exactly the natural Dutch-market title the EN DECISIONS.md
"NL-translation potential" bullet recorded. Alternatives considered:

- ***De papieren sinaasappel* without subtitle** — viable, but the subtitle
  does real market work in NL: "Hongerwinter" is the load-bearing search
  term and instantly places the book for Dutch readers.
- ***Oranje*** — punchy and thematic, but generic to the point of
  invisibility in the NL market (collides with royal-house nonfiction and
  football titles) and loses the object the whole book hangs on.
- ***Achter het behang*** — evocative of the wallpaper seam, but reads as
  domestic suspense and surrenders the title image the EN establishes.

**Gloss-reversion list** — places where the EN glossed a Dutch reality for
Anglophone readers and the NL text speaks plainly again:

1. **noodkacheltje** — EN: "the noodkacheltje, a little tin emergency
   stove…"; NL keeps the word and the hatbox simile, drops the
   explanation.
2. **Koninginnedag** — EN: "the thirty-first of August, the Queen's
   birthday"; NL: "de eenendertigste augustus, Koninginnedag".
3. **hongertocht** — EN: "She joined the hunger trek"; NL: "Ze ging op
   hongertocht" (and "hongertochten" for the practice).
4. ***Beter.*** — EN prints the Dutch pencil word and translates it
   ("*Beter.* Better."); NL needs no translation — the gloss sentence is
   absorbed.
5. **Landwacht** — EN explains "the occupier's Dutch help"; NL compresses
   to "eigen kweek" — the name itself carries the charge for Dutch
   readers.
6. **onderduikers** — EN paraphrases ("people who do not exist", "every
   deserter and diver"); NL uses the plain period words ondergedokenen /
   onderduikers where the EN paraphrase was a gloss, keeping "mensen die
   niet bestaan" where it is Vos's rhetoric rather than a gloss.
7. **VERHOEVEN — DRUKKERIJ** — EN adds ": Verhoeven, printers."; NL drops
   the appositive.
8. **ze hebben gecapituleerd** — EN carries it as italic foreign speech;
   in NL it merges into the narration's own language (kept italic as the
   street's spoken words).
9. **Sinterklaas** — EN "Sint Nicolaas came on the fifth"; NL uses the
   everyday "Sinterklaas" for the feast while the network name stays
   formal ("vrienden van Sint Nicolaas"), mirroring the EN's own split.

Terms the EN already used untranslated (bonkaart, bonnen, stamkaart,
persoonsbewijs/PB, razzia, Hongerwinter, Jordaan, Grüne Polizei,
Hollandsche Mediaeval) carry over unchanged. All personal names unchanged
(Mies/Hendrik/Fien/Dirk Verhoeven, Vos, mevrouw Aalders, De Wit, Joop,
Blom, Trijn, Sal Winkler). Idioms adapted, not calqued (e.g. "the way you
touch wood" → "zoals je iets afklopt op hout"; "It isn't over till it's
over over" → "Het is pas voorbij als het voorbij voorbij is"). Forms of
address: Mies–Vos and Mies–officials are "u" both ways; adults address the
children as "jij"; Trijn the farmwife uses "je" to Mies (rural register).
"Zuiderzee" kept as the EN has it (colloquially current in 1945; the
DECISIONS ledger governs, no fact drift).

## Market position

- **Format:** novella ebook (reflowable, cover-only production), same tier
  logic as the EN edition; the EN cover brief (a single orange folded from
  newspaper against winter-grey wallpaper) translates unchanged.
- **Market:** unlike the catalog's other NL edition, this book's setting
  and subject are *native* to the NL market — Hongerwinter fiction is an
  established, actively read Dutch category with living family memory
  behind it. That is a qualitative argument only: **no comps pulled, no
  NL-storefront category/keyword research done tonight — NL-market
  specifics not measured.** Price band carried over conservatively from
  the EN packet ($3.99–$5.99, rec. $4.99 ≈ €3.49–€5.49 equivalent), not
  re-derived for NL.
- Base-case sales for an unknown author remain ≈ $0, as for every
  candidate on this shelf; unit economics only, no forecast.
