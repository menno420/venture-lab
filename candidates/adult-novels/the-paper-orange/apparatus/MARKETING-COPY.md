## One-line hook

> A printer's widow forges ration coupons to feed the hidden through Amsterdam's
> Hunger Winter — while behind her wallpaper waits a paper orange, folded for
> the day the lights come back.

*Alternate, shorter:* **In the winter Amsterdam starved, one widow's forged
coupons were the difference between eating and not.**

## Short blurb (~50 words — for ads, catalog lines, social)

> Amsterdam, 1944. The gas is gone, the canals are freezing, and Mies
> Verhoeven — a printer's widow with two children and half a sack of tulip
> bulbs — discovers her dead husband's press can still make one thing: ration
> coupons good enough to feed the hidden. A quiet, luminous novel of the Dutch
> Hunger Winter.

*(52 words.)*

## Long blurb (~150 words — KDP book description / back cover)

> Amsterdam, December 1944. The gas is gone, the canals are freezing over, and
> Mies Verhoeven — a printer's widow with two children and half a sack of tulip
> bulbs — is learning the arithmetic of the Hunger Winter: what a stove will
> burn, what a bulb will yield, what a child can be told.
>
> Below her kitchen, her late husband's print shop stands shuttered — until she
> understands that the one thing the press can still produce is the difference
> between eating and not: ration coupons good enough to pass a checkpoint at
> dusk. As the famine tightens and the network of the hidden depends on her,
> Mies keeps her own ledger — what the press gives, who comes knocking, what one
> person owes another when there is nothing left to share.
>
> And behind the wallpaper, in a seam nobody else knows, waits a small orange
> folded from paper — the forbidden royal colour, inked by her husband in the
> first summer of the occupation, for the day the lights come back.
>
> *A novel of forgery as mercy, courage in small rooms, and what people feed
> each other when there is nothing left.*

## Comp / positioning lines ("for readers of…")

Honest tonal-and-thematic positioning against widely known titles — comparisons
of register and subject, not endorsements, not claims of equivalence:

1. **For readers of Anthony Doerr's *All the Light We Cannot See*** — occupation,
   restraint, and one small hidden object carrying the whole weight of hope.
2. **For readers of Kristin Hannah's *The Nightingale*** — the home-front
   courage of women under occupation, and the private cost of keeping others
   alive.
3. **For readers of Stephen P. Kiernan's *The Baker's Secret*** — quiet
   deception as an act of mercy, feeding a community through the enemy's own
   rules.

*(A fourth natural touchpoint, for print-and-word resonance specifically:
Markus Zusak's *The Book Thief* — a story where paper, type, and small
defiances are the instruments of survival.)*

## Book-club & educator pitch angle

**Why this title works for reading groups and classrooms** (the strongest
gate-free demand lever for a short literary-historical novella by an unknown
author):

- **Discussion-dense at novella length.** ~15,800 words — readable in a single
  sitting or one week — but built on debatable moral choices: the refused
  staircase (Chapter Five), forgery-as-mercy, the ledger of "the owed," keeping
  the children home three extra days after liberation. A companion
  `BOOK-CLUB-GUIDE.md` ships alongside this file with a thematic overview,
  ~12 chapter-anchored questions, and a topics-to-research list.
- **History with a built-in study aid.** A companion `HISTORICAL-NOTE.md`
  grounds the novel in the documented Hunger Winter, marks fiction from record
  honestly, and points to public-history further reading — ready for a teacher
  or a group leader to hand out.
- **Curriculum fit.** Pairs naturally with WWII / occupied-Europe units,
  Anne Frank and the Dutch home front, and units on rationing, resistance, and
  civilian survival — a woman's-eye, home-front counterpart to the more
  familiar frontline or camp narratives.
- **Content-appropriate and clearly labelled.** The manuscript carries a
  content note (wartime famine and its effects, restrained and largely offpage;
  bereavement; references to the deportation of Amsterdam's Jews; one distant
  shooting; no graphic violence, no explicit content) — usable verbatim by
  educators and group leaders vetting suitability.
- **Sales angle for the owner:** lead outreach to book clubs, libraries, and
  history/English teachers with the free companion guide + historical note as
  the hook; the guide is the discovery surface that a standalone novella
  otherwise lacks.
