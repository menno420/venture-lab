# Cover Brief — The Winter Wheel (Book 4)

> Production direction only. Cover-only production gate (adult text title, no
> character art needed). Any art spend is an **owner decision** — this brief
> commissions nothing. Consistent with the series look established in the vetting
> packet §5 (`docs/publishing/vetting/the-night-kiln.md`): warm light in a cold
> dark frame, one luminous vessel, no faces.

## Spec

- **Trim / format:** reflowable ebook; cover **1600×2560 px min** (KDP), same as
  Books 1–3. Series-consistent; no print in first-release scope.
- **Subtitle on cover:** *A Cozy Fantasy Novella*. Series line: *The Night Kiln ·
  Book Four*.

## Palette

- Deep frost-night blues and blacks for the frame (colder than Books 1–3 — this
  is the deep-winter book), against a single warm kiln-glow / lamplight source:
  honey-amber to straw-gold. Snow-blue mid-tones. The contrast IS the subgenre's
  signature — warm light in a cold dark frame.
- One accent of *lamp-in-a-window* gold, echoing the cradle-song at the book's
  heart ("the lamp is in the window") and the series' recurring ferry-lamp motif.

## Motif

- **Primary:** a small, homely, thumb-marked pot on a potter's wheel, lit warm
  from one side against the blue winter dark — the "first pot" and the "winter
  wheel" of the title in one image. A faint hairline crack may catch the light on
  the thin side (the frost-crack), understated, not distressed/gothic.
- **Optional secondary:** snow at a lit workshop window behind, or the kiln's
  warm mouth glowing in a frosted garden. Keep it quiet; one hero object.
- **Series continuity:** Book 1 = luminous mug on a doorstep; Book 2 = the jar
  with its lid ajar; Book 3 = the mended dish, gold seam. Book 4 = the first pot
  on the wheel. One warm vessel per cover; frame gets colder as the seasons turn
  Night → Morning → Harvest → Winter.

## Typography

- Match the series: warm serif display for the title, generous letter-spacing,
  cream/honey ink on the dark ground; author name smaller beneath; subtitle in
  small caps. Title should sit legibly as a thumbnail (cozy readers browse small).

## Comparables (look-and-feel, not claims)

- The warm-window cozy-fantasy shelf: *Legends & Lattes* / *Bookshops & Bonedust*
  (Travis Baldree); *The House in the Cerulean Sea* (TJ Klune); *Can't Spell
  Treason Without Tea* (Rebecca Thorne) — comfort-lit covers that sell warmth,
  a single cozy object, and light-in-dark framing. Series should read as a matched
  set on one product page.
