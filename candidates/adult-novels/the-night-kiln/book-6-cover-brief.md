# Cover Brief — The Summer Ember (Book 6)

> Production direction only. Cover-only production gate (adult text title, no
> character art needed). Any art spend is an **owner decision** — this brief
> commissions nothing. Consistent with the series look established in the vetting
> packet §5 (`docs/publishing/vetting/the-night-kiln.md`): warm light in a cold
> dark frame, one luminous vessel/source, no faces.

## Spec

- **Trim / format:** reflowable ebook; cover **1600×2560 px min** (KDP), same as
  Books 1–5. Series-consistent; no print in first-release scope.
- **Subtitle on cover:** *A Cozy Fantasy Novella*. Series line: *The Night Kiln ·
  Book Six*.

## Palette

- The series' frame, which **warmed** for the first time at Book 5 (spring/thaw),
  goes **full warm** here — this is the high-summer book, the year at its most
  generous. A deep, soft summer-night blue (short midsummer dark, not the frost-
  black of Book 4), warm at the horizon; the one hot source is the **live ember**
  itself and, small and far, a second warm point: a hearth-glow waking on a distant
  hill across dark low water.
- The recurring *lamp-in-a-window* gold (the ferry-lamp / lit-workshop motif) is
  now doubled on purpose — a near light and a far light across the water — the
  book's whole image in one glance: **two lamps, one on each bank**.

## Motif

- **Primary:** a single glowing **ember / live coal**, cupped or carried (the
  cover's one hot object, the series' "one warm vessel per cover" turned into its
  most literal form — the fire itself, mid-crossing). Optionally shown nested in a
  plain **iron pot** banked in ash, breathing red, held careful in the dark.
- **Secondary (quiet but load-bearing this time):** across low, dark, gleaming
  summer water — the stumps of a **fallen bridge**, and beyond them, up a far hill,
  a small **new hearth-glow** coming alight where there has been only dark. This is
  the one cover where the *second* light matters: the far fire relit.
- **Series continuity:** Book 1 = luminous mug on a doorstep; Book 2 = the jar with
  its lid ajar; Book 3 = the mended dish, gold seam; Book 4 = the first pot on the
  wheel; Book 5 = the two-handled spring cup, blossom + ash-veils. Book 6 = the
  carried ember + the far hearth waking. The frame's arc across the series turns
  Night → Morning → Harvest → Winter → Spring → **Summer**: after Book 5's first
  thaw, Book 6 is the year in **full warmth** — and the first cover to show **two**
  lights, near and far, across the water.

## Typography

- Match the series: warm serif display for the title, generous letter-spacing,
  cream/honey ink on the deep summer-night ground; author name smaller beneath;
  subtitle in small caps. Title should sit legibly as a thumbnail (cozy readers
  browse small). "Ember" wants the warmest ink on the cover — let the word carry
  the heat.

## Comparables (look-and-feel, not claims)

- The warm-window cozy-fantasy shelf: *Legends & Lattes* / *Bookshops & Bonedust*
  (Travis Baldree); *The House in the Cerulean Sea* (TJ Klune); *Can't Spell
  Treason Without Tea* (Rebecca Thorne) — comfort-lit covers that sell warmth, a
  single cozy source of light, and light-in-dark framing. Series should read as a
  matched set of six on one product page, the frame warming across the year to the
  full-summer glow of Book 6 — the only one of the six to carry two lights.
