## (b) Consistency / house-style questions flagged for a human (NOT auto-fixed)

These are judgement calls a mechanical pass must not make on its own. None is a
clear error; each is a decision for the author/editor.

1. **Straight vs curly quotation marks (typography).** The manuscript uses **straight
   ASCII** apostrophes and quotes throughout (503 `'`, 272 `"`, and **zero** curly
   quotes) — a consistent house style, not an error. Most published ebooks use
   typographic (curly) quotes and apostrophes. This is a global typesetting decision
   for the owner: keep straight, or run a single consistent smart-quotes conversion
   at typesetting time. **Deliberately NOT auto-converted** — it is a ~775-character
   mass transformation, not a mechanical typo fix, and would break byte-for-byte
   fidelity. Decide once, apply consistently.

2. **Character name consistency — confirmed consistent, noted for the human.**
   Principals are stable across the book: **Edda Marren** (witch), **Perrin Loft**
   (apprentice; "Perrin Loft" given in full at Ch.4 and Ch.11), **Grams Ilsabet**
   (late teacher), **Grams Wenna** (the witch before Ilsabet), **Ansel Rooke**
   (mason; also called "Uncle Anvil" once, Ch.6, clearly an affectionate nickname
   in the boy's voice, not an inconsistency), **Odile Vance** (reeve), **Hessa**
   (Copper Kettle), **Old Wim** and **Griet** (ferry), **Maud/Tally/Gran Brock**.
   No name drift found. Flagged only so a human editor can confirm the "Uncle
   Anvil" nickname is intentional.

3. **Series continuity terms — consistent with `series-arc.md`.** The catechism
   lines, the two-rook mason's mark, the private-shelf inventory (jar-lid ajar,
   empty race-dish board, first-pot board), and the Book-5 hook (the humming wedding
   cup / "inherited the not-happening") all match the series bible. The closing
   chapter plants Book 5 exactly as `series-arc.md` describes. No continuity
   question raised; noted as checked.

4. **Timeline internal check.** "Six days to Wintermark" (Ch.3) counts down
   coherently through the frost breaking "on the third morning" (Ch.6) to Wintermark
   eve and morning (Ch.8–11) and Wintermark night (Ch.12). No off-by-one found. A
   human should still re-read the day-count as a sanity pass.

5. **Spelling register — British.** Consistent British spellings throughout
   (*honour, neighbours, colour, celadon, marvelling*). Intentional and uniform;
   ensure any marketing/description copy matches the chosen register (the KDP
   description as drafted also uses British *honour/neighbours*).

## (c) Caveat — this does NOT replace a professional proofread

**This self-edit pass shrinks the risk of obvious mechanical errors; it does NOT
replace a professional native-speaker proofread, and this manuscript should not be
published until that proofread has been done.** An automated + single-agent read
reliably catches doubled words, spacing, and gross punctuation slips (of which there
were none here), but it does not reliably catch subtle grammar, tense/agreement
drift in long literary sentences, missing or wrong small words, near-homophones,
rhythm/repetition problems, or line-level style issues that a human proofreader is
paid to catch. The catalog's own gate treats the whole book slate as hard-gated on a
native-speaker proofread pass; this file does not clear that gate. **Owner: commission
a professional proofread before publishing.**
