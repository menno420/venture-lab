## Where each book lands against the ~20k–30k band

- **De Oogstslag (Book 3): 24,655 words — squarely IN band.** No length action
  is owed; it conforms to the packet. Its only open owner item is title
  ratification (a separate §7 gate, not a length question).
- **De Morgendeur (Book 2): 16,730 words — BELOW band, at Book-1 parity.**
  Book 1 sits at 16,840, so Books 1 and 2 currently read as a matched pair of
  same-length companion novellas; Book 3 is the longer outlier. This is the
  standing tension `DECISIONS.md` left unruled.

## The trade-off (Book 2 is the only real decision; Book 3 already conforms)

**Option A — keep De Morgendeur at ~16k (Book-1 parity).**
- (+) Books 1 & 2 sit honestly side-by-side, same length, on the same product
  page — a visible companion-novella virtue for a cozy series.
- (+) Zero new work; both books are complete, standalone arcs ("THE END").
- (−) Neither Book 1 nor Book 2 hits the packet's committed ~20k–30k band;
  Book 3 stands apart at ~24k.

**Option B — extend De Morgendeur into the ~20k–30k band.**
- (+) Book 2 conforms to the packet band and moves toward Book 3's length.
- (−) It then **diverges from Book 1**, breaking the 1↔2 parity that is
  currently the series' cleanest reader-facing fact — *unless Book 1 is
  extended too*, which doubles the spend.
- (−) A real, speculative extension slice. `DECISIONS.md` documents the natural
  joints (the Hessa/Col subplot, the between-fires carrying days, the Stonebeck
  homegoing), so it is reversible/additive — but it is unmeasured spend on a
  cozy line where complete 16k arcs already satisfy.

## RECOMMENDATION (for a one-word owner ratify)

**Ratify the current lengths as they stand** — De Morgendeur at ~16k Book-1
parity, De Oogstslag at ~24k in-band — and treat band-uniformity extension as a
**deferred, reversible option** to revisit only if sales signal demand for
longer entries.

Rationale: the 1↔2 parity is a concrete product virtue today; Book 3 reading
longer is defensible as the series' expanded finale; and extending Book 2
*alone* would break that parity while extending *both* 1 & 2 is large
speculative spend against complete, satisfying 16k arcs. If the owner instead
prefers band-uniformity, the correct move is to extend **Books 1 and 2
together** (never Book 2 alone) — but that is a separate, owner-authorized
slice, not a default.

**Owner ratify:** *"lengths as-is"* — or *"extend 1+2 to band"* to open an
extension slice. Either way the call is yours; this file does not make it.
