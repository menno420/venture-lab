## Title decision

**Chosen: *De Nachtoven*** (subtitle *Een cozy-fantasynovelle*). The
direct, natural Dutch of the EN title: one compound, names the object the
whole book turns on, and keeps the series lattice intact (Book Two's
morning/night inversion — *The Morning Door* — still lands in Dutch).

Collision scan, dated **2026-07-13** (`date -u`: Mon Jul 13 23:10 UTC 2026;
WebSearch, three queries):

- **`"De Nachtoven" boek roman titel`** — **no same-named book found.**
  Hits were night-titled Dutch shelf noise, none colliding: *De nacht*
  (Merijn de Boer,
  [bol.com](https://www.bol.com/nl/nl/f/de-nacht/9200000016321434/),
  [hebban](https://www.hebban.nl/boek/de-nacht)), *De nacht* (Sara Gordan,
  Uitgeverij Oevers), *Nacht* (Wiesel; Hilsenrath), *Het boek van de nacht*
  (Bazterrica), *De nachtraven* (Snieders, 19th c.,
  [DBNL](https://www.dbnl.org/titels/titel.php?id=snie001nach01)).
- **`"Nachtoven" bol.com boek`** — no "Nachtoven" listing surfaced; nearest
  shape-adjacent shelf neighbour is *De nachtegaal* (Kristin Hannah,
  [bol.com](https://www.bol.com/nl/nl/f/de-nachtegaal/9200000036135016/)) —
  different word, different genre; logged as adjacency, not collision.
- **`"nachtoven" Nederlands`** — the compound barely exists in Dutch at
  all (results degrade to "Dutch oven" and Nachttopf dictionary noise),
  which is favourable: the coinage is distinctive and searchable.

Alternatives considered and rejected without scan (quality grounds):
*De oven van de nacht* (wordier, weak spine title), *Het nachtvuur* (loses
the kiln — the made-animal object is the book), *De ovenheks* (character
title, surrenders the EN series' object-per-book naming convention).

Standing caveat carried from all prior NL packets: Dutch self-pub titles
are unevenly web-indexed; "none found" is weaker evidence than for
trad-pub. Final KDP/bol.com title recheck at upload is the owner's §7
step.

None required — unlike the catalog's earlier NL editions, the EN text
glosses no Dutch realities (invented cozy-fantasy world). The EN's
