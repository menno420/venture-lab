# Nederlandse editie (NL) — Boek twee — NOTES

## What this is

A complete literary Dutch (Netherlands register) translation of *The
Morning Door* (The Night Kiln, Book Two). Source text:
`../../en/the-morning-door.md` at its merged state on `main` @
`d93aee502a4daf2b3f7cd249067eb5c5a5ac046c` (verbatim `wc -w` **15,995**
words, including title, front matter, and chapter headings; measured
tonight in this worktree). All 12 chapters translated in full — every
line finished prose, no summary placeholders. The EN manuscript stays the
source of truth for the story; fixes propagate EN → NL, never the
reverse.

The manuscript is translated **as written, at its current length**: the
⚑ OWNER length-band question on Book Two (`../../DECISIONS.md`,
2026-07-13; `../README.md` convention note) is owner-queued and stays
untouched by this slice — if that ruling later changes the EN text, the
change propagates EN → NL like any other fix. This edition tracks the
source as-written pending that gate.

Series continuity: every recurring term, name, and load-bearing line was
taken from the **series-safe glossary of Book One's NL edition**
(`../nl/NOTES.md`, glossary section), which was explicitly built "so a
future NL Book Two can carry it unchanged." Inherited renderings are
marked in the glossary below; only Book-Two-new terms were added.

Provenance: run under **ORDER 011 item 2** (owner night run 2026-07-14,

## Title decision

**Chosen: *De Morgendeur*** (subtitle *Een cozy-fantasynovelle · De
Nachtoven, boek twee*). **The title was pre-named, not re-decided
here:** Book One's NL NOTES (`../nl/NOTES.md`, "⚑ NL title
ratification" bullet, lines 173–176: "an NL *De Morgendeur* pairs
naturally") and the De Nachtoven vetting packet
(`docs/publishing/vetting/de-nachtoven.md` §7, step 2: "an NL *De
Morgendeur* pairs naturally with Book Two — NOTES.md") both record the
pairing; this slice carries it out.

One in-text note on the pair *morgen*/*ochtend*: the running text uses
**ochtend** for the common noun throughout (matching Book One's
register — *ochtendkop*, *papkom*, "laatste ochtenden"), including the
held line "een ochtend wil een kamer." The title compound keeps
**morgen-** as pre-named: Dutch compounds strongly prefer *morgen-*
(*morgenlicht*, *morgenster*, *morgenrood*), "De Ochtenddeur" is
cacophonous, and *De Morgendeur* preserves the series' morning/night
inversion against *De Nachtoven*.

Collision scan, dated **2026-07-14** (run anyway despite the
pre-naming; WebSearch, three queries):

- **`"De Morgendeur" boek roman titel`** — **no same-named book found.**
  Hits were morning-titled Dutch shelf noise, none colliding: *De
  morgenster* (Knausgård,
  [hebban](https://www.hebban.nl/boek/de-morgenster-karl-ove-knausgard),
  [Singel](https://www.singeluitgeverijen.nl/de-geus/boek/de-morgenster/)),
  *Morgenster* (Jaap Scholten), *Morgenrood* (Willem van Toorn; also
  the NL title of Stephenie Meyer's *Breaking Dawn*,
  [bibliotheek.nl](https://www.bibliotheek.nl/catalogus/titel.426278798.html/morgenrood/)),
  *De morgen loeit weer aan* (Marugg). All different words, different
  genres; logged as adjacency, not collision.
- **`"Morgendeur" bol.com boek`** — no "Morgendeur" listing surfaced;
  results degraded to generic bol.com book-category pages
  ([bol.com](https://www.bol.com/nl/nl/l/boeken/8299/)), i.e. the
  storefront has nothing under the word.
- **`"morgendeur" Nederlands`** — the compound barely exists in Dutch
  at all; results degrade to dictionary noise for *morgen*. The search
  digest surfaced one poetic use attributed to Gerrit Achterberg
  ('Gevecht', light through "the cracks of the morning door") —
  unverified beyond the search digest, and a poem line, not a title;
  favourable if anything: the coinage is distinctive, searchable, and
  has a literary echo rather than a competitor.

**Verdict: no collision found; distinctive coinage; pairs with Book
One.** Standing caveat carried from all prior NL packets: Dutch
self-pub titles are unevenly web-indexed; "none found" is weaker
evidence than for trad-pub. Final KDP/bol.com title recheck at upload is
the owner's §7 step, and the pick itself is ratified by the owner
together with the EN and Book One titles.

map: Perrin gives Edda "u"/"meesteres"; villagers and Edda are "je/jij";

Edda shifts to "je" at "Juffrouw Hale. Mercy." (Ch 4, exactly where the

Ansel address Mercy as "schooljuffrouw" (EN "mistress schoolteacher").
