# Cover Brief — The Spring Cup (Book 5)

> Production direction only. Cover-only production gate (adult text title, no
> character art needed). Any art spend is an **owner decision** — this brief
> commissions nothing. Consistent with the series look established in the vetting
> packet §5 (`docs/publishing/vetting/the-night-kiln.md`): warm light in a cold
> dark frame, one luminous vessel, no faces.

## Spec

- **Trim / format:** reflowable ebook; cover **1600×2560 px min** (KDP), same as
  Books 1–4. Series-consistent; no print in first-release scope.
- **Subtitle on cover:** *A Cozy Fantasy Novella*. Series line: *The Night Kiln ·
  Book Five*.

## Palette

- The frame **warms and lightens** for the first time in the series — this is the
  spring/thaw book. Soft rain-washed dusk-blues and dove-greys rather than the
  deep frost-black of Book 4, with the first thread of new-leaf green at the edges
  and a low sky going from grey to clearing. Against that cooler-but-thawing dark,
  the one warm source: the deep red-brown iron-celadon glow of the cup itself,
  honey-amber where the applewood ash veils run.
- One accent of *lamp-in-a-window* gold, the series' recurring ferry-lamp / lit-
  workshop motif, kept small.

## Motif

- **Primary:** a single warm two-handled **loving-cup** (a wedding cup), deep
  red-brown iron celadon with faint gold ash-veils running down the shoulder over
  a pressed line of **apple-blossom**, lit warm from one side against the cool
  thaw-dusk. One hero vessel, quiet, luminous.
- **Optional secondary (very quiet):** the faintest suggestion of water — a high
  spring river / a ford under flood, or blossom on the wind — behind or beneath,
  never competing with the cup. A hint of white apple-blossom is on-brief; keep it
  to a whisper.
- **Series continuity:** Book 1 = luminous mug on a doorstep; Book 2 = the jar with
  its lid ajar; Book 3 = the mended dish, gold seam; Book 4 = the first pot on the
  wheel. Book 5 = the two-handled spring cup with blossom and ash-veils. One warm
  vessel per cover. The frame's arc across the series turns Night → Morning →
  Harvest → Winter → **Spring**: after four books cooling toward the deep frost of
  Book 4, Book 5 is the visible **thaw** — the frame should read, at a glance, as
  the series' first warming.

## Typography

- Match the series: warm serif display for the title, generous letter-spacing,
  cream/honey ink on the (now softer, thawing) dark ground; author name smaller
  beneath; subtitle in small caps. Title should sit legibly as a thumbnail (cozy
  readers browse small).

## Comparables (look-and-feel, not claims)

- The warm-window cozy-fantasy shelf: *Legends & Lattes* / *Bookshops & Bonedust*
  (Travis Baldree); *The House in the Cerulean Sea* (TJ Klune); *Can't Spell
  Treason Without Tea* (Rebecca Thorne) — comfort-lit covers that sell warmth, a
  single cozy object, and light-in-dark framing. Series should read as a matched
  set of five on one product page, the frame warming as the year turns to spring.
