# Cover Brief — *The Blue and the White* (Ultramarine, Book 2)

> Production direction only. Cover-only production gate (adult text title, no
> character art required). Any art spend is an **owner decision** — this brief
> commissions nothing. Consistent with the literary/historical look of Book 1
> (*Ultramarine*): a single luminous pigment-and-material image, no faces, the
> Dutch Golden Age palette used as the book's own metaphor.

## Spec

- **Trim / format:** reflowable ebook; cover **1600×2560 px min** (KDP),
  series-consistent with Book 1. Optional 5×8 or 5.25×8 paperback later; not in
  first-release scope.
- **Series line on cover:** *Ultramarine · Book Two* (or, if a series name is
  adopted, *A Delft Novel · Book Two*). **Title:** *The Blue and the White.*
- **Subtitle (metadata, optional on cover):** *A Novella of Delft.*

## Palette

- Book 1 sold **ultramarine against dark** — the ground stone-blue, lapis, the
  cold north light. Book 2 turns on the **fired blue-and-white**: keep the deep
  Delft blue, but pair it now with the **chalk-white of tin-glaze** and one warm
  ember/kiln-glow accent (straw-gold to red-amber) at a low corner or edge — the
  fire that both destroyed the town and now makes the colour. The **blue/white
  contrast is the whole subgenre signature here** (it is literally Delftware), and
  it doubles the book's inner meaning: the blue (Clara's colour, her endurance)
  and the white (*loodwit*; and "the white" — the blast — that shaped her).
- Cooler and cleaner than Book 1's cover; Book 1 = pigment in shadow, Book 2 =
  pigment fired into light.

## Motif

- **Primary (recommended):** a single **blue-and-white delftware plate or tile
  with a goldfinch on it**, lit warm from one low side against a blue-dark ground
  — the fired bird that carries Fabritius's *Goldfinch* forward onto glazed clay
  (the book's central image and its forward hook in one). Understated, hand-
  painted look; one hero object.
- **Alternative:** a potter's slab/muller with a smear of **drab grey zaffer**
  turning, along its length, into true fired blue — "the blue you can't see till
  the fire has it." Or the mouth of a kiln glowing warm with white ware inside.
- **Series continuity with Book 1:** Book 1's cover image is the **ground stone
  and the muller** (the cold blue, the easel world). Book 2 = the **fired plate**
  (the kiln world). One luminous blue object per cover; the frame warms and cleans
  from the disaster-shadow of Book 1 toward the fired light of Book 2.

## Typography

- Match Book 1: a restrained literary serif display for the title, generous
  spacing, ink in a bone/chalk white against the blue ground; author name smaller
  beneath; series line small. Must read as a matched set with Book 1 on one
  product page. No ornament that reads as cozy or romance — this is literary
  historical fiction.

## Comparables (look-and-feel, not claims)

- The literary Dutch-Golden-Age shelf, same as Book 1: Tracy Chevalier (*Girl
  with a Pearl Earring*), Deborah Moggach (*Tulip Fever*), Jessie Burton (*The
  Miniaturist*). Covers that sell a single luminous object, period restraint, and
  colour-as-subject. Book 2 should sit beside Book 1 as an obvious pair.
