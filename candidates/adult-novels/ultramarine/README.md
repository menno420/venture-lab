# Ultramarine

> **Status:** `draft`

*A literary novella of Delft, 1654.*

**Logline.** When a gunpowder magazine called the Secret of Holland tears the heart out of Delft, a widowed colour-grinder must dig her life, her orphaned niece, and a hoard of the world's most precious blue out of the rubble — and decide whether beauty is worth what it costs to make.

**Audience.** Adult literary / historical fiction. Readers of Tracy Chevalier (*Girl with a Pearl Earring*), Deborah Moggach (*Tulip Fever*), Jessie Burton (*The Miniaturist*).

**Length / format.** Complete novella, ~27,000 words, 12 chapters in three parts. English. Standalone.

**Setting.** Delft, in the province of Holland, autumn 1654 into the following spring — before, during, and after the historical "Delft Thunderclap," the explosion of 12 October 1654 that destroyed a quarter of the city and killed the painter Carel Fabritius.

## Pitch (2-line)

A widow who grinds colour for the painters of Delft loses everything in the blast that kills Carel Fabritius — and reclaims her life, grain by grain, from the same mud that swallowed her precious ultramarine. A story about the cost of beauty, the precariousness of a woman's independence in the Dutch Golden Age, and learning to see again after the world goes dark.

## Cast

- **Clara Wijnants** — a widowed *verfmaalster* (colour-grinder), keeping her late husband's colour-and-dealing trade on the Verwersdijk.
- **Grietje** — her niece, eight, orphaned daughter of Clara's dead sister.
- **Barent** — Clara's apprentice grinding-boy, fifteen, an orphan of the almshouse.
- **Carel Fabritius** — the real painter, Rembrandt's boldest pupil; Clara's customer and something more nearly a friend.
- **Willem Doncker** — a colour-merchant and guild elder who covets Clara's stall and doubts a woman's right to it.
- **Agatha** — Fabritius's wife.
- **Cornelis Soetens** — keeper of the powder store, the Secret of Holland.

## Content note

Adult literary fiction. Contains a mass-casualty disaster rendered on the page (blast injury, death, and bodies that cannot be identified), the death of a teenage character and a child in peril, bereavement and grief, references to death by fever, and the social and economic hardship of a widow in a guild-governed city. No explicit sexual content. Suitable for adult general readers; not for children.

## Historical note

The Delft Thunderclap, Carel Fabritius's death in it, the powder store nicknamed *Het Secreet van Holland* in a former convent of the Poor Clares, the Guild of St Luke, and the pigment economics of the period are drawn from the historical record (see `bible/world.md` for sources). Clara Wijnants, her household, and Willem Doncker are invented. Where the record is uncertain — above all the death toll, which contemporaries could not count — the novella keeps faith with that uncertainty rather than inventing false precision.
