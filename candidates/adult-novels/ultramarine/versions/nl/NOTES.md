# Nederlandse editie (NL) — NOTES

## What this is

A complete literary Dutch (Netherlands register) translation of
*Ultramarine*. Source text: `../../manuscript/ultramarine.md` at its merged
state on `main` (verbatim `wc -w` 27,865 words including title page, content
note, and part/chapter headings). All 12 chapters in three parts translated
in full — every line finished prose, no summary placeholders. The EN
manuscript stays the source of truth for the story; fixes propagate EN → NL,
never the reverse. The EN vetting packet
(`docs/publishing/vetting/ultramarine.md` §5) notes NL/DE translations as
deferred follow-ups per the publishing plan — this edition is that follow-on
for NL.

Run under ORDER 008 (owner night run 2026-07-13, BOOKS clause: versions
across "different angles, audiences, lengths" — this version adds a
different-**language** audience). Of the whole catalog this is the most
market-native translation there is: the novella is set in Delft, 1654,
inside the historical Delftse donderslag — the Verwersdijk, the
Voldersgracht, the Sint-Lucasgilde, the Clarissenklooster full of powder —
and the EN text glosses Dutch Golden Age realities that the NL edition
simply speaks.

## Title decision

**Chosen: *Weduwenblauw*** (subtitle *Een roman over Delft, 1654*).

The pick is constrained by a live upstream decision: the EN packet's §2/§7
carries a ⚑ OWNER title pick with ***The Widow's Blue*** as the recommended
default (rename away from *Ultramarine*, which is collision-buried by
Navarro/Lowry/Warhammer). The NL title must stay compatible with that
default, and *Weduwenblauw* is its natural Dutch form — one compound word,
the Ch. 11 title made the book's title, naming the widow and the pigment at
once. It also stays serviceable if the owner keeps *Ultramarine* for EN,
because the direct Dutch title is independently blocked (below).

Collision scan, dated **2026-07-13** (WebSearch, three queries):

- **Weduwenblauw** — `"Weduwenblauw" boek roman titel`: **no same-named
  book found.** Notable same-shelf adjacency (logged, not a collision):
  Simone van der Vlugt, *Nachtblauw*
  ([bol.com](https://www.bol.com/nl/nl/f/nachtblauw/9200000052033846/)) — a
  bestselling Dutch historical novel about **Delft Blue pottery in the
  17th century**: same city, same century, colour-compound title, same
  shelf. This is simultaneously the strongest comp evidence the NL shelf
  offers (the register sells in Dutch) and the book to differentiate from
  in the blurb. Other hits were generic colour-title lists (Hebban) and
  *Al het blauw (van de hemel)* — different titles, search noise.
- ***Ultramarijn* — REJECTED (head-on collision):** Henk van Woerden,
  *Ultramarijn* (2005), a prominent Dutch literary novel, **winner of the
  Gouden Uil 2006**, still in print and on reading lists
  ([hebban](https://www.hebban.nl/boek/ultramarijn-h-van-woerden),
  [bol.com](https://www.bol.com/nl/f/ultramarijn/9200000079749911),
  [DBNL](https://www.dbnl.org/titels/titel.php?id=woer001ultr01)). The
  direct translation of the working title is taken, in-register, in the
  NL market. Do not use.
- ***Het blauw van de weduwe*** — scanned clean (no hits) but rejected on
  quality: wordier, weaker as a spine title, and shape-adjacent to the
  *Al het blauw van de hemel* noise cluster. Kept as the fallback if the
  owner wants a fully literal mirror of *The Widow's Blue*.
- ***Het geheim van Holland*** — rejected: an existing nonfiction title
  (Uitgeverij Noord-Holland, Hembrugterrein local history,
  [uitgeverij-noord-holland.nl](https://www.uitgeverij-noord-holland.nl/de-zaanstreek/het-geheim-van-holland/))
  plus the same register mis-signal the EN packet flagged for *The Secret
  of Holland*. (In the text itself the powder store is rendered with its
  historical Dutch nickname, *het Secreet van Holland*.)
- ***Het blauwe uur*** — rejected upstream: the EN packet rejected *The
  Blue Hour* on the Paula Hawkins collision (Very strong); Hawkins's
  Dutch edition occupies the phrase on the NL shelf. Not re-scanned.

Standing caveat carried from all three prior NL packets: Dutch self-pub
titles are unevenly web-indexed; "none found" is weaker evidence than for
trad-pub. Final KDP/bol.com title recheck at upload is the owner's §7 step.
⚑ The **final NL title is owner-gated together with the EN pick** — if the

## Register and terminology

Netherlands (NL) register, literary-historical; period texture without
fake archaic spelling. Domain vocabulary held to real Dutch terms:

- **Pigment-grinding:** *verfmaalster* (native, was italicized-glossed in
  EN), *loper* (muller), *wrijfsteen* (slab/grinding stone), *vijzel en
  stamper* (mortar and pestle), *lazuursteen/lapis*, *ultramarijn*,
  *loodwit*, *azuriet/bergblauw*, *omber*, *oker*, *gebrande siena*,
  *loodglit* (litharge), *meekraprood*, *wedeblauw*, *wouw* (weld) →
  *woudekuipen*, *lijnolie/walnootolie*, *pleisterkalk*, *pyriet* /
  *gekkengoud*.
- **Guild and town:** *Sint-Lucasgilde*, *gildeoudste*, *keur* (charter),
  *weduwenrecht/weduwenverlof*, *in de leer doen* (to article), *gebeurnis*
  (period continuance-fee; deliberate period lexis), *rooilijn*,
  *plateelbakker* (Delft potter), *glazenier*, *chirurgijn*, *reverence*.
- **The disaster:** *de donderslag*, *het Secreet van Holland* (historical
  nickname, replacing the EN gloss "the Secret of Holland"),
  *Clarissenklooster* (convent of the Poor Clares), *kruitmagazijn*.
- **Money and daily life:** *gulden/stuiver*, *dun bier* (small beer; the
  chapter title uses the idiom *Klein bier*), *erwtenbrij*, *turf/turfrook*,
  *grootboek* (ledger), *op de pof* (on tick), *Sint-Maarten* (Martinmas).

**Motif systems preserved whole:** the wash-and-settle lesson ("het beste
is het traagst / het dierbaarste het laatst"); *het goud dat geen goud was*
(pyrite); *de blauwste handen van Delft*; *een blauw dat je ademen kunt*;
the chained goldfinch as "stilhoudend om gezien te worden"; *de zwaardere
helft (in het donker)*; "het wit" as the name the survivors give the blast;
the colourless Delft light "zonder vleierij" returning transformed in the
closing blue hour; Doncker's "correct als een diaken" and the
appetite-dressed-as-duty theme; the ledger's "waarste kolom".

## Gloss reversions (EN glossed Dutch realities; the NL speaks plainly)

Per the convention set by *De papieren sinaasappel* (PR #130) and carried
by *De Waag* (PR #134):

1. "a goldfinch, a *puttertje*" → *een puttertje* (no gloss; the chapter
   title becomes *Het puttertje*); the EN's "put-birds … draw up their own
   water" etymology aside becomes the native wordplay *puttertjes … die hun
   eigen water putten*.
2. "the Secret of Holland" → *het Secreet van Holland* (the magazine's
   actual historical nickname).
3. "a widowed *verfmaalster* (colour-grinder)" → *verfmaalster*, unglossed.
4. "the dyers' water" appositions for the Verwersdijk → the street name
   carries its own meaning in Dutch; the apposition is kept once (Ch. 1)
   as rhythm, dropped where the EN used it as pure gloss.
5. "the fullers' bridge" on the Voldersgracht → *de vollersbrug* (the
   gracht name already says it).
6. "azurite, the mountain-blue" → *azuriet* / *bergblauw* used as the
   plain trade synonyms they are.
7. "the weigh-house clerk" → *de klerk van de waag*.
8. "guild-chest" → *gildekas*.
9. "faber, the maker" (Fabritius's Latin name-etymology) — **kept as-is**:
   it is Latin, not a Dutch gloss, and the beat depends on it.

## Known EN-source wobbles (carried, not repaired)

Per fixes-propagate-EN→NL, nothing was silently fixed. One candidate found
during translation, flagged for an EN-side look rather than patched here:
Ch. 5 has Grietje's treasures "shifted … to the sill of the shop-window"
(ground floor) that morning, while Ch. 6 finds Grietje under the fallen
staircase and Ch. 8/9 have her carrying all four treasures "out of the
ruin in her fist" — workable (she checked them at dawn and could have
pocketed them), but the EN never says she took them back off the sill; a
one-line EN clarification would close the seam. The NL renders the scenes
exactly as the EN has them.
