## What this version is

The novella cut as a **three-episode serial**, each episode a purchasable
unit: the corresponding part's full text, unabridged and unaltered, plus
written-for-serial connective tissue — a cold-open hook, in-voice "story so
far" recaps, and episode-ending hooks. All additions are finished prose in
the book's register; no bullet summaries, no truncation mid-scene, no canon
changes.

## What was added (and where)

- **Episode 1** — *Part One: The Muller* (chapters 1–4, full text):
  - **Cold open** (new, ~200 words, italic, before ch. 1): a flash-forward
    frame naming the Secret of Holland and the morning of the twelfth,
    ending "…with a woman breaking a beautiful stone." The base opening is
    a slow literary open; the cold open gives a browsing serial reader the
    stakes on page one without spoiling more than the book's own dramatic
    irony (ch. 4 already closes on "the last ordinary night").
  - **Episode-ending hook** (new, ~140 words, italic, after ch. 4's final
    line): the sleeping town, the powder behind the convent wall, the key
    on its nail, "the morning… would come at half past ten." Written prose,
    not a truncation — ch. 4's own closing line is kept intact above it.
- **Episode 2** — *Part Two: The Thunderclap* (chapters 5–8, full text):
  - **"The Story So Far"** (new, 446 words): narrative recap of Part One in
    the book's register — Clara, Grietje, Barent, Fabritius and the
    goldfinch, Doncker's offer, the hidden hoard, the Secret of Holland.
  - **Episode-ending hook** (new, ~140 words, italic, after ch. 8's final
    line): the frost sealing the blue into the mud, "no frost yet made had
    held anything forever" — points at Part Three without resolving it.
- **Episode 3** — *Part Three: The Blue Hour* (chapters 9–12, full text):
  - **"The Story So Far"** (new, 469 words): narrative recap of Parts One
    and Two. No closing hook — the book's own ending stands as written.
- Each episode carries front matter: title, `draft` status with episode
  number, edition note, and the whole-work content note (each episode is a
  separate purchase; the disclosure must travel with each unit — episode 2
  flags the disaster content specifically).

## Scene-boundary moves

**None.** The base part boundaries are clean act breaks — Part One ends on
the eve of the Thunderclap ("the last ordinary night"), Part Two ends at
the winter nadir ("the blue was in the mud, and she did not know how to
start"), Part Three carries the rebuilding to the true final scene. No
split lands mid-arc, so no scene was moved between episodes.

## Market position

- **Format:** serialized historical fiction ebooks — three standalone-
  purchasable episodes (~9.2k / 9.8k / 10.4k words), complete story in
  three units.
- **Pricing frame:** per-episode **$2.99** — the KDP 70%-royalty floor
  ($2.99–$9.99 band per `docs/publishing/CHECKLIST.md` §4; ≈ $2.09/episode
  at 70% before delivery fee) — versus the full-novel single volume at
  **$3.99–$5.99** ($4.99 recommended, ≈ $3.49/sale, per the vetting
  packet §4). A completed serial run grosses ≈ $8.97 / nets ≈ $6.27 per
  reader who buys all three, vs ≈ $3.49 for the single volume — IF readers
  carry through all three episodes.
- **Honest caveat — serialization economics are unverified.** The 2–3×
  revenue math above assumes episode-2/3 carry-through rates that nobody
  has measured for this catalog, and literary historical fiction is not a
  proven Kindle serial category (serial reading on Amazon skews to genre
  fiction, and Amazon's dedicated serial surface, Kindle Vella, shut down
  in early 2025). A $2.99 price on a ~9–10k-word episode also risks poor
  value perception next to $4.99 full novellas. Unknown-author base case
  remains ≈ $0 either way; no sales projection is made.
  **SIM-REQUEST: serial vs single-volume pricing feasibility** — model
  carry-through breakeven (what episode-2/3 continuation rate makes 3 ×
  $2.99 beat 1 × $4.99 per browsing reader) before any publish decision.
- ⚑ **Publishing is owner-gated** via the existing vetting packet
  (`docs/publishing/vetting/ultramarine.md` §7) — this version adds NO new
  publish surface; any serial release rides the same owner clicks (KDP
  account, cover, price, publish), extended to three listings if the owner
  chooses the serial route.
- ⚑ **Pending D3 retitle applies to any published edition:** the vetting
  packet's recommended-but-undecided rename to **"The Widow's Blue — A
  Novel of Delft, 1654"** is an OWNER decision. These files keep the
  working title *Ultramarine*; if the owner picks the rename, episode
  titles/front matter (and filenames at packaging time) follow it —
  e.g. "The Widow's Blue — Part One: The Muller".
