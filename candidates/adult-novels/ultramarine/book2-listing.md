## Blurb (short — store / product page)

*A year after the gunpowder blast that tore the heart out of Delft, the widowed
colour-grinder Clara Wijnants is running out of the two things that keep her
free: the precious ultramarine she washed grain by grain from the frozen ruin,
and the painters left alive to buy it. As the last of the old blue passes into
the one painting that will outlast them all, a rougher trade comes knocking — the
blue-and-white potteries rising in the town's dead breweries need a colour-hand
who understands blue. But this is a fired blue, born of the same element that
killed the town, invisible in the raw ore until the kiln makes it. To keep her
trade, her orphaned niece, and her hard-won independence, Clara must learn to
trust a colour she cannot see — and answer one last quiet attempt to shut the
door the catastrophe left her. A luminous, restrained novel about grief and the
making of beauty, the turning of a great craft from one age to the next, and a
blue that death cannot get at.*

## Back-cover copy (longer)

*The painters of Delft are dying out. The kilns are just beginning. And the widow
who grinds the truest blue in the province must decide which age she belongs to.*

Clara Wijnants kept her trade through the worst year of her life — the year the
Secret of Holland blew a quarter of Delft into the sky and took her apprentice,
her shop, and the painter Carel Fabritius with it. She dug her life out of the
mud grain by grain, and the guild grudged her the right to go on. But a widow's
leave stretches only so far, her lease runs out at the turn of the year, and the
painters left alive are too few and too poor to keep her.

Then a broad, burn-scarred potter comes to her door at first light with a sack of
drab grey ore and an impossible claim: *this is the finest blue in the world — or
it will be, once the fire has it.* The delftware potteries are rising in the
shells of the town's failed breweries, hungry for a colour no painter's boy can
grind clean, and they want Clara's hands. To take the work she must learn a blue
she cannot judge with her eye, master the very fire that broke the town, and face
down the guild elder who has spent two years trying, sincerely and without once
raising his hand, to close her door for good.

*The Blue and the White* is the second Ultramarine novel: a story of the Dutch
Golden Age at its turning point, when Delft stopped being a town of painters and
became the town of the blue-and-white that would sail to the far side of the
world. About the cost and worth of beauty, the mercy at the root of a craft
handed on, and learning to lay the colour down in the dark and trust the fire.
Contains bereavement and remembered disaster handled with restraint; no explicit
content. Stands alone; deeper after *Ultramarine* (Book 1).

bread you cannot replace.*

content. Suitable for adult general readers; not for children.
