# Ultramarine — Outline

> **Status:** `draft`

**Premise.** When a gunpowder magazine called the Secret of Holland tears the heart out of Delft, a widowed colour-grinder must dig her life, her orphaned niece, and a hoard of the world's most precious blue out of the rubble — and decide whether beauty is worth what it costs to make.

This outline is the shared source of truth for all three part-writers. Respect every continuity anchor below and every beat in the sheet.

## Continuity anchors (fixed facts every chapter must respect)

- **Clara Wijnants**, 34, *verfmaalster* (colour-grinder). Shop: a narrow brick house on the **Verwersdijk** (the dyers' canal), two streets from the powder store nicknamed the **Secret of Holland** (in the old convent of the Poor Clares by the Geerweg, the NE Doelen quarter). Ground-floor shop; grinding room at the back with a **porphyry slab and muller**; living rooms above. Hands permanently blue-stained.
- **Gerrit Wijnants**, her husband, a colour-merchant and Guild of St Luke member, **dead two winters** (a fever, early 1652). Clara holds the trade by a widow's right the guild grudgingly allows.
- **Neeltje**, Clara's sister, dead; her daughter **Grietje** (8) is the orphan Clara now raises. Grietje collects small bright things (blue tile-shard, a button, a pyrite pebble); **struck mute after the blast** (Part Two), voice returns slowly (Part Three).
- **Barent**, 15, grinding-boy from the Heilige Geest almshouse; strong, cheerful, worships painters, dreams of the guild; **KILLED in the blast** (ch5/6). The story's chief personal cost — establish him vividly in Part One so the loss lands.
- **The hoard:** Clara's secret is a stoppered pot of purified ultramarine she has ground and washed grain by grain over years — worth more than the shop; her security and her way out from under debt and the guild's Doncker. Kept hidden in a plain grey gallipot behind the slab. **Scattered/lost in the blast; reclaimed from the frozen mud in Part Three, grain by grain.**
- **Carel Fabritius** (real), 32, Rembrandt's boldest pupil, studio near the Doelen (in the blast zone). Buys ultramarine and lead-white (*loodwit*) from Clara, often on credit; **owes her six gulden**. Painting a small panel of a chained goldfinch (a *puttertje*) and a portrait of the deacon Simon Decker. Talks of perspective and seeing true. **Dies at his easel on 12 Oct** with his pupil **Mathias Spoors**; his wife **Agatha** survives. The little goldfinch panel **survives the blast** (a small miracle amid the loss).
- **Willem Doncker** (invented), 50s, colour-merchant and guild elder; covets Clara's stall, holds that a woman's independent trade is unnatural. **Self-righteous, sincere, not a cartoon.**
- Money in **gulden/stuiver**. Cold grey October, Little Ice Age. **Blast = mid-morning, about half past ten, 12 October 1654.** Refrain phrase available: **"not to grieve the breaking."**

## Beat sheet

### Part One — The Muller

**1. Lapis.** Before dawn, Clara grinds a knuckle of lapis into ultramarine on the porphyry slab; establish the craft, the shop on the Verwersdijk, the working canal waking, peat-smoke, the powder store two streets off taken for granted. Grietje wakes; Barent arrives. Establish widowhood (Gerrit two winters gone), the debt, and the hidden hoard of blue. End looking toward the day's customers. Motif open: freeing blue by breaking stone; *"not to grieve the breaking."*

**2. The Goldfinch.** Fabritius comes to the shop for ultramarine and *loodwit*, short of coin, buys on credit (his debt now six gulden); he talks of his little chained-goldfinch panel, of perspective glasses and seeing true; his warmth and faint recklessness; Clara's guardedness and the attraction she will not name. He mentions the Decker portrait. Turn: she stakes him more blue than she should. Theme: seeing.

**3. The Widow's Right.** Market/guild-adjacent scene. Willem Doncker presses Clara — a woman ought not hold a colour-stall; the guild elders murmur; Clara defends her widow's right to continue Gerrit's trade. Economic precarity; the ledger of painters' debts; woven backstory of Gerrit's death in the fever winter. Turn: Doncker makes a veiled offer/threat for her stall. She refuses.

**4. Small Beer.** Domestic night. Clara and Grietje above the shop; the dead sister Neeltje; Clara teaches Grietje to wash the blue (craft and love passed down); private grief and the small hope the hoard represents; the ledger by candlelight. October dusk deepening toward the morning of the 12th. End on quiet foreboding — the ordinary last evening.

### Part Two — The Thunderclap

**5. The Twelfth of October.** An ordinary grey morning; errands on the Verwersdijk; Cornelis Soetens crossing toward the Secret with his lantern, greeting Clara, unaware; the light; then the detonation rendered from inside Clara's body — pressure, sound gone white, the world torn. (Barent is at the shop.)

**6. In the Dust.** Clara buried in the wreck of her own shop; digs out; the flattened quarter, fire, dust, unrecognisable dead; she searches for Grietje and Barent; finds Grietje alive under the collapsed stair, injured and mute; Barent dead. The hoard's pot shattered, blue in the rubble.

**7. At the Easel.** Clara carries Grietje out and, half-mad, makes her way toward Fabritius's studio; finds it destroyed, Fabritius dead near his easel, Mathias dead; Agatha's grief; the little goldfinch panel improbably intact in the ruin. Witness and carrying-out.

**8. The Reckoning.** The days after: a counting that cannot be counted; mass hasty burials; Barent buried; the city's shock (Bleyswijck recording ruins); the relief money and the roof-tile profiteers; Clara's shop and livelihood gone into the mud, the guild question now sharp. End on Clara at the edge of defeat.

### Part Three — The Blue Hour

**9. Ashes and Tiles.** Winter; Clara and Grietje sheltering (a cousin's cold back room); Doncker moves to seize her ruined plot and take over the widow's right; the choice — leave Delft or rebuild with nothing; Grietje barely speaking; the cold and the debt.

**10. Blue in the Mud.** Clara returns to the ruin and sifts the frozen mud, recovering her ground ultramarine grain by grain — reclaiming the blue; the turn from grief to work; a meeting with Agatha and/or a glimpse of the young newly-enrolled painter (Vermeer, never named); the "new seeing"; resolve to reopen the trade.

**11. The Widow's Blue.** Clara reopens on her own terms; confronts Doncker and the guild and wins a hard, narrow victory (the widow's right upheld, or a bargain that keeps her free); Grietje begins to grind alongside her, the craft passed on, her voice returning.

**12. The Colour That Remains.** Spring. Clara supplies her reclaimed ultramarine to a new painting that will outlast them all (bridging Fabritius's lost light and the new seeing); the blue hour on the canal; the seeing-motif resolves; a coda on endurance and the cost and worth of beauty. End on light.
