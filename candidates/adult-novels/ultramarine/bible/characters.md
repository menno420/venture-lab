# Ultramarine — Character Reference

> **Status:** `draft`

A working reference for the chapter-writers. Clara holds the point of view throughout (see `style.md`); everyone else is seen through her.

## Clara Wijnants — protagonist, 34

A ***verfmaalster*** — she grinds and sells colour to the painters of Delft from a narrow shop on the **Verwersdijk**, the dyers' canal, two streets from the powder store. She is the widow of **Gerrit Wijnants**, a colour-merchant and member of the Guild of St Luke, dead two winters of a fever; she holds the trade by a **widow's right the guild grudgingly allows** rather than by any standing of her own. Precise, guarded, dry-humoured, grief-worn, she reads the world in terms of pigment and light, and her hands are permanently blue-stained. Her one secret extravagance is a **slowly-built hoard of purified ultramarine**, ground grain by grain over years and saved against one great commission that might set her free of debt. Her arc runs from guarded endurance, through catastrophe and near-defeat, to a chosen, clear-eyed remaking of her life and trade on her own terms. She is the still, exact centre the whole book is weighed against; give her competence and reticence, and let feeling surface in gesture and in what she does with her hands, not in speech.

## Grietje — Clara's niece, 8

Daughter of Clara's dead sister **Neeltje**, and now Clara's charge. Bright, watchful, a collector of small bright things (a fragment of blue tile, a button, a pebble of pyrite). **Struck mute by shock after the blast**, she recovers her voice only slowly as the book heals — a barometer of the household's return to life. She is the child Clara did not bear and did not expect to love; much of Clara's arc is legible in how she comes to hold this child. Keep her a real, specific eight-year-old, not a symbol — stubborn, literal, frightened, funny.

## Barent — apprentice grinding-boy, 15

An orphan of the almshouse, taken on to work the muller. Strong-armed, cheerful, he **worships the painters and dreams of the guild** he can barely afford to dream of. He is the book's early warmth and comic relief — clumsy, hungry, quick with the wrong word. He is **killed in the explosion**, and his death is the story's **chief personal cost**: the loss the reader has been made to love before it is taken. Establish him vividly and quickly in Part One so the loss lands.

## Carel Fabritius — real, 32

**Rembrandt's boldest pupil**, painting light and space no one else in Delft can. He buys **ultramarine and lead-white from Clara, often on credit**, and talks with her of perspective, of a little painting of a chained goldfinch, of seeing true. Warm, quick, faintly reckless with money — nearer a friend than a customer, though the friendship stays within period propriety. He **dies at his easel on 12 October**. Handle strictly within the documented record (see `DECISIONS.md` [D-0003]): no invented romance, no defamation; his death and his art are historical, his conversations with the invented Clara are the licence the fiction takes.

## Willem Doncker — invented, 50s

A prosperous **colour-merchant and guild elder** who covets Clara's stall and her custom and frames a woman's independent trade as unnatural, a threat to good order. **Not a cartoon villain** — self-righteous, aggrieved, sincerely convinced he is defending propriety and the guild. He is the antagonist of the social and economic plot; his pressure on Clara is the slow vice that the catastrophe both worsens and, unexpectedly, gives her the means to answer. Let his menace be bureaucratic and moral, not physical.

## Agatha — real (Agatha van Pruyssen), Fabritius's wife

She **survives** the explosion that kills her husband. Her grief is a key note of Part Two — a mirror to Clara's own widowhood, and a woman Clara can neither comfort nor abandon. Keep her within record; she is a real person handled with respect.

## Cornelis Soetens — real, keeper of the Secret of Holland

Keeper of the powder store. Give him **one brief, humane appearance on the morning of the 12th** — carrying his lantern, unhurried, unaware of what he is about to do. He is not a villain and not a fool; his ordinariness that morning is the point. He dies in the blast.

## Peripheral

- A **young, newly-enrolled painter** glimpsed once or twice — the Bolnes girl's husband, joined the guild at Christmas — never named outright (this is Vermeer; keep him anonymous and slight, per `DECISIONS.md` [D-0003]).
- **Dirck van Bleyswijck**, seen recording the ruins in Part Three — the town's chronicler, a real figure, used only as witness.
