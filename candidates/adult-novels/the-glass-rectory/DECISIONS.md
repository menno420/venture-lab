# Decisions — The Glass Rectory

Run under the BOOKS lane of the day run 2026-07-13. First complete manuscript for the concept vetted in `docs/publishing/vetting/the-glass-rectory.md` (concept-stage packet, landed via PR #143, collision Low); this is the write-slice that packet parks on.

- **Premise fidelity** — Delivers the vetted packet exactly: 1893, a moorland parish (Wythershaw); Honor Vane comes to keep house for her brother Edward, the new curate-in-charge; the two instructions from the packet's blurb are given verbatim on the page ("do not mind the cold, and do not go into the glass wing after dark"); the previous incumbent (the Rev. Dr. Ambrose Snow) built the glass conservatory wing and walked into it one January night in 1881 and was never seen again; the glass never fogs, the plants never die, and the tapping-back is the engine of the plot. Register is the packet's classic-ghost-story register throughout (M.R. James / Susan Hill shelf): dread by restraint, no gore, no jump-scare pulp; the packet's closing line — "about the things we grow in place of grief" — is the book's stated theme and its resolution.
- **Scope/length** — One complete, standalone gothic ghost novella, English. Honest count: `wc -w` = **15,117** on `en/the-glass-rectory.md` (includes front matter and chapter headings), inside the 15,000–16,000 target. Note: the packet's §3 planned length class was ~20k–30k; this run's parity default (the proven adult-novella band) set 15k–16k and the shorter target won — flagged honestly rather than padded.
- **POV/tense** — First-person retrospective (Honor Vane, narrating some thirty years on), past tense — the fireside told-tale convention of the register (*The Woman in Black* frame), and deliberately distinct from the night-kiln manuscript's close third.
- **Structure** — 12 chapters, three movements: I (ch. 1–3) arrival, the green wing by daylight, the history of Doctor Snow and Eugenie; II (ch. 4–9) escalation — the tapping answered, the cold-house economy (the house starves so the garden lives), the garden-book diary, the village's withheld testimony, the sexton's confession, the last entry reframing the haunting as a twelve-year-old plea; III (ch. 10–12) the after-dark entry, the temptation refused, the garden let die (letting in January), the discovery and double burial, and a daylight coda. Ghost logic is emotionally coherent and single-spined: Snow grew a kept grief in glass, went in on the anniversary to end it, and was kept by it; the tapping asks a fellow griever to finish his night's work. The haunting has meaning; nothing is arbitrary.
- **Imagery discipline** — Dread is built through architecture, light, and glass per the brief: the unfogging panes as the central image (seeded in the burial-register marginal note "The glass never fogged", paid off when the dying garden fogs every pane at once); the single fogged pane (the packet §5 cover direction) is the mid-book apparition beat; frost-fern, breath on glass, and the fogged potting-shed of the coda close the image system.
- **Content** — Adult, quiet-horror clean: all deaths off the page and in the past (consumption 1869, disappearance 1881, the fiance's typhoid 1890); remains discovered are handled reverently, no gore; no violence, no explicit content. Content note in front matter.
- **Series potential** — None claimed, per the packet §3: standalone, complete arc, THE END on the page; no hook planted.
- **⚑ Publishing** — Owner-gated, click-level, per the vetting packet §7 OWNER-GATE block (KDP account/tax interview, cover approval, $4.99 price, the two §6 categories + 7 keywords re-verified at graduation, KDP Select enrollment, title recheck at upload, October/Christmas launch window). Nothing published, no accounts touched, no spend by this session.
- **Cover** — Cover-only production gate per packet §5 (a lit glass conservatory against black moorland, one pane fogged from the inside; 1600×2560 px min; no skulls, no spectres); any art spend is an owner decision.
- **Production/metering** — Drafted single-session, chapter-by-chapter, committed in 3-chapter chunks; no parallel workers.
