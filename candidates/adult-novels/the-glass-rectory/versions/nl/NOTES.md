## What this is

A complete literary Dutch (Netherlands register) translation of *The Glass
Rectory*. Source text: `../../en/the-glass-rectory.md` at its merged state
on `main` @ `ed3f2dfa679bdd527e14d78818bf9ea8594caf8b` (the fork point of
this branch; the EN manuscript landed via PR #148; verbatim `wc -w` 15,117
words including title, front matter, and chapter headings). All 12 chapters
translated in full — every line finished prose, no summary placeholders;
per-chapter paragraph counts verified identical EN ↔ NL for all twelve
chapters. The EN manuscript stays the source of truth for the story; fixes
propagate EN → NL, never the reverse. The register target is the EN
DECISIONS.md's own brief — classic-ghost-story register (M.R. James /
Susan Hill shelf), dread by restraint — carried into formal, lightly
period-flavoured literary Dutch: first-person retrospective told-tale
voice, the narrator addressing the reader as "u", no gore, no pulp. All
dates, ages, temperatures, and the ghost logic held exactly to the EN text
and `../../DECISIONS.md` (nothing added, nothing drifted; the story's
single invented world — Wythershaw, Aldergate, Scarhead — carries over as
proper nouns).

## Title decision

**Chosen: *De glazen pastorie*** (subtitle *Een spookverhaal*). The direct,
natural Dutch of the EN title: "pastorie" is exactly the haunted-rectory
word the NL gothic shelf already uses (the Dutch-market framing of the
Borley Rectory legend runs on "de pastorie"), and "glazen" keeps the
central image the whole book hangs on. Dutch sentence-case title
capitalization per modern NL trade convention.

Alternatives considered and rejected without scan (quality grounds): *De
glazen vleugel* (names the wing, loses the rectory — the house-with-a-room
architecture is the book), *De serre* (generic to invisibility; reads as
literary domestic fiction), *Het glas besloeg nooit* (the register-margin
line — strong, but a spoiler-shaped sentence title that surrenders the
packet's cover direction).

Standing caveat carried from all prior NL packets: Dutch self-pub titles
are unevenly web-indexed; "none found" is weaker evidence than for
trad-pub. Final KDP/bol.com title recheck at upload is the owner's §7
step.

## Gloss reversions

Near-none required — the EN glosses no Dutch realities (invented Yorkshire
parish). Two EN-internal aids absorbed where Dutch has the native word:

1. **frost-fern** — the EN builds the image in parts ("grey with
   frost-fern", "the fern-work whitened", "the grey lace going white and
   crystal"); NL has the single household word **ijsbloemen** and uses it,
   keeping the EN's lace/crystal elaborations as elaborations rather than
   explanations.
2. **cherry-pie** — EN: "cherry-pie, they call it, that heavy almond
   sweetness"; NL keeps the aside but marks it as the English folk-name
   heard abroad ("kersenvlaai, zeggen ze daarginds"), since the calque is
   not a Dutch plant name.
