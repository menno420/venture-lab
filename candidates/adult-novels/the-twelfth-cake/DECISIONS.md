- **Premise fidelity** — Delivers the vetted packet exactly: London, the fifth of January, 1847 (Twelfth Night; a Tuesday, verified — see chronology ledger); Pridd's bakery of Pannier Court, off Bread Street, on its last night before it is sold; the sale papers signed at three o'clock that afternoon; the ovens drawn and going cold; one enormous Twelfth Night cake in the window, made for nobody; twelve snowbound strangers counted twice by a nine-year-old crier; the bean rule stated in the packet's own words — king of the feast, "apprentice or alderman, no appeals" — and the bean found in the most inconvenient slice in London: the incognito purchaser's.
