## Long description
```
Most side projects don't fail at launch — they fail at intake, because there never was one. The Kill-Rule Intake Kit is the decision paperwork a working autonomous-agent revenue lane fills in before any candidate gets a line of code, packaged for any indie builder.

You get a fillable intake template with three binding kill-rule fields: a time-boxed validation signal (hit it or the idea is ledgered dead), a named first-ten-customers path with its weaknesses admitted in writing, and a hard effort budget where over-budget-without-signal means the kill decision was already made. Alongside it: a distribution-first weighted scoring rubric (distribution is 35% of the total, because that's where side projects die), a one-page kill-rules discipline, and a negative-result ledger template so killed ideas stay killed.

The two worked examples are adapted from the lane's own real intakes, unflattering scores included — one that barely earned its build at 3.55/5, and one that scored 3.10 and honestly wrote "realistically ~$0" about itself. Plain Markdown, no dependencies, no runtime, nothing to install. Conventions on paper: the kit only works if you hold yourself to the numbers you wrote down.
```

## Bullets
```
- Fillable intake template with binding kill-rule fields ([[fill]] slots throughout)
- Distribution-first weighted rubric: distribution 35%, buildability 20%, launch-effort 15%, speed-to-first-dollar 15%, WTP/moat 15%
- Score anchors for every axis, so 0–5 means the same thing every time
- Anti-gaming rules: score distribution first, show the arithmetic, never tune weights to save an idea
- One-page kill-rules discipline: over budget without the signal = ledgered negative, decided in advance
- Negative-result ledger template with a revival bar, so kills stay killed
- Two worked examples from real intakes — a borderline build (3.55) and an honest longshot (3.10)
- Plain Markdown, zero dependencies, zero runtime — fill it in anywhere
```

## FAQ
```
Q: Does anything enforce the kill rules?
A: No. This is plain Markdown — conventions and templates, not software. The discipline works only if you hold yourself to the thresholds you wrote down at intake. The kit's job is to make you write them down before you're attached.

Q: Are the worked examples real?
A: They are adapted from real candidate intakes in the autonomous-agent revenue lane that produced this kit — real scores, real arithmetic, real "why this might fail" sections — with names and identifying details changed. Nothing was invented for marketing effect.

Q: Will the rubric tell me if my idea is good?
A: No, and it says so in the kit. The rubric keeps comparisons between YOUR ideas consistent and forces the weak axes into the open. The kill-rule fields decide when a built idea dies.

Q: What's included?
A: kill-rule-intake-kit-v0.1.zip: README, QUICKSTART, manifest, and the pack/ — intake template, scoring rubric, kill rules, negative-ledger template, and two worked examples. Nine Markdown files.

Q: What was NOT machine-verified?
A: This is a zero-runtime product, so there is no test suite. What was verified by execution: the archive rebuilds byte-identically, every file is valid UTF-8 Markdown, the examples' scoring arithmetic recomputes to the shipped totals, and a secret scan of the bundle came back clean.

Q: License?
A: Single-user license; use the templates in as many of your own projects as you like. v0.1 — free updates to the v0.x line.

Q: Support?
A: Best-effort, community-level. It's a template kit, not a managed service.
```
