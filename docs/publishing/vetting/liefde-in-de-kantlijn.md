## 1. Title

- [x] Working title: **Liefde in de kantlijn** — locked by the
      manuscript's own title decision (NOTES.md: a literal rendering of
      the EN title compounds badly in Dutch; alternatives *De
      zaadcatalogus-hofmakerij*, *Wat zou u planten?*, *De laatste
      zaadlijst* considered and rejected with reasons; not relitigated
      here). Subtitle: ***Een roman in brieven*** — recommended ON: the
      epistolary form is the selling point, say it on the cover (the EN
      packet's own §1 logic, carried over).
- [x] Bare-word searchability: the title deliberately joins the visible
      "Liefde in …" NL romance-title family without duplicating any
      member — genre-legible at a glance, distinctive as a full phrase
      (the margin is the book's engine and the blurb's closing line).
- [x] Homophone / autocorrect drift: none serious; "kantlijn" is a
      common, stable Dutch word. The EN Catalogue/Catalog spelling fork
      does not exist in Dutch — one worry fewer than the EN listing.
