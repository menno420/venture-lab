## 0. Reclassification — plan §2/§4 corrected by this packet

The plan (§2) placed The Painted Stones in **Tier 1 "adult/literary
standalone, cover-only"** and queued a §4 OWNER-ACTION at **$4.99 reflowable
ebook**. The plan's own §2 caveat — *"We did not read the manuscripts"* —
anticipated exactly this failure mode. This session read the manuscript:

- `candidates/childrens-books/painted-stones/painted-stones.en.md` is a
  **13-spread read-aloud picture book, ages 4–8**, EN body ~556–565 words
  (self-report 556; `wc -w` measured this session, §3). It is a cozy kids
  mystery in the *Little Notebook Mysteries* series frame.
- There is **no adult "The Painted Stones" manuscript**:
  `candidates/adult-novels/` contains only `README.md`, `the-slow-word/`,
  `the-weigh-house/`, and `ultramarine/` (verified at repo HEAD this session).

Consequences: the title's production gate is **cover + full interior
illustration** (plan §3 / checklist §5), not cover-only; the price band is
**kids paperback $12.99–$14.99 print**, not $4.99 ebook; and the plan's §4
"Publish The Painted Stones" block is superseded by §7 below. This packet
does **not** edit `PUBLISHING-PLAN.md`; the plan amendment (move the title to
the illustration-gated tier) is **flagged for the coordinator**.

## 4. Price band

- [x] Band: **kids paperback list $12.99–$14.99** (plan §1, verified) →
      **recommended $12.99**. Rationale: unknown author, first entry in an
      unproven series — enter at the bottom of the band to minimize buyer
      resistance and raise later on evidence; $12.99 still clears a healthy
      per-unit margin (next line).
- [x] Print royalty computed per checklist: **60% of list − print cost**. A
      24–40pp premium-color paperback prints at a flat **~$3.60** on
      Amazon.com ([KDP printing cost via plan Correction #1, accessed
      2026-07-12](https://kdp.amazon.com/en_US/help/topic/G201834340));
      13 spreads ≈ **26–32pp** — inside that flat band. At $12.99:
      0.60 × 12.99 − 3.60 ≈ **$4.19/unit**; at $14.99 ≈ **$5.39/unit**.
      $12.99 is safely above KDP's $9.99 threshold below which print royalty
      drops to 50% (§3 citation).
- [x] Ebook: **secondary format only** — the primary format for a picture
      book is print (checklist §4 print line; plan §1 kids row is a print
      price). If the owner later ships a fixed-layout kids ebook, a
      $2.99–$9.99 price keeps the 70% ebook tier (e.g. $4.99 ≈ $3.49/sale
      before delivery fee — image-heavy files carry a real per-MB fee at
      70%). The plan §4 block's "$4.99 reflowable ebook" is **wrong for this
      title** twice over: picture books are fixed-layout, not reflowable, and
      ebook is not the lead format. No $0.99 impulse price is recommended
      (35% tier).

### 6a. Cover + interior illustration brief (pre-chews the §5/§7 owner decision)

- **Format:** cover for a 26–32pp premium-color paperback (final trim/count
  set at layout); 13 interior spreads. The manuscript's per-spread italic
  notes are the art direction of record — this brief adds the connective
  tissue.
- **Style/mood:** warm, soft, storybook-cozy — misty watercolor/gouache
  register, not flat vector "app style". A gentle mystery, never spooky:
  mist reads as morning-soft, not ominous.
- **Palette:** harbor blue-greys and sea-glass greens for the town and mist;
  **Wren's red raincoat as the constant warm accent** in every scene; warm
  cinnamon-yellows for the bakery and the boathouse sunbeam finale. The
  painted stones carry small saturated pops: blue boat, orange cat, gold
  star, moon, fish, tiny red raincoat.
- **Characters:** Wren — small girl, red raincoat, well-worn notebook +
  pencil, alert and delighted, never frightened. Milo — boy about her size,
  shy body language (behind knees, pink cheeks, paintbrush in hand) turning
  to warmth. Keep both designs exactly consistent across all 13 spreads —
  the reveal spread (10) and the shared-stone finale (13) are the emotional
  anchors.
- **Recurring motifs (continuity checklist for the illustrator):** the red
  raincoat; the notebook; the stone-of-the-morning series (boat → cat →
  star); the clue chain (blue smudge, speckled yellow ribbon, chalk arrows,
  wet footprint); the boathouse's blue door. Spread 12's
  red-raincoat-stone close-up must visibly match Wren's coat.
- **Cover direction:** Wren mid-discovery on the misty cobblestone quay —
  red raincoat focal point, painted stone in hand, boathouse or harbor soft
  in the background; title large and legible at thumbnail size; series tag
  "A Little Notebook Mystery" as a small consistent band (future books
  reuse it).
- **Child-safety / care lines:** children are never depicted in water-peril —
  the jetty/harbor scenes read as safe small-town mornings (per the
  manuscript's tone: no threat anywhere). Everyday small-town cast; no
  cultural props or ethnicity-as-decoration; Saltwick is an invented,
  generic North-Sea-ish harbor town. If option (B) AI art is chosen, the
  KDP AI-generated disclosure applies (§5/§7) and human review of every
  spread for artifact errors (hands, text-in-image, stone motifs) is part
  of the owner's approval.
