**Title:** The Halfway Ferry · **Category:** kids (middle-grade, ages 8–12)
/ fantasy series · **Date vetted:** 2026-07-13 (concept-stage)

**One-line pitch:** The last jetty on the river runs a ferry that doesn't
appear on any timetable, because its other terminus isn't anywhere in
particular. Eleven-year-old Wren Tolliver — too practical to be scared and
too curious to stay home — is taken on as deckhand, and learns the three
rules: fares are never money, the fog decides the route, and you always
count the passengers twice. Book 1 of a planned series: one crossing per
book.

**Catalog fit (why this tiles):** the catalog's first MIDDLE-GRADE title —
a full age-band gap between the kids picture-book cluster (Painted Stones,
Windmill Mouse, Puddle Museum, Comet Biscuit et al.) and the YA pair (Pepper
Ledger, Marginalia Society). Kids-cluster rules respected: Painted Stones
owns kids mystery/friendship phrases, Comet Biscuit + Star Pirates own the
space shelf, Lantern Door/Pippa own bedtime/night-comfort — a river-ferry
portal fantasy touches none of them. Series potential is structural
(one-crossing-per-book = the proven one-vessel-per-book pattern).

## 1. Title

- [ ] Working title: **The Halfway Ferry**. Series-tag candidate: *"The
      Halfway Ferry, Book 1: The Fogline Crossing"* — MG buyers (parents,
      librarians) shop by series; the series IS the title; recommended ON.
- [ ] Bare-word searchability: "halfway" and "ferry" are each generic; the
      pairing is distinctive (scan found it unclaimed) — and "ferry" search
      noise is mostly *travel booking* traffic, not books (see §2), which a
      "Book 1" tag filters at a glance.
- [ ] Homophone / autocorrect drift: **ferry ↔ fairy** — a genuinely funny
      and genuinely real drift for a kids fantasy ("The Halfway Fairy");
      noted; arguably drift-positive, since a fairy-seeking child lands on a
      kids fantasy anyway. No other drift found.
