**Title:** The Salvage Orchard · **Category:** adult / solarpunk SF novella
· **Date vetted:** 2026-07-13 (concept-stage)

**One-line pitch:** A generation after the grid came apart and got rebuilt
smaller, Ash Okafor grafts fruit trees through the rusting acres of a
decommissioned vehicle yard — apples out of axle grease, cherries through
crushed glass — while the neighborhood argues about what the land is FOR.
Solarpunk in the repair register: community, compost, consensus meetings
that run long. Nobody shoots anybody; something still has to be saved.

**Catalog fit (why this tiles):** the catalog's second SF shelf, in a
register The Slow Word doesn't touch: Slow Word owns literary
first-contact / deep-time / radio-signal SF (its keyword rows are explicit:
`first contact novella`, `radio telescope alien signal story`, `deep time
science fiction`). This concept is near-future, Earthbound, no aliens, no
signals, no deep time — the hopeful climate-repair register the Becky
Chambers novella wave proved. Zero stem overlap by construction (checked
in §6).
