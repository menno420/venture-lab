**Title:** The Marmalade Post · **Category:** adult / contemporary cozy
mystery (series) · **Date vetted:** 2026-07-13 (concept-stage)

**One-line pitch:** Little Dunnock's sub-post-office is the village's
confessional — every secret passes over Vera Cusworth's counter, franked and
tied with string. When a jar of prize marmalade arrives addressed to a man
who's been dead for a decade, the new postmistress starts steaming open the
village's past. Book 1 of a planned cozy series (one misdelivered parcel per
book), warm, bloodless, tea-forward.

**Catalog fit (why this tiles):** the catalog's first adult COZY MYSTERY
shelf — a genuinely open gap: The Weigh House owns contemporary-crime
*procedural* register (and its Dutch register per C3); this is English-village
amateur-sleuth cozy, a different buyer and different browse nodes. Series
potential is the point: the one-vessel-per-book pattern (Comet Biscuit)
maps directly to one-parcel-per-book, and cozy is the most series-hungry
adult genre on KDP.

## 1. Title

- [ ] Working title: **The Marmalade Post**. Series name candidate: *"A
      Little Dunnock Post Office Mystery"* — the cozy convention is a series
      tag doing subtitle duty; recommended ON from book 1.
- [ ] Bare-word searchability: "marmalade" and "post" are individually
      generic, but the two-word collocation is distinctive; the series tag
      carries genre signal. Paddington adjacency noted honestly: "marmalade"
      pulls some children's-bear noise in search — the series tag and cozy
      cover code steer it adult.
- [ ] Homophone / autocorrect drift: "marmalade" is commonly misspelled
      *marmelade* (the Dutch/German spelling) — a real typed-search leak;
      note for the keyword pass at graduation (KDP keywords can absorb the
      misspelling if it proves material).
