**Title:** The Pepper Ledger · **Category:** YA / historical adventure
(age of sail) · **Date vetted:** 2026-07-13 (concept-stage)

**One-line pitch:** 1620s spice route — a merchant clerk's daughter who
keeps her father's books discovers a second, secret ledger, and stows away
on an East Indiaman to find out why a cargo of pepper is worth more than
the crew sailing it. YA maritime adventure: navigation, ledger-craft as a
girl's weapon, mutiny arithmetic, and the true price of a sack of spice.

**Catalog fit (why this tiles + the C3 discipline):** this is the wave's
designated **cheap-research-reuse** concept — it reuses the 17th-century
research base built for Ultramarine (era texture, trade, material culture)
in a *different genre lane and audience*: shipboard YA adventure vs adult
domestic-literary. Per the keyword map's **C3 rule** (Netherlands phrases
split by genre era), this packet drafts **ZERO Netherlands-branded
phrases**: no "Dutch", "Golden Age", "Delft", or "Amsterdam" keyword stems —
the maritime-adventure register is claimed instead, leaving Ultramarine's
Golden-Age-historical register and Weigh House's modern-crime register
untouched.
