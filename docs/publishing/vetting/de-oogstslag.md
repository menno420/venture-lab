## 1. Title

- [x] Working title: **De Oogstslag** — **derived this slice, not
      pre-named:** no NL title for Book Three existed anywhere in the
      series paper trail (checked in DECISIONS.md, both NL NOTES, both
      NL packets), so the pick was made per the series'
      object-per-book compound convention (*De Nachtoven* / *De
      Morgendeur*) and recorded with alternatives in the series
      DECISIONS.md (2026-07-14). *Slag* is the rowing stroke the
      story's crack falls on ("midden in een slag", Book Two's own NL
      text for this dish) and the contest word at once; chapter 10
      matches the book title exactly as the EN does. Subtitle: ***Een
      cozy-fantasynovelle · De Nachtoven, boek drie*** — recommended
      ON: genre phrase + explicit series tag, and it disambiguates the
      one weighed register risk (bare *slag* can read as "battle";
      the cozy genre phrase kills that on sight).
- [x] Bare-word searchability: "oogstslag" barely exists as a Dutch
      compound (NOTES.md scan: results degrade to dictionary and
      farm-news noise for *oogst* and *slag* separately) — distinctive
      and searchable, the same favourable position as "nachtoven" and
      "morgendeur".
- [x] Homophone / autocorrect drift: "oogstslag" → "oogst slag" is a
      spacing typo, not a drift target; no kiln→kill-class hazard. The
      series' object-per-book lattice is preserved by construction.
