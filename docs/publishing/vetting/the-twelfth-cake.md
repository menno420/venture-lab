**Title:** The Twelfth Cake · **Category:** adult / seasonal-holiday
historical novella · **Date vetted:** 2026-07-13 (concept-stage)

**One-line pitch:** London, the fifth of January, 1847. Pridd's bakery has
one night left before it is sold, one enormous Twelfth Night cake in the
window, and — by ancient rule of the feast — whoever finds the bean in
their slice is king for the evening, whatever their station. One snowbound
night, a dozen strangers, and a bakery that decides its own succession.
Warm Dickensian seasonal fiction; a holiday book for the week AFTER
Christmas.

**Catalog fit (why this tiles):** the catalog's first SEASONAL/HOLIDAY
shelf — an annually recurring sales window nothing in the 14-title catalog
serves. Deliberately distinct from the reserved Hollowtide niche (seasonal
*folk dark fantasy* — dread register); this is the opposite pole, warm
hearth-register historical. Victorian-London setting keeps every C3
Netherlands register untouched, and the *Twelfth Night* (Jan 5) hook gives
it a niche inside the crowded Christmas shelf instead of head-on
competition.

## 1. Title

- [ ] Working title: **The Twelfth Cake**. Subtitle candidate: *"A Twelfth
      Night Novella"* — REQUIRED, not optional (see §2: the bare phrase is a
      historical common noun and needs the fiction signal); recommended ON.
- [ ] Bare-word searchability: "twelfth" is a strong, rare stem; "cake"
      alone drifts to baking content. The §2 scan shows the full phrase's
      search space is occupied by food-history material, not fiction — the
      subtitle is what separates the novella from the recipe.
- [ ] Homophone / autocorrect drift: "twelfth" is one of English's most
      misspelled words (*twelth*, *twelvth*) — real typed-search leakage;
      note for the keyword pass at graduation. "Twelfth Night" also pulls
      Shakespeare adjacency — free association, wrong shelf; the word
      "cake" in the title does the disambiguating.

## 3. Market verification

- [ ] Length class: **planned novella (~20k–30k words)** — the Victorian

- [ ] Category comps: the seasonal-mystery/seasonal-fiction shelf reliably
      supports short books at full price — e.g. P.D. James, *The Mistletoe

      accessed 2026-07-13); the Twelfth-Night-specific angle rides the same
      shelf with less head-on competition (the Pen Vogler / Dickens Museum
      material in §2 shows live reader interest in the tradition itself).
      Adult/literary novella ebook band inherited from the plan's verified
      §1 table
      ([sources cited there](../PUBLISHING-PLAN.md#1-verified-price-points-per-category)).
- [ ] Series/KU context: standalone, BUT with a real annual-backlist
      property: holiday titles resell every December–January without new
      spend — the closest thing to series economics a standalone can have.
      A "feast-day novella" line (Michaelmas, Midsummer) is *possible* and
      recorded as possibility only. KDP Select/KU per plan §4 (90-day
      reversible) fits the seasonal spike pattern well.
- [ ] Seasonality stated plainly: sales window ≈ late November – early
      January; the book is expected near-dormant otherwise. Launch timing is
      an owner call in §7.
- [ ] Revenue framing: **unknown-author base case ≈ $0.** Unit economics
      only: $3.99 at the 70% tier ≈ $2.79/sale. No projections.

## 4. Price band

- [ ] Band: adult/literary novella ebook **$3.99–$5.99** → **recommended
