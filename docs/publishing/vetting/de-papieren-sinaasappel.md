## 1. Title

- [x] Working title: **De papieren sinaasappel** — locked by the
      manuscript's own title decision (NOTES.md: alternatives *Oranje*,
      *Achter het behang* considered and rejected with reasons; not
      relitigated here). Subtitle: ***Een roman over de Hongerwinter*** —
      recommended ON and already part of the manuscript's title page:
      "Hongerwinter" is the load-bearing Dutch search term and instantly
      places the book for Dutch readers.
- [x] Bare-word searchability: full phrase is distinctive; component words
      are individually hopeless, so the subtitle carries the search weight
      — same structure as the EN packet's call, and stronger here because
      the subtitle keyword is a native category name.
- [x] Homophone / autocorrect drift: none serious in Dutch
      ("sinaasappel" has no close title-frequency neighbor;
      "papieren"/"papier en" split is a spacing typo, not a drift target).

> dicht, en Mies Verhoeven — een drukkersweduwe met twee kinderen en een
