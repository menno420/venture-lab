**Title:** The Puddle Museum · **Category:** kids / picture book
(ages 4–8) · **Date vetted:** 2026-07-13 (concept-stage)

**One-line pitch:** After the rain, Juno opens her museum — admission: one
leaf. Every puddle on the lane is an exhibit: the Sky Mirror, the Upside-
Down Tree, the Worm Rescue Wing. But the sun is coming out, and a museum
that evaporates teaches its curator (and one skeptical big brother) what
collections are really for. Rainy-day imagination picture book, 12–14
spreads, about wonder you can't keep.

**Catalog fit (why this tiles):** kids cluster gap — weather/wonder/
imaginative-play register. Overlaps nothing reserved: not space (Comet
Biscuit, Star Pirates), not loud-animal humor (Bram), not mystery/
friendship (Painted Stones), not bedtime/night (Lantern Door, Pippa), not
river-journey (Tummel). Pure daytime-wonder shelf, currently empty in the
catalog.

## 1. Title

- [ ] Working title: **The Puddle Museum**. Subtitle candidate: none needed

      genre); optional *"A Rainy-Day Story"* — default OFF.
- [ ] Bare-word searchability: full phrase distinctive; "puddle" alone is a
      crowded kids stem (see §2) — full phrase does the work.

      is real and funny-wrong; noted so the blurb's first sentence anchors
