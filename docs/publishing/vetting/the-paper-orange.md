**Title:** The Paper Orange · **Category:** adult / literary-historical
(WWII) · **Date vetted:** 2026-07-13 (concept-stage)

**One-line pitch:** Hunger Winter, Amsterdam 1944–45 — a printer's widow
survives the famine winter by forging ration coupons on her late husband's
press, while hiding a paper orange (the forbidden royal color) folded into
the wallpaper for the day the country comes back. Literary-historical
novella; grief, courage-in-small-rooms, and what people feed each other when
there is nothing left.

**Catalog fit (why this tiles):** third Netherlands era-register alongside
the keyword map's C3 pair — The Weigh House owns *modern* Netherlands crime,
Ultramarine owns *Golden-Age* historical; WWII-occupation is an unclaimed
era with a large proven readership. Cheap research reuse: Amsterdam
geography (Weigh House) + Dutch cultural/historical grounding (Ultramarine)
carry over; only the 1944–45 layer is new.

## 1. Title

- [ ] Working title: **The Paper Orange**. Subtitle candidate: *"A Novel of
      the Hunger Winter"* — genre- and era-signalling in five words;
      recommended ON (the bare title reads ambiguous without it).
- [ ] Bare-word searchability: full three-word phrase is distinctive; the
      component words ("paper", "orange") are individually hopeless, so the
      subtitle carries the search weight. No Lull-style bare-word handicap
      for the full phrase.
- [ ] Homophone / autocorrect drift: none serious. In-catalog note: sits one
      consonant cluster from this same wave's *The Pepper Ledger*
      (paper/pepper) — different audiences (adult vs YA), so confusion cost
      is low; recorded for honesty.
