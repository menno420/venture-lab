## 1. Title

- [x] Working title: **De Driekoningentaart** — locked by the
      manuscript's own title decision (NOTES.md: alternatives *De
      twaalfde taart*, *Driekoningenavond*, *De taart in het raam*
      considered and rejected with reasons — notably *Driekoningenavond*
      rejected as a head-on collision with the conventional Dutch title
      of Shakespeare's *Twelfth Night*; not relitigated here). Subtitle:
      ***Een driekoningennovelle*** — **mandatory ON**: it is the NL
      carrier of the EN packet's MANDATORY fiction-marker subtitle (the
      §2 recipe-crowding mitigation).
- [x] Bare-word searchability: "driekoningentaart" is a real,
      high-recognition Dutch word — searchable, but crowded by *recipes*
      (the same honest Moderate common-noun crowding the EN packet priced
      in); the fiction-marker subtitle is the standing mitigation.
- [x] Homophone / autocorrect drift: none serious ("driekoningentaart"
      has no close title-frequency neighbor; capitalization variants are
      search-neutral).
