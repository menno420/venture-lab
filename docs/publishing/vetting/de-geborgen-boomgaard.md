## 1. Title

- [x] Working title: **De geborgen boomgaard** — locked by the
      manuscript's own title decision (NOTES.md: alternatives *De
      sloopboomgaard*, *De boomgaard op de werf*, *De wrakhoutgaard*
      considered and rejected with reasons; not relitigated here).
      Subtitle: ***Een solarpunknovelle*** — recommended ON: solarpunk is
      barely established as a Dutch shelf, so the subtitle has to do the
      subgenre-search work the shelf cannot.
- [x] Bare-word searchability: "geborgen boomgaard" is natural Dutch but
      unclaimed as a title (NOTES.md scan: the exact phrase surfaces only
      as loose real-estate/school copy) — favourable; and *geborgen*
      carries the book's own double meaning (salvaged AND sheltered,
      *geborgenheid*).
- [x] Homophone / autocorrect drift: the EN's salvage→savage drift has no
      Dutch analogue; "geborgen" ↔ "verborgen" (hidden) is a one-letter
      neighbor that would still read as a plausible book title — noted as
      low-cost, the subtitle carries the genre signal either way.
