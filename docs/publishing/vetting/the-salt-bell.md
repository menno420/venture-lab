**Title:** The Salt Bell · **Category:** adult / literary-historical
(postwar Netherlands — the 1953 Zeeland flood) · **Date vetted:** 2026-07-16
(manuscript-backed)

**One-line pitch:** Zoutkerke, February 1953. Marinus de Ridder has kept the
church, wound the tower clock, and rung the one bell of his Zeeland village
for thirty years — a bell recast after the war to replace the two the
occupiers took for their metal. On the last night of January the North Sea
comes over the dike in the dark, before any warning can reach the sleeping
polder, and the bell that has struck so many ordinary hours becomes the
alarm, then the beacon that steers the rescue boat in, then the toll for the
dead — and finally, in the long rebuilding, a bell that has to learn its
ordinary job again. No villain: the sea, and the low ground itself, and the
great sea-answer (the Delta Works) that comes only after the count.

**Catalog fit (why this tiles):** the SIXTH Netherlands era-register
(recorded in the map's C3 this PR): The Weigh House owns modern-crime,
Ultramarine owns Golden-Age 1654, The Paper Orange owns WWII-occupation,
The Sweetwater Sea owns interwar 1927–1932, The Wire Garden owns WWI
1915–1918 neutral-border — the **postwar 1953-flood / Zeeland-Watersnood
register** was unclaimed, and the `DECISIONS.md` keyword-discipline entry
pre-checked the disjointness (a 1953 coastal-disaster novel shares no
occupation, war, Golden-Age, or interwar-engineering stems; the only shared
bare stems — `Dutch`, `Netherlands`, `historical`, oyster/`fishermen` — are
watched adjacencies, disjoint intent). Note the deliberate near-neighbour to
The Sweetwater Sea: both are Dutch water-and-a-drowned-trade novellas, but
disjoint by decade, disaster-vs-slow-closure, and trade (oyster culture vs
anchovy salting; 1953 flood vs 1927–32 Zuiderzee enclosure).

## 1. Title

- [x] Working title: **The Salt Bell** — the book's own final image (the
      green-white bloom of dried salt the flood leaves filmed on the recast
      bronze, which the koster chooses never to scour off, so that the salt
      becomes "the bell's inscription now, the true one"). Subtitle:
      ***A novella of the Zeeland flood*** (the manuscript's own front-matter
      subtitle) — **mandatory ON**, because it does the disambiguation work
      §2 requires and names the register.
- [x] Bare-word searchability: "salt" alone and "bell" alone are
      near-unsearchable common nouns drifting to unrelated shelves (see §2);
      the full phrase + subtitle carry the search weight.
- [x] Homophone / autocorrect drift: none serious. The real drift is
      common-noun search crowding — see §2.
