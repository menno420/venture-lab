**Title:** The Sweetwater Sea · **Category:** adult / literary-historical
(interwar Netherlands) · **Date vetted:** 2026-07-14 (manuscript-backed)

**One-line pitch:** Wieringen, 1927–1932. Jaap Rotgans has salted anchovy
for forty years on a sea the whole country has decided to close; his only
son takes work on the Afsluitdijk that will do it. Five years — of tides,
runs, a marriage kept in a ledger, and a trade — before the last gap
shuts at De Vlieter and the salt goes out of the water for good. No
villain: the dike was right, and that is the tragedy.

(recorded in the map's C3 this PR): The Weigh House owns modern-crime,

surfaced — but the phrase is an established geographic epithet**, which
is a real finding this packet records rather than buries:

| Title | Collision | Finding | Action |
|---|---|---|---|
| The Sweetwater Sea | **Low–Moderate (epithet drift, not a book collision)** | No exact-title novel. But "Sweetwater Sea" is the standing English rendering of *La Mer Douce* — Champlain's 1615 name for Lake Huron, in live use for the Great Lakes: a shipwreck history — "Shipwrecks of Lake Huron: The Great Sweetwater Sea" (Jack Parker, nonfiction; [amazon.com](https://www.amazon.com/Shipwrecks-Lake-Huron-Great-Sweetwater/dp/093221245X), accessed 2026-07-14); a Great Lakes documentary site ("The Sweetwater Seas", [thesweetwaterseas.com](https://www.thesweetwaterseas.com/), accessed 2026-07-14); and a 2010 Lake Huron conservation strategy titled "The Sweetwater Sea". Nearest fiction shares one stem only: *Sweetwater* (Jason Heaton, Great Lakes dive thriller; [jasonheatonauthor.com](https://jasonheatonauthor.com/shop/p/depthcharge-h3zny)), *Sweetwater* (Laurence Yep, SF), *Sweetwater Creek* (Anne Rivers Siddons), *Sweet Water* (Christina Baker Kline) — all accessed 2026-07-14. | Keep title — the epithet is a shared poetic commons, not a book claim, and the Zuiderzee is *also* a sweetwater sea by the book's own closing logic. Subtitle *"A novella of the Zuiderzee"* is MANDATORY on cover and listing to steer Great Lakes searchers away and Netherlands readers in. |
