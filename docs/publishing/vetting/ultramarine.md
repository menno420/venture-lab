## 1. Title

- [x] Working title: **Ultramarine** — the costliest pigment, ground from
      imported lapis lazuli; the book's central material and metaphor
      (Ch. 1 opens on the grinding of it; Ch. 12, "The Colour That
      Remains", closes on "the blue you could breathe"). Manuscript
      alternatives already on record in
      [`DECISIONS.md` D-0005](../../../candidates/adult-novels/ultramarine/DECISIONS.md):
      *The Secret of Holland*, *The Blue Hour*.
- [x] Subtitle candidates: **"A Novel of Delft, 1654"** (recommended
      default, ON — two searchable, genre-signalling anchors: place +
      date); alt **"A Novella of the Delft Thunderclap"**. Length
      (novella-class, §3) is disclosed in the blurb, not the subtitle,
      per the Weigh House precedent.
- [x] Bare-word searchability: **poor** — a single evocative word, and a
      crowded one (§2): two same-named literary novels plus the Warhammer
      40k *Ultramarines* franchise dominating fuzzy search. The checklist's
      bare-word warning applies in full; a subtitle is mandatory whichever
      title survives.
- [x] Homophone / autocorrect drift: low (the word is distinctive and
      spell-stable); the risk here is not drift but head-on collision and
      franchise noise ("ultramarine" → "ultramarines" pluralization pulls
      Warhammer results).

## 6. Listing copy (drafted in-repo — nothing published)

- [x] **Blurb/description** (KDP book description, draft v1 — written from
      the manuscript: opening, middle, and closing chapters read this
      session; names, stakes, and phrases taken from the text):

> Delft, autumn 1654. Blue comes hard to Clara Wijnants — by weight, by
> grinding, by the patient ruin of one thing to make another. A widowed
> colour-grinder on the dyers' canal, she keeps her late husband's trade
> alive breaking lapis lazuli into ultramarine, a powder dearer than the
> same weight in coin, for painters like Carel Fabritius — Rembrandt's
> boldest pupil, her customer and something nearly a friend, who dreams
> aloud at her counter of a blue no one has put in a painting before. A
> blue you could breathe.
>
> Two streets from her door, behind the walls of an old convent, the town
> keeps eighty thousand pounds of gunpowder and calls it the Secret of
> Holland — half in joke, the way you make small a thing too large to
> keep looking at.
>
> On the twelfth of October the Secret speaks, and a quarter of Delft is
> gone in a single white instant: Clara's shop, her grinding-boy, her
> painter, and very nearly the orphaned niece who is all the family she
> has left. What remains is a child gone silent, a guild elder who has
> always doubted a woman's right to her stall, and a hoard of the world's
> most precious blue buried somewhere under the ruin. To dig her life
> back out, Clara must do what her trade taught her: break what is
> beautiful, wash what is broken, and learn to be grateful for what
> settles.
>
> A standalone literary novella (~28,000 words) of the historical Delft
> Thunderclap — grief, craft, and the cost of beauty — for readers of
> Tracy Chevalier, Deborah Moggach, and Jessie Burton.

### 6a. Cover design brief (pre-chews the owner's §7 cover click)

- **Mood:** literary-historical, painterly, quiet — Dutch Golden Age
  still-life gravity, not action or romance.
- **Core image direction:** a field of deep ultramarine — a drift of
  ground pigment on a dark table, or a knuckle of raw lapis "the colour of
  a bruised evening, veined with fool's gold" beside a stone muller; low
  Vermeer-style window light from the left. Alternative motif: the black
  silhouette of Delft's Nieuwe Kerk tower against a deepening blue-hour
  sky. One focal element only. (Avoid a goldfinch motif — owned in the
  reader's eye by Donna Tartt's jacket.)
- **Palette:** saturated lapis/ultramarine blues, near-black shadows, one
  warm accent (pyrite gold / candle-flame ochre).
- **Type:** an elegant, slightly antique serif in the literary-historical
  convention (Chevalier/Burton jackets); title large, author name small.
  Must survive thumbnail size.
- **Comp covers:** *Girl with a Pearl Earring* (single luminous subject on
  dark ground), *The Miniaturist* (ornamented period object, rich dark
  palette).
- **Format:** ebook cover, 1600×2560 px minimum, RGB.
