**Title:** The Night Kiln · **Category:** adult / cozy fantasy ·
**Date vetted:** 2026-07-13 (concept-stage)

**One-line pitch:** A village kiln-witch fires people's spoken memories into
everyday pottery — a mug that remembers a grandmother's laugh, a bowl that
holds a first snowfall — until a stranger brings her a memory nobody should
want to keep. Low-stakes, found-family cozy fantasy novella: tea, glaze
chemistry as soft magic, and the ethics of what we choose to preserve.

**Catalog fit (why this tiles):** the catalog's first adult fantasy title
and its first *comfort-register* speculative work — deliberately the
opposite shelf from Hollowtide's reserved YA seasonal/folk **dark** fantasy
niche (comfort vs dread; adult vs YA). Craft-as-spine rhymes with
Ultramarine (pigment) without touching its historical register.

## 1. Title

- [ ] Working title: **The Night Kiln**. Subtitle candidate: *"A Cozy
      Fantasy Novella"* — recommended ON: it is the genre's strongest
      search phrase and signals length honestly.
- [ ] Bare-word searchability: full phrase distinctive; "kiln" alone is a
      pottery-nonfiction shelf — the subtitle steers to fiction.
- [ ] Homophone / autocorrect drift: **kiln → kill** is one letter ("The
      Night Kill" reads as a thriller) and "kiln" is commonly mistyped
      "klin". Real but low-cost: the subtitle and cover carry the cozy
      signal. Noted for the blurb's first line too ("kiln" spelled out in
      context immediately).
