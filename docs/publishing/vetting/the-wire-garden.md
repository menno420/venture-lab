**Title:** The Wire Garden · **Category:** adult / literary-historical
(WWI neutral Netherlands) · **Date vetted:** 2026-07-14 (manuscript-backed)

**One-line pitch:** Castelré, 1915. Lena Cuypers keeps twelve hives on a
meadow that runs down to the Dutch–Belgian border stream. In July the
occupiers string the Dodendraad — a fence with death in the middle of
it — along the far bank; her bees cross it every fair morning at no
cost, and what else learns to cross (letters, then people) is the matter
of the book. No villain: the fence itself is the antagonist, and every
human under it is a person.

**Catalog fit (why this tiles):** the FIFTH Netherlands era-register
(recorded in the map's C3 this PR): The Weigh House owns modern-crime,
Ultramarine owns Golden-Age 1654, The Paper Orange owns WWII-occupation,
The Sweetwater Sea owns interwar 1927–1932 — the WWI 1915–1918
neutral-border / Dodendraad register was unclaimed, and the
`DECISIONS.md` keyword-discipline entry pre-checked the disjointness
(era AND valence vs Paper Orange: neutrality/witness at a watched border
versus occupation/survival under it — different war, different decade).
