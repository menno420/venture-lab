**Title:** The Seed Catalogue Courtship · **Category:** adult / epistolary
romance (clean & wholesome) · **Date vetted:** 2026-07-13 (concept-stage)

**One-line pitch:** Edith Prowse, 61, is closing her family's heirloom seed
catalogue after three generations. Her last customer, a retired engineer
restoring a derelict walled garden, keeps sending order forms with questions
in the margins. Told entirely in order forms, margin notes, seed-packet
instructions, and finally letters, across one full growing year — a
late-life love story that never once says the word.

**Catalog fit (why this tiles):** the catalog's first ROMANCE shelf of any
kind — a whole top-level KDP storefront the 14-title catalog doesn't touch.
Epistolary + older-protagonist + clean is a proven trad niche (Youngson,
Shaffer/Barrows) that self-pub undersupplies; it also stays maximally clear
of every owned register (no mystery, no fantasy, no SF, no Netherlands, no
kids).

## 1. Title

- [ ] Working title: **The Seed Catalogue Courtship**. Subtitle candidate:
      *"A Novel in Letters"* — form-signalling; recommended ON (the
      epistolary form is the selling point, say it on the cover).
- [ ] Bare-word searchability: the full four-word phrase is unique; the
      component phrase "seed catalogue" alone drifts to actual seed
      companies (Baker Creek et al.) — expected and harmless, the full title
      and subtitle carry the search weight.
- [ ] Homophone / autocorrect drift: **catalogue vs catalog** — a real
      regional-spelling fork. Decision: keep the UK spelling (it does
      register work: heirloom, paper, unhurried); the US-spelled variant is
      absorbed as a keyword at graduation, and the KDP title-availability
      recheck (§7) checks BOTH spellings.
