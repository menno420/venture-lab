**Title:** The Windmill Mouse · **Category:** kids / picture book
(ages 3–6) · **Date vetted:** 2026-07-13 (concept-stage)

**One-line pitch:** Mies the mouse lives in the smallest room of a working
windmill and believes she keeps the whole mill turning — oiling one tiny
gear, sweeping one wooden tooth — until the night of the big storm, when
the miller finds out she's been right all along. Gentle competence-and-
kindness picture book, 12–14 spreads, natural fit for the catalog's
existing trilingual EN/NL/DE pipeline.

**Catalog fit (why this tiles):** kids cluster gap — the catalog reserves
space adventure (Comet Biscuit ×3, Star Pirates), loud-animal humor (Bram),
seaside mystery/friendship (Painted Stones), bedtime/door (Lantern Door),
and night-comfort (Pippa). A daytime windmill/miller-mouse *little-helper*
register overlaps none of them, and deliberately does NOT use "bedtime"
phrasing (Lantern Door/Pippa own the night shelf). Netherlands-flavored at
the kids register only — outside both of C3's adult registers
(modern-crime, Golden-Age-historical); reuses the lane's Dutch cultural
grounding for near-free.
