## 1. Title

- [x] Working title: **The Weigh House** — named for De Waag on the
      Nieuwmarkt, load-bearing in the text (Ch. 1 opening image; Ch. 16 is
      titled "The Weigh House" and closes the theme: "the civic faith that
      a thing had a true weight").
- [x] Subtitle candidates: **"An Amsterdam Crime Novel"** (recommended
      default, ON — refines the plan §7 action "Subtitle *A Novel*" by
      adding two searchable, genre-signalling words that also break the
      homophone drift, §2); alt **"An Amsterdam Canal Mystery"**. Length
      (novella-class, §3) is disclosed in the blurb, not the subtitle —
      "novel" on a 36k text follows the crime-genre convention where
      "novella" undersells; owner may swap in "A Novella" for strict length
      signalling. ⚑ Final title+subtitle pick is an OWNER choice (§7).
- [x] Bare-word searchability: not a bare word — three-word phrase; but see
      §2: the phrase's dominant search neighbour is "The Weight", so the
      subtitle is doing real work here, not decoration.
- [x] Homophone / autocorrect drift: **YES — the plan's flagged risk is
      real and was observed live**: weigh→weight drift returned *The
      Weight* titles for every "The Weigh House" book search run this
      session (§2). Also weigh house→"weight house"/"way house" autocorrect
      potential. Mitigation: the subtitle carries distinctive terms
      ("Amsterdam", "Crime") that survive the drift.

## 6. Listing copy (drafted in-repo — nothing published)

- [x] **Blurb/description** (KDP book description, draft v1 — written from
      the manuscript: opening chapters and closing chapters read this
      session):

> Amsterdam, January. Police diver Sanne Kessler goes down into the black
> water of a canal off the Nieuwmarkt and brings up a man who did not
> drown by accident. He has been weighted to the bottom with dive belts —
> police-issue, stamped with the serial of her own unit's store.
>
> Two years ago, the same water took her husband. The department ruled it
> an accident, and the man who held her safety line put a warm towel
> around her shoulders and told her to go get in the van. Now the same
> quiet machinery is closing over a new body — stress, low mood, a man who
> walked into a canal — and this time Sanne will not let the door shut.
>
> With Nadia Okonkwo, a detective new to the city and not yet owned by it,
> she follows the dead man — a frightened compliance clerk at a canal-house
> notary — into a laundering scheme running through Amsterdam's freshly
> renovated grachtenpanden. But the killings carry a diver's signature.
> Whoever weighted the bodies knows the water the way she does. And to
> prove it, she must go back down into the dark that took her husband — on
> a safety line held by the one man she trusts most.
>
> A standalone Amsterdam crime novella (~36,000 words) about grief, trust,
> and the true weight of things — for readers of atmospheric European
> police noir.

### 6a. Cover design brief (pre-chews the owner's §7 cover click)

- **Mood:** cold, atmospheric European police noir — literary-leaning
  crime, not action-thriller. Winter, water, restraint.
- **Core image direction:** a dark Amsterdam canal at winter dusk — black
  water, sleet, canal-house gables or De Waag's towers as low silhouettes;
  a single taut dive line (rope) descending into the water may carry the
  eye down the composition. Alternative motif: a diver's weight belt on
  wet quay stones under scene lights. One focal element only.
- **Palette:** petrol green-black water, slate grey sky, one cold accent
  (the pale blue of a police tent lit from inside, or a strobe-white
  highlight).
- **Type:** bold, calm type in the Euro-noir convention — large title
  (three short words stack well), either a heavy modern serif or a
  condensed sans; author name small. Must survive thumbnail size.
- **Comp covers:** Anja de Jager's Lotte Meerman jackets (Amsterdam canal
  scenes, moody light), Scandinavian/Dutch crime paperback convention
  (large type over a cold landscape).
- **Format:** ebook cover, 1600×2560 px minimum, RGB.
