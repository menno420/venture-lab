## 6. Listing copy (drafted in-repo — nothing published)

- [x] **Blurb/description** (KDP book description, draft v1):

> The Consortium is coming to switch off the ears of Fenua Observatory for
> good. Dr. Teuila "Tuli" Va‘a — the last scientist on a Pacific atoll her
> family has fished for a thousand years — is boxing three decades of data
> for deletion when she finds something impossible in a pulsar's light: a
> modulation so slow that a single syllable takes a century to say. A
> message ten thousand years long, from minds for whom a human life is less
> than one heartbeat.
>
> Decoded, it is a greeting shaped like a question — one that can only be
> answered by a civilization continuous enough to keep speaking for
> centuries. No institution lasts that long. No language. No archive. But
> the island's genealogy — the gafa Tuli left behind as a girl — has carried
> names and meaning faithfully across thirty generations without a single
> written word.
>
> Dying, and racing a defense contractor that wants to haul the discovery
> off the reef, Tuli must marry the machine to the lineage: encode
> humanity's reply, and the promise to keep sending it, into a chant that
> will outlive everyone who will ever hear her name.
>
> A standalone literary science-fiction novella about deep time,
> inheritance, and the only kind of meaning large enough to answer the
> stars — the kind that outlives the one who begins it.

### 6a. Cover design brief (pre-chews the owner's §7 cover click)

- **Mood:** quiet, vast, literary — closer to a lit-fic jacket than a
  genre-SF cover. Not spaceships; not neon.
- **Core image direction:** a radio-telescope dish (or dish silhouette) low
  over a Pacific reef/lagoon at night, star field above; a single slow
  waveform line may run across the composition. Alternative: the waveform
  rendered as a woven/tatau-like pattern band — echoes the genealogy-chant
  spine without costume-shop cultural props.
- **Cultural care:** no sacred iconography, no stock "tribal" clip-art; the
  Samoan elements in the text are load-bearing and respectful — the cover
  should signal *place and ocean*, not ethnicity-as-decoration.
- **Type:** large, calm serif for the title (three short words stack well);
  author name small. Must survive thumbnail size — one focal element.
- **Format:** ebook cover, 1600×2560 px minimum, RGB.
