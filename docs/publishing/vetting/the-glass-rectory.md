**Title:** The Glass Rectory · **Category:** adult / gothic ghost novella ·
**Date vetted:** 2026-07-13 (concept-stage)

**One-line pitch:** 1893, a moorland parish. A curate's sister keeps house
in a rectory whose previous incumbent built an enormous glass conservatory
wing — and then walked into it one January night and was never seen again.
The glass never fogs. The plants never die. And something in there has
started tapping back. Classic-register ghost novella: dread by restraint,
no gore.

**Catalog fit (why this tiles):** the catalog's first adult GOTHIC/GHOST
shelf. Deliberately distinct from both reserved dark niches: Hollowtide
(reserved) is seasonal *folk dark fantasy* — folklore-magic register; The
Undertow (reserved) is *coastal psychological* suspense — non-supernatural,
sea-flavored. This is an inland, supernatural, Victorian classic-ghost-story
register (M.R. James / Susan Hill shelf) that neither reservation touches.

## 1. Title

- [ ] Working title: **The Glass Rectory**. Subtitle candidate: *"A Ghost
      Story"* — the Susan Hill convention, two words of pure genre signal;
      recommended ON.
- [ ] Bare-word searchability: "glass" is hopeless alone; "rectory" is
      distinctive and strongly genre-coded (Borley Rectory, "the most
      haunted house in England", does free atmospheric work). The two-word
      phrase is unique in search today.
- [ ] Homophone / autocorrect drift: "rectory" sits one letter from
      *rectors* and autocorrects toward *directory* on some keyboards —
      noted; the subtitle rescues any drifted search. In-catalog note: this
      same wave's *The Salvage Orchard* also has a glasshouse image in its
      register; keyword sets share zero stems (checked in §6).
