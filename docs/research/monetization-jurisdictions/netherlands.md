# Monetizing Online from the Netherlands — Rules & Requirements

> **Status:** `reference` — owner-directed research (2026-07-11)
>
> **Disclaimer:** Research summary compiled from public sources on 2026-07-11. This is **not legal or tax advice**. Rules change and individual circumstances vary — verify with a qualified Dutch accountant or the Belastingdienst before acting. Figures marked "not verified" could not be confirmed against a primary source this session. Sourcing note: direct fetches to belastingdienst.nl / kvk.nl / business.gov.nl were egress-blocked this session, so figures are cited to those official pages as surfaced via web search, with secondary corroboration; the few unverifiable items are flagged inline.

Scope: an individual starting to monetize online from NL — digital products (paid zip/kit/ebook downloads via Gumroad-style checkout, possible Stripe), photography/stock licensing, and (future) physical collectibles.

## 1. Hobby vs. Entrepreneur ("ondernemer") vs. "Resultaat uit overige werkzaamheden"

There is **no fixed revenue line** — the Belastingdienst weighs the whole picture:
- **Profit / profit expectation** — you must structurally profit or realistically plan to (a "bron van inkomen"). ([belastingdienst.nl — wanneer bent u ondernemer](https://www.belastingdienst.nl/wps/wcm/connect/bldcontentnl/belastingdienst/zakelijk/winst/inkomstenbelasting/wanneer_bent_u_ondernemer_voor_de_inkomstenbelasting/), 2026-07-11)
- **Entrepreneurial risk**, **independence**, and **number of clients** (relying on one client looks like employment). (same page, 2026-07-11)
- **Time invested** — the 1,225-hour "urencriterium" separately gates deductions (§4). ([zzp-nederland.nl](https://www.zzp-nederland.nl/kennisbank/ondernemer-belastingdienst), 2026-07-11)

**Hobby sphere:** no realistic profit prospect → not an income-tax entrepreneur, no fixed turnover threshold. ([zzp-nederland.nl — hobby naar ondernemen](https://www.zzp-nederland.nl/kennisbank/van-hobby-naar-ondernemen-wanneer-wel-en-wanneer-niet-inschrijven-bij-de-kvk), 2026-07-11)

**Middle category — Resultaat uit overige werkzaamheden (ROW):** more than a hobby but not an enterprise (a durable organisation of capital+labour is lacking) → taxed in **box 1** as ROW; you declare net result but **cannot** use entrepreneur deductions. ([dezaak.nl](https://www.dezaak.nl/financien/belastingen/inkomsten-uit-overige-werkzaamheden-zo-werkt-het/), 2026-07-11)

Entrepreneurship is assessed **separately for income tax and for VAT** — you can be a VAT entrepreneur without being an income-tax entrepreneur. ([zzp-belasting.tips](https://zzp-belasting.tips/ondernemer-voor-de-inkomstenbelasting-en-btw/), 2026-07-11)

## 2. KvK (Chamber of Commerce) Registration

- **When required:** structural economic activity with a profit aim → must register in the Handelsregister; pure hobby → no. ([zzp-nederland.nl](https://www.zzp-nederland.nl/kennisbank/van-hobby-naar-ondernemen-wanneer-wel-en-wanneer-niet-inschrijven-bij-de-kvk), 2026-07-11)
- **One-time registration fee (2026): €85.15** (card only, no cash). Optional extract (uittreksel) €9.60; later changes/deregistration generally free. ([kvk.nl — inschrijfvergoeding](https://www.kvk.nl/inschrijven/inschrijfvergoeding/), 2026-07-11)
- **Eenmanszaak / ZZP setup:** simplest sole-trader form; KvK forwards your data to the Belastingdienst, which issues your VAT numbers (btw-id + OB-nummer). ([kvk.nl — fotograaf](https://www.kvk.nl/starten/voor-jezelf-beginnen-als-fotograaf/), 2026-07-11)

## 3. VAT / BTW — standard rate, KOR, and EU OSS

**Standard rate: 21%** (9% reduced for food/books/etc.; 0% exports/intra-EU B2B). Photography/digital-product licences are **21%**, not 9%. ([government.nl — VAT rates](https://www.government.nl/themes/taxes-benefits-and-allowances/vat/vat-rates-and-exemptions), 2026-07-11; [kvk.nl — fotograaf](https://www.kvk.nl/starten/voor-jezelf-beginnen-als-fotograaf/), 2026-07-11)

**KOR — Kleineondernemersregeling (small-business scheme):**
- **Annual turnover threshold: €20,000** (NL turnover per calendar year). ([belastingdienst.nl — KOR voorwaarden](https://www.belastingdienst.nl/wps/wcm/connect/nl/btw/content/kor-voorwaarden), 2026-07-11)
- **How it works:** charge **no BTW**, file **no** BTW returns — but **cannot reclaim** input BTW. It's an opt-in VAT **exemption**. (same, 2026-07-11)
- **Opt-in:** register via Mijn Belastingdienst Zakelijk; to join from 1 Jan 2026 you had to enrol by 3 Dec 2025. No minimum term since 2025; to stop, notify ≥1 month before quarter-end. ([dezzp.nl](https://www.dezzp.nl/in-2026-deelnemen-aan-de-kleineondernemersregeling-meld-je-voor-3-december-aan/), 2026-07-11)
- Exceed €20,000 mid-year → leave KOR, charge BTW from that point. ([kvk.nl — KOR](https://www.kvk.nl/geldzaken/kleineondernemersregeling-gebruiken/), 2026-07-11)

**EU OSS / One-Stop-Shop (successor to MOSS) — cross-border B2C digital sales:**
- A single **€10,000/year** threshold covers combined intra-EU B2C distance sales of goods + digital/TBE services (downloads, e-books, software). ([EC — VAT One-Stop-Shop](https://vat-one-stop-shop.ec.europa.eu/index_en), 2026-07-11)
- **Below €10,000:** keep charging your **own Dutch VAT rate** on cross-border B2C digital sales. **Above €10,000:** from the crossing sale (no grace period), charge the **customer's country VAT rate**, remitted via one OSS return instead of registering in each state. ([amavat.eu](https://amavat.eu/vat-oss-threshold-explained-what-happens-after-e10000/), 2026-07-11)
- KOR (domestic exemption) vs OSS (cross-border) interaction: confirm with Belastingdienst for your case — **not verified (2026-07-11)**.

## 4. Income Tax — box 1 and self-employed deductions

Sole-trader profit is taxed as **winst uit onderneming in box 1** (progressive). ([kvk.nl — fotograaf](https://www.kvk.nl/starten/voor-jezelf-beginnen-als-fotograaf/), 2026-07-11)
- **Zelfstandigenaftrek 2026: €1,200** — **phasing down**: €6,310 (2022) → €2,470 (2025) → €1,200 (2026) → €900 (2027 target). ([belastingdienst.nl — zelfstandigenaftrek 2026](https://www.belastingdienst.nl/wps/wcm/connect/bldcontentnl/belastingdienst/zakelijk/winst/inkomstenbelasting/veranderingen-inkomstenbelasting-2026/ondernemersaftrek-2026/zelfstandigenaftrek-2026), 2026-07-11)
- **Startersaftrek: €2,123** (2025/2026), usable up to 3× in the first 5 years; add-on to zelfstandigenaftrek; needs the hours criterion. ([mkbservicedesk.nl](https://www.mkbservicedesk.nl/belastingen/aftrekposten/startersaftrek), 2026-07-11)
- **MKB-winstvrijstelling: 12.70%** of profit (after entrepreneur deductions), 2026; applies to all entrepreneurs, no hours criterion. ([deonlinerekenmachine.nl](https://deonlinerekenmachine.nl/zelfstandigenaftrek-en-mkb-winstvrijstelling-2026-bedragen-en-berekening/), 2026-07-11)
- **Urencriterium: 1,225 hours/year** — required for zelfstandigenaftrek + startersaftrek (not MKB-winstvrijstelling). ([belastingdienst.nl](https://www.belastingdienst.nl/wps/wcm/connect/bldcontentnl/belastingdienst/zakelijk/winst/inkomstenbelasting/veranderingen-inkomstenbelasting-2026/ondernemersaftrek-2026/zelfstandigenaftrek-2026), 2026-07-11)

The €1,200/€900 figures rest on 2026 pages that could not be opened directly but are corroborated across multiple 2026 sources (2026-07-11).

## 5. Invoicing Requirements

A valid VAT invoice must contain: sequential invoice number + invoice date; date of supply; your full name/address and the customer's; your **btw-id ("NL…")**; description and quantity of goods/services; per rate the unit price excl. VAT, discounts, VAT rate and VAT amount in euros; the customer's VAT number for intra-EU/reverse-charge ("btw verlegd"). A **simplified invoice** is allowed at **€100 or less incl. VAT**. ([belastingdienst.nl — factuureisen](https://www.belastingdienst.nl/wps/wcm/connect/bldcontentnl/belastingdienst/zakelijk/btw/administratie_bijhouden/facturen_maken/factuureisen/factuureisen), 2026-07-11) Under the KOR you charge no VAT, so invoices show no VAT and typically note the KOR applies (exact wording — **not verified (2026-07-11)**).

## 6. Consumer Protection — Distance Selling of Digital Content

- **14-day right of withdrawal (herroepingsrecht)** for online consumer purchases. ([acm.nl — bedenktijd](https://www.acm.nl/nl/verkoop-aan-consumenten/klantenservice/bedenktijd), 2026-07-11)
- **Instant-download exception:** the withdrawal right can be excluded **only if both** (1) the consumer gives **explicit prior consent** to immediate delivery, and (2) **acknowledges they thereby lose** the withdrawal right — both captured in the order confirmation. ([thuiswinkel.org](https://www.thuiswinkel.org/kennisbank/kennisartikelen/uitzonderingen-op-het-herroepingsrecht/), 2026-07-11; [ictrecht.nl](https://www.ictrecht.nl/blog/herroepingsrecht-op-digitale-inhoud-kun-je-dat-uitsluiten), 2026-07-11)
- If not met, the consumer keeps the right and need not pay for content already received; **ACM** enforces and can fine. (same, 2026-07-11)

**Practical:** a Gumroad/Stripe checkout for instant downloads should present a tick-box where the buyer consents to immediate delivery and acknowledges loss of the 14-day right, reflected in the confirmation email.

## 7. Platform Reporting — DAC7

- **DAC7** (EU Dir. 2021/514): since 1 Jan 2024, platforms (Gumroad, Etsy, eBay…) must collect, verify and **report seller data to tax authorities**. ([belastingdienst.nl — DAC7 for sellers](https://www.belastingdienst.nl/wps/wcm/connect/en/business/content/information-for-sellers-dac7), 2026-07-11)
- **Threshold:** reported if in a calendar year you have **>30 transactions** OR receive **>€2,000**. Reported data: identity, tax ID/VAT number, bank identifier, income paid. Platforms send an annual overview each January. (same, 2026-07-11)
- **Context (not yet in force):** an EU proposal would drop the 30-transaction threshold for goods and raise the annual threshold to €3,000, targeted 2028. ([vatcalc.com](https://www.vatcalc.com/eu/eu-2028-overhaul-of-dac7-marketplace-reporting-rules/), 2026-07-11)

## 8. Payment Rails for NL Individuals / Sole Traders

- **Stripe — available in NL**, pays out to a euro IBAN in the business name; setup expects KvK + VAT number; supports iDEAL. ([stripe.com — payments NL](https://stripe.com/resources/more/how-to-accept-online-payments-in-the-netherlands), 2026-07-11)
- **Mollie — Dutch**, no monthly fee/minimum, pay-per-transaction, fast payouts (e.g. iDEAL €0.29). ([mollie.com](https://www.mollie.com/payments/bank-transfer), 2026-07-11)
- **PayPal** — available; a PayPal Business account lets a sole proprietor trade under a business name. ([zenind.com](https://www.zenind.com/en-US/help/post/how-to-open-a-paypal-account-in-the-netherlands-in-2026-requirements-steps-and-business-tips), 2026-07-11)
- **Gumroad** — supports **direct bank (SEPA) payouts** across the EU including NL; does not support Payoneer/Wise/wire/checks. Verify current Gumroad EU-VAT handling against your own OSS obligations. ([gumroad.com — getting paid](https://gumroad.com/help/article/13-getting-paid), 2026-07-11)

**Bottom line: payment-rail access in NL is easy** — Stripe, Mollie, PayPal and Gumroad SEPA all work directly to a euro bank account.

## 9. Photography / Stock Licensing — and a note on physical toys (CE)

- As **maker you automatically hold copyright (auteursrecht)**; buyers get a **licence**, not ownership. ([kvk.nl — fotograaf](https://www.kvk.nl/starten/voor-jezelf-beginnen-als-fotograaf/), 2026-07-11)
- Licences are **VAT-taxable at 21%**; royalties from foreign stock platforms (Shutterstock/Getty/Adobe) are **taxable Dutch turnover** even though the platform is abroad. (same; [virtualoutcomes.io](https://www.virtualoutcomes.io/boekhouding/blog/boekhouding-voor-fotografen), 2026-07-11)
- The €20,000 scheme is the **KOR**, not the KIA.

**Physical collectibles / plush (reference only, future workstream):** toys on the EU market need **CE marking** + toy-safety compliance; a new **EU Toy Safety Regulation** entered into force 1 Jan 2026 (transition to 1 Aug 2030), adding stricter chemical rules and a digital product passport. Baseline is GPSR (EU) 2023/988; NVWA supervises in NL. ([EC — toys](https://single-market-economy.ec.europa.eu/sectors/toys/placing-toys-eu-market_en), 2026-07-11; [kvk.nl — importing toys](https://www.kvk.nl/en/international/importing-toys-these-are-the-rules/), 2026-07-11)
