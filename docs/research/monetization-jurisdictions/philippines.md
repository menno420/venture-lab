## 1. BIR / DTI Registration for Self-Employed & Sole Proprietors

- **BIR registration:** register with the Revenue District Office covering your business address → TIN + Form 2303 Certificate of Registration, before commencing business. ([respicio.ph](https://www.respicio.ph/commentaries/how-to-register-as-a-freelancer-with-the-bir-in-the-philippines-1), 2026-07-11)
- **DTI trade-name registration:** required only if you trade under a business name different from your legal name. Fees are tiered by scope: ₱200 (Barangay), ₱500 (City/Municipality), ₱1,000 (Regional), ₱2,000 (National), + ₱30 Documentary Stamp Tax (₱230–₱2,030 total); valid 5 years. Scope governs *name protection*, not where you may sell. ([orkids.ph](https://orkids.ph/guides/dti-business-name-registration), 2026-07-11; [DTI BNRS FAQ](https://bnrs.dti.gov.ph/faq), 2026-07-11)
- **Books of accounts:** sole proprietors must register books with the BIR (manual/loose-leaf/computerized). ([respicio.ph](https://www.respicio.ph/commentaries/how-to-register-as-a-freelancer-with-the-bir-in-the-philippines-1), 2026-07-11)
- **Annual Registration Fee — CONFIRMED REMOVED:** the ₱500 ARF was abolished by the **Ease of Paying Taxes Act (RA 11976)**, effective 22 Jan 2024 — no more Form 0605 / ₱500 each January. ([alasoplascpas.com](https://alasoplascpas.com/publications/tax-advisory/no-more-annual-registration-fee/), 2026-07-11; [PIA](https://mirror.pia.gov.ph/news/2024/01/15/bir-waives-annual-registration-fee-for-business-taxpayers), 2026-07-11)
- **Invoicing shift (RR 7-2024, implementing RA 11976):** a single **"Invoice"** is now the primary document for both goods and services (the Official Receipt is demoted to a supplementary document). Non-VAT sellers issue an invoice when a single transaction exceeds ₱500 or on request; old ORs were convertible and usable until 31 Dec 2024. ([Grant Thornton](https://www.grantthornton.com.ph/insights/articles-and-updates1/lets-talk-tax/clarification-for-compliance-with-invoicing-requirements-bir-rr-no-7-2024/), 2026-07-11; ₱500 threshold later amended by RR 11-2024: [Grant Thornton](https://www.grantthornton.com.ph/insights/articles-and-updates1/tax-notes/clarification-on-the-invoicing-requirements-per-rr-no-7-2024-as-amended-by-rr-no-11-2024/), 2026-07-11)

## 2. Income Tax — graduated vs 8% flat

A self-employed individual with gross sales/receipts **≤ ₱3,000,000** and **not VAT-registered** elects each year either:
- **Graduated rates** (0%–35% on net) + separate **percentage tax** (§3); or
- **8% flat tax** on gross sales/receipts + non-operating income **in excess of ₱250,000**, **in lieu of both** graduated income tax **and** percentage tax. ([Grant Thornton — 8% tax](https://www.grantthornton.com.ph/insights/articles-and-updates1/lets-talk-tax/the-8-tax-for-self-employed-individuals/), 2026-07-11; [juan.ac](https://www.juan.ac/blog/know-taxes-8-income-tax-rate), 2026-07-11)

**Mechanics:** for a *purely* self-employed person, the first ₱250,000 of gross is effectively tax-free and 8% applies only above it (no expense deductions — 8% is on **gross**). The ₱250,000 deduction under the 8% option applies **only to purely self-employed** (not mixed-income earners with a salary). ([juan.ac](https://www.juan.ac/blog/know-taxes-8-income-tax-rate), 2026-07-11; [Grant Thornton](https://www.grantthornton.com.ph/insights/articles-and-updates1/lets-talk-tax/the-8-tax-for-self-employed-individuals/), 2026-07-11)

**Election:** signified each year (on the Q1 return or at registration); irrevocable for that year. Cross ₱3M mid-year → reverted to graduated rates + VAT liability. ([BIR RMO 23-2018](https://juan.tax/wp-content/uploads/2018/05/RMO_No.-23-2018.pdf), 2026-07-11)

## 3. Business Tax — percentage tax (3%) vs VAT (12%); ₱3M threshold

- **Percentage tax — 3%** (NIRC Sec. 116, Form 2551Q) for non-VAT persons ≤ ₱3M gross. (Temporarily 1% Jul 2020–Jun 2023 under CREATE; **reverted to 3% from 1 Jul 2023**.) If you elect the 8% option, you do **not** separately pay percentage tax. ([Grant Thornton](https://www.grantthornton.com.ph/insights/articles-and-updates1/tax-notes/reversion-of-rates-of-percentage-tax-minimum-corporate-income-tax-and-regular-income-tax-on-proprietary-educational-institutions-and-not-for-profit-hospitals/), 2026-07-11)
- **VAT — 12%**, **mandatory registration once gross exceeds ₱3,000,000** in any 12-month period (or you expect to). Below ₱3M you stay non-VAT (percentage tax or 8%). ([juan.ac](https://www.juan.ac/blog/know-taxes-8-income-tax-rate), 2026-07-11)

**Relevance:** a creator under ₱3M/year is non-VAT — the **8% flat option** is usually the simplest and often lowest-tax choice; VAT compliance isn't triggered until ₱3M.

## 4. Internet Transactions Act — RA 11967 (2023)

Signed Dec 2023; IRR issued 24 May 2024; created the **DTI E-Commerce Bureau**; 18-month transitory compliance period. ([lawphil.net — RA 11967](https://lawphil.net/statutes/repacts/ra2023/ra_11967_2023.html), 2026-07-11; [Cruz Marcelo primer](https://cruzmarcelo.com/navigating-the-internet-transactions-act-r-a-no-11967-a-primer-on-opportunities-and-risks-for-digital-economy-players/), 2026-07-11)

**Online-merchant obligations:** register/provide identifying info (trade name, address, contacts, business-registration/ID where applicable); publish key identity details prominently on the storefront; ensure goods/services comply with law and that price/description/condition are clearly disclosed (consumer protection). E-marketplaces have added duties (verify/collect merchant identity, maintain a registry). ([Global Compliance News — IRR](https://www.globalcompliancenews.com/2024/10/22/https-insightplus-bakermckenzie-com-bm-consumer-goods-retail_1-philippines-implementing-rules-of-the-internet-transactions-act-issued_10152024/), 2026-07-11)

**Takeaway:** expect to disclose real identity/contact + registration status on your storefront and honor consumer-protection rules; register with the E-Commerce Bureau once its mechanics are operational.

## 5. Withholding on Online Platform Sellers — RR 16-2023

- **Rate — CONFIRMED:** e-marketplace operators and digital financial-service providers withhold **1% creditable withholding tax on one-half of gross remittances** — i.e. an **effective 0.5%** of gross. ([Grant Thornton — digital commerce withholding](https://www.grantthornton.com.ph/insights/articles-and-updates1/lets-talk-tax/withholding-tax-rules-on-digital-commerce/), 2026-07-11)
- **Threshold — CONFIRMED ₱500,000:** no withholding if the seller's cumulative annual gross remittances through that platform have not exceeded **₱500,000** (current or prior year). ([Triple i Consulting](https://www.tripleiconsulting.com/what-philippine-rr-16-2023-withholding-tax-online-sellers/), 2026-07-11; [BusinessWorld](https://www.bworldonline.com/economy/2024/09/02/617654/revisiting-the-tax-compliance-requirements-of-online-sellers/), 2026-07-11)
- **Nature:** *creditable* (advance) tax, offset against annual income tax; platform reports on Form 1601-EQ. Applies to PH e-marketplaces (Shopee/Lazada/TikTok Shop/Grab). Whether it reaches foreign non-registered platforms like **Gumroad is likely NO** but **not verified (2026-07-11)** — no official ruling found; the seller instead self-reports income.

## 6. Payment Rails Reality for PH Residents (decisive)

- **Stripe — NOT available to PH-based businesses (2026).** PH is not among Stripe's fully supported countries; access requires incorporating an entity in a supported jurisdiction (e.g. a US LLC). ([stripe.com/global](https://stripe.com/global), 2026-07-11; [cs-cart.com](https://www.cs-cart.com/blog/stripe-supported-countries/), 2026-07-11)
- **Gumroad payouts to PH:** Gumroad's direct **local-bank (Stripe Connect)** payout **excludes the Philippines** (alongside Brazil/India/Indonesia/Malaysia/Mexico/Thailand). Where local bank isn't supported, Gumroad pays via **PayPal in USD** (~2% payout fee, weekly Fridays UTC, min payout balance **$100** — one source said $10; **not fully verified (2026-07-11)** as Gumroad's live pages returned 403). Gumroad does **not** support Payoneer/Wise/wire/checks directly. ([wise.com — Gumroad fees](https://wise.com/us/blog/gumroad-fees), 2026-07-11; [gumroad.com — Payoneer](https://gumroad.com/help/article/223-payoneer-and-gumroad), 2026-07-11)
- **RISK (verify before relying):** PayPal **suspended** Gumroad's payment service in **Oct 2024**, temporarily blocking withdrawals for PayPal-dependent (bank-unsupported) countries like PH; reporting indicates PayPal **restored** it in **early 2025** and Gumroad expanded direct-bank support. Whether PH was added to the expanded direct-bank list is **not verified (2026-07-11)**. **This is the single biggest open risk for a PH Gumroad lane — confirm directly on Gumroad before committing.** ([AlternativeTo](https://alternativeto.net/news/2024/12/paypal-ends-service-with-gumroad-a-major-blow-to-creators-and-sales/), 2026-07-11)
- **PayPal (PH)** — available; receive USD, transfer to local banks or GCash (fees + FX margin). ([paysend.com](https://paysend.com/en-us/blog/paypal-to-gcash-transfer-philippines-guide), 2026-07-11)
- **Payoneer (PH)** — available/widely used; receive international payments, withdraw to PH banks or cash-in to GCash (~1% Payoneer→GCash fee from 8 Jul 2024). ([Payoneer PH guide](https://www.payoneer.com/resources/country-guides/how-to-receive-international-payments-in-the-philippines/), 2026-07-11)
- **GCash / Maya** — domestic e-wallets, common final hop for withdrawing USD earnings to PHP; not international USD-acquiring solutions themselves.
- **USD→PHP practicality:** typical chain is foreign platform → PayPal/Payoneer (USD) → PH bank/GCash (PHP); each hop adds fees (PayPal ~2% + FX, Payoneer FX ~2%, GCash ~1%). Keep source documentation for AMLA/BIR substantiation.

## 7. Digital-Goods / Cross-Border VAT — RA 12023

**RA 12023 (VAT on Digital Services, signed Oct 2024; collection ~mid-2025)** imposes **12% VAT on digital services consumed in the PH** and **targets NON-RESIDENT digital service providers** selling INTO the PH market (threshold ₱3M PH-sourced sales). ([PwC](https://www.pwc.com/ph/en/tax/tax-publications/taxwise-or-otherwise/2025/soon-taking-effect-the-12-vat-on-digital-services.html), 2026-07-11; [ACCRALAW](https://accralaw.com/2025/02/20/game-changer-for-e-commerce-how-r-a-no-12023s-vat-rules-will-reshape-digital-transactions-in-the-philippines/), 2026-07-11)

**Effect on a PH resident selling OUT:** RA 12023 taxes *inbound* consumption; it does **not** create a new VAT category for a PH-resident creator — you remain under ordinary NIRC rules (§3): non-VAT below ₱3M, VAT above. Sales to foreign customers are generally zero-rated/outside PH VAT scope for a VAT-registered person; a small non-VAT seller simply pays percentage/income tax on the peso equivalent. ([ACCRALAW](https://accralaw.com/2025/02/20/game-changer-for-e-commerce-how-r-a-no-12023s-vat-rules-will-reshape-digital-transactions-in-the-philippines/), 2026-07-11)
