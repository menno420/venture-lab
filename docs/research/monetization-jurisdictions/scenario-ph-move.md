## 1. Immigration / work-rights reality (unmarried)

**The marriage distinction matters.** The **13(a)** resident visa requires a **valid marriage to a Filipino citizen** — an unmarried girlfriend does **not** qualify, and the Philippines has **no de-facto-partner / cohabitation resident visa**. ([immigration.gov.ph 13(a)](https://immigration.gov.ph/visas/immigrant-visa-by-marriage-13a/), 2026-07-11; [filepino](https://www.filepino.com/13a-spouse-visa-guide-foreign-spouses-filipino-citizens/), 2026-07-11)

Realistic long-stay routes for an unmarried NL national:
- **9(a) tourist visa, extended:** Dutch passport = **30 days visa-free** on arrival, extendable to **59 days**, then 1/2/6-month extensions at the Bureau of Immigration (incl. a 6-month LSVVE), **up to a ~36-month continuous cap** for non-visa-required nationals — after which you must exit and re-enter to reset. Any stay ≥6 months needs an **ECC-A** exit clearance. A legal but perpetual-renewal treadmill, not residency. ([incorp.asia — tourist visa extension](https://philippines.incorp.asia/guides/tourist-visa-extension/), 2026-07-11; [DFA LSVVE](https://bangkokpe.dfa.gov.ph/visa/lsvve), 2026-07-11)
- **SRRV (Special Resident Retiree's Visa, via PRA)** — the cleanest unmarried path; grants **indefinite** stay and does not depend on the relationship. **Age floor lowered to 40** (since 1 Sep 2025). Required USD time-deposit (secondary sources, **not verified** vs PRA): no pension — **US$50,000 (40–49)** / **US$30,000 (50+)**; with a qualifying pension (≥US$800/mo) — **US$25,000 / US$15,000**. ([sebsauerborn — SRRV Sept 2025](https://www.sebsauerborn.com/the-brief/philippines-srrv-changes-effective-september-1), 2026-07-11; [PRA](https://pra.gov.ph/SRRVisa), 2026-07-11)
- **SIRV (investor visa)** — ~US$75,000 inward investment (general knowledge, **not verified (2026-07-11)**); capital-heavy, business-tied.

**⚠️ Work-rights gray zone (top immigration-lawyer question — NOT resolved here):** the 9(a) is "for tourism… does not allow you to run a business long-term," and work rights hinge on **whether work is physically performed inside PH**, not where the business is registered — "operating an offshore online business could be interpreted as gainful employment… may trigger the need for a work permit… despite the business being offshore." Many do it informally at real risk (deportation/fines/blacklisting). Clean routes: **9(g) work visa, SWP (≤6 mo), or AEP**. ([lawyer-philippines.com](https://www.lawyer-philippines.com/articles/operating-an-offshore-online-business-while-on-a-tourist-visa-in-the-philippines), 2026-07-11) **#1 lawyer question:** *"Living in PH (tourist visa or SRRV) and running my own foreign-registered digital business earning only from foreign platforms — do I need an AEP/9(g)/SWP, or is it outside PH work-permit scope (no local employer, no local-market activity)?"*

## 2. Tax transition (NL exit → PH residency)

**NL side:** the emigration year you file an **M-form** (hybrid resident/non-resident return); you **de-register from the municipal BRP** (which notifies the Belastingdienst/IND). After genuine emigration you become a **non-resident taxpayer** — NL then taxes only Dutch-source income; worldwide-income taxation ends when Dutch tax residency ends (fact-driven: habitual abode/centre of life, not a day-count). Possible **exit tax / conserverende aanslag** on substantial shareholdings or pensions — flag for the Dutch accountant. ([belastingdienst — M return](https://www.belastingdienst.nl/wps/wcm/connect/en/individuals/content/partly-living-outside-the-netherlands-m-tax-return), 2026-07-11; [netherlandsworldwide](https://www.netherlandsworldwide.nl/tax-return-abroad/year-movement-abroad), 2026-07-11)

**PH side — the decisive rule:** a **resident alien is taxed by PH only on Philippine-source income, NOT on foreign-source income** (unlike resident *citizens*, taxed worldwide). Residency/183-day tests establish when you become a resident alien. ([PwC — PH residence](https://taxsummaries.pwc.com/philippines/individual/residence), 2026-07-11; [respicio](https://www.respicio.ph/commentaries/determining-tax-residency-status-for-foreign-nationals-in-the-philippines), 2026-07-11)

**⚠️ The hard question (top PH-accountant question — NOT resolved here):** is income from digital products sold to **foreign** customers via a **foreign** platform, while the seller sits physically in PH, **Philippine-source** (services are sourced by **place of performance** → work done in PH → taxable to a resident alien) or **foreign-source** (benefit consumed abroad → exempt)? Sources genuinely split — the general services rule points to place-of-performance (PH-source), while consumption-based framing and the new digital-VAT "consumed in PH" test point the other way. **This determines whether the entire PH tax advantage holds.** ([PwC — income determination](https://taxsummaries.pwc.com/philippines/individual/income-determination), 2026-07-11)

## 3. Rails under PH residency + fee leakage

- **Wise** — a PH resident (citizenship not required) can hold USD + PHP, withdraw to PH banks/GCash/Maya; FX from ~0.57%, inbound USD SWIFT ~US$6.11. ([wise.com/ph](https://wise.com/ph/blog/how-to-use-wise-philippines), 2026-07-11; [pricing](https://wise.com/ph/pricing/), 2026-07-11)
- **Payoneer** — receive USD, withdraw to PH bank/GCash; ~2% conversion, +1% GCash fee. ([payoneer PH fees](https://www.payoneer.com/resources/country-guides/payoneer-fees-and-costs-for-filipinos/), 2026-07-11)
- **PayPal PH** — receiving foreign currency then converting to PHP ~**4%** (search-derived, **not verified** vs rendered fee page). ([wise — paypal to bank PH](https://wise.com/ph/blog/paypal-to-bank-account-ph), 2026-07-11)
- **Gumroad → PH** — Stripe payout unavailable in PH, so Gumroad pays PH via **PayPal in USD, ~2%**. ([gumroad — getting paid](https://help.gumroad.com/article/13-getting-paid), 2026-07-11)

**Fee leakage per $100 of gross Gumroad payout** (excludes Gumroad's upstream product fee; conservative):
- **All-PayPal (Gumroad→PayPal→convert in PayPal): ≈ 6%** (~2% payout + ~4% PayPal FX).
- **USD-held → Wise/Payoneer → PHP: ≈ 3% (Wise-optimized) to ~5% (Payoneer + PayPal exit).**
- **Range: ~3%–6%** per $100; PayPal's ~4% FX and Gumroad's ~2% dominate. Holding USD and converting via Wise is cheapest; converting inside PayPal is the most expensive.

## 4. Scenario C — the two-entity NL/PH A/B experiment

The owner's idea: run **two businesses — one from NL, one from PH — and compare results.** Which version is coherent matters.

### 4a. The CLEAN version (the only one that fairly tests "PH-run")
**Two genuinely separate businesses with two genuinely separate owners:**
- **Owner runs the NL business** in his own name (eenmanszaak/KvK, his NL tax) — Gumroad/Stripe, EUR, full EU rails.
- **His PH-resident partner runs the PH business in HER name** as a **Filipino-citizen sole proprietor**. A citizen faces **no foreign-ownership barrier, no minimum capital, no CAEB**: DTI business name (₱ + ₱30 DST, 5-yr) → Barangay/Mayor's permit → BIR Form 1901 → COR (2303) + books + invoices (Form 1906). She gets **full local rails** — PH banks, GCash/Maya business wallets, PayMongo/Xendit/HitPay, PayPal PH — none gated by nationality. Each partner pays tax where they live. ([respicio — foreigner DTI](https://www.respicio.ph/commentaries/can-a-foreigner-register-a-business-name-with-dti-in-the-philippines), 2026-07-11; [permitly — sole prop](https://www.permitly.ph/post/permitly-guide-how-to-register-as-a-sole-proprietor-in-the-philippines), 2026-07-11)

### 4b. The INCOHERENT versions (why they don't work)
- **Owner himself owning/running the PH entity from NL** does **not move his tax** (still NL-resident → NL taxes it) and hits **PH foreign-ownership limits**: a foreigner can't do a plain DTI sole prop; foreign-owned domestic-market enterprises face **~US$200,000** minimum capital (FIA), and **retail** needs **PHP 25M (~US$500k)** under RA 11595. "Same person, two flags" just **doubles compliance without moving results**. ([Grant Thornton — FIA/RTLA](https://www.grantthornton.com.ph/insights/articles-and-updates1/lets-talk-tax/amendments-to-the-foreign-investments-and-retail-trade-liberalization-laws/), 2026-07-11; [aseanbriefing — RA 11595](https://www.aseanbriefing.com/news/philippines-amends-retail-trade-liberalization-act-to-attract-foreign-investment/), 2026-07-11)
- **Registering the PH business in the partner's name while the owner actually funds/controls it** is exactly what the **Anti-Dummy Law** penalizes (beneficial control of a Filipino-reserved activity → up to ~10 years' imprisonment, fines). The clean version only works if the partner **genuinely owns and controls** it, arms-length. ([ndvlaw — Anti-Dummy](https://ndvlaw.com/control-is-the-risk-anti-dummy-law-compliance-for-ceos-cfos-and-general-counsel/), 2026-07-11)
- **Relationship/income-mixing angle (honest):** funds moving between two **unmarried** partners across borders (gifts, shared accounts, one funding the other's business) raises its own tax/anti-dummy questions — **another accountant question**, not resolved here.

### 4c. Experiment design (practical)
Have each entity sell into its **natural channel** rather than splitting one market:
- **NL entity:** Gumroad/Stripe, **EUR** digital products, EU/global buyers.
- **PH entity:** a **GCash/Maya-native own-storefront** (HitPay/PayMongo/Xendit) — because **Shopee/Lazada/TikTok Shop PH prohibit digital-download sales** (Shopee's policy explicitly bars instantly-redeemable digital products; tightened for the Internet Transactions Act). PH domestic digital-goods selling is **plausible but modest and price-sensitive**; revenue potential **not verified**. ([shopee — prohibited items](https://help.shopee.ph/portal/4/article/77276-Prohibited-and-Restricted-Items-Policy), 2026-07-11; [hitpay — digital products](https://hitpayapp.com/blog/best-online-store-builder-philippines), 2026-07-11)
- **Metrics (define now, 90-day window):** net revenue after fees per entity; fee-leakage %; time-to-first-sale; compliance hours logged. 
- **Honest confounder:** different products + different markets means you're comparing **channels + jurisdictions together**, not jurisdictions alone. Keep the digital products as identical as the channels allow to reduce (not eliminate) the confound.

**Partner's PH side (plain-language — her decision entirely; nothing the lane initiates):**
1. Register a DTI business name (bnrs.dti.gov.ph) in her own name as a Filipino citizen.
2. Get Barangay clearance + Mayor's/business permit for her address.
3. Register with BIR (Form 1901) → COR (2303), register books, get Authority to Print/issue invoices (Form 1906); choose the **8% flat** option if gross will stay under ₱3M.
4. Set up a GCash/Maya business wallet + a PH bank account, and a storefront with HitPay/PayMongo (GCash/Maya-native).
5. She owns, controls, and banks the business — not a nominee arrangement. *(These are partner actions, her decision entirely.)*

## 5. Verdict update (for the now-real scenario)

- **Launch from NL now.** Today's rails work (Stripe/Mollie/PayPal/Gumroad → euro IBAN), setup is a €85.15 registration, and the products are **portable** — nothing about starting in NL forecloses a later move.
- **Treat a PH move as a planned transition, not a shortcut.** For an unmarried NL national the realistic path is **SRRV (age 40+, ~US$30–50k parked deposit)** or the **36-month tourist treadmill** — and **two unresolved gates** must be cleared with professionals first: (1) do you need a PH work permit to run a foreign online business from PH soil; (2) is your foreign-platform income PH-source (taxable) or foreign-source (exempt). If it's foreign-source and no work permit is needed, PH residency becomes genuinely attractive on tax; if not, the NL advantage stands.
- **The two-entity A/B test is worth running — but only the clean version:** owner runs the NL entity; his partner runs a **genuinely her-own** PH citizen sole proprietorship into GCash/Maya channels. Anything where the owner controls the PH entity from NL is incoherent (no tax move) and legally risky (Anti-Dummy). Read the results as channel+jurisdiction combined, not jurisdiction alone.

**Bottom line:** portability means there's no rush and no fork — **start earning from NL immediately**, and run the PH arm as a real, partner-owned experiment in parallel while an immigration lawyer and a PH/NL accountant clear the work-permit and source-of-income questions that decide whether a personal PH move actually pays.
