## 1. The Sweetwater Sea — **WRITTEN this session** (28/30: E5 C5 X4 M4 S5 F5)

Wieringen, 1927–1932. Jaap Rotgans, an anchovy-salter in his late fifties,
watches the Afsluitdijk close the mouth of his sea while his only son takes
work on the dike itself. Five years between the first dredgers and the
closing of De Vlieter: a man whose trade is salt watching the water ordered
fresh. No villain — the antagonist is a public work of genuine national
good, which is the point. Market: literary historical, Dutch-setting shelf
continuity with The Paper Orange (different era-register: interwar
engineering vs WWII occupation); comps in the quiet-maritime lane.
Collision note: "Sweetwater Sea(s)" is a Great Lakes nickname in some
nonfiction — full phrase distinct; re-scan at packet time.

## 2. The Wire Garden — **WRITTEN this session** (26/30: E4 C4 X5 M4 S4 F5)

Brabant border village, 1915–1918. The Dodendraad — the lethal electric
fence the occupiers of Belgium strung along the neutral Dutch border — runs
at the bottom of a beekeeper's meadow; her hives stand within sound of it,
and what crosses with the bees is letters, then people. The Netherlands
neutral: a war watched at the fence, not lived under. Risk to manage:
forbidden-aid register adjacency to The Paper Orange — disjoint by valence
(neutrality/witness vs occupation/survival). Clean title collision scan.
